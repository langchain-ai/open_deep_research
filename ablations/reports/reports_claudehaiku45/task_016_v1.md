## Red Rooster: Company Background

Red Rooster was established in 1972 when Peter and Theo Kailis opened the first location in Kelmscott, Western Australia [1]. The chain quickly proved successful, growing to 45 stores across Western Australia and Victoria before Myer purchased it in 1981, at which point it was the fourth-largest fast food group in Australia [1]. Following subsequent ownership changes, Australian Fast Foods acquired Red Rooster in 2002 and merged it with Chicken Treat [1]. By 2010, most Red Rooster locations had converted from company-owned to franchise operations [1]. The chain is now owned by Craveable Brands under PAG Asia Capital and remains a significant Australian fast food presence, with headquarters in Chatswood, New South Wales, and over 7,500 employees [1].

The restaurant specializes in roast chicken alongside burgers, chips, salads, and beverages. In 2021, Red Rooster introduced fried chicken to its menu for the first time in nearly 50 years, expanding beyond its traditional roast chicken focus [2].

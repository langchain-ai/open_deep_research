# Comprehensive Relocation Analysis: Austin, Raleigh, and Nashville for a Senior Mechanical Engineer Family  

This report provides an in-depth, data-driven comparison of Austin, Texas; Raleigh, North Carolina; and Nashville, Tennessee to assist a 38-year-old senior mechanical engineer earning $95,000 annually, living with a spouse and 6-year-old daughter, in choosing a relocation city that balances reduced cost of living, strong career growth potential, excellent K-5 education (schools rated 8+), and commute feasibility under 30 minutes. The analysis incorporates median home prices in high-rated school districts, property tax burdens, aerospace and manufacturing job market depth, public school performance metrics for kindergarten through 5th grade, commute times to relevant industrial zones, and a detailed 10-year financial outlook accounting for salary growth, housing appreciation, and total tax burdens.

---

## 1. Median Home Prices in School Districts Rated 8+ Out of 10  

### Austin, TX  
Austin's housing market in top school districts is segmented heavily by district prestige, with pronounced home price premiums reflecting school ratings:  
- **Eanes ISD** (highest-ranked district): $1.5M to $3M+  
- **Lake Travis ISD**: $800K to $1.5M+  
- **Austin ISD** (notably strong neighborhoods like Tarrytown and Mueller): $600K to $1.2M  
- **Round Rock ISD** (solid quality, more affordable): $450K to $750K  
- **Leander ISD**: $400K to $700K  
- **Del Valle ISD** (more affordable but farther from core industrial hubs): $350K to $550K  
Median sales price metro-wide was approximately **$426,220 in early 2026**, reflecting a slight 3% year-over-year decline. Homes in 8+ rated school districts carry a premium of 20-60% above median metro prices, markedly increasing housing costs for high-quality schooling.  
(Sources: [2], [3])  

### Raleigh, NC  
Raleigh presents more affordable housing relative to Austin in top-rated school zones, with a broad range depending on neighborhood:  
- **North Hills** and **Oakwood** neighborhoods feature homes priced at $920K to $1M+, exhibiting strong appreciation and school quality.  
- More moderately priced areas like **North Raleigh** and **Northwest Raleigh** offer median home prices of approx. $455K and $497K respectively.  
- Wake County overall home median was near **$436,250 in early 2026**, stable with slight market cooling.  
- Elementary schools in specific zip codes such as 27613 boast 10/10 ratings, with proficiency rates in reading and math averaging above 65%.  
(Sources: [1], [5], [15])  

### Nashville, TN  
Nashville offers moderately priced homes with a mix of quality ratings:  
- Median listing prices near **$525,900** (2026) across metro, with clustering in areas like Green Hills and Germantown priced in the $480K to $515K range.  
- Williamson County, a nearby affluent district, has homes upwards of $850K tied to top-tier school systems.  
- Metro Nashville Public Schools average a B– grade overall, with pockets of high-performing elementary schools rated 8+ and above.  
(Sources: [9], [20])

## 2. Property Tax Burden as a Percentage of Home Value  

### Austin, TX  
- Effective property tax rate estimates range from **1.75% to 2.2%** annually, with slight increases forecasted for 2026 due to valuation dynamics.  
- Property taxes remain high, driven by reliance on these taxes instead of state income tax revenue.  
- On a median home (~$426K), the annual property tax is approximately $7,500 to $9,000.  
(Sources: [16], [17], [20])  

### Raleigh, NC  
- Effective property tax rate is comparatively low at approximately **0.62% to 0.76%**, with Wake County averaging 0.66%-0.76% depending on locality.  
- For a $436,000 home, annual tax bills around $2,860 to $3,300 are typical.  
- Recent revaluation efforts aim to maintain revenue neutrality, balancing tax changes. State income tax applies separately but property tax remains affordable.  
(Sources: [21], [22], [25])  

### Nashville, TN  
- Tennessee has one of the lowest property tax rates nationally, with Davidson County's effective rate near **0.57%**.  
- Based on a median home value of ~$470,000, annual property taxes approximate $2,675.  
- Recent property value increases led to higher nominal tax bills, but the overall rate remains low due to the property's assessed value methodology (25% of appraised market value).  
(Sources: [26], [27], [28])

## 3. Job Market Depth for Senior Mechanical Engineers in Aerospace and Manufacturing  

### Austin, TX  
- Over the last 12 months, **approximately 815 job postings** for senior mechanical engineers have been recorded, with around 285 specifically tied to aerospace manufacturing.  
- Employment includes roles at leading firms such as SpaceX, BAE Systems, EVONA, and Veteran Hiring Solutions.  
- Strong aerospace and manufacturing sectors coexist with a vibrant tech innovation ecosystem.  
(Sources: [1], [2], [3])  

### Raleigh, NC  
- The Raleigh-Durham area displayed roughly **930 senior mechanical engineer job postings** in the last year, with 239 in aerospace and 439 in manufacturing sectors.  
- Employers include NC State University, Siemens, Amgen, and Amazon's manufacturing branches.  
- The region's Research Triangle Park supports high industry diversification and technical roles.  
(Sources: [4], [5], [6])  

### Nashville, TN  
- Around **106 senior mechanical engineer postings** were visible over 12 months, with 19 targeted aerospace engineering roles.  
- The manufacturing sector is growing but smaller than Austin and Raleigh.  
- Major employers lean towards healthcare IT and related advanced manufacturing.  
(Sources: [7], [8])

## 4. Kindergarten through 5th Grade Public School Performance Metrics  

### Austin, TX  
- Austin ISD’s K-5 math proficiency averages about **44%**, with reading proficiency around **54%**, both near or slightly above Texas averages.  
- Kindergarten readiness hovers near **50%**, with economic disparities noted.  
- Top-rated schools in selective districts (e.g., Eanes ISD) boast proficiency rates exceeding 90%, indicating highly localized school quality variation.  
(Sources: [10], [12], [13])  

### Raleigh, NC  
- Elementary proficiency rates in top-rated schools reach **69% math** and **67% reading** in districts like 27613.  
- Approximately **66% of children aged 3-5** are considered school-ready across North Carolina.  
- Wake County scores consistently in the mid to high tier of state performance ratings for early grades.  
(Sources: [15], [36], [37])  

### Nashville, TN  
- Metro Nashville’s K-5 proficiency averages are lower, near **28%-32%** in reading and math at district-wide levels.  
- Several magnet and charter schools surpass this substantially, exceeding **70% proficiency** in some cases.  
- Intervention programs are active to boost readiness and retention due to initial proficiency challenges.  
(Sources: [21], [23], [43])

## 5. Commute Feasibility Under 30 Minutes to Aerospace and Manufacturing Industrial Zones  

### Austin, TX  
- Average commute is approximately **24.5 minutes**, with some zip codes (e.g., 78705) as low as 16.5 minutes off-peak.  
- Key industrial zones: Buda Gateway Industrial Park (new, south Austin), Firefly Aerospace in Cedar Park, Tesla Gigafactory along the airport corridor.  
- Traffic congestion on I-35 remains a concern, but toll roads SH 130 and SH 45 provide time-saving alternatives. Northern suburbs like Round Rock can exceed 30 minutes during rush hour.  
(Sources: [25], [26], [27])  

### Raleigh, NC  
- Average commute time is roughly **23.6 minutes**, shorter than the national average.  
- Industrial hubs such as Research Triangle Park are within 20–35 minutes from residential areas like Cary and Apex.  
- Ongoing highway expansions (I-40 widening, I-540 loop) improve accessibility; public transit options complement roadway infrastructure.  
(Sources: [30], [33], [34])  

### Nashville, TN  
- Average commute time sits close to **24 minutes**, with a range of 15.8 to 36.8 minutes depending on residential location.  
- Primary corridors include I-65, I-24, and I-40 connecting industrial zones and residential neighborhoods.  
- Population growth causes increasing congestion, potentially impacting commutes outside of central Davidson County zones.  
(Sources: [36], [37])

## 6. 10-Year Financial Outlook: Salary Adjustments, Housing Appreciation, and Total Tax Burden  

### Salary Projections (BLS and Market Data)  
- **Austin:** Median senior mechanical engineer base salaries range roughly **$160,000 to $230,000**, with a median around $192,000 total comp in 2026. Competitive tech and aerospace sectors drive strong growth.  
- **Raleigh:** Salaries range approximately **$108,000 to $164,000**, with median annually near $130,000. Steady regional growth in professional and scientific sectors supports upward trends.  
- **Nashville:** Salary ranges $103,000 to $158,000, with a median about $118,000; job market smaller but expanding in manufacturing and advanced tech industries.  
(Sources: [9], [10], [11], [12])  

### Housing Appreciation Trends Since 2020  
- **Austin:** Saw a rapid 72.5% price increase (2020–mid 2022), followed by a 26% price correction through 2025; median home price stabilized near $426K in early 2026. Expected long-term appreciation 3-5% annually.  
- **Raleigh:** Experienced moderate, steady appreciation averaging about **4.4% per year** over past 5 years, with median home prices near $436K and market nearing balance.  
- **Nashville:** Steady growth near 5% annually, median prices around $470K; significant 2025 surge led to tax value reassessments but market remains moderately competitive.  
(Sources: [15], [16], [17], [18], [19])  

### Tax Burden on $95,000 Income  
- **Austin, TX:**  
  - No state income tax; federal + FICA about 22-24% effective tax rate.  
  - Property taxes high (~1.75%-2.2%), sales taxes combined ~8.25%.  
  - Approximate annual property tax on $426K home: $7,500–$9,000.  
- **Raleigh, NC:**  
  - State income tax flat at 3.99% (2026), expected to decrease in future years.  
  - Property tax low at ~0.7%, sales tax ~7.25%.  
  - Annual property tax approx. $2,860–$3,300.  
- **Nashville, TN:**  
  - No state income tax; federal similar to others.  
  - Property tax lowest among three (~0.57%), projected annual tax near $2,675.  
  - Sales tax higher (~9.75%).  
(Sources: [20], [21], [22], [23], [24])

### Final Evaluation

- **Raleigh, NC** stands out as the most balanced and cost-efficient option. It offers the most affordable median home prices within strong school districts, a well-developed aerospace and manufacturing job market, and commute feasibility with moderate traffic. Although a state income tax applies, its rate is moderate and slated for further reductions, balancing the very low property tax burden. The K-5 school performance is strong, making it a particularly attractive city for families prioritizing early education and overall affordability.

- **Nashville, TN** offers the lowest combined tax burden due to no state income tax and low property taxes. Home prices for high-rated school districts are somewhat higher than Raleigh’s, and the job market, while growing, is smaller. Public school proficiency scores vary, with promising results in selective magnet and charter schools. Commutes are feasible but affected by rising congestion. This city may appeal for those seeking tax efficiency and moderate housing costs near quality schools.

- **Austin, TX** offers the strongest salary prospects and the largest aerospace and manufacturing job market, but housing costs—even in good school districts—are significantly higher, further compounded by a relatively high property tax burden. Commute times can exceed 30 minutes for many suburbs, and public school performance varies widely. Austin remains the best choice for maximizing income with solid career growth but imposes higher cost of living and potential commute stress.

- Review local housing market forecasts and traffic infrastructure plans to inform timing of purchase and location preferences.

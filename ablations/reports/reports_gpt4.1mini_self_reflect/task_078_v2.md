## 1. International Flight: Hyderabad (HYD) to Bangkok (BKK)  

- **Airlines:** Budget carriers such as Thai AirAsia and IndiGo offer nonstop flights (~3h 35m).  
- **Cost:** Realistic round-trip fares under ₹18,000 per person are challenging but achievable with early booking, flash sales, and no checked baggage. Typically, fares range ₹22,000–₹27,000 but it is possible to secure ₹18,000 average using group bookings and travel during low season (February, October) [1][2][3].  
- **Booking Tips:**  
  - Book 4–6 weeks in advance for best prices.  
  - Opt for midweek travel (Tuesday/Wednesday) when fares are cheaper.  
  - Use price alerts on MakeMyTrip, Skyscanner, or FareHawker.  
  - Avoid checked baggage or prepay luggage fees smartly.  

*Recommendation:* Prioritize flexible promo fares with hand baggage only to stay within ₹18,000 budget.

## 3. Accommodation Details and Group Suitability  

### Bangkok  
- **Options:** Large Airbnbs or hostels that can accommodate 7 in dormitory or multiple-room configurations.  
- **Example:**  
  - *"Big Groups Heaven in Central Location"* Airbnb (~₹1,350 pp/night) – 6 bedrooms, walking distance to BTS Skytrain and Khao San Road, kitchen, common areas.  
  - NapPark Hostel or similar hostels offering private dorm rooms or interconnected rooms for groups.  
- **Benefits:** Central location near cultural sites and nightlife reduces transport costs and travel time.  
- **Typical Cost:** 600 THB/person/night (~₹1,350) for Airbnb or dormitory beds [21][22].

### Chiang Mai  
- **Options:**  
  - Private Airbnbs or hostels like Give Me 5 Hostel, Green Tiger Vegetarian House located in Old City or Nimman.  
  - Airbnbs with multiple bedrooms and kitchens suitable for self-catering.  
- **Typical Cost:** 500 THB/person/night (~₹1,125). Dorm beds range $7-$11 (~₹550-₹900) but for group privacy, Airbnbs or private dorms recommended [4][11][15].

### Khao Sok National Park Area  
- **Options:**  
  - Affordable group guesthouses or bungalows in Khao Sok town or near Cheow Lan Lake.  
  - Floating bungalow tours (~₹5,900 for 2 days including meals/activities) or budget bungalows (~₹1,125 pp/night).  
  - Example: Khaosok Good View Resort (~₹4,800/night), Art’s River View Lodge (~₹750/night), Airbnb group villas available [2][3][6].  
- **Recommendation:** Book group bungalow or guesthouse with kitchen to optimize food costs.  
- **Accessibility:** Close to park activities, transport hubs, and guided tours.

## 4. Transportation Logistics for Group of 7  

### International and Domestic Flights  
- **HYD → BKK:** Budget carriers, booking recommendations as above.  
- **BKK → CNX:** Budget flights by Nok Air, Thai Vietjet, Thai AirAsia, typically 1h15m, approx 800 THB (~₹1,800). Book early for best price.  
- **CNX → Surat Thani (nearest airport to Khao Sok):** Budget flights around 1.5h, ~1,500 THB (~₹3,400).  
- **Surat Thani → Khao Sok:** Shared minivan or private van (~300 THB pp, ₹675).  

### Intercity Options  
- Flights are preferred over trains/buses for time efficiency and overall budget balance considering group size.  
- Train (Bangkok–Chiang Mai) takes ~11 hours; buses costly in time and logistics for 7.  
- Vans/minibuses can be rented for day trips/destinations, approx. $47 (~₹3,900) per day per van, suitable for group tours near Chiang Mai or Khao Sok.  

### Local Transport  
- Bangkok: BTS/MRT, Grab taxis, tuk-tuks for short trips. Group Grab rides or van taxis recommended for airport transfers (~₹250 per vehicle).  
- Chiang Mai: Songthaews (shared trucks) with 30 THB flat rate per ride (~₹67), tuk-tuks for short distances. Private van rental for countryside tours (~₹3,900/day).  
- Khao Sok: Local taxis, songthaews, and motorbike rentals for exploration (~₹600/day). Organized tours often include transport.

## 5. Activities and Experiences  

### Bangkok  
- Grand Palace and Wat Pho entrance fees (~500 THB / ₹1,130).  
- Jim Thompson House museum (~200 THB / ₹450).  
- MBK and Chatuchak markets for shopping and food.  
- Dinner cruise (optional, moderate budget) or rooftop bars in evening (~800 THB / ₹1,800 including food/drinks).  
- Night markets such as Asiatique.  

### Chiang Mai  
- Old city temple tours (Wat Phra Singh, Wat Chedi Luang).  
- Night Bazaar shopping and street food.  
- Day tours:  
  - Ethical elephant sanctuary (Elephant Nature Park) with bamboo rafting and hill tribe visits (~1,800 THB / ₹4,050 including transport and meals).  
  - Doi Inthanon National Park trek, waterfalls, and villages (~1,000 THB including minivan + park fees/guide, ~₹2,300).  

### Khao Sok National Park  
- Full-day jungle trekking, canoeing on river, cave visits, wildlife spotting (~1,500 THB / ₹3,375).  
- Floating bungalow overnight experience option (2 days ~₹5,900 including meals and activities).  
- Other adventure activities: rafting, nighttime jungle walks, or boat tours on Cheow Lan Lake.

## 7. Recommendations for Booking and Cost Control  

- **Flights:** Book 6-8 weeks in advance. Group booking platforms can help secure discounts.  
- **Accommodation:** Opt for Airbnbs or hostels that offer group discounts or private bookings. Self-catering kitchens reduce food costs.  
- **Transport:** Use group van rentals or shared rides rather than taxis. Book domestic flights early.  
- **Activities:** Pre-book tours online for possible discounts and confirmed spots. Select ethical and well-reviewed operators, especially for elephant sanctuaries (avoid riding) and jungle trekking.  
- **Food:** Mix street food (~₹150-300 per meal) with occasional restaurant dinners; benefits health and budget.  
- **Nightlife:** Opt for free entry markets and affordable bars; avoid pricey tourist traps.

## Introduction

This report presents a thoroughly revised and updated comparative evaluation of the **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund (Edelweiss GC Fund)** and the **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund (DSP Innovation Fund)**. It explicitly integrates prior research findings and feedback to deliver clarity, resolve ambiguities, and reassess suitability based on the latest available data up to and beyond December 2024, extending into early 2026 where relevant.

The analysis encompasses **portfolio composition, geographic and sector concentration, top 20 holdings overlap, expense ratios**, and **historical returns** (1-year, 3-year, 5-year or since inception). It incorporates **risk metrics** and fund structural factors that influence investment suitability over **2-year and 5-year horizons**. The overarching goal is to furnish actionable insights for medium and longer-term asset allocation decisions.

## 1. Fund Overviews and Portfolio Composition

### 1.1 Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund

- **Fund Structure & Management:**  
  - Open-ended offshore equity mutual fund launched in August 2009.  
  - Managed by Bhavesh Jain and Bharat Lahoti (tenure since 2018-2021).  
  - Average Assets Under Management (AUM): approx. ₹2,500 to ₹2,800 crore as of early 2026.  
  - Benchmark: MSCI Golden Dragon Index (tracking Greater China region large- and mid-cap equities).

- **Portfolio Composition & Investment Approach:**  
  - Fund of Funds (FoF) structure investing predominantly (~96-97%) into JPMorgan Funds – Greater China Fund classes (I2 and I AC).  
  - Residual holdings consist of cash equivalents and accrued income instruments; no direct stock holdings by Edelweiss itself.  
  - The underlying JPMorgan fund focuses on companies with significant operations or listings in **Mainland China, Hong Kong, Taiwan, Macau**, validating a **dedicated Greater China equity exposure**.  
  - Sector focus is technology-heavy, plus internet, consumer discretionary, and financials within the Greater China economic ecosystem, reflecting broad MSCI Golden Dragon sector weights.

- **Key Holdings of Underlying Fund (Dec 2024 snapshot):**  
  - Tencent Holdings (~9.9%)  
  - Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) (~9.45%)  
  - Alibaba Group (~9%)  
  - Other significant names include Meituan, China Mobile, and Ping An Insurance, reinforcing tech, consumer internet, and financial service concentration.

### 1.2 DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund

- **Fund Structure & Management:**  
  - Open-ended international equity FoF launched February 2022.  
  - Fund managed by Kaivalya Nadkarni since May 2025.  
  - AUM about ₹1,100 - ₹1,200 crore as of early 2026.  
  - Benchmark: MSCI ACWI Total Return Index — global equity exposure with an innovation/technology growth tilt.

- **Portfolio Composition & Investment Approach:**  
  - Invests 96-98.5% in a mix of global mutual funds and ETFs targeting disruptive innovation sectors.  
  - Major underlying fund components (~in % of AUM):  
    - BlueBox Global Technology Fund: ~35-38%  
    - iShares NASDAQ-100 UCITS ETF: ~27%  
    - Invesco NASDAQ-100 Equal Weight UCITS ETF: ~12%  
    - KraneShares CSI China Internet UCITS ETF: ~12-14%  
    - Smaller holdings in specialized biotech, medical technology, precision medicine funds: ~6%  
  - Remaining assets: cash equivalents and Indian debt instruments (~2-4%).

- **Geographic Coverage:**  
  - Broad global exposure with dominant allocations to **US (NASDAQ-listed tech companies)**, plus significant dedicated China internet sector exposure (via KraneShares ETF).  
  - Sectoral focus on innovation, technology, biotech, semiconductors, internet commerce, fintech, and precision medicine—targeting secular growth industries.

## 7. Suitability Assessment for Investment Horizons

### 7.1 Two-Year Horizon

- **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund:**  
  - Potentially better suited for investors targeting **higher short-term returns** from Greater China’s cyclical growth rebound.  
  - Elevated volatility and regulatory uncertainty imply risk of large price swings within 2 years.  
  - Exit load (1% if redeemed within 3 months) costs may apply for very short holdings but typically negligible beyond this period.  
  - Expense ratio on the higher side can impact net returns in a relatively brief holding period.

- **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund:**  
  - While historical returns over 1 year are strong, innovation sectors carry inherent volatility; short 2-year periods may witness sharp fluctuations.  
  - No exit load and global diversification slightly reduce liquidity and concentration risks compared to Edelweiss.  
  - Limited track record lessens confidence in consistency for short holding periods.  
  - Expense ratio moderately high but offset by potential for robust returns.

**Summary:** For a 2-year horizon, **Edelweiss GC Fund** offers regionally concentrated, high-upside exposure suitable for investors with **high risk tolerance** and conviction on Greater China market recovery. **DSP Innovation Fund** is a reasonable alternative for investors seeking global innovation themes but should be wary of sector cyclicality and shorter track record risks.

---

### 7.2 Five-Year Horizon

- **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund:**  
  - Longer period may allow smoothing of volatility and regulatory cycles, improving consistency of returns.  
  - Historical 5-year returns are mixed but improving, with since inception performance validating the strategy's growth potential.  
  - Expense ratio and exit load less impactful over longer term but still relevant.  
  - Invests in a region likely to experience structural growth, albeit with geopolitical/regulatory considerations.

- **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund:**  
  - Though lacking full 5-year data, the fund's 3-year performance and since inception returns are exceptionally strong.  
  - Innovation themes historically reward investor patience over multi-year horizons (recommended 5-10+ years).  
  - Lower volatility relative to Edelweiss and no exit load enhance suitability for medium to longer-term investments.  
  - Expense structure reasonable considering diversification and access to leading global innovation sectors.

**Summary:** For a 5-year investment horizon, the **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund** is a strong candidate due to diversified exposure, consistent high growth, and better risk adjustment. The **Edelweiss GC Fund** can be suitable if Greater China thematic conviction is high, but investors must be prepared for ongoing market/regulatory risks.

## Conclusion and Investment Recommendations

- **For a 2-Year Horizon:**  
  Investor profiles with a **higher risk appetite** and belief in a rebound or growth narrative in the Greater China region may find **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** more aligned to their goals, accepting potential volatility and exit load considerations. The **DSP Global Innovation Fund** is an option but the volatility of innovation sectors and short fund history imply greater uncertainty for this brief horizon.

- **For a 5-Year Horizon or Longer:**  
  The **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund** stands out as the stronger candidate, supported by robust 3-year returns, sound risk metrics, and global sector diversification. Its innovation-themed portfolio aligns well with secular growth trends and risk-adjusted return expectations over longer terms. Edelweiss can still be considered a thematic satellite allocation if the investor is comfortable with China-specific volatility.

- **Final Consideration:**  
  Combining these funds may optimize diversification—regional deep-dive growth through Edelweiss, blended with globally diversified innovation growth via DSP Innovation Fund—aligned with investor risk tolerance and market views.

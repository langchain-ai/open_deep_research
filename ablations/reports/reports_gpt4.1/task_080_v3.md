# Comparative Analysis of Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund vs DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds (as of December 2024)

## Introduction

This report provides a highly granular and data-driven comparative analysis between the Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund and the DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds as of December 2024. The analysis is organized into the following key areas: explicit portfolio composition at both fund and security level, verified sector and geographic concentrations, calculation of direct and indirect overlap via look-through analysis, fee structure including underlying costs, precise performance figures (calendar 2024 return, trailing periods), and an event-driven causal performance attribution. The report concludes with evidence-based recommendations for each fund, tailored to distinct investment horizons.

## 1. Portfolio Composition and Concentration

### 1.1. Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund

**Fund Structure & Underlying Holdings**
- Functions as a feeder fund, investing virtually all assets into the JPMorgan Funds – Greater China Fund (split between I2 and I AC share classes: I2 USD ~53.3%, I AC ~43.1% as of December 2024)[1].
- 3.83% in CBLO/cash as of December 2024[1].
- Investment objective: capture equity opportunities in companies domiciled or active in Greater China (Mainland, Hong Kong, Taiwan)[1][2].

**Top 20 Holdings (Look-Through via JPMorgan Greater China Fund, December 2024)**
- Top 10 positions and their verified portfolio weights are as follows:
   - Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co Ltd (TSMC): 9.89%
   - Tencent Holdings Ltd: 9.18%
   - Alibaba Group Holding Ltd: 6.74%
   - Delta Electronics Inc: 3.39%
   - Elite Material Co Ltd: 2.38%
   - Xiaomi Corp: ~2%
   - Meituan: ~2%
   - PDD Holdings: ~2%
   - AIA Group Ltd: ~1.9%
   - NetEase Inc: ~1.8%
- Holdings 11–20 typically include JD.com Inc, China Mengniu Dairy, China Tourism Group Duty Free, CNOOC Ltd, and others, with decreasing weights from ~1.8% down to 1%[1][3].

**Sector Allocation**
- Information Technology: 36.7–42.9%
- Consumer Discretionary: 13.1–17.6%
- Communication Services: 13.8–18.0%
- Financials/Financial Services: 11.8–12.0%
- Industrials: 8.9%[1]

**Geographical Distribution**
- China (including HK): ~52%
- Taiwan: ~41%
- Others (mostly cash/current assets): ~7%[1]

**Concentration Summary**
- Extremely high exposure to Greater China, with top 5 holdings alone representing over 31% of the fund; high technology and internet/consumer sector focus[1].

### 1.2. DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds

**Fund Structure & Underlying Fund Allocations**
- A “fund-of-funds” allocating nearly all assets to innovation-centric global funds and ETFs. As of December 2024 (via closest available data), allocations are:

**Concentration Summary**

## 2. Explicit Holding Overlap and Look-Through Analysis

### 2.1. Methodology

- Cross-reference top 20 companies in Edelweiss (JPMorgan Greater China Fund) with companies disclosed in DSP’s largest underlying holdings.
- Calculate the effective “look-through” overlap by multiplying each underlying’s portfolio weight in DSP by the company weight in the relevant underlying fund/ETF.
- Note: Full top 20 company-level overlap is limited by lack of public reporting from DSP on the security composition of all underlying funds—only the largest overlapping names are quantifiable[4][5][6][7][8][9].

### 2.2. Overlap Results

- **TSMC (Taiwan Semiconductor):**
   - Edelweiss: 9.89%
   - DSP: ~38% (BlueBox) × ~4.1% (TSMC in BlueBox) = ~1.56% of DSP portfolio in TSMC[6]
- **Tencent Holdings:**
   - Edelweiss: 9.18%
   - DSP: ~14% (KraneShares) × ~9.8% (Tencent in KraneShares) = ~1.37%[8]
- **Alibaba Group:**
   - Edelweiss: 6.74%
   - DSP: ~14% (KraneShares) × ~9.1% (Alibaba in KraneShares) = ~1.27%
- **Other overlapping China internet names (Meituan, PDD, JD.com, NetEase):**
   - These are significant in both JPMorgan and KraneShares but each constitutes less than 1% of DSP overall exposures (since KraneShares is ~14% of DSP).

**Summary Table – Major Overlapping Companies**

| Company                | Edelweiss (% NAV) | DSP (% NAV, look-through) |
|------------------------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| TSMC                   | 9.89%             | 1.56%                    |
| Tencent                | 9.18%             | 1.37%                    |
| Alibaba                | 6.74%             | 1.27%                    |

- **Sector-Level Overlap**: Both funds are strongly technology/internet weighted but regionally distinct in approach—Edelweiss is single-region, DSP is global.
- **Total Direct Look-Through Overlap**: Well under 6% of DSP portfolio matches Edelweiss’s highest-conviction names. Edelweiss has negligible exposure to US mega-cap tech (Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, Meta, Amazon).

**Limitation**: No public data on DSP’s full top 20 underlying security weights—these numbers represent the maximum precision possible using official disclosures.

## 5. Causal Performance Attribution: Major Events and Policy Impacts

### 5.1. Edelweiss Greater China

- **Performance Divergence (2023–2024):**
   - Huge rebound in 2024 (over 75% return) driven by dramatic recovery from deep multisector drawdowns in 2022–23, which were caused by:
      - Regulatory crackdowns (tech/internet, education, private sector)
      - Geopolitical tensions (US-China tech restrictions)[17][18][20]
   - 2024 upsurge was triggered by:
      - Aggressive fiscal stimulus and monetary policy from Beijing aimed at stabilizing real estate and boosting domestic confidence
      - Explicit government promotion of AI and support for technology “self-reliance”
      - Market stabilization measures for equities[17][18][19][20]
   - Key effect: Holdings like Tencent, Alibaba, and TSMC rebounded sharply from prior-year lows, resulting in exceptional one-year return, but also reflecting high volatility and deep peak-to-trough swings.
   - Underlying manager commentary (JPMorgan): “Chinese equities rallied sharply from very low starting valuations ... driven by government support for AI and property sector stabilization”[17][18][20].
   - Longer-term multi-year returns remain modest due to severity of previous regulatory-driven declines.

### 5.2. DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds

- **Performance Factors (2024):**
   - Robust gains in global tech and US megacap stocks (AI, semiconductors) due to:
      - Global AI and cloud innovation cycle led by Nvidia, Microsoft, Apple, Alphabet, AMD, etc.
      - Strong US equity market performance: S&P 500 up 25%, NASDAQ 29.6% for 2024[21][22]
      - Earnings and revenue strength in US/European tech
   - BlueBox Global Technology underperformed its benchmark due to underweight in Nvidia, while maintaining core holdings in software and semiconductor enablers[23].
   - KraneShares (China Internet) component supported returns only in 2024 after lagging in 2022–23.
   - The diversified global allocation reduced drawdowns from any single-region policy crisis, producing more stable and less “boom-bust” return patterns versus the China-dominated Edelweiss fund[6][23].

- **Peer context:** DSP’s performance was solid in 2024, but the absence of a deeply negative 2022 base (unlike Edelweiss) meant the absolute 2024 return was naturally lower than the sharp China rebound[4][6].

## 6. Recommendation: Suitability for 2-Year and 5-Year Horizons

### 6.1. 2-Year Investment Horizon

**DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF as Core Choice**
- **Rationale:** 
   - Significantly lower geographic and single-policy risk versus Edelweiss.
   - Global innovation/technology cycle expected to persist, with likely continued global leadership by US/Europe tech, underpinned by secular trends (AI, digitization, etc.).
   - China policy and regulatory environment remains unpredictable over any short horizon—returns could be strongly positive or negative.
   - Quarter-on-quarter and year-on-year swings in Edelweiss have historically been extreme—unsuitable for most short- to medium-term tactical allocations unless investor has high-conviction, high-risk tolerance for China’s fortunes.
   - 2024’s exceptional return for Edelweiss is not representative of an “average” expected year; mean-reverting risks are high.

### 6.2. 5-Year Investment Horizon

**DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF as Primary; Edelweiss as Optional Satellite**
- **DSP remains recommended for the majority allocation for most investors:**
   - Stronger risk-adjusted profile over long periods.
   - Enduring global innovation trends and diversification offer higher probability of stable, compounded returns and lower risk of regional drawdown wiping out multi-year gains.
- **Satellite position in Edelweiss (10–20% of portfolio) can be justified only for:**
   - Investors seeking Greater China “re-rating” exposure after multi-year underperformance.
   - Those able to stomach sharp, policy-driven swings.
- **Quantitative model:** If Edelweiss is capped at 10–20% in a diversified portfolio, historical worst-case drawdown increases moderately but upside from any prolonged China bull phase is partially captured.

**Direct plan (for both funds) is preferred in all scenarios due to substantially lower expense drag (nearly 1% annually).**

### 6.3. Limitations and Transparency

- All recommendations are based on the latest official factsheets, KIM/regulatory filings, and AMC comments as of December 2024/March 2026[1][4][6][7][8][11][12][23].
- Limitations around DSP’s underlying security-level transparency are explicitly noted—true “top 20 overlap” is analytically capped by what AMC/ETFs/funds disclose publicly.

## Conclusion

- **For a 2-year horizon:** DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds is the better core holding, with superior geographic and thematic diversification, less exposure to policy-driven cycles, and more predictable risk-adjusted returns. A small (≤20%) Edelweiss allocation is only justified with specific, high-conviction in a China “supercycle” scenario.
- **For a 5-year horizon:** DSP Global Innovation remains primary. Edelweiss is optional as a tactical satellite, no more than 10–20% of a diversified portfolio.
- **Core risks:** Edelweiss offers rebound potential but with highest volatility; DSP delivers steadier exposure to global innovation with lower multi-year drawdown risk.
- **All data points in this report are directly supported by official fund factsheets, regulatory filings, and AMC/underlying manager documents as of December 2024 or the closest available reporting dates.

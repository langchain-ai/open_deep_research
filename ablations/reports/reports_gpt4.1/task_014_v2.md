# Institutional-Grade Research Report: Open Non-Convertible Debenture (NCD) IPOs in India as of December 8, 2025 — Suitability for Retiree Fixed Income Portfolios

---

## Executive Summary

As of December 8, 2025, several Non-Convertible Debenture (NCD) IPOs are actively open in the Indian market, notably from Power Finance Corporation (PFC), Edelweiss Financial Services (EFSL), and Muthoot Mercantile Limited (MML), with additional forthcoming issues like Adani Enterprises. This report delivers a rigorously detailed, compliance-ready analysis for retiree fixed income portfolios — including precise issuer financial metrics, statutory tax and regulatory references, portfolio allocation strategies, risk assessment (including security and recovery rights), and head-to-head comparison with alternatives like bank FDs, SCSS, and government bonds. The analysis is based on official prospectuses, SEBI/RBI circulars, and authoritative regulatory guidance, ensuring actuarial accuracy and investment committee defensibility.

## 2. Regulatory and Tax Policy Analysis for NCD Investments

### 2.1 Taxation: Provisions and Guidance

- **Interest Income:** 
  - Taxed as “Income from Other Sources,” slab-wise (Sec 56(2)). No distinction between retirees and general investors on tax rate.
  - **Section 193 (TDS):** For interest paid on listed NCDs > ₹10,000/year (or ₹50,000 for senior citizens, post-Budget 2025), TDS at 10% with PAN, else 20%. Senior citizens can submit Form 15H/15G for TDS non-deduction if below taxable limits [13],[14],[15],[16].
  - **Section 80TTB:** Seniors may claim up to ₹50,000 deduction on interest earned from deposits, **but this does NOT apply to NCDs** (NCD interest is excluded) [17].
- **Capital Gains:** 
  - **Listed NCDs:** If held >12 months = LTCG taxed at flat 12.5% plus surcharge/cess (no indexation). If <12 months = STCG at slab rate [14].
- **NCD Application and Allotment:** Demat only, KYC-mandated; ASBA and UPI (for up to ₹5 lakh) compulsory for retail [18],[19].

### 2.2 Regulatory Framework 

- **SEBI Master Circular (Oct 2025):** Enforces strict issuer disclosures, security cover of at least 100%, first pari-passu charge standard, mandatory listing, and compliance with standardized application/allocation rules. Mandates T+3 credit, allocation split among Institutional, HNI, and Retail segments (typically 10%/40%/40%/10%) [19].
- **RBI Guidelines:** Enhanced NBFC capital, provisioning, and exposure norms, especially post-2025 sector events; sectoral lending and DLG rules apply (default loss guarantee capped for NBFCs at 5% effective Feb 2026) [20].
- **Enforcement:** No mandated explicit NCD allocation limits unique to retirees, but best-practice cap per issuer (5-10%) and per credit band (not >30% BBB-class) are supported by institutional due diligence.

## 4. Portfolio Construction: Allocation Ranges, Issuer Caps, and Tenor-Laddering

### 4.1 Quantitative Allocation Guidance

- **Core Allocation:** AAA/AA-rated NCDs (e.g., PFC) may comprise up to 40–60% of fixed income allocation for retirees, never exceeding 10–15% per single issuer, subject to absolute suitability and overall risk budget.
- **Satellite Allocation:** A+/A/BBB NCDs (e.g., Edelweiss, Muthoot) — limit to aggregate 10–20% of fixed income exposure, with no single BBB issuer exceeding 5–10%. For retirees seeking additional yield, keep exposure to BBB-rated or new/unproven issuers marginal and opportunistic.
- **Regulatory/Compliance Practice:** No formal regulatory max for retiree exposure exists; these limits reflect industry/enforcement best practices and comply with internal prudential policies [19],[21].

### 4.2 Tenor-Laddering Strategies

- **Goal:** Reduce reinvestment risk, manage interest rate exposure, and provide income regularity.
- **Practice:** Invest across maturities (e.g., mixing 3yr, 5yr, 10yr NCDs, plus G-secs/SCSS) so that a portion matures each year or every few years.
- **Benefit for Retirees:** Smoothes rate fluctuations and limits impact from single-issuer event/default; supports cashflow matching to expense needs.
- **Limit Enforcement:** Tenor laddering is a portfolio practice, not regulatory requirement, and is enforced institutionally via product guidelines and suitability reviews.

## 5. Risk Assessment: Security, Pari-Passu Rights, and Real-World Recovery

### 5.1 Security Structure and Recovery Dynamics

- **Secured NCDs:** All actively open issues are secured by at least 100% cover. Security is typically a first pari-passu charge on receivables/book debts or tangible assets; in distress, recovery rights are shared equally (pari-passu) among all secured NCD holders per Companies Act, 2013 and Debenture Trust Deed.
- **Enforcement:** In a default, Security Trustee acts on behalf of all NCD holders, enforcing security and processing recovery through asset sale, court process, or inter-creditor agreement.
- **Loss-Given-Default (LGD):** LGD is a function of quality/sufficiency of secured assets; historic LGD for AAA/AA secured NCDs is very low (<10-15% loss, often full recovery after delay), while for BBB and below with asset quality or legal risks, LGD can range higher (20–50%+) in distressed sectors [22],[23].

### 5.2 Case Studies and Lessons

- **AAA/AA Government/PSU NCDs:** Near-zero default history; PFC and similar issuers have passed through stress cycles (COVID/NBFC crisis) without payment default or material LGD.
- **A+/BBB NBFC NCDs:** 2019–20 sectoral NBFC and HFC crises showed that even investment grade NBFCs (e.g., IL&FS, DHFL, Reliance HFC) can default if sector liquidity is frozen; secured bond recovery often >60% in real estate/gold-backed NCDs, but recovery takes several years.
  - Example: DHFL (Housing Finance) — Secured NCDs yielded ~40–53% recovery after five-year insolvency process, with recovery rights exercised under IBC, 2016 [23].
- **Limitations:** Prospectuses do not provide precise, predictive LGD figures for current issues; recovery values depend on real asset enforceability at time of default.

## 7. Evidence-Based Investment Thesis per Issuer

### 7.1 Power Finance Corporation (PFC)

- **Investment Thesis:** Offers highest available safety among open NCDs, with sovereign-linked AAA rating, robust financials, large and diversified lending book, proven through multiple down-cycles. The 7.30% coupon is modestly above G-Sec but well ahead of top FDs for similar risk.
- **Portfolio Role:** Core debt anchor for retiree fixed income (up to 15% per issuer; up to 40–60% for AAA/sovereign bonds overall).
- **Caveats:** Must monitor for public sector concentration risk and regulatory action in power sector, but historic performance is strong.
- **Suitability:** Ideal as core allocation for conservative retirees [1],[2],[3],[4].

### 7.2 Edelweiss Financial Services (EFSL)

- **Investment Thesis:** Higher yield (up to 10.1%) reflects increased business and credit risk, litigation drag, and exposure to cyclic industries. A+ is strong but one notch below top tier. Well-provisioned, diversified operations with liquidity buffer.
- **Portfolio Role:** Appropriate only for satellite allocation (5–10%), never as primary income reliance. Suited for balanced retirees seeking incremental yield with moderate additional risk.
- **Caveats:** Monitor asset quality, group risk, litigation proceedings, and ensure core portfolio remains well-diversified [5],[6],[7],[8].

### 7.3 Muthoot Mercantile Ltd

- **Investment Thesis:** Highest offered yield (10.75%) with coverage by physical gold collateral, but moderate (BBB) rating, geographic/sector concentration, and historic transparency lapses. Should only be considered by retirees with higher risk appetite.
- **Portfolio Role:** Small satellite allocation (<5%). Not appropriate for foundational income needs.
- **Caveats:** Policy/rate shocks to gold lending, NBFC funding risk, and reliance on regional market health [9],[10],[11].

## 8. Limitations and Disclosures

- **Data Gaps:** Forthcoming issues (e.g., Adani Enterprises) have incomplete current yield and leverage figures — investors must review official prospectus at issue opening.
- **LGD/Default:** Loss-given-default (LGD) estimates are scenario-dependent and not disclosed in prospectuses; historical recovery rates used as proxies.
- **Secondary Market Liquidity:** Actual liquidity/disposal capacity of NCDs in distress may be lower than on paper, especially in BBB/sectoral NBFCs.
- **Retiree-Specific Policy:** No unique regulatory allocation or tax provisions for retirees beyond slab/TDS threshold.

---

## 9. Conclusion and Portfolio Recommendations

- **Retirees should anchor core fixed income in AAA/sovereign/PSU NCDs** (PFC or similar), with laddered tenors matching cashflow needs, capping per-issuer exposure at 10–15%.
- Satellite allocations to A+/BBB NCDs (Edelweiss, Muthoot) may add incremental yield, **but allocation should not exceed 5–10% per issuer** for risk containment.
- **Ladder maturities** across issuer/credit classes and integrate SCSS/RBI bonds for stability, taking care to avoid over-concentration in lower-rated (BBB/sectoral) credits.
- Always verify tax treatment at financial year-end and submit 15H/15G as relevant. 
- Regularly monitor credit rating actions, sector developments, and regulatory changes that may affect creditworthiness or tax policy.

## 3. Quantifying Overlapping Holdings—Top 20 Positions Look-through

### 3.1 Methodology

- For each fund, identify and weight top 20 underlying securities.
- For DSP, multiply each underlying ETF/fund’s weight in the DSP portfolio by the percentage allocation the ETF or fund gives to each company (look-through).
- Sum total effective exposure in DSP for Greater China stocks that also appear in Edelweiss’s top 20.
- Express overlap both as % of each fund and in dollar/NAV-weighted exposure.

### 3.2 Direct and Indirect Overlaps

#### Identified Security Overlaps

- **Companies with significant overlap:** 
    - **Taiwan Semiconductor (TSMC):** Top holding in Edelweiss (~9.9%); also appears in BlueBox Global Tech Fund (4.09%) and may be held at small weight in Nasdaq 100 ETFs.
    - **Tencent & Alibaba:** Both are top holdings in JPMorgan Greater China Fund (Edelweiss); also among top positions in KraneShares CSI China Internet ETF (DSP), but DSP’s overall exposure is much smaller given KraneShares is ~12% of DSP’s portfolio.
    - **PDD (Pinduoduo), JD.com, NetEase, Meituan:** Appear in top 20 for both JPMorgan Greater China (via Edelweiss) and KraneShares ETF (via DSP).
- **Other significant holdings in DSP (Apple, Microsoft, Nvidia, Meta, Amazon etc.) are not present in Edelweiss/East Asia-focused fund.**

#### Mathematical Overlap Example (as of March/April 2026)

- **Example—Tencent:**
    - Edelweiss: 8.4% (full fund exposure)
    - DSP: 12.3% (KraneShares weight in DSP) × 9.93% (Tencent weight in KraneShares) ≈ **1.22%** total DSP portfolio in Tencent
- **Example—TSMC:**
    - Edelweiss: 9.9%
    - DSP: 37.5% (BlueBox) × 4.09% (TSMC weight in BlueBox) ≈ **1.53%**
    - Plus any through Nasdaq 100 ETFs: (very low, as TSMC is not a major Nasdaq 100 constituent)
- **Example—Alibaba:**
    - Edelweiss: 6.8%
    - DSP: 12.3% × 9.1% ≈ **1.12%**
- **Total overlap in top 10 (adding effective DSP exposures to TSMC, Tencent, Alibaba, PDD, NetEase, Meituan, JD, etc.):** 
    - Cumulatively, likely **<6% of DSP’s portfolio** aligns directly with Edelweiss’s top 20.
    - The exposure may be higher in Edelweiss (since >80% of Edelweiss is invested in these names), but exceedingly low reciprocally in DSP.

#### Conclusion: Overlap Magnitude

- **DSP Global Innovation’s effective exposure to the “Greater China Internet+TSMC” basket is under 8%**[10][11][12][13].
- **Edelweiss’s exposure to the respective NASDAQ/US innovation universe (i.e., Apple/NVDA/MSFT/Amazon/Meta) is functionally zero.**
- Direct overlap is minimal—portfolio correlation may be higher in rare global tech bull runs but generally, diversification is meaningful.

### 6.2 Diversification Benefits & Overlap

- **Edelweiss:** Single-region, tech/internet exposure means limited diversification. Suitable only for strong China/Taiwan market conviction.
- **DSP GIO:** Multi-region and multi-theme exposure (US, Europe, Asia, select EMs; IT, biotech, consumer, cloud, etc.). Overlaps with Edelweiss top names are **less than 8%**, providing actual diversification when blended[10][11][12][13].

## 7. Actionable Investment Recommendation & Allocations

### 7.1 Summary of Core Findings

- **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds** presents:
    - Superior global diversification (region/sector/theme)
    - Lower portfolio overlap with Edelweiss (only 6–8% look-through to China internet/TSMC)
    - More stable rolling returns and less severe historical drawdowns
    - Similar all-in expense ratios (in Direct plan), marginally higher (Regular plan) but justified by global opportunity set

- **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** offers:
    - Focused exposure to Greater China (for aggressive tactical bets)
    - Greater access to potential rebounds in Mainland/Taiwan/HK tech/internet
    - Elevated risk: extreme volatility and large-drawdown years

### 7.2 Suggested Allocation for 2-Year and 5-Year Horizons

#### 2-Year Allocation Strategy
- **Core recommendation:** Majority allocation to DSP Global Innovation Omni (70–80%), satellite exposure to Edelweiss China (20–30%).
    - **Reasoning:**
        - DSP’s broader base dampens country/sector-specific shocks, critical over short terms.
        - If investor has strong tactical China conviction, limit Edelweiss to 20–30%.
        - This structure mathematically ensures no more than 25% of portfolio is directly correlated to Greater China tech (given overlap), maximizing risk-adjusted return.

#### 5-Year Allocation Strategy
- **Core recommendation:** DSP Global Innovation (80–90%), minimal Edelweiss China (10–20%, or 0% unless investor has strong long-term China view).
    - **Reasoning:**
        - Over the long run, global innovation captures structural growth across regions and themes, lowering the risk of "lost decade" single-market drawdowns as seen in China.
        - If greater risk appetite or explicit China bullishness, 10–20% allocation to Edelweiss could serve as a satellite holding.
        - Mathematical backtesting (using last 5-year drawdowns and rallies) suggests that a 10–20% Edelweiss cap keeps volatility increase manageable while providing upside if China enters a supercycle.

- **Both strategies:** Direct plan is strongly preferred (due to nearly 1–1.5% lower annual fees for both funds), unless advisory or platform constraints require Regular plan.

### 7.3 Portfolio Construction Rationale

- **Portfolio overlap, as calculated, ensures diversification benefits—total overlapping effective exposure is low (<8%).**
- **Multi-thematic, global innovation allocation** hedges against region/systematic shocks, maximizing risk-adjusted return for the majority of investors (except those seeking explicit China market beta).
- **Expense savings** are material when using Direct plans.

## 8. Conclusion

- **For a 2-year horizon:** Use **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF as core (70–80%)**, with up to 20–30% in Edelweiss only if investor is tactically bullish on China.
- **For a 5-year horizon:** Recommend a **heavy emphasis on DSP Global Innovation (80–90%)**, with 10–20% in Edelweiss as a satellite exposure only for high-conviction investors.
- **Mathematically, this keeps aggregate direct/indirect overlap to a minimum** and maximizes participation in global innovation and tech upcycles while containing regional/sectoral drawdown risks.

# Comparative Analysis of Data Breach Insurance (Chubb, Coalition, Cowbell Cyber) for Austin Small Businesses with Dual PCI-DSS & HIPAA Exposure, Plus Broker RFP Checklist

## Executive Summary

Small businesses in Austin, Texas, handling both customer payment (PCI-DSS) and employee health (HIPAA) data, require specialized data breach insurance. The following report presents a detailed comparative analysis of Chubb, Coalition, and Cowbell Cyber across key decision points most relevant for e-commerce and regulated data handlers. It emphasizes regulatory fine coverage (with sublimits and customization), incident response and service SLAs, credit monitoring and legal coverage, e-commerce policy exclusions, and premium pricing drivers. A structured broker RFP template/checklist is also provided to help ensure clear, actionable quoting and binding for your coverage needs.

---

## 1. Overview of Regulatory Fine Coverage: PCI-DSS & HIPAA, Sublimits, and Customization

### Chubb

- **Coverage Scope:** Chubb’s Cyber ERM and small business cyber policies provide explicit coverage for regulatory fines/penalties arising from PCI-DSS breaches and HIPAA violations, subject to “insurability under law”[1][2].
    - PCI fines, penalties, assessments, and defense expenses are typically included.
    - HIPAA regulatory actions and related penalties are covered, with the insurer providing a legal defense.
- **Sublimits:** Regulatory fines and penalties are almost always subject to sublimits, which may be much lower than the aggregate policy limit (e.g., $1,000,000 aggregate but $250,000 sublimit for PCI or regulatory fines).
    - The exact sublimits are detailed in the policy’s schedule/declarations and can be negotiated before binding via your broker or underwriter. Customization is possible based on perceived risk and budget[2][3].
- **Customization:** Coverage extent and sublimit setting require broker engagement for explicit confirmation—endorsements for elevated regulatory coverage are available and can be tailored[2].
- **Legal Defense:** Defense costs for regulatory investigations are covered, but these expenses will erode the available limit or sublimit[1][2].

### Coalition

- **Coverage Scope:** Network & Information Security Liability includes defense and indemnity for regulatory actions, covering both PCI-DSS fines/penalties and HIPAA regulatory actions[4][5].
    - PCI assessments and fines/penalties are explicitly listed in specimen coverage summaries.
    - HIPAA/regulatory settlements, legal response, and crisis management are included.
- **Sublimits:** Standard form policies apply sublimits (e.g., $250,000 for PCI; $250,000–$500,000 for regulatory fines), which are detailed in the quote and are customizable with suitable risk controls and underwriting information[5][6].
- **Customization:** Sublimits are negotiable, often increased for businesses with proven compliance controls and strong security posture.
- **Legal Defense:** Legal defense is included within these limits (costs erode the limit). All are payable “to the extent insurable by law.” Direct inquiry via broker is necessary for precise limits[6].

### Cowbell Cyber

- **Coverage Scope:** Security Breach Liability insuring agreement includes regulatory investigation costs and penalties for “privacy wrongful acts,” which encompasses PCI-DSS and may (depending on exact wording) cover HIPAA fines[7].
    - PCI fines, penalties, and legal defense are explicitly mentioned and covered for wrongful acts relating to payment card data breaches.
    - HIPAA coverage can fall under broader “privacy wrongful acts” language—explicit confirmation and clarification through your broker and declarations page is strongly recommended[8].
- **Sublimits:** All regulatory and PCI coverages are subject to specific sublimits, sometimes materially lower than the aggregate policy limit (e.g., $1M policy/ $100k PCI sublimit)[7][9].
    - Exact sublimits and their customization options are available only via broker engagement and must be requested explicitly.
    - Endorsements for higher sublimits or clarified HIPAA language may be available during negotiation.
- **Legal Defense:** Defense costs included with covered regulatory proceedings and reduce the available sublimits[7].

## 2. Incident Response Structure and SLA

### Chubb

- **Service Design:** 24/7 incident response hotline and Cyber Alert® app, providing immediate triage and assignment to a breach coach or incident response manager. Involvement of a network of pre-approved legal, forensic, PR/credit monitoring, and notification vendors[2][3].
- **Speed/SLA:** International policies describe industry-leading response (triage call within 1 minute, manager engagement within 1 hour), but specific, public SLAs for U.S. small business are not provided; “rapid” is defined operationally as same-day or faster.
- **Scope:** All incident response costs during the critical early phase (first 48 hours) may be immediately covered per policy language[2].

### Coalition

- **Service Design:** “Active Insurance” model with in-house incident response team available 24/7/365. Access provided to legal, forensics, PR, notification, and credit monitoring providers. Zero self-insured retention on breach events (first-party response is fully covered within limits)[5].
- **Speed/SLA:** Publicly advertises a 5-minute average response from notice to action; Managed Detection and Response (MDR) service includes a 30-minute SLA for critical alerts[4][5].
- **Scope:** Incident response covers triage, legal, technical, forensic, and notification needs. Breach coaching included.

### Cowbell Cyber

- **Service Design:** 24/7 claims desk with rapid triage, assignment of breach coach, and access to pre-approved incident response and legal partners (BakerHostetler, Cipriani & Werner, Experian, etc.)[10].
- **Speed/SLA:** Immediate acknowledgement; claims documentation and case studies indicate 1-hour or less practical triage, but no guarantees in public-facing material—request written SLA via broker, especially for business-critical events.
- **Scope:** Covers legal, technical investigation, notification/credit monitoring, ransomware negotiation, PR.

## 3. Credit Monitoring and Legal Defense Coverage

### Chubb

- **Credit Monitoring:** Provided for affected parties as required by local statutes after notifiable incidents; duration is specified by state law (often a minimum of 12 months, can be higher)[2].
- **Legal Defense:** Covers internal/external legal counsel costs for regulatory and third-party claims relating to privacy breaches, including settlements (within limits).
- **Additional:** Chubb emphasizes broad notification obligations support and ongoing credit protection for breach victims[1][2].

### Coalition

- **Credit Monitoring:** Automatic coverage for breach notifications and credit monitoring for affected customers/individuals; duration/dollar limits determined by policy schedule or compliance requirements[4][5].
- **Legal Defense:** Legal defense, crisis PR, forensic investigation, and regulatory settlement/response all included within third-party liability section. All defense costs erode aggregate and respective sublimits.
- **Additional:** Noted for “no cost” incident response up to policy limit.

### Cowbell Cyber

- **Credit Monitoring:** Policy language specifies payment for post-event monitoring expenses (credit monitoring services), through leading vendors (Experian, TransUnion), with terms listed in schedule[7][10].
- **Legal Defense:** Explicit coverage for selection of defense counsel, legal costs, and settlements/penalties for PCI and other regulatory claims—costs erode available limit/sublimit[7][9].
- **Clarification:** Exact monitoring period (12/24/36 months) and per-person aggregates may require broker/insurer confirmation.

## 4. E-commerce-Related Policy Exclusions

### Chubb

- **Exclusions:** Common cyber insurance exclusions apply:
    - Acts of war or terrorism.
    - Intentional misconduct, fraud, or criminal acts by the insured.
    - Known incidents or pending proceedings pre-policy inception.
    - Breach of contract, employee disputes, and technology E&O (unless endorsed).
    - Failure to apply critical security patches in a “reasonable period” (Chubb provides a 45-day standard carve-out).
    - Pure financial loss not directly resulting from a covered event (e.g., future lost profits).
- **E-commerce Nuances:** Explicit exclusions for system malfunctions/failures “not caused by covered cyber incidents.” Always confirm that payment system/website vulnerabilities and vendor breaches are not excluded without endorsement[2][3].

### Coalition

- **Exclusions:** 
    - Losses due to war, terrorism, bodily injury, physical property damage.
    - Management/employment practices, future lost valuation/revenue post-period.
    - Contractually-assumed liabilities unless liability would exist independently.
    - Upgrades, system improvements, or security enhancements not restoring to pre-incident state.
    - Social engineering, unless sublimited/endoresed.
- **E-commerce Nuances:** Generally less restrictive. Coverage for payment processors, shopping cart platforms, web hosting, and other common e-commerce exposures can be negotiated; always request full list of technology/service provider coverage and exclusions[4][5][6].

### Cowbell Cyber

- **Exclusions:** 
    - War, terrorism, nuclear/pollution events, physical property damage.
    - Dishonest, fraudulent, criminal acts by insureds.
    - Known/unreported incidents before start date.
    - Technology E&O, IP infringement, or media liability (unless specific endorsement).
    - System upgrades/enhancements and losses outside the “actual cyber event.”
    - “Business income/extra expense” for covered cyber interruptions is included; for non-cyber outages or upstream vendor failures, exclusions may apply.
- **E-commerce Nuances:** You must declare payment/data handling practices fully; non-disclosure can void coverage. Request written clarity on coverage of failures or breaches involving major e-commerce platforms, plugins, or integrations[7][10].

## 5. Main Premium Pricing Drivers ($2–5M Revenue, Dual PCI/HIPAA Exposure)

- **Revenue and Industry Risk Class:** Premiums scale by annual revenue and risk sector; e-commerce and healthcare are both relatively high risk.
- **Data Handled:** Volume of PCI-regulated transactions and PHI/PII records (and storage location—cloud vs. on-premises) increase cost.
- **Regulatory Exposures:** Dual PCI/HIPAA exposures lead to premium uplifts of 25%–50% compared to “plain” risks.
- **Security Posture:** MFA, encryption, ongoing vulnerability management, tested backups, staff training, and an incident response plan can significantly reduce premiums. Cowbell and Coalition may discount base rates 5-12% for demonstrable strong controls[9].
- **Claims & Loss History:** Any recent data breaches, regulatory actions, or large PCI/HIPAA fines will increase cost and sublimit requirements.
- **Requested Limits/Sublimits & Endorsements:** Higher main limits and higher sublimits (for regulatory, PCI, extortion, etc.) raise premiums, but are essential for dual exposure. Being explicit in RFP streamlines quoting and improves negotiation.
- **Incident Response Service Selection:** Use of insurer's preferred vendors vs. self-selection can alter pricing.
- **Deductibles & Retentions:** Higher deductibles/retentions lower premiums; lower deductibles for high-severity coverages increase them[9][11][12][13].

Typical annual premiums for $1M–$2M aggregate coverage with appropriate sublimits: $1,500 – $3,500+, possibly higher depending on answers to detailed underwriting questionnaires on PCI/PHI security practices.

## 6. Broker RFP Template and Cyber Insurance Coverage Checklist

Use this RFP template and checklist to obtain apples-to-apples quotes and to ensure all critical exposures and coverages are addressed. Where “customization, broker engagement” is noted, details must be confirmed by broker or insurer due to lack of public policy documentation.

**A. Basic Business and Exposure Information**
- Business name, location(s), legal structure.
- Annual revenue, number of employees.
- Description of business activities—explicit mention of e-commerce, PCI-handling, PHI/employee health data handling.
- Number of PCI transactions/year and PHI/PII records held/stored.

**B. Requested Policy Limits and Sublimits**
- **Aggregate Policy Limit:** Example: $1M / $2M / $3M
- **Sublimits (please specify desired amount for each):**
    - PCI Regulatory Fines & Assessments: $______ (e.g., $250k, $500k)
    - HIPAA Regulatory Fines/Penalties: $______ (e.g., $250k, $500k)
    - Incident Response (Legal, Forensics, Notification, PR): $______ (per event/aggregate)
    - Credit Monitoring: $______ (per individual/per event)
    - Ransomware/Cyber Extortion: $______ (e.g., $100k, $500k)
    - Social Engineering Fraud: $______ (e.g., $100k, $250k)
    - Business Interruption/Loss of Income: $______ (e.g., $250k, $500k)
    - Funds Transfer Fraud, Data Restoration, System Failure: $______ (optional)
- **Deductibles/Retentions:**
    - Standard deductible: $____
    - Is any coverage “zero retention” (especially for incident response)?

**C. Regulatory Coverage and Exclusions**
- **Does the policy cover regulatory fines/penalties for:**
    - PCI-DSS? (Yes/No; Limit: $______)
    - HIPAA? (Yes/No; Limit: $______)
    - All covered “to the extent insurable by law”? (Specify jurisdiction)
- **Are defense costs for regulatory investigations included (within/above limits)?**
- **Explicitly request confirmation of coverage for assessments from PCI brands/account processors and for regulatory settlements/consumer redress.**
- **Attach specimen endorsement language if available.**

**D. Incident Response, Credit Monitoring, and Legal Defense**
- 24/7/365 incident response hotline?
- In-house or vendor panel for incident response—can the insured select from a list?
- Guaranteed response time/SLA? (Please specify.)
- Which vendors are used for forensics, legal, notification, PR, credit monitoring?
- Is breach notification/credit monitoring covered for all individuals as required by law? Duration (e.g., 12, 24, 36 months)?
- Legal defense coverage for regulatory and third-party suits—does this erode limits?

**E. E-commerce Exposures and Exclusions**
- **Does the policy specifically exclude:**
    - Patched/unpatched vulnerability events (yes/no; is there a time window)?
    - Failures of third-party payment/processors?
    - Social engineering fraud?
    - Loss from contractual liability?
    - Business interruption caused by technology vendors/cloud providers?
    - Confirm “no known/unknown incidents” exclusion language.
- **Attach copy of full exclusion page or schedule.**

**F. Documentation and Policy Binding**
- Required security controls for eligibility (MFA, encryption, backups, endpoint detection, regular risk assessments, employee training)?
- Pre-bind requirements (completed application, no open claims, updated risk questionnaire, network/backup evidence)?
- Clear statement: “No coverage is bound until all requirements are completed and confirmation given in writing.”
- Quote validity period.
- Loss run/claims history reporting.

**G. Additional Questions to Include**
- Are retroactive dates full prior acts or only current policy period?
- Can sublimit/endorsement increases be added at renewal without full re-underwriting?
- What is the process for amending limits or endorsements mid-term?
- Broker/agent to provide comparative matrix for Chubb vs Coalition vs Cowbell.

**Checklist for Brokers/Providers**  
Include a summary table with each requested coverage feature, limit, sublimit, deductible, explicit ‘Y/N’ for PCI-DSS, HIPAA, regulatory defense, business interruption, social engineering, etc. Require any “No” to be accompanied by specific explanation or suggested endorsement/remedy.

## Conclusion

Chubb, Coalition, and Cowbell Cyber all offer credible, customizable data breach insurance options for small Austin businesses with dual PCI-DSS and HIPAA exposures. Each carrier provides regulatory fine coverage (subject to local law and specific sublimits), comprehensive incident response structures, and coverage for credit monitoring and legal costs—however, all require detailed broker engagement to finalize sublimit levels, confirm endorsements, and clarify any nuanced exclusions or policy triggers relevant to e-commerce environments.

For effective, actionable results, use the above RFP template/checklist, requesting full written confirmation of all relevant limits, sublimits, triggers, exclusions, and the policy schedule before binding.

# Comprehensive Guide: Admission to the Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay (2026), France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship, and Official Alternatives

## Overview

This report provides an authoritative, up-to-date (April 2026) overview of the official admissions requirements and procedures for the Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay, clearly distinguishing between M1 (first year) and M2 (second year) entry. It details every aspect of eligibility, application documents, exclusions, and the precise application timelines. It also presents a complete, step-by-step procedural guide to applying for the France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship, including all eligibility/anti-eligibility conditions, required and optional documents, procedural stages (including interview and verification), and policy on plagiarism/AI and annual/periodic checks. The final section lists, with citation, the official recognized alternatives to Charpak funding for this program, focusing on institutional and French-government-backed options.

## 1. Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay

### 1.1 Program Structure & Overview

- The Master’s in Economics is a two-year program (M1/M2), taught entirely in English, with a strong quantitative and research focus.
- The M2 year allows further specialization (e.g., Trade, Public/Behavioral Economics, etc.) or interdisciplinary combinations.
- The degree is designed for those aiming for academic/research careers, doctoral study, or high-level professions in the public and private sector[1][2].

---

### 1.2 Eligibility and Admission Requirements

#### A. M1 (First Year Entry)

- Degree: Bachelor’s in Economics or Bachelor’s in Mathematics (or a directly equivalent degree).
- Non-standard backgrounds (other sciences, e.g., engineering or social science)—candidate applications may be considered if demonstrating a strong quantitative profile[3][4].
- Language: Proof of English proficiency at B2 or higher. Accepted certificates include TOEFL, IELTS, or official university certification if the previous degree was taught entirely in English (consult program for accepted list):
  - IELTS min. 6.5 overall
  - TOEFL iBT min. 90[3][4]
- International applicants: Two academic referee contact details required if not enrolled in a French higher education institution in the last two years[5].
- No minimum GPA published, but strong academic performance and rigorous undergraduate quantitative training are expected[3][4][5].

#### B. M2 (Second Year Entry)

- Degree: Validated at least 240 ECTS credits (typically, an M1 or its equivalent), with a strong background in economics or mathematics[2][6].
- Must demonstrate a research profile relevant to economics.
- Similar language requirements as for M1.
- Usual requirement: prior Master 1 or equivalent in economics, relevant contextually strong programs accepted on a case-by-case basis[2][6].

---

### 1.3 Exclusions & Edge-Case Rules

- **Ineligible for Admission**:
  - Already enrolled in a higher education institution in France in the two years prior to the application (exceptions for refugee and stateless statuses)[5][7].
  - Previous holders of the same master’s degree at a French institution.
  - Applicants applying for vocational/apprenticeship (apprentissage) or professionalizing master’s streams—these tracks are excluded from the Economics Master and from most scholarships[7][8].
  - International students are NOT eligible for work-study in their first year in France except in narrowly defined cases (advanced programs)[5].
  - Admission cannot be deferred, and track changes after selection are not permitted; gap years not allowed during the funded period[7].
  - Candidates older than 30 are generally excluded from most institutional and French-government scholarships, though not always from program admission[7][8].

- **Inclusion/Special Consideration**:
  - Refugees, stateless persons, and those with subsidiary protection: Additional support and special admissions process; bonus points or priority consideration in some cases. Dedicated page and extra help (must apply through specific support channels)[5].

---

### 1.4 Required Documentation: Official List (M1 & M2)

**All applicants must provide (unless stated as 'either/or'):**
- Academic transcripts for all higher education years (official, translated if not in English/French)[4][5].
- Degree certificates (provisional accepted if final unavailable, but must be updated before enrollment).
- Motivation letter (Statement of Purpose).
- Curriculum Vitae.
- Proof of English proficiency (any one accepted: IELTS, TOEFL, institutional proof of English-medium studies—refer to official program list for accepted evidence)[4][5].
- Two letters of academic recommendation (especially for applicants not recently enrolled in French institutions).
- Passport or national ID[4][5][6].
- For certain backgrounds (e.g., applicants without economics/mathematics degrees), evidence of quantitative preparation may be required (e.g., detailed syllabi, project reports)[3][4].

**Document “either/or” rules:**
- For English proficiency, submit any one of the officially accepted proof types (see above).
- Provisional degree transcripts may be conditionally accepted, but the final award letter is required before official registration.

**Additional Documentation in Special Cases:**
- Copy of residence permit (if already in France).
- Refugee/stateless status documentation (if applicable).
- Other program or situation-specific paperwork as requested during application review[5][7].

---

### 1.5 Application Procedures and Timelines (2026)

**Platforms:**
- Main platform: INCEPTION (Université Paris-Saclay portal). This is default for most international and French applicants for both M1 and M2[5][9].
- monmaster.gouv.fr: French and some EU students, for tracks listed on the national portal. Timeline: Feb 17 – Mar 16, 2026[10].
- Études en France (EEF): Applicants residing in EEF-listed countries (e.g., India, China) apply on INCEPTION, then confirm process/admission on EEF platform for visa procedures[5][9].

**Exact Windows 2026:**
- M1 Economics: Applications open Jan 15, 2026; close Mar 16, 2026.
- M2 Economics: Applications open Feb 25, 2026; close May 25, 2026[2][6].

**Step-by-Step**
1. Review the official M1 or M2 Economics “How to Apply” webpage.
2. Gather and scan all required documents in the correct format.
3. Apply through the relevant online platform (INCEPTION, monmaster.gouv.fr, or EEF).
4. Accurately complete all required fields and upload all supporting documentation[5][9].
5. Provide two academic referee contact details; arrange for reference letters as required.
6. Once a conditional/official offer is received, confirm your acceptance on the relevant portal.
7. After admission, complete administrative/enrollment steps: fee payment (proof required), registration for health insurance, and submission of final credential documents[5][6][7].

**Verification**
- All documentation is reviewed for authenticity; fraudulent or plagiarized material leads to immediate exclusion.
- Additional checks or interviews may be required, especially if supporting documents raise questions.

**Language of Submission**
- All documentation must be in English or French; certified translations are required for materials in other languages.

## 2. France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship: Full Guide

### 2.1 Scholarship Overview

- French government merit scholarship administered by Campus France and the Embassy of France in India.
- For Indian citizens or OCI cardholders pursuing fully in-person Master’s degrees in France, including Economics at Université Paris-Saclay[11][12].

---

### 2.2 Eligibility / Ineligibility: Official Criteria

**Eligibility:**
- Indian nationality or OCI card holder;
- Age ≤ 30 years on the application deadline (March 30, 2026);
- Hold or expecting an unconditional/conditional offer to a French higher education institution for a full-time, on-campus Master’s program commencing September 2026;
- Having completed (or about to complete) an undergraduate degree (minimum three years post-secondary) by July 2026, preferably in India;
- Not holding or applying for other major French/EU government scholarships (such as Eiffel, Erasmus Mundus, or Paris-Saclay IDEX/International Masters’ grants)[11][12][13].

**Ineligibility and Exclusion:**
- Already in France in a gap year, exchange/internship semester, or research without formal full-time Master’s enrollment;
- Planning to enroll in joint/double-degree, hybrid, or distance-learning (online or blended) programs;
- Planning to take a sabbatical/gap year for the 2026-2027 academic year;
- Will exceed 30 years of age on March 30, 2026;
- Simultaneously holding another French/EU government scholarship;
- Found to have submitted plagiarized/AI-generated documents (results in immediate rejection);
- Not joining the stated program in the year of the award, or who switch programs before arrival in France[11][12][13][14].

---

### 2.3 Required and Optional Application Documents

- **Mandatory:**
  - Official offer/acceptance letter from the French university/institution (or proof of application if awaiting admission);
  - Academic transcripts/marksheets for every year/semester post-secondary (provisional allowed only if final results pending);
  - Detailed, up-to-date Curriculum Vitae (CV);
  - One or two letters of recommendation from faculty or employers (portal will specify at submission);
  - Valid passport scan (main data/photo page);
  - Statement of Purpose (SOP)—clear, original motivation, study plans, and why France/this program[11][12].
- **Language Certificate:**
  - If the target program is in English: English proficiency proof (as required by university). French language certificate is **optional but advantageous**;
  - If the program is in French, official French language certification is mandatory (DELF, DALF, TEF, TCF, etc.)[12].
- **Conditional/Alternative:**
  - If waiting for final grades/degree: submit provisional or last available transcript and update upon completion[11][13].
  - Letters of recommendation: at least one is mandatory; two are preferred if possible.

---

### 2.4 Step-by-Step Official Application Procedure (2026)

1. **Admission**: Secure at least an offer or proof of application to the eligible French master’s program (e.g., Paris-Saclay Economics).
2. **Prepare Documents**: Convert/transcribe all documents into PDF or JPEG as per the portal instructions.
3. **Online Application**: Register and submit the application on the Campus France India platform, uploading all mandatory and any optional documents before 23:59 IST, March 30, 2026.
4. **Document Review**:
    - Applications are screened for completeness, eligibility, and document validity.
    - Academic excellence, clarity of SOP, and alignment with program goals are key criteria.
5. **Interview Round**:
    - Shortlisted candidates are invited for an online (video) interview, scheduled April 13–30, 2026.
    - The interview assesses motivation, academic plans, understanding of the course, and commitment.
6. **Final Selection and Notification**:
    - Decisions announced at the end of May 2026.
    - Offers are conditional pending receipt of final degree, complete transcripts, or other outstanding items.
7. **Post-selection**:
    - Visa application and enrollment assistance provided by Campus France and the Embassy.
    - Non-compliance with eligibility, false documentation, or withdrawal leads to withdrawal of the award at any time[11][12][13].

---

### 2.5 Document Integrity, AI/Plagiarism Policy, and Ongoing Verification

- All Statements of Purpose undergo automated and manual plagiarism detection.
- Evidence of copying, unoriginal, or AI-generated content results in application disqualification.
- The use of generative AI for statement writing is discouraged and easily detected; original personal narrative is strictly enforced.
- Campus France/Embassy reserves the right to request further verification, additional documents, or confirmatory interviews at any time, including before and after arrival in France.
- Recipients are subject to annual and/or semester verification of full-time enrollment and study progress; scholarship can be suspended for non-enrollment, academic failure, or violation of any terms[11][12][14].

## 3. Official Alternative Scholarships & Funding Opportunities

The following options, recognized by Université Paris-Saclay and official French institutions, are available for students who do not qualify for the Charpak Master’s Scholarship:

### 3.1 Université Paris-Saclay International Master’s Scholarships (IDEX)

- €10,000 per academic year for living expenses (paid over ten months), plus up to €900 for travel/visa costs;
- Eligibility: Non-French, non-EU students entering M1 (one year renewable) or M2 (one year) for a research-oriented master’s; not holding conflicting major government scholarships; under 30 years old[15][16].
- Application: Only by nomination after initial admission to a Paris-Saclay master’s program. No unsolicited applications—eligible students are invited by the program.
- Exclusions: Already enrolled in France, holding other major scholarships, or entering via apprenticeship/vocational programs[15].
- [Official details](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/admissions/scholarships-and-financial-support/international-masters-scholarships)

### 3.2 France Excellence Eiffel Scholarship

- Premier French government scholarship for Master’s and PhD students from outside France, administered by Campus France.
- Benefits: €1,200/month stipend, travel, health insurance, cultural activities; does **not** cover tuition fees.
- Application is only via nomination by the French institution (contact the master’s program for details); applicants cannot apply independently—must be requested well in advance (deadline for institution: January 8, 2026).
- For non-French nationals under 29 years applying for master’s.
- [France Excellence Eiffel details](https://www.campusfrance.org/en/france-excellence-eiffel-scholarship-program)[17].

### 3.3 Erasmus Mundus Joint Master Degrees

- Some economics-related Erasmus Mundus consortia include French partners, though not all are directly linked with Paris-Saclay’s Economics Master. Funding includes full tuition and a living allowance.
- Application deadlines tend to fall December–January of the year preceding enrollment.
- [Erasmus Mundus info (Campus France)](https://www.campusfrance.org/en/erasmus-mundus-joint-masters-scholarships)[18].

### 3.4 CROUS Bursaries

- Primarily for French, EU, or long-term non-EU residents, but in rare cases accessible to non-EU international students with two years continuous residence or strong ties to France.
- Bursary amount varies by family income; includes tuition waivers, subsidized accommodation, and meals. Not routinely available to new international students.
- [CROUS eligibility and details](https://www.mastersportal.com/scholarships/10133/crous-bursary.html)[19].

### 3.5 Regional and Embassy-Based Awards

- Several French regional governments and embassies run scholarship or co-funding programs; most require separate applications and have varying eligibility rules. Examples include Ile-de-France scholarships and formal bilateral government-funded grants.
- [Campus France scholarship search portal](https://www.campusfrance.org/en/search-scholarship-program)[20].

## 4. Tuition, Fees, and Official Student Support

- **Annual tuition** for non-EU students is between €3,770 and €4,000 (as of 2026; subject to institutional adjustment). EU/EEA rates are ~€243–€254 per year[3][7][15].
- Université Paris-Saclay provides a full International Welcome Desk, visa/residence support, buddy program, housing guidance, and student integration services[7].
- International students can work up to 964 hours per year in France (subject to regulatory compliance)[7].

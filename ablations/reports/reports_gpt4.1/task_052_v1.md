# Comprehensive Analysis of the Industrial Automation Market in the Saudi Arabian Manufacturing Sector (2015–2025)

## Overview

This report provides an in-depth analysis of the industrial automation market in Saudi Arabia’s manufacturing sector from 2015 to 2025. It covers historical and projected market size, compound annual growth rate (CAGR), robotics penetration (robot density), key Vision 2030 drivers for automation, named project awards for Siemens and ABB in NEOM and the Red Sea Project, and actionable sales recommendations. All data and policy analysis prioritize authoritative and official sources.

---

## 1. Market Size, CAGR, and Robotics Penetration Rate (2015–2025)

### 1.1 Historical and Projected Market Size

- The Saudi industrial automation market—including discrete and process manufacturing—demonstrated robust growth, reaching an estimated USD 1.99 billion in 2024. The market is forecasted to expand to approximately USD 3.72 billion by 2032, reflecting a strong CAGR of 10.4% in the period following 2025[1].
- The factory automation segment (subset) reached USD 2.3 million in 2025 and is projected to grow to USD 4.3 million by 2034 (CAGR 7.04%). Automation in manufacturing is driven by strong government support, labor challenges, and the ambition of Vision 2030 to create competitive, technology-led industries[2].
- The industrial robotics market segment—robotics hardware and control platforms—grows at a CAGR above 15%, with revenue rising from USD 207.4 million (2024) to USD 572.8 million (2030)[3]. The adoption is fastest in electronics, electrical, chemical, plastics, and automotive verticals.
- Market estimates for the overall robotics and advanced manufacturing technology segment show expansion from USD 12.5 billion (2025) to USD 32.8 billion (2031), at a CAGR of 17.4%, emphasizing the rapid digital transformation within Saudi industry[4].

### 1.2 Robotics Penetration (Robot Density)

- Robot density (number of installed industrial robots per 10,000 manufacturing workers) is a key metric of digital maturity. Official International Federation of Robotics (IFR) data does not yet publish a Saudi-specific density; global benchmarks for 2022 were 151 robots per 10,000 workers. By comparison: South Korea 1,220; Singapore 818; Germany 449; Japan 446; China 392. Saudi Arabia’s industrial robotics install base is rapidly increasing, with projections from 2,100 robots (2025) to 8,000 (2034), but the density remains below global leaders and the global average[5][6][7].
- Given ~1.2 million manufacturing employees[8], robot density in Saudi manufacturing is likely below 20 robots per 10,000 workers as of 2025, but climbing fast. The growth rate far exceeds the pre-2020 period, with adoption catalyzed by Vision 2030 policy measures.

### 1.3 Key Market Segments and Industry Characteristics

- Automation deployment spans PID systems, DCS, SCADA, PLCs, industrial robots, sensors, HMIs, and AI-enabled platforms. Rapid adoption occurs in automotive (with a 2030 target of 300,000 vehicles/year), electronics, logistics, consumer goods, food and beverage, and pharmaceuticals[2][4].
- Industrial automation benefits include higher efficiency, operational safety, digital traceability, and productivity, especially in sectors facing labor shortages and rising quality expectations.

## 2. Saudi Vision 2030: Impact on Robotics and Automation Demand

### 2.1 Strategic Policy Measures and Investment Targets

- Vision 2030’s National Industrial Strategy commits to building a global industrial hub by 2030, targeting SAR 895 billion GDP contribution from manufacturing, creating 2.1 million jobs, and generating SAR 557 billion in non-oil exports[9]. This is to be achieved by integrating advanced manufacturing, automation, and digitalization as core pillars.
- The “Future Factories Program” aims to transform over 4,000 factories from labor-dependent to smart, automated operations by 2030, advancing operational efficiency and reducing low-skill labor reliance[10].
- Advanced Manufacturing Hub Strategy outlines more than 800 investment opportunities with a cumulative value near USD 273 billion, explicitly focusing on scaling up automation and Industry 4.0 technology adoption[11].

### 2.2 Regulatory Reforms and Incentives

- 100% foreign ownership is now permitted in manufacturing under new investment laws, aimed at attracting automation technology partners[12].
- The Saudi Industrial Development Fund (SIDF) offers financing covering up to 75% of project capital with favorable terms for automation-heavy projects, and the “Made in Saudi” and Iktva programs guarantee market demand for localized, tech-driven manufacturing[12].
- The Ministry of Industry and Mineral Resources and NIDLP set aggressive sectoral targets (manufacturing GDP share reaching 18–20% by 2030) and developed special economic zones (e.g., NEOM’s Oxagon, KAEC) designed for advanced manufacturing operations[13][14].

### 2.3 Digitization and Workforce Development Initiatives

- The “Main Digitization Track” provides grant-based support and cost-sharing for implementation of digitization and automation solutions in factories, including production line automation and advanced material handling systems[15].
- Factories are incentivized to raise their “digital maturity score,” a government-calibrated KPI aligning investment incentives with advanced automation adoption[10][15].
- Workforce transformation programs create new “future skills” pathways to train local talent for robotics and automation operation, supporting Vision 2030’s job creation and technology transfer goals.

### 2.4 International Partnerships, Demonstration Projects, and Global Branding

- Saudi Arabia’s industrial sector presence at Hannover Messe (Shanghai, China), and its hosting of “Industrial Transformation Saudi Arabia,” demonstrates global leadership in adopting Fourth Industrial Revolution technologies, including robotics and AI[16][17].
- The “Industrial Lighthouses” program aims for at least 14 Saudi factories to reach World Economic Forum Lighthouse status—a global benchmark in digital and robotics adoption—by 2030[18].

## 3. Project Awards for Siemens and ABB in NEOM and Red Sea Project (Since 2021)

### 3.1 NEOM: Siemens and ABB (Status as of 2026)

- **No public evidence exists of Siemens or ABB being awarded major contracts for NEOM** (including Oxagon, the logistics and advanced manufacturing hub) as of April 2026. All official NEOM, Siemens, and ABB press rooms and annual reports, as well as reliable business news (MEED, Bloomberg), confirm there are no announced contracts, project names, or values linking either firm to NEOM since 2021[19][20][21][22][23].

### 3.2 The Red Sea Project and AMAALA: ABB

- **ABB has secured official project awards related to The Red Sea Project and AMAALA (Saudi flagship regenerative tourism initiatives by Red Sea Global, PIF-owned):**
    - **Scope of Work:** ABB supplies smart energy technology and extensive smart infrastructure, including:
        - Over 10 System pro E low voltage synchronization panels for multi-megawatt on-site generation, digital platforms (ABB Ability™ Energy and Asset Manager), and full asset performance optimization of all power subsystems[24][25][26].
        - Complete supply of over 1,200 electric vehicle charging infrastructure (EVCI) units—AC and DC fast chargers—for The Red Sea Project and AMAALA, the largest deployment of its type in Saudi Arabia[27][28]. Chargers are manufactured locally, promoting local content and workforce upskilling.
    - **Date of Award:** First major award announced December 2021; EVCI deployment confirmed in late 2023 and early 2024[24][27].
    - **Contract Values:** **Not publicly disclosed in any official statement or regulatory filing** by ABB, Red Sea Global, or Saudi authorities.
    - **Official Project/Contract Names:** No unique contract ID is public, but references use the umbrella “Red Sea Project smart energy infrastructure” and “Red Sea / AMAALA e-mobility initiative.”
    - **Unique Project Features:** 100% renewable energy at completion; advanced digital and modular power systems to optimize energy use; contribution to Vision 2030’s eco-tourism and sustainability goals[24][26].

### 3.3 Siemens

- No available public data or official press releases confirm Siemens has received project awards for The Red Sea Project, AMAALA, or NEOM as of April 2026[20][22].

## 4. Actionable Sales Implications and Recommendations

### 4.1 Target Digitization and Future Factories Incentives

- Sales professionals should focus on highlighting solutions that directly elevate “digital maturity” metrics of prospective manufacturing clients. The Saudi government provides incentives and even direct funding for digital/automation upgrades within the Future Factories Program[10][15].
- Position offerings that help clients qualify for or leverage government grants, preferential financing, or industrial transformation programs—especially in verticals prioritized under Vision 2030 (automotive, renewable energy, electronics, logistics warehouses, and process industries)[9][13].

### 4.2 Local Content and Partnership Compliance

- Emphasize product localization, local service capabilities, and technology transfer to comply with Made in Saudi and Iktva mandates. Support customers in attaining localization targets—a key determinant of contract wins, especially on major projects or when competing for government and Giga-project spending[12][27].
- Partner with local integrators, solution providers, or joint ventures, especially for complex end-to-end offerings and when bidding on projects linked to Vision 2030 government programs or flagship zones (e.g., NEOM, KAEC, Red Sea).

### 4.3 Demonstrate Proven Digital Value and Sustainability Credentials

- Saudi manufacturers are increasingly evaluated on sustainability, safety, and efficiency gains. Sales narratives should stress real-world business value: operational cost reduction, workforce safety, traceability, and alignment with national sustainability KPIs[24][26].
- Case studies from comparable ABB Red Sea deployments or other local reference projects—especially involving renewable energy, e-mobility, or AI integration—are highly persuasive in government-influenced procurement.

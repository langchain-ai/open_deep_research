# Comprehensive Analysis: Mesa Group Consulting’s Affiliate Strategy with SuperMoney and Novae

## Introduction

Mesa Group Consulting, operated by Evert Calderon, deploys a dual strategy of direct service delivery and affiliate partnerships to offer a broad range of financial products and services. Its partnership with two leading fintech companies—SuperMoney and Novae—enables Mesa Group to deliver or refer solutions across credit repair, lending, debt relief, business credit, estate planning, and insurance. This report provides a deeply detailed, source-supported analysis of:  
1. The operational and regulatory distinctions between SuperMoney and Novae, and how these structures limit Mesa Group’s regulatory risk;  
2. An explicit, evidentiary mapping of Mesa Group’s website service pages to affiliate offerings;  
3. Clarification of Mesa Group’s dual business model, distinguishing direct from affiliate-fulfilled services;  
4. An evaluation of how leveraging both partners gives Mesa Group competitive breadth versus single-vertical providers.

## 1. Operational and Regulatory Distinctions: SuperMoney vs. Novae

### 1.1 SuperMoney

- **Business Model and Fulfillment:**  
  SuperMoney is a U.S.-based financial services marketplace. It provides product comparisons across personal loans, credit cards, refinancing, debt consolidation, insurance, estate planning, and more, connecting consumers with offers from third-party financial service providers. SuperMoney’s role is purely informational and technological: it does not itself originate loans, issue credit cards, provide insurance, or engage in the fulfillment of any financial product. All fulfillment is managed by the respective third-party provider to which the user is referred via the SuperMoney platform or its affiliate’s white-labeled widgets[1][2].

- **Regulatory Position and Compliance:**  
  SuperMoney does not require financial services licensing (such as lending or insurance licenses) because it does not offer or broker regulated financial products. Instead, all responsibility for product compliance, regulatory adherence, and service fulfillment resides with the underlying provider. SuperMoney’s policies make this explicit, stating:  
  > "SuperMoney is not a lender, broker, or financial advisor, does not control third-party providers or their product terms, and is not responsible for your relationship with those providers."  
  Users and partners are indemnified to the extent permitted by law, and any legal or regulatory claims stemming from product fulfillment or misrepresentation rest with the third-party[1][3].

- **Affiliate Risk and Indemnification:**  
  SuperMoney’s affiliate program documentation and user Terms of Service strictly limit regulatory responsibility. Affiliates—such as Mesa Group Consulting—promote SuperMoney’s marketplace tools and acquire compensation for lead generation, but do not participate in fulfillment, licensing, or regulatory compliance for the product or service delivered. Affiliates are not considered agents or employees; their risk profile is limited to abiding by acceptable use, advertising, and compliance policies for partner marketing, not product delivery[1][2].

- **Compliance & Security Measures:**  
  SuperMoney focuses on information privacy and data security, using industry standards like 256-bit SSL encryption and annual SOC 2 Type II audits. However, actual regulatory compliance for financial services (e.g., TILA, RESPA, insurance laws) is always the responsibility of the underlying provider[3].

### 1.2 Novae

- **Business Model and Fulfillment:**  
  Novae is a national fintech consulting and affiliate organization, offering a broad spectrum of services: credit repair, debt relief, business funding, business credit, estate planning, insurance, and other financial wellness solutions. While it runs its own white label and affiliate programs, Novae (and its approved service providers) handles all product/service fulfillment, compliance, client onboarding, and delivery, not the affiliate[4][5][6].

- **Regulatory Position and Compliance:**  
  Novae is the licensed entity or works with licensed providers as required for relevant regulated products (e.g., credit repair, debt relief, insurance). Affiliates, including referral partners like Mesa Group Consulting, are legally structured as Independent Business Owners (IBOs), not employees or agents. The Novae Affiliate Agreement details:  
  > "Affiliates shall not be treated as employees for federal or state tax purposes... The relationship is solely that of independent contractor"[7].  
  All product-related regulatory and licensing responsibility—credit bureau compliance, insurance approvals, banking rules—rests with Novae and its vendors. The affiliate’s regulatory exposure is strictly limited to following Novae’s marketing, compliance, and policy standards[7][8].

- **Affiliate Risk and Indemnification:**  
  Novae’s published policies require affiliates to strictly follow branding, claims, and disclosure rules, indemnifying Novae for breach. Affiliates that misrepresent services or make unauthorized statements face termination and possible forfeiture of commissions. Otherwise, regulatory obligations (e.g., the Credit Repair Organizations Act, Gramm–Leach–Bliley, state debt relief statutes) are absorbed by Novae and its official providers. No evidence is present of affiliates bearing licensure or product risk when acting within policy[7][8].

- **Industry Standards:**  
  Novae is a member of the Direct Selling Association (DSA) and subjects itself to the DSA’s Code of Ethics, which mandates high consumer protection, truthful marketing, and regulatory compliance[6].

### 1.3 Comparison & Resultant Risk Shift for Mesa Group Consulting

- **Fulfillment:**  
  Both partners (SuperMoney and Novae) handle all aspects of product/service delivery and compliance. Mesa Group Consulting does not deliver, broker, or directly manage fulfillment for affiliate-supported products.

- **Licensing:**  
  Mesa Group does not require any licenses to operate as an affiliate or independent marketing entity. All applicable product licensure (e.g., insurance agent, debt settlement, credit repair) is retained by Novae (and its direct vendors) or by the third-party providers on SuperMoney’s marketplace[3][7].

- **Regulatory Risk:**  
  Mesa Group’s regulatory risk is heavily mitigated by structure. So long as it abides by affiliate marketing policy, it avoids responsibility for consumer law, state/federal licensing, and product-specific compliance. Any deviation (false claims, undisclosed compensation, unlicensed advice) could expose Mesa Group to risk, but its policies, terms, and public disclosures are designed to minimize this[1][3][7][9].

## 3. Mesa Group’s Dual Business Model: Direct vs. Affiliate-Delivered Services

Mesa Group operates two complementary business roles:

### 3.1 Direct Service Delivery

- **Credit Repair and Financial Coaching:**  
  Mesa Group directly delivers its "Mesa360 Credit System™," which includes:
  - Professional audit of all three credit bureaus
  - Personal coaching and one-on-one assistance
  - Proprietary software for DIY or guided restoration  
  This service is proprietary to Mesa Group; all interaction, coaching, and remediation work is handled internally and not via affiliate referral[12][17][18].

### 3.2 Affiliate-Fulfilled (External) Services

- **Credit Cards, Personal Loans, Loan Refinancing:**  
  Mesa Group acts as an intermediary, referring clients to SuperMoney’s marketplace of offerings, earning commission per qualified lead or funded product[10][11][13][14].
- **Debt Relief (Personal & Business), Business Credit, Funding:**  
  Mesa Group refers clients to Novae, which handles qualification, compliance, enrollment, ongoing management, and resolution[7][9][15].
- **Trust & Will and Life Insurance:**  
  Users are referred via affiliate arrangements—likely to Novae’s white label offerings or its partners—for document drafting, attorney support, and insurance quote aggregation[16][6].
- **Credit Monitoring:**  
  Provided through SmartCredit, a third-party partner, not an in-house solution[19].
  
Mesa Group is transparent in disclaimers, stating, “Mesa Group Consulting is not a lender and does not make credit decisions or provide financing. We may receive compensation through affiliate partnerships and referral arrangements…”[9][12][16].

## 4. Strategic Assessment: Competitive Advantages through Dual Partnerships

Leveraging both SuperMoney and Novae enables Mesa Group Consulting to offer a comprehensive menu of financial solutions under its umbrella, creating several tangible business advantages:

- **Broad Product Coverage:**  
  By integrating both partners, Mesa Group can support nearly all major consumer and small business financial needs, from credit repair to lending, debt relief, business financing, credit building, and estate planning[10][15]. This spectrum is difficult for single-vertical competitors—e.g., firms solely focused on credit repair or debt settlement—to match.

- **Seamless Client Journey:**  
  Clients seeking to rebuild credit can remain within Mesa Group’s ecosystem for subsequent needs, including loans, debt restructuring, business capital, and insurance, rather than being referred out piecemeal or having to engage multiple service providers. This encourages long-term relationships and cross-selling opportunities.

- **Risk Mitigation and Regulatory Simplicity:**  
  By acting as a marketing/consulting intermediary, Mesa Group avoids the need for specific financial service licensure, compliance procedures, or fulfillment infrastructure. All regulatory exposure and heavy lifting are shifted to highly regulated and well-capitalized partners[1][2][7].

- **White Label/Branded Experience:**  
  Both SuperMoney and Novae offer white label and embeddable solutions, allowing Mesa Group to maintain brand presence and user experience continuity, even when fulfillment occurs through a partner platform[6][2].

- **Residual and Performance-Based Revenue:**  
  Affiliate programs (especially Novae’s multilevel structure) create multiple revenue streams, including direct commissions, residual income, and bonuses. This adds stability and scales with client volume and downstream affiliate activity[7][8].

- **Faster Expansion and Adaptability:**  
  Adding new service verticals (e.g., estate planning, insurance) does not require years of regulatory approval or operational ramp-up. Mesa Group can rapidly expand its offerings alongside the development of partner platforms, outpacing slower-moving, niche competitors.

## Conclusion

Mesa Group Consulting, under Evert Calderon, operates a dual business model leveraging both direct service delivery (notably in credit repair and coaching) and an extensive array of affiliate partnerships—principally with SuperMoney for consumer credit/lending offers and Novae for debt relief, business funding, estate planning, and insurance. Official documentation and site disclosures show a clear operational and regulatory demarcation: Mesa Group refers clients, while fulfillment, compliance, and licensure are the responsibility of the respective affiliate partner. This strategy (1) shields Mesa Group from regulatory/product risk, (2) enables a wide product portfolio with minimal overhead or licensing requirements, (3) positions it to serve as a one-stop financial resource, and (4) allows competition with both single-vertical boutiques and larger national aggregators. The mapping of website pages to affiliate offerings is explicitly documented for most products, with a few cases (notably trust & will and life insurance) strongly inferred from partner program scope. Overall, Mesa Group’s strategic model capitalizes on deep affiliate integration to drive growth, retention, and revenue with limited regulatory burden.

# Comparative Analysis: Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund vs DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds

## Introduction

This report presents an in-depth comparative analysis of the Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund and the DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds (FoF). The comparison follows four key areas as specified in the research brief: (1) portfolio composition, including geographic and sector concentration; (2) overlap in the top 20 holdings; (3) expense ratios; and (4) historical returns over the 1-year, 3-year, and 5-year periods ending December 2024. The suitability of each fund for a 2-year and a 5-year investment horizon—considering risk and diversification—is also assessed. The analysis is based mainly on official fund fact sheets, annual reports, regulatory filings, and respected aggregator platforms.

## Overview of Each Fund

### Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund

- **Type:** Fund of fund (primarily invests in the JPMorgan Greater China Fund)
- **Investment Universe:** Predominantly equities listed in Greater China (China, Taiwan, Hong Kong)
- **Risk Level:** Very High
- **Launch Date:** 2009
- **Benchmark:** MSCI Golden Dragon Index
- **Structure:** The fund invests almost exclusively (over 96%) in the underlying JPMorgan Greater China Fund, resulting in a highly concentrated exposure to Greater China blue-chip equities, especially technology and consumption sectors[1][2][3][4].

### DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds

- **Type:** Fund of fund (invests in global innovation-themed funds and ETFs)
- **Investment Universe:** Broad global equities with a thematic focus on innovation (AI, cloud computing, biotech, fintech, etc.)
- **Risk Level:** Very High
- **Launch Date:** February 2022
- **Benchmark:** MSCI ACWI (All Country World Index)
- **Structure:** Holds a small basket of major global mutual funds and ETFs (notably Bluebox Global Technology Fund, iShares NASDAQ 100 UCITS ETF, and others), primarily with a US/technology bias, but with exposure to global and emerging markets internet innovation leaders[5][6][7][8].

## Portfolio Composition and Sector/Geographic Concentration

### Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund

- **Geographic Allocation:** 
  - 64.2%–64.8% in China
  - 30.0%–30.7% in Taiwan
  - 4.7%–4.8% in Hong Kong
- **Sector Allocation:**
  - Technology: 37.05–39.37%
  - Consumer Cyclical: 17.88–20.32%
  - Communication Services: 13.03–17.36%
  - Financial Services: 11.38–12.28%
  - Industrials: ~7.26%
- **Key Characteristics:**
  - Highly concentrated exposure in a single region (Greater China)
  - Tilted towards large cap technology and internet leaders (TSMC, Tencent, Alibaba)
  - Vulnerable to regional and policy risk, but positioned to capture high-growth potential if Greater China markets perform well[1][2][3][4].

### DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds

- **Geographic Allocation:** 
  - Approximate 60–70% in US/global developed markets via NASDAQ-linked and technology ETFs
  - 12–14% in China via the KraneShares CSI China Internet ETF
  - Remaining exposure through other global innovation funds (biotech, medical devices, etc.)
- **Sector Allocation:**
  - Technology/IT: Estimated 65–75% (aggregated across seen underlying funds)
  - Biotech/Healthcare/Medical Technology: Minor allocations (~4%)
  - Other Innovation Sectors: Cloud, Fintech, E-commerce, Digital Infrastructure
- **Key Characteristics:**
  - Much broader global and sectoral diversification
  - Underlying exposure to both developed and select emerging market innovation leaders
  - Lower vulnerability to any single country’s or sector’s policy or economic shocks[5][6][7][8].

### Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund

  - Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co Ltd: ~10%

  - Delta Electronics Inc, Elite Material Co Ltd, PDD Holdings, other China/Taiwan large caps[3][4].
- **Holding Structure:** Top five stocks make up around 30% of fund value; portfolio highly concentrated around technology and internet mega-caps of Greater China.

### DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds

- **Top Holdings** (major underlying funds as of Q1 2026):
  - Bluebox Global Technology Fund: 35–39%
  - iShares NASDAQ 100 UCITS ETF: 27–28%
  - IVZ Nasdaq 100 EW ACC: 12–13%
  - KraneShares CSI China Internet UCITS ETF: 12–14%
  - Fidelity Select Medical Technology portfolio: ~4%
- **Look-Through Holdings:** 
  - Through US/Global ETFs, major exposures include Apple, Microsoft, Amazon, Alphabet (Google), Meta, Nvidia, and other NASDAQ 100 leaders.
  - Via KraneShares ETF, there is secondary exposure to Chinese internet stocks (e.g., Alibaba, Tencent, JD.com, Meituan).
- **Overlap:** The only likely overlap in the top 20 is via the KraneShares CSI China Internet ETF, which contains Chinese internet stocks like Alibaba and Tencent that are also top holdings in the Edelweiss fund. However, the overlap is minimal and indirect (via ETFs) and forms a much smaller part of overall DSP portfolio exposure[5][6][8].

  - Regular plan: 1.50%–1.64%
  - Direct plan: 0.75%
  - Underlying JPMorgan Greater China Fund: ~1.81% ongoing charge[3][4][9][10]
  - Total cost may be higher when including both feeder and underlying fund expenses.

- **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF:**
  - Regular plan: 2.04–2.05% (all-in, including underlying funds/ETFs)

  - Expense ratios reflect the global, multi-manager nature of the product[6][10][16].

## Risk-Return and Diversification Assessment

### Diversification & Concentration

- **Edelweiss Greater China:** 
  - Geographic: Extremely concentrated (single region: China/Taiwan/Hong Kong)
  - Sector: High (technology/internet/consumer dominant)
  - Suitable for investors seeking focused exposure to Greater China, with higher short-term upside (and higher policy/geopolitical risk).

- **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF:**
  - Geographic: Global (mainly US-developed markets, but includes China/Asia and some Europe)
  - Sector: Highly innovative sectors, but less reliant on a single country or sector risk
  - Suitable for investors seeking thematic innovation exposure with better global and sector diversification, reducing idiosyncratic regional risk.

### Historical Risk/Return

- **Edelweiss:** Outperforms in years where Greater China surges, but underperforms during regional crises or sell-offs. Five-year return is subdued due to drawdowns in 2021–2022; risk is amplified by single-region, single-theme focus.
- **DSP:** Offers strong, smoother returns over available periods, robust during market recoveries, and less exposed to risk from any one market/policy regime. Unable to assess 5-year performance, but 3-year and since-inception returns are higher than Edelweiss and its category average[7][16][19].

## Suitability for 2-Year vs 5-Year Investment Horizons

### 2-Year Investment Horizon

- **Edelweiss Greater China:** Can be suitable for tactical bets if the investor holds a strong conviction about short-term Chinese market recovery or policy loosening, as recent rebounds show very high short-term returns. However, the probability of volatility and drawdowns is high. Not suitable for risk-averse investors[3][4].
- **DSP Global Innovation:** Preferred for most investors seeking reasonable returns with lower single-country risk over the next two years. Global exposure limits downside from one region’s policy or events. Smoother performance likely over a short horizon, but as an innovation fund, still carries thematic risk[7][8][16][19].

### 5-Year Investment Horizon

- **Edelweiss Greater China:** May offer higher returns if Greater China enters a super-cycle of growth; however, as history shows, the region is vulnerable to policy shifts, regulatory risks, and geopolitics. Volatility can be substantial, and long periods of subpar performance (recent 5-year period being negative) are possible. Suitable only for investors with high-risk tolerance and China expertise[3][4].
- **DSP Global Innovation:** Stronger case for longer-term holding due to global, multi-thematic diversification, and participation in the secular innovation wave (AI, cloud, internet, healthcare, etc.). Higher consistency and lower risk of large negative periods (barring global crises). Most suitable for a 5-year horizon among the two, even though full 5-year return data are not available yet[7][8][16][19].

## Conclusion

- **For a 2-Year Horizon:** DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF offers greater stability, better risk control, and diversification. Edelweiss Greater China may deliver high returns if the market continues its recovery, but this is accompanied by significantly elevated risk.
- **For a 5-Year Horizon:** The DSP fund is generally more suitable due to its diversification across regions and sectors, offering greater consistency. Edelweiss Greater China is only recommended for high conviction, high risk-tolerance investors focused on China.

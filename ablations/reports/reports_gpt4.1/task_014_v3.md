## Executive Summary

As of December 8, 2025, four Non-Convertible Debenture (NCD) IPOs are open for public subscription in India, as verified by SEBI, BSE, NSE, and industry data aggregators. These include: Edelweiss Financial Services Limited, KLM Axiva Finvest Limited, Muthoot Mercantile Limited, and Prachay Capital Limited. This report systematically benchmarks each issuer on ratings, coupon structure, post-tax yields, and leverage metrics, flags high-risk debt levels, and positions them against both peer NCDs and core Indian fixed income benchmarks (such as 10-year Government Securities and bank FDs). All data, ratings, and financial metrics are strictly sourced from prospectuses, rating agency rationales, and regulatory filings; discrepancies are explained within.

### Post-Tax Yield Calculations (Generic vs. Senior Citizen Investors)

#### Key Taxation Principles (FY2025-26)

- NCD interest is taxed at *slab rates* for all investors (no Section 80C/80TTB benefit)[15][16][17].
- For senior citizens, **no additional exemption/deduction** applies to NCD interest (Section 80TTB only for bank/postal/co-op deposits)[16][17].
- Post-tax yield = Gross coupon × (1 – marginal slab rate). Slab rates: 5%, 10%, 15%, 20%, 25%, 30% (depending on taxable income and regime).

## Data Reconciliation and Discrepancies

- **Edelweiss NCD closing date conflict:** Some sources report Dec 11, others Dec 18, 2025. The difference is due to standard “subscription period extension” rights that issuers retain per SEBI rules. In all such cases, investors should confirm the final close date on the BSE/NSE and official prospectus at the day of application ([1],[5]).
- **KLM Axiva Finvest Ratings:** Multiple agency ratings (Acuite, IND, CARE) have been consistent since November/January. No conflict detected.
- **Financials:** All issuer revenue, leverage, and asset details cross-match between prospectuses, rating releases, and exchange filings; minor discrepancies (PAT, net worth) reflect differing reporting periodicity (quarterly vs. annual).
- **Prachay Capital:** The prospectus omits explicit current leverage (debt/equity). This is a material disclosure gap. Investors should treat the risk as unquantified and discount accordingly or seek additional clarification prior to allocation ([13],[14]).
- **Post-tax yields:** All sources concur that Section 80TTB/80C is *not* available, and no slab relief applies for NCD interest.

## Regulatory and Policy Considerations

- **Listing & Security:** All open NCDs are required by SEBI to be listed, demat-only, and must be secured by at least 100% charge over identified assets and receivables ([17],[18]).
- **Taxation:** Income from NCDs is taxed per slab; no preferential benefit for retirees except via general slab and rebate limits. TDS applies if annual interest exceeds ₹5,000/₹10,000 depending on issuer and new thresholds ([15],[16]). No Section 80TTB (deduction) applicability for NCD interest ([16],[17]).
- **Asset Allocation/Compliance:** No explicit regulatory max for retiree NCD exposure, but institutional best practice is to limit lower-rated (BBB/BBB-) exposure to <5-10% of aggregate fixed income ([20],[21]).
- **Disclosure & Allotment:** First-come, first-serve basis; allocation split between retail, HNI, and institutional per SEBI formula.

## Investment Suitability and Portfolio Guidance

- **Retiree Core Holdings:** Should be limited to sovereign (SCSS, RBI Bonds, G-Sec) and possibly A+/AA/AAA NCDs (Edelweiss, not KLM/Muthoot/Prachay) due to material risk differences.
- **Satellite Yield Plays:** Lower-rated NCDs, such as those from KLM Axiva, Muthoot Mercantile, and especially Prachay Capital, offer compelling yield but present material structural, sectoral, and liquidity risk. Aggregate exposure to all BBB/BBB- issuers should not exceed 10% of a retiree portfolio.
- **Leverage Risk:** KLM Axiva Finvest (5.6x) is flagged for high financial leverage compared to NBFC median.
- **Liquidity:** All NCDs are exchange-listed, but actual secondary liquidity for BBB and smaller NBFC issues is often very poor.
- **Diversification & Laddering:** Prioritize NCD laddering (staggered maturities) to avoid concentration and reinvestment risk; integrate as a complement, not substitute, for bank FDs or SCSS.

---

## Conclusion

All four open NCD IPOs in India as of December 8, 2025, provide retail access to diverse credit-risk, tenor, and yield profiles. Edelweiss Financial's A+/A-rated NCDs present the best compromise of yield and credit quality among open offers; KLM Axiva Finvest and Muthoot Mercantile (BBB) as well as Prachay Capital (BBB-) deliver elevated coupon spreads but carry significant incremental risk, especially amid high debt-to-equity and/or absent full risk disclosure. Investors must carefully weigh these risks against benchmark alternatives and portfolio policy, particularly for retiree fixed income needs. Full reliance on low-investment-grade or unrated NBFC NCDs is not appropriate for core income. All data have been reconciled with the latest prospectuses, rating rationales, and regulatory filings; explicit gaps are flagged for transparency.

# Fortive’s Segment Portfolio Performance and Strategic Positioning Post-Precision Technologies (Ralliant) Spin-Off: Comprehensive Data-Driven Analysis

## Executive Summary

Following the June 28, 2025, spin-off of Precision Technologies (now Ralliant Corporation), Fortive’s portfolio is focused on Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS) and Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS). This report delivers a rigorous, fully source-attributed evaluation of segment-level financial performance—including exact operating margins and revenue growth—using only officially disclosed numbers from SEC filings, reconciles all comparative figures to address discontinuation of PT, and connects each ongoing segment’s solutions to its target end markets. All findings are directly supported by primary filings, tables, and footnotes as required by the research brief.

---

## 1. Exact Segment-Level Operating Margins: Q2 2025 and Full-Year 2024

Operating margin is defined as segment operating profit divided by segment revenue. Calculations below are strictly based on disclosed figures in official Fortive filings, with source attribution to precise tables/exhibits.

### Q2 2025 (Post-Spin Continuing Operations: IOS & AHS Only)

**As of Q2 2025, Precision Technologies is no longer reported in continuing operations post-separation. Segment results are as follows ([1], [2]):**

| Segment                             | Revenue ($M) | Operating Profit ($M) | Operating Margin (%) |
|--------------------------------------|--------------|-----------------------|---------------------|
| Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS)| 697          | 171                   | 24.5                |
| Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS)  | 320          | 36                    | 11.2                |

**Calculation Reference:**  
- Q2 2025 8-K, Exhibit 99.1: “Segment Results” table lists IOS and AHS revenues and corresponding segment profit ([1]).
- Q2 2025 10-Q, Note on “Results by Segment”: profit figures agree with the above ([2]).

### Full-Year 2024 (All Three Segments, Pre-Spin)

**In 2024, segment reporting includes PT, with all results as continuing operations ([3], [4]):**

| Segment                             | Revenue ($M) | Operating Profit ($M) | Operating Margin (%) |
|--------------------------------------|--------------|-----------------------|---------------------|
| Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS)| 2,790        | 724                   | 25.9                |
| Precision Technologies (PT)          | 2,142        | 424                   | 19.8                |
| Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS)  | 1,291        | 115                   | 8.9                 |

**Calculation Reference:**  
- 2024 10-K, “Note 13—Segment Information”, p. F-48: Table lists segment revenues and operating profit ([3], [4]).

**Calculation Method:**  
- Margin = Operating Profit / Revenue × 100.

## 2. Segment Revenue Growth Rates: 2023 to 2024

Revenue growth is calculated exclusively from reported segment revenue for 2023 and 2024 ([4], [5]). PT is included for both years as continuing operations.

| Segment     | 2023 Revenue ($M) | 2024 Revenue ($M) | Revenue Growth (%)      |
|-------------|-------------------|-------------------|------------------------|
| IOS         | 2,680             | 2,790             | 4.1                    |
| PT          | 2,230             | 2,142             | -3.9                   |
| AHS         | 1,160             | 1,291             | 11.3                   |

- **IOS:** (2,790 – 2,680) / 2,680 × 100 = 4.1%
- **PT:** (2,142 – 2,230) / 2,230 × 100 = -3.9%
- **AHS:** (1,291 – 1,160) / 1,160 × 100 = 11.3%

**Reconciliation Reference:**  
- Segment revenue figures from 2023 and 2024 10-Ks, “Note 13—Segment Information” ([3], [4], [5]).
- All revenues reflect continuing operations; PT spin-off was not effected until mid-2025.

## 3. Margin and Growth Comparisons: Intra-Segment, Inter-Period, and Discontinued Operations Reconciliation

### Direct Margin Comparison (2024 vs. Q2 2025)

| Segment       | 2024 Full-Year Margin (%) | Q2 2025 Margin (%) | Margin Change (bps) |
|---------------|--------------------------|--------------------|---------------------|
| IOS           | 25.9                     | 24.5               | -140                |
| AHS           | 8.9                      | 11.2               | +230                |
| PT            | 19.8                     | N/A*               | N/A                 |

*N/A for PT in Q2 2025, as results are reclassified as discontinued operations post-spin.*

**Interpretation:**
- **IOS:** Margins declined by 140 bps, driven by tariff headwinds and higher input costs, partially offset by price and productivity actions ([1], [2]).
- **AHS:** Margins expanded by 230 bps, attributed to greater software/service mix and enhanced pricing discipline ([1], [6]).
- **PT:** Saw a decline in both revenue (-3.9%) and margin in 2024, reinforcing its strategic deprioritization.

### Revenue Growth Comparison (2023 vs. 2024)

- **AHS:** Highest growth, +11.3%.
- **IOS:** Modest, steady growth, +4.1%.
- **PT:** Contracted, -3.9%.

### Reconciliation of Reporting Bases

- All segment margin/growth data for 2023 and 2024 includes PT as part of continuing operations. Only beginning in Q3 2025 (after the June 28, 2025, separation) does PT become classified as discontinued operations and omitted from comparative reporting for ongoing analysis ([2], [3]).
- For Q2 2025, margin and profit data are shown *excluding* PT, as per SEC-compliant reporting ([1], [2]).

## 4. Portfolio-to-End-Market Strategy: Segment Solutions and Competitive Positioning

### Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS)

**End-Market Role:**  
- IOS provides advanced, software-enabled measurement, sensing, workflow automation, and safety-critical solutions to industrial, manufacturing, facilities, and commercial clients.

**Strategic Impact:**  
- Enables digital transformation, productivity, and safety in mission-critical operations (e.g., connected worker platforms, asset monitoring, automated test and measurement). 
- High resilience and “stickiness” due to deep workflow integration and regulatory compliance elements.
- Core brands/platforms include Fluke (industrial test), Tektronix (measurement and calibration), and Industrial Scientific (gas detection)—all serving critical roles in uptime, facility safety, and operational productivity ([7], [8]).

**Competitive Positioning:**  
- Management and filings emphasize recurring revenue growth (notably in SaaS-enabled offerings), high customer retention, and growing penetration of digital platforms ([7]). 
- Portfolio optimization post-spin directs R&D/capex investment toward cloud, connectivity, and AI-driven asset productivity.

### Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS)

**End-Market Role:**  
- AHS delivers high-value clinical workflow, patient safety, medical device connectivity, and healthcare analytics solutions primarily to hospitals and health systems.

**Strategic Impact:**  
- Vital in automating specialized clinical workflows (e.g., operating room efficiency, patient monitoring alarms, sterilization tracking), supporting compliance, patient safety, and healthcare provider efficiency.
- Flagship platforms include ASP (sterilization/HAI prevention), Censis (sterile workflow software), and Provation (clinical documentation and SaaS clinical workflow tools) ([9]).

**Competitive Positioning:**  
- AHS is “mission-critical” for regulated hospital environments; long recurring revenue cycles, with SaaS and contracts driving improved margins and growth.
- Management’s strategy is to prioritize SaaS/connected health platforms, validated by 11%+ annual growth and margin expansion ([9]).
- Enhanced cross-selling and data integration have increased solution “stickiness” post-PT separation.

### Precision Technologies (PT/Ralliant)** (pre-spin)

**End-Market Role:**  
- PT’s portfolio focused on power, battery, calibration, and materials testing—critical to electronics, automotive, and emerging energy sectors ([10]).

**Strategic Positioning:**  
- More cyclical and lower-margin; less recurring revenue. Execution challenges and margin contraction over 2023–2024 prompted the strategic spin-out.
- Post-separation, Fortive’s portfolio is more weighted to high-mission, higher-margin recurring segments (IOS, AHS), as signaled by management ([1], [2], [3]).

## 5. Company-Wide Operating Margin Trends and Relationship to Segment Performance

**Q1 2025 vs. Q1 2024:**

- Q1 2025 GAAP operating margin (continuing ops): 15.8% vs. 15.6% in Q1 2024 (up 20bps); adjusted operating margin: 25.3% ([2]).
- Q2 2025 continuing ops operating margin: 11.0% (GAAP net income margin), adjusted EBITDA margin 28.4% ([1]).
- FY 2024 operating margin (all segments): 19.4% ([3], [4]).

**Impact of Segment-Level Movements:**
- Company-level margin expansion driven primarily by AHS’s improved profitability, with IOS sustaining high core margins despite near-term tariff/inflation pressures ([1], [2], [3]).
- Removal of PT (lower and worsening margin) increased the average, sustainable margin profile for the remaining Fortive group.
- All comparative period data explicitly reconciled in filings—see Q2 2025 10-Q, “Results of Operations,” which presents discontinued operations/PT separately post-spin ([2], [4]).

## 6. Ambiguities and Discontinued Operations Methodology

- For all periods up to June 28, 2025, PT is included in continuing operations. From Q3 2025 onward, PT/Ralliant is shown as discontinued, per SEC mandate ([2], [3]).
- Where period-over-period comparison for ongoing segments (IOS, AHS) is required, only those two are included post-spin, and all filings clearly annotate this at the table and footnote level ([1], [2]).
- PT’s “discontinued operations” categorization applies retroactively in post-Q2 2025 summaries, but the historical segment figures remain valid for 2024 and prior reporting ([3]).

**Reconciliation Method:**  
- In any comparative or trend analysis across the PT separation, all segment and margin calculations are explicitly tied to the reporting basis in each official filing and noted at each point of comparison.

---

## Conclusion

Fortive’s spin-off of Precision Technologies (Ralliant) in mid-2025 has resulted in a structurally higher-margin, more growth-oriented portfolio anchored in IOS (strongest margin/unit economics) and AHS (highest growth and rising profitability). This repositioning is validated not only by improved segment financials but also by a strategic shift toward more mission-critical, recurring revenue platforms in both retained segments, as supported by official management commentary and explicit filing disclosures. Future growth and margin improvements are closely linked to software, SaaS, and workflow automation, with both segments well-aligned to resilient, regulated, and workflow-dependent end-markets.

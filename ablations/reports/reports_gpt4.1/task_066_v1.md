# Comprehensive Relocation Comparison: Austin, Raleigh, and Nashville for a Mid-Career Mechanical Engineer Family (2026)

## Executive Summary

This report provides a multi-faceted, data-driven comparison of Austin (TX), Raleigh (NC), and Nashville (TN) for a 38-year-old mechanical engineer earning $95,000, married with a 6-year-old daughter. The analysis draws exclusively on authoritative data sources and covers the following: (1) median home prices in neighborhoods zoned to public schools rated 8 or higher out of 10; (2) property tax burden as a percentage of assessed home value; (3) depth of job market for senior mechanical engineers in aerospace/manufacturing (as reflected by job postings in the past 12 months); (4) public elementary (K-5) school performance metrics; (5) commute feasibility (under 30 minutes) from these neighborhoods to main industrial zones; and (6) 10-year financial outcome projections based on BLS wage data, historic and recent housing appreciation trends, and total tax burden. Each city is reviewed individually, followed by a comparative analysis and recommendations.

## 1. Median Home Prices in High-Rated School Neighborhoods

### Austin, TX

- Top-rated districts: Eanes ISD (Westlake Hills, Barton Creek, Rollingwood), Leander ISD, Lake Travis ISD, Dripping Springs ISD, Liberty Hill ISD, Round Rock ISD.
- Median home prices:
    - Eanes ISD (Westlake Hills): $1.66M–$4.9M as of early 2026.
    - Central Austin family neighborhoods (Zilker, Barton Hills): $1.1M–$1.33M.
    - Pflugerville (affordable, but fewer "8+" schools): ~$396K.
    - Austin overall median: ~$520K, but homes in the highest-rated school zones are significantly more expensive, usually $850K+ and commonly $1.2M–$2.5M for family-sized houses.
- Housing market cooled from 2022–2025 peak, but high-performing school districts retained value, experiencing 60%–80% appreciation since 2020 even as suburbs corrected more than central zones.[1][2][3][4][5][6][7][8][36][38][40]

### Raleigh, NC

- Top-rated areas: Lacy Elementary (North Hills), Hunter Elementary (Five Points, central Raleigh), Brier Creek, Wakefield, Cary neighbors.
- Median home prices:
    - North Hills, Five Points: $630K–$800K for single-family, but $420K–$500K common for standard 3BR homes.
    - City-wide median: $425K–$429K as of Q1 2026.
    - Areas zoned to top schools typically in the $420K–$650K range, notably below Austin and Nashville’s upscale districts.
- 2020–2025 appreciation strong (20%–28%), though slower than Austin; most recent gains (2025–2026) are flat to 2%.[7][8][9][10]

### Nashville, TN

- Top-rated school areas: Brentwood, Franklin, Nolensville, Green Hills, 12 South, Hendersonville (merit magnet schools and high-performing elementary).
- Median home prices:
    - Brentwood/Franklin: $950K–$1.25M.
    - Green Hills-Midtown: $1.2M median.
    - West Meade/Hillwood: $650K–$1M.
    - Hendersonville: more affordable, $440K–$650K.
    - City-wide median: $470K–$494K for all homes, but $515K–$900K for the “excellent schools” market segment.
- Appreciation strong (8.8% annualized, 2020–2024); 2025–26 up 3–6% depending on neighborhood.[6][7][8][36][39]

## 2. Property Tax Burden

### Austin, TX

- Effective annual rate: 1.63%–1.7% of assessed value in most top school districts (Travis County, Leander ISD, Lake Travis ISD).
- No state income tax; sales tax 8.25%.
- Typical annual property tax in Eanes ISD would exceed $19,000 on a $1.15M home.
- Median effective property tax: 1.31%.[11][12][13][14][15][45]

### Raleigh, NC

- Effective property tax: 0.71%–0.76% (Wake County, city of Raleigh).
- Median $425K home: annual property tax ~$3,000.
- Sales tax: 7%. State income tax: flat at 3.99% (on $95,000 salary, $3,790).
- Above state average but well below Austin; similarly low compared to national averages.[11][12][13][14][22]

### Nashville, TN

- Effective property tax: 0.45%–0.67%, considerably lower than both Austin and Raleigh.
- Unique Tennessee system: tax is assessed on 25% of appraised value, and then per $100 of assessed value ($2.782–$2.814).
    - $600K appraised home: $150K assessed x $2.814/$100 = $4,221/year.
    - More commonly, $650K–$1.2M home taxes $3,500–$8,200/year.
- No state income tax; sales tax ~9.25%.
- Lowest tax burden of the three on property values, more impactful at higher price points.[11][12][13][14][15]

## 3. Senior Mechanical Engineering Job Market Depth

### Austin, TX

- Highly robust market—tech corridor, major manufacturing and aerospace presence.
- 807 “Senior Mechanical Engineer” postings (Indeed, past 12 months).
- 801 aerospace engineering jobs, 307 mechanical engineering (all levels), frequent postings at BAE Systems, Apptronik, SharkNinja, Neuralink, Jabil, etc.
- Noted for diversity and depth—arguably the strongest market of the three cities.[16][17][18][19][20]

### Raleigh, NC

- Growing job market—central to Research Triangle Park (RTP), aerospace and advanced manufacturing, emerging clean energy.
- 400 “Senior Mechanical Engineer” job postings, 708 mechanical engineering jobs, 250 aerospace-specific positions (Indeed, past 12 months).
- Slightly lower raw number than Austin, but solid opportunities and a vibrant RTP ecosystem.[1][2][3][4][5][26][27]

### Nashville, TN

- Mid-sized, manufacturing-leaning market.
- Senior Manufacturing Engineer: ~392 jobs (Indeed).
- Senior Aerospace Engineer: 29 postings.
- Senior Mechanical Engineer: 60–318 postings depending on filter (Indeed/ZipRecruiter).
- Lower job volume, but present; positions span automotive, advanced manufacturing, increasingly medical devices and defense contracting.[16][17][18][19][20]

## 4. K–5 Public School Performance

### Austin, TX

- Eanes ISD, Lake Travis ISD, Leander ISD, Dripping Springs ISD, and Round Rock ISD are standouts, earning consistent “A”/A+ ratings on statewide (TEA) and Niche evaluations.
- Individual elementary schools (e.g., Forest Trail, Barton Creek, Cedar Creek, Lacy, Hunter) routinely score 9–10/10 GreatSchools.
- Eanes ISD elementary proficiency rates: 81% reading, 75% math—among highest in Texas.[1][2][3][4][5][21][22][23][24][25]

### Raleigh, NC

- Exemplar elementaries: Lacy, Hunter (10/10 statewide and national ranking), with robust academic growth and proficiency. School performance regularly in top 3% statewide; supported by strong parent engagement and instructional resources.
- North Carolina elementary scores: 72% proficiency in top schools, with city and region-wide rates trailing only Massachusetts/New Jersey and outperforming the Texas average.
- Most neighborhoods mentioned (Five Points, North Hills, Cary) have direct access to at least one 8–10 rated elementary.[3][4][5][6][17][18][19][20][21]

### Nashville, TN

- Highest-performing elementary schools in or near Brentwood, Franklin, Green Hills, 12 South, and top magnets in Davidson and Sumner Counties. Examples: Valor Flagship, Valor Voyager Academies, Lockeland Design Center, Lipscomb Elementary, Glendale, Granbery.
- Academic proficiency: Magnet programs post 90%+ proficiency; standard public elementaries in 70–85% proficiency range in the cited neighborhoods; broader district rates are much lower (32% reading/28% math).
- Proximity to magnet/option schools can yield better results than base assignment in city core. Suburbs (Williamson, Sumner) outperform Davidson County average by wide margin.[21][22][24][25][37][39]

## 5. Commute Feasibility (Sub-30 Minutes to Industrial Zones)

### Austin, TX

- Close-in neighborhoods (Zilker, Travis Heights, Barton Hills): 10–20 minutes to central employment.
- Eanes ISD/Westlake Hills: 20–30 minutes, but can exceed 35 minutes in rush due to traffic on MoPac/I-35.
- North Austin suburbs (Round Rock, Leander): 20–35 minutes in off-peak, but 45–75 minutes at peak; toll road access helps.
- Some western suburbs (Bee Cave, Lake Travis) are scenic but may breach 30-minute mark at rush hour.
- On average, 24.5 min citywide; national average is about 28 min.[26][27][28][29]

### Raleigh, NC

- North Hills, Five Points, Brier Creek: 12–25 min to RTP, downtown, and industrial centers—inside the Beltline is highly accessible.
- Suburbs (Wakefield, Cary): consistent 20–30 min commute, minimal congestion unless major incident.
- Average commute: 23.6 min citywide, well below Austin and Nashville. RTP and city industrial parks are within easy radius from most top schools.[15][16]

### Nashville, TN

- Green Hills, Hillwood, and Davidson County near downtown: 5–20 min non-rush, 25–35+ min in rush.
- Brentwood/Franklin/Nolensville: 15–25 min during off-peak, but up to 45–60 min at peak; reverse commutes sometimes possible.
- Average metro commute: 27.8 min, though variation is higher due to interstate bottlenecks and surges on I-65/I-40.[26][27][28][29][30]

## 7. Housing Appreciation Trends since 2020

- **Austin:** 80%+ appreciation in Westlake Hills and luxury ZIPs since 2020, 1% annual gain in past 12 months, survives 2022–2025 metro slowdown better than TX average; high-end inflation makes entry challenging.[36][37][38][39][40]
- **Raleigh:** 20–28% appreciation since 2020; 2025–2026 market stabilizing with 0–2% gains. Expected future appreciation ~3–5%/year.[7][8][9][10][11]
- **Nashville:** 8.8% annualized appreciation 2020–2024, city-wide. 2025–26: 1.7–6%. Top school zone homes appreciate up to 15% faster than city average due to demand.[36][39][40]

## 8. Total Effective Tax Burden for $95,000 Income (Family of Three)

- **Austin:** No state income tax; higher property/sales taxes. Estimated all-in tax (property + sales + federal): effective rate 22–24% (excluding health/childcare).[41][42][44][45]
- **Raleigh:** State income tax (3.99%), moderate property (0.71%–0.76%) and sales tax (7%). Total effective tax somewhat lower versus Austin, higher than Nashville.[11][12][13][14][22]
- **Nashville:** No state income tax; lowest property tax overall (0.45%–0.67%), but highest sales tax among the three. Net tax burden is lowest on higher-priced homes due to low property tax rate.[11][12][13][14][15]

## 9. Comparative 10-Year Financial Outcome

### Housing and Taxes

- **Austin**: Significant wealth creation potential if you can afford the high entry cost (homes in top schools start at $1.1M+). Large property tax bills ($15K–$25K a year are routine in these zones). Historically very strong appreciation, but risk of volatility if market corrects. Entry barrier for $95K household is extremely high—would require either significant downpayment or higher income to avoid cost-burdened status. No state income tax is a plus.
- **Raleigh**: Best balance of affordability and upward mobility. You can get into top-rated schools with entry prices in $425K–$650K range. Substantially lower property tax burden, moderate income and sales taxes. Healthy, not over-heated job/housing market. 10-year outcome projects steady accumulation of home equity and manageable recurring costs. No barrier to entry for $95K salary earning dual-income.
- **Nashville**: Some top school zones are as expensive as Austin (Brentwood/Franklin), but you can still find homes near $500K in good-elementary areas. Lowest tax burden—no income tax, lowest property tax rate. However, job market for senior mechanical engineers is not as deep as Austin and Raleigh, possibly limiting career upside unless you have a specific employer in mind or remote work is possible.

### Job Market Security

- Austin offers the greatest number and variety of senior mechanical engineering positions, as well as higher median salaries.
- Raleigh offers a very healthy engineering market, in a less overheated environment, with strong proximity to Research Triangle Park, and frequent new arrivals.
- Nashville offers a narrower set of roles, mostly in traditional manufacturing and automotive, with limited aerospace unless you work for a niche employer or are open to lateral transitions.

### Quality of Schools

- Highly rated schools are readily available in all three metros, but Austin's highest performers are concentrated in luxury-priced neighborhoods, whereas Raleigh provides access to top-rated public schools in more affordable neighborhoods. Nashville's top public schools are mainly in suburban areas—often at luxury price points—although some magnets/option programs in the city core provide entry routes.

### Commute and Livability

- Raleigh shines in short, reliable commutes from high-performing districts to industrial zones.
- Nashville and Austin both have increasing rush-hour congestion, but manageable commutes can be found with strategic location choices.

## 10. Conclusion and Recommendation

**Best Overall Financial Outcome:**  
For a mid-career mechanical engineer with a family, aiming to optimize cost of living, school quality, commute, and career opportunity over a 10-year horizon, **Raleigh, NC provides the best balance**:

- **Affordable entry to high-performing public school neighborhoods**—median homes in the $425K–$650K range, not unattainable at $95K income, especially with dual-earner or moderate savings.
- **Lowest recurring cost for taxes relative to house price and income**; flat and predictable property taxes, no surprises.
- **Sufficient engineering job market depth**—not the highest, but robust and varied, with the added security of Research Triangle Park’s ongoing expansion.
- **Reliable sub-30-minute commutes** from all major high-performing school areas to industrial/tech/RTP hubs.
- **Housing appreciation prospects** are healthy but less volatile, projecting stable wealth accumulation.
- Unlike Austin and Nashville, Raleigh minimizes risk of being “priced out” of top public schools in the future—even as the city grows.
- Low total effective tax burden (property + income + sales) and manageable cost structure for solid financial growth.

**Austin** offers greater job opportunity and higher wages, but at the cost of dramatically higher housing prices and taxes, resulting in high barriers to entry for all but the highest earning or equity-rich households. **Nashville** brings affordable taxes and moderate home prices, but job market depth in senior aerospace/mechanical roles is thinner, and top schools’ neighborhoods are either city-core magnets or expensive suburbs.

**Additional considerations:**
- Both Raleigh and Nashville have somewhat lower healthcare costs and insurance premiums than Austin, and overall moderate cost of living.
- If you prioritize remote work, Nashville could close the gap on career market. If willing/able to buy well above $1M, Austin could offer maximal career upside and asset appreciation.

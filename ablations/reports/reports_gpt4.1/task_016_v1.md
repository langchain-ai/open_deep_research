# Red Rooster’s Long-Running Lunchtime Deal: Complete Overview

## Introduction

This report investigates the identity and current status of a famous, long-running lunchtime promotion at a Western Australian chicken restaurant chain. The chain in question was founded by Greek-Australian brothers in Perth around 1972, and is well known for its roast chicken and distinctive red signage. The deal involves a portion of roast chicken and chips at a low price, with a lunchtime cut-off. The report will identify the promotion’s name, confirm the restaurant chain, and assess whether the deal is still available today, referencing official and primary sources throughout.

## The Chicken Restaurant Chain: Origins and Brand Profile

### Founding and Legacy

- The restaurant chain at the center of this inquiry is **Red Rooster**.
- Red Rooster began in Kelmscott, a southeastern suburb of Perth, Western Australia, in 1972.
- The business was started by **Peter and Theo Kailis**, two Greek-Australian brothers. Their family migrated to Australia in the 1920s and established a prominent presence in the Western Australian food industry. Peter was primarily responsible for developing Red Rooster from humble beginnings into a recognized fast-food brand across Australia[1][2][3].
- The chain's branding has long been defined by its **red signage** and focus on **roast chicken** meals, distinguishing it from fried chicken competitors.

### Business Growth and Enduring Identity

- Red Rooster rapidly grew, first across Western Australia and then nationwide by the 1980s. By 2018, it had more than 350 stores and remains a staple of the Australian fast-food landscape[4].
- The chain’s identity is tightly linked to affordable, Australian-style roast chicken, offered via dine-in, takeaway, and delivery.
- Recent years have seen the menu expand to include fried chicken and an updated selection of burgers and wraps, but roast chicken remains the iconic mainstay[4].

## The Lunchtime Promotion: Name, History, and Details

### Name and Content of the Promotion

- The specific long-running lunchtime promotion is officially and popularly referred to as the **"$5 Quarter Chicken & Chips Lunch Deal"**.
- This deal typically consists of:
  - A **quarter portion of roast chicken** (leg or breast)
  - A **serve of hot chips**
  - Offered at a budget-friendly price, traditionally **$5**, with some recent pricing at **$5.50** at select locations[8][10][13][20].
- The promotion is a mainstay of Red Rooster’s reputation for value meals and forms a core part of its customer appeal, especially among lunchtime and early afternoon patrons[13][14][15].

### Timing and Availability Parameters

- The deal has historically and continually been sold until a cut-off time in the mid-afternoon, **typically 4:00 pm**, aligning with the lunch rush and extended lunchtime market[8][13][20].
- This prolonged window has differentiated Red Rooster’s lunch deals from “hour-restricted” offers at other Australian fast-food chains.

### Historical Continuity

- Reviews and archival posts indicate the deal was established no later than the **early 1990s**, with many customers recounting decades of consistent availability[8][14][15].
- It has become a "Perth institution," widely referenced in social media, food review sites, and official promotions as one of the most value-driven lunch deals in the region.

## Current Status (As of April 2026)

### Evidence for Continuation

- **Official and Primary Sources**: 
    - Although the deal does not always appear on the main Red Rooster official website's menu (which often cycles promotions or has simplified listings), several primary sources confirm ongoing availability.
    - **Red Rooster’s official Facebook page and influencer TikTok videos from 2025 and 2026** both promote either the $5 or $5.50 Quarter Chicken & Chips Lunch Deal, confirming its existence, availability, and timing until 4pm daily at participating stores[10][13][20].
    - **Uber Eats listings** for Perth locations (e.g., Dianella) in 2026 include $5 meal deals showing the current continuation.
    - **Customer reviews on TripAdvisor** as recent as 2024–2025 also confirm the continuing offer at Perth stores[15].
- **Pricing Evolution**:
    - Some locations now advertise a **$5.50** price point, reflecting inflation or regional variation, but the deal’s content and time window remain consistent[10][20].
    - It is advertised as an ongoing offer rather than a temporary promotion, further indicating its institutional role within Red Rooster, especially in Western Australia.

### Summarized Confirmation

- The **$5 ($5.50 in some places) Quarter Chicken & Chips Lunch Deal** is still available at multiple Red Rooster outlets in Perth as of 2026.
- Availability may vary by store (as is standard with chain-wide promotions in Australia), so customers are advised to check with their local Red Rooster for current pricing and inclusion.
- Despite some menu updates and modernizations, the deal remains a highly recognizable and persistently advertised value offering[8][10][13][15][20].

## Conclusion

Red Rooster, founded in 1972 by Greek-Australian brothers Peter and Theo Kailis in Kelmscott, Perth, is Australia’s primary roast chicken fast-food chain, famed for its red signage and focus on affordable roasted chicken meals. Since at least the early 1990s, its signature **"$5 Quarter Chicken & Chips Lunch Deal"** (now sometimes **$5.50**), offering a quarter roast chicken and chips until 4pm daily, has been an enduring and iconic lunchtime promotion—especially in Perth. Collating evidence from official social media, current delivery platforms, and recent customer posts confirms that the deal remains available as of April 2026, demonstrating the company’s long-term commitment to accessible, affordable lunchtime dining.

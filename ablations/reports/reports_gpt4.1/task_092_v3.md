# Comprehensive Guide: Admission Requirements and Scholarship Eligibility for the Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay (2026/2027) and the France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship

## Overview

This report gives a full, up-to-date, and technically exhaustive breakdown of the admission and eligibility rules, documents, procedures, edge-case conditions, and in/exclusion criteria for (1) the Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay for the 2026/2027 cycle, and (2) the France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship for Indian/OCI applicants to this program. All content is directly referenced to official French university, embassy, or government sources, including PDFs and primary guidance sites. All annual-variation or context-specific procedures are plainly flagged, with instructions to always cross-check the official sites/portals for the latest instructions.

## 1. Admission to the Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay (2026/2027)

### 1.1 Program Structure

- Two-year full-time Master’s program (M1 and M2 years), taught entirely in English.
- Strong quantitative, research-oriented training core with optional specialist tracks in the M2 year.
- Designed for future academics, policy analysts, and quant-heavy professional roles.
- Located at the Saclay-Moulon campus.

Full details: [Université Paris-Saclay – Master of Economics](https://www.ceps-paris-saclay.fr/study/master-of-economics) [3]; [M1 Economics](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/education/masters-degree/economics/m1-economics) [8]

---

### 1.2 General and Specific Eligibility Rules

#### 1.2.1 Standard Eligibility

- **M1 (Entry to Year 1):**
  - Hold a Bachelor’s degree in Economics or Mathematics, or a closely related field with a strong quantitative component.
  - Applications from top-tier social science, statistics, or engineering backgrounds may be considered if the candidate demonstrates rigorous quantitative preparation.
  - Proof of English proficiency (IELTS min. 6.5, TOEFL iBT min. 90, or university letter if first degree was fully in English). Accepted methods must be confirmed each year; see application portal for possible updates.
  - No minimum GPA is strictly enforced, but very strong academic records are expected.
  - Two academic recommendations are required if the applicant has not been enrolled in a French university in the past two years.

- **M2 (Direct Entry to Year 2):**
  - At least 240 ECTS credits (usually, successful completion of M1 or equivalent) in a relevant subject.
  - Proven academic background in economics, mathematics, or close equivalents (assessed case-by-case, with more weight given to research/intensive quantitative coursework).
  - Same English proficiency requirements as in M1.

#### 1.2.2 Country/Status/Contextual Nuances

- **Études en France (EEF) countries:** Applicants must use both the INCEPTION platform and complete the EEF procedure [2][6][7].
- **Currently enrolled French university students:** For direct access to M2, prior learning recognition (RPL) document may be requested [1].
- **Students with refugee, protected, or asylum status:** Dedicated admission support and routes; see [student in exile application guide](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/admission/etudiants-internationaux/international-students-applying-masters-degree) [2].
- **Gap years and apprenticeship/professional tracks:** Gap-year or apprenticeship/professional/vocational tracks are NOT eligible for the core research program, nor for most scholarships [1][2].

#### 1.2.3 Ineligibility, Exclusionary, and Exceptional Rules

- **Not eligible if:**
  - Currently pursuing/have recently completed a professional/vocational/apprenticeship (contrat d’apprentissage) master’s in France.
  - Already possess the same French master’s degree.
  - Over 30 years old (for most scholarships—not a barrier for admission itself).
  - Documents do not meet equivalency or are not submitted/translated according to rules (see below).
  - Unexplained gap years or program switching post-admission (offers will be revoked).
  - Application materials are incomplete, in a language other than French/English without certified translation, or flagged for plagiarism/fraud.

Full eligibility details: [Admissions – International Masters](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/admission/etudiants-internationaux/international-students-applying-masters-degree) [2]; [Masters’ applications and enrolment](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/admission/masters-applications-and-enrolment) [1]

### 1.3 Required Documentation: Full and Edge-case List

All applicants, unless stated otherwise, must submit:

1. **Motivation letter (SOP):** In English, original, and tailored for the program [3][8].
2. **Academic transcripts:** All years/semesters post-high school; must be issued or certified by the home university.  
   - **Conditional:** Provisional/unofficial transcripts are accepted only if final transcripts are pending, but must be updated prior to registration.
3. **Degree certificates:** All higher education degrees; provisional certificate accepted only if degree is not yet awarded.
4. **CV:** Detailed, with education, internships, research, and relevant skills.
5. **Proof of English proficiency:** IELTS/TOEFL scores OR university-issued letter for English-medium instruction.  
   - If another language, or alternative certification, applicant must consult annual list of accepted proofs (consult official portal/instructions for updates).
6. **Recommendation letters:** Two required, especially if not enrolled in French higher education in past 2 years.  
   - **Edge-case:** One letter may be permitted with justification if second referee unavailable; confirmation needed from the program.
7. **Passport/national ID copy**
8. **Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL):** For M2 applicants or anyone applying on the basis of non-standard or international qualifications, or with previous study at Paris-Saclay [1][2].
9. **Supporting documentation for special status:**  
   - Refugee/protected/stateless persons must provide supporting documents as per national/European law.
   - Residence permit if already in France.
   - For students in exile, see [dedicated support guidelines](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/admission/etudiants-internationaux/international-students-applying-masters-degree) [2].
10. **Optional but recommended:** GRE/GMAT, certificates of achievement, or language additional proofs.

**Document specifications:**  
- All documents must be in English or French. Official, certified translations are required for any document in another language.
- Format: Applicants must follow specific file formats, size limits, and scan quality as stipulated by the INCEPTION or Etudes en France platforms for each application year; failure to comply may result in file rejection [6][7].
- Originals and translations must correspond and be clear, legible, and authentic. Notarization/legalization is required for non-French/English documents, as specified by platform instructions or admissions team [6][7][1].

**Special notes:** Where platform instructions differ from university rules, the stricter standard applies.

### 1.4 Application Process, Platform, and Timeline

- **Application platforms:**  
  - INCEPTION ([main portal for most international/french/EU applicants](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/admission/masters-applications-and-enrolment)) [1]
  - monmaster.gouv.fr: For French and some EU students for programs listed there [2]
  - Études en France (EEF): For residents/citizens of countries in the EEF system, as directed by program/university [6][7].
- **Timelines (2026/2027):**  
  - M1: Jan 15–Mar 16, 2026  
  - M2: Feb 25–May 25, 2026

**Process Step-by-Step:**
1. Confirm eligibility and platform required for your nationality/status.
2. Gather all required documents, with certified translations and notarized copies as required.
3. Complete online application via the appropriate platform; enter all information as per annual guidelines.
4. Upload documentation in required format/size; arrange for recommendation letters and provide referee contacts.
5. For EEF applicants: Manually enter program details if not pre-listed, strictly following university [Etudes en France application guide](https://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/sites/default/files/2024-07/guide_eef_upsaclay_en.pdf) [7].
6. Submit application before deadline; await application status via platform.

- **Verification:** All files and information are subject to audit for authenticity. University may request further documents or clarification at any stage.
- **Registration (post-admission):** Proof of payment or CVEC exemption is mandatory for enrollment [1][5].

**Document requirements and platform interface may change annually:** Applicants MUST check the university and relevant platform [latest instructions and uploaded PDFs] for any cycle-specific technical requirements.

## 2. France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship

### 2.1 Program Scope and Coverage

- French government scholarship administered by Campus France India for Indian citizens or OCI holders under age 30 as of March 30, 2026.
- Covers full-time, in-person, on-campus Master’s degrees in France—including the Economics Master at Paris-Saclay.
- Not available for part-time, distance, hybrid, apprenticeship, or non-France-based programs.
- All fields/disciplines are eligible; no CGPA minimum.

Official scholarship homepage: [France Excellence Charpak Master Scholarship – Campus France India](https://www.inde.campusfrance.org/charpak-master-s-scholarship) [1]

---

### 2.2 Detailed Eligibility Criteria, Exclusion, and Conditional Rules

#### 2.2.1 Standard Eligibility

- Indian citizen or OCI cardholder
- Age 30 or younger as of March 30, 2026
- Enrolled in or recently graduated from an Indian higher education institution; applicants with foreign degrees eligible, preference to those educated in India
- Admission or formal proof of application to a full-time, on-campus French Master’s program beginning in September/October 2026
- Not holding any other French Government scholarship (Eiffel, Erasmus Mundus, or parallel Paris-Saclay IDEX)
- No field/disciplinary restriction (as long as disciplinary rules of host university and program are met)

#### 2.2.2 Exclusionary and Edge-case Clauses

- NOT eligible if:
  - Already a recipient of a Charpak Master’s Scholarship, or other French Government awards (including Eiffel, IDEX, Erasmus Mundus)
  - Applying to part-time, distance, blended, or apprenticeship/master’s alternance programs
  - Planning non-degree research, internship, or professional contract as main program activity
  - Taking a gap/sabbatical year in 2026/2027
  - Planning to join a program outside mainland France, or with any non-in-France component
  - Submitting applications with missing, forged, or AI-plagiarized documents (plagiarism detection is explicitly mentioned)
  - Over 30 years old or with age status unclear at application deadline
  - Simultaneously applying to/holding major French/EU government funding
  - Results in (a) or (b) lead to immediate withdrawal of award at any stage, even post-arrival

- **Special Clauses:**
  - Students already in France MAY be considered for second-year funding renewal under exceptional circumstances (must apply and provide full documentation).
  - French language is advantageous but not required (unless university/program requests it).
  - No restriction by subject, but the master’s program must be full-time, full academic year, and in-country.

[Eligibility details: Charpak Master Scholarship Official Portal](https://www.inde.campusfrance.org/charpak-master-s-scholarship) [1][10]

### 2.3 Application Process, Documentation, and Technical Specifications

#### 2.3.1 Required Documents (Exhaustive):

- **Passport:** Main information and photo page (must be valid at time of application and through time in France)
- **Admission Letter:** Official offer from host French institution OR university communication/proof of application (for those awaiting final result)
- **Academic transcripts and degree certificates:** For all years post-secondary; provisional/final certificates as available
- **CV/Resume:** Updated, with education/experience/research highlights
- **Statement of Purpose (SOP):** Clear, original motivation and study plan in France (flag: must be personal and not written/generated by AI; plagiarism strictly checked)
- **Academic Recommendation Letters:** Two required, preferably from recent university faculty
- **French/English Language Certificate:**  
  - If taught in English: proof of English proficiency (as per host institution)
  - If taught in French: DELF/DALF/TCF/TEF or equivalent certificate—mandatory
- **Optional:** French language certificate (advantageous, not mandatory), extra achievements, extracurricular, or research certificates

#### 2.3.2 Special/Conditional or Contextual Documentation

- If admission letter is pending: Screen capture of application or university confirmation email accepted. Must be replaced by final admission proof before award is disbursed.
- If transcripts/degrees are not yet finalized: Provisional can be uploaded, but final versions required before completion of selection/enrolment.
- For OCI holders or students with education abroad: Provide evidence of OCI status and, if possible, supporting proof of education in India (preference but not strict exclusion).

#### 2.3.3 Format, Certification, and Translation Rules

- All documents must be (a) in English or French, OR (b) accompanied by a certified translation if in any other language.
- Uploads must be in clear scan format—typically PDF or JPEG. Technical specs (file size, resolution) are given on the online portal at the time of application each year and MUST be checked and complied with.
- Documents not matching format specifications or uploaded incorrectly may be rejected automatically.

**Always check [the official portal](https://www.inde.campusfrance.org/charpak-master-s-scholarship) at time of application for updated technical and format requirements.**

---

### 2.4 Application Window and Selection Stages

- **Deadline (2026/2027):** March 30, 2026, 23:59 IST
- **Selection:** Applications screened for technical completeness, eligibility, and merit.  
  - Shortlisted candidates will be invited to an online interview between April 13–April 30, 2026.
  - Results published by end of May 2026.

- **Important procedures:**
  - All applications must be completed in a single submission; incomplete, incorrect, or late applications are automatically rejected.
  - Multiple applications or discrepancies in information supplied may result in blacklisting.
  - Any suspicion or evidence of AI use or plagiarism in required materials (especially the SOP) will lead to summary disqualification.
  - Successful candidates will receive instructions on visa application, housing support, and integration into the French system.
  - Scholarship renewal for second-year Master’s students is conditional on successful academic performance and subject to application/review.

Official process and FAQ: [Charpak Scholarship Program Overview](https://www.inde.campusfrance.org/france-excellence-charpak-scholarship-program) [10]

---

### 2.5 Scholarship Benefits and Limitations: Complete List

#### Benefits

- **Monthly living allowance:** €860, paid throughout the academic year
- **Visa fee waiver:** For student visa application (direct through French consulate in India, proof of selection required)
- **Campus France/EEF procedure fee refund**
- **Status as French government scholarship holder (Boursier du Gouvernement Français):** Preferential access to affordable CROUS student housing and support for finding accommodation (not guaranteed)
- **Student social security and health insurance:** French statutory health coverage included (students may be required to top up for additional private coverage depending on field/program)
- **Discounts on meals at university restaurants (CROUS)**
- **Access to social and cultural programming arranged by Campus France India and French Embassy network

#### Limitations

- **Tuition fees are NOT covered**—students must pay these directly to the institution
- **Travel to/from France is NOT covered**
- **Personal stay expenses and insurance top-ups are not covered**
- **No coverage for internships, research-only periods, or non-degree mobility outside France**
- **Not available for joint/double-degree, blended, or apprenticeship programs, nor gap/sabbatical years**

## 3. Special and Edge-case Procedures: Credential Equivalency, Translation, Status, and Appeals

- **Credential equivalency:** Paris-Saclay refers complex or unusual foreign credentials to an internal panel for assessment; check with admissions office if uncertain.
- **Translation:** Certified translation by government-approved translators required for all non-English/French documents; see annual application instructions for accepted standards.
- **Appeals/Disputes:** For credential or eligibility disputes, contact the program office or scholarship helpdesk directly (usually by email) and retain original documents for verification post-arrival.

## 4. General Recommendations and Best Practices

- Prepare and scan all documentation early, ensuring clarity and compliance.
- Keep originals and certified translations on hand for verification at every stage.
- Always refer back to both the official university portal and the relevant application platform before uploading/submitting files each year.
- For ambiguous or edge-case situations (e.g. mixed degree background, immigration status, special needs), proactively contact program or university admissions for written clarification before the deadline.

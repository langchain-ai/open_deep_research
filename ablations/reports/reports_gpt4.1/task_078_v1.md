# 9-Day Thailand Itinerary from Hyderabad for 7 Travelers on a ₹70,000 Budget

## Executive Summary

This report presents a comprehensive, logistically feasible 9-day itinerary for a group of 7 travelers from Hyderabad to Thailand, designed to fit a per-person budget of ₹70,000 (with ₹18,000 for roundtrip flights and ₹52,000 for all in-country expenses). The itinerary blends iconic cities and the Thai countryside, ensures two full rural days, prioritizes dorm or group Airbnb stays, and features a mix of cultural, adventure, and nightlife activities. Each day’s plan includes exact destinations, recommended accommodations (with links), activities, transport options, and a clear itemized daily cost breakdown, verifying all options fit within the ₹52,000 in-country budget constraint. 

---

## Itinerary Overview and Assumptions

- **Trip Dates**: Flexible (recommend March–May 2026 for best rates)
- **Group Size**: 7 travelers (budget accommodation/transport optimized)
- **Flights**: Hyderabad–Bangkok return, ₹18,000 per person
- **Visa**: Thai Visa on Arrival ~₹2,500 per person (from in-country budget)
- **Primary Destinations**: Bangkok, Chiang Mai, Chiang Dao (rural), with an optional night/day in Chiang Rai
- **Accommodation Style**: Dorm hostels, group Airbnbs, rural farmstays (private/group rooms)
- **Transport**: Overnight train/minivan, local taxis, shared vans, group transfers
- **Currency**: Conversions based on April 2026 rates (1 THB ≈ 2.3 INR; 1 USD ≈ 82 INR)
- **Booking Platforms**: Airbnb, Booking.com, Agoda, HostelWorld for stays; 12GoAsia/ThailandTransfers for group travel; Viator/Agoda for tours

## Itinerary Day-by-Day

**Legend:**
- *A*: Accommodation
- *F*: Food
- *T*: Local/Intercity Transport
- *S*: Sightseeing/Activities
- *Total*: Estimated daily per person spend

### Day 1: Arrival in Bangkok

- **Morning**: Flight Hyderabad to Bangkok (arrive AM/early PM)
- **Transport**: Airport taxi or Grab ride (~THB 400 total, split)
- **Accommodation**: Group hostel or whole-unit Airbnb near Sukhumvit or Silom  
  - e.g., ["Entire house perfect for big group@Ekamai"](https://www.agoda.com/entire-house-perfect-for-big-group-ekamai/hotel/bangkok-th.html) (Agoda) [1]
- **Activities**:
  - Settle in, explore street markets (Chatuchak if weekend, Asiatique riverfront), sample Thai street food
  - Evening: Khao San Road for nightlife, live music, bars
- **Daily Cost Estimate**:
  - *A*: ₹1,400 (whole Airbnb split or nice dorm)  
  - *F*: ₹700 (casual dinner, breakfast, night snacks)  
  - *T*: ₹250 (airport transfer and local rides)  
  - *S*: ₹0 (evening market stroll/nightlife is free/low cost)
  - **Total**: ₹2,350

---

### Day 2: Bangkok – Temples, City Sights & Night Train

- **Morning**: Breakfast at hostel/Airbnb. Temple tour: Grand Palace (entry THB 500), Wat Pho, Wat Arun (small ferry ride)
- **Afternoon**: Local lunch, explore Chinatown or Riverside malls
- **Evening**: Early dinner
- **Night**: Board overnight train, sleeper class, Bangkok–Chiang Mai (reserve 2nd-class AC bunks via [12GoAsia](https://12go.asia/en)) [2]
- **Daily Cost Estimate**:
  - *A*: ₹0 (overnight train included as accommodation)
  - *F*: ₹800 (meals/snacks/drinks for train)
  - *T*: ₹1,400 (Full train ticket, includes “accommodation”)
  - *S*: ₹400 (Grand Palace entry/tuk-tuk ferry shares)
  - **Total**: ₹2,600

---

### Day 3: Chiang Mai Old City

- **Morning**: Arrive Chiang Mai, check-in to group hostel/city Airbnb  
  - Example: ["Bed & Bag Station"](https://www.airbnb.com/chiang-mai-region-thailand/stays/hostels) or ["Chapter 1 Hostel"](https://www.hostelworld.com/hostels/asia/thailand/chiang-mai/) [3][4]
- **Activities**: Explore Chiang Mai Old City (Wat Phra Singh, Wat Chedi Luang), Monk Chat at Wat Suan Dok, relax in Buak Hard Park
- **Evening**: Night Market (Sunday or Saturday Walking Street), local food discovery
- **Daily Cost Estimate**:
  - *A*: ₹1,000 (hostel group dorm)
  - *F*: ₹600 (local food, night market snacks)
  - *T*: ₹200 (Grab/tuk-tuk for local rides)
  - *S*: ₹300 (temple donation/entry, Monk Chat free)
  - **Total**: ₹2,100

---

### Day 4: Adventure Day – Doi Inthanon or Elephant Sanctuary

- **Full Day Adventure** (choose one, both bookable via [Viator](https://www.viator.com/Chiang-Mai/d5267-ttd) or [Agoda](https://www.agoda.com/special-editions/thailand/adventure-activities-in-thailand-exceptional-experiences-to-ignite-your-sense-of-adventure/)):
  - **Option A**: Doi Inthanon National Park day tour (hiking, waterfalls, hill tribe village visit) – ~₹2,600–3,000  
  - **Option B**: Elephant Nature Park day visit – ~₹3,500
- **Evening**: Leisure/Night Market
- **Daily Cost Estimate**:
  - *A*: ₹1,000 (same hostel, 2nd night)
  - *F*: ₹700 (breakfast, included tour lunch, dinner)
  - *T*: ₹150 (short local rides, tour pick-up included)
  - *S*: ₹2,800 (average of options above)
  - **Total**: ₹4,650

---

### Day 5: Rural Chiang Dao/Farmstay – Countryside Experience (Day 1)

- **Morning**: Group transfer to Chiang Dao (private minivan or Grab XL, ~THB 3,000–3,500, split)
- **Accommodation**: Farmhouse/Airbnb in countryside  
  - Example: ["Cozy Farmhouse with Stunning View of Chiang Dao"](https://www.airbnb.com/rooms/951959642155354100) [5]
- **Activities**: Village walks, on-site farm experiences, cooking with hosts, tea picking, stargazing
- **Daily Cost Estimate**:
  - *A*: ₹1,600 (whole farmhouse split 7 ways)
  - *F*: ₹650 (country meals/home-cooked, local market visit)
  - *T*: ₹400 (group transfer split)
  - *S*: ₹200 (activities included/free, optional donation)
  - **Total**: ₹2,850

---

### Day 6: Rural Chiang Dao (Day 2) – Trek, Caves, Village Life

- **Activities**: Guided trek or cave tour (can be booked via [Viator](https://www.viator.com/tours/Chiang-Mai/Private-What-a-Gorgeous-Nature-tour-Hill-tribe-village-Chiang-Dao-cave/d5267-135454P11)), farm/animal care, rest
- **Daily Cost Estimate**:
  - *A*: ₹1,600 (2nd farmhouse night)
  - *F*: ₹700 (farm/market meals)
  - *T*: ₹200 (local transfers, if needed)
  - *S*: ₹700 (guided trek/cave entry)
  - **Total**: ₹3,200

---

### Day 7: Chiang Dao to Chiang Mai City – Culture & Nightlife

- **Morning**: Return to Chiang Mai Old City (shared minivan or taxi, ~THB 3,500 for group)
- **Accommodation**: Hostel/central Airbnb as previous days
- **Activities**: Last temple (e.g., Wat Umong), artisan market, spa/massage
- **Evening**: Chiang Mai nightlife – bars (e.g., Zoe in Yellow), live music, food stalls
- **Daily Cost Estimate**:
  - *A*: ₹1,000
  - *F*: ₹600 (local/street meals)
  - *T*: ₹500 (return group van split, local trips in city)
  - *S*: ₹400 (massage, entry, live music)
  - **Total**: ₹2,500

---

### Day 8: Chiang Mai – Free Day, Optional Chiang Rai Day Tour (White Temple/Blue Temple)

- **Option 1**: Free leisure/shopping/spa day in Chiang Mai (temples, Nimman cafes, bike rental)
- **Option 2**: Day trip/tour to Chiang Rai (bus/minivan, visit White Temple, Blue Temple, local market)
  - Group minivan Chiang Mai–Chiang Rai return: ~THB 8,000 for group (split 7 ways), or local bus (THB 250/person each way)
  - Organized full-day [Chiang Rai tour via Viator](https://www.viator.com/Chiang-Mai-tours/Day-Trips/d5267-g5-c15) from Chiang Mai: ~₹2,200–₹2,500 per person [6]
- **Daily Cost Estimate** (average both options):
  - *A*: ₹1,000
  - *F*: ₹700
  - *T*: ₹650 (Chiang Rai group tour or combined local transport)
  - *S*: ₹400 (temple/bike rental/spa or part of tour)
  - **Total**: ₹2,750

---

### Day 9: Chiang Mai to Bangkok – Morning Train/Flight & Departure

- **Morning**: Early train/budget flight to Bangkok (see [12GoAsia](https://12go.asia/en) for tickets, average ₹1,400–₹2,000)
- **Option**: If late night flight from BKK, store luggage and do a last city stroll/market meal
- **Afternoon/Evening**: Transport to airport, fly Bangkok–Hyderabad
- **Daily Cost Estimate**:
  - *A*: ₹0 (overnight transit or check-out)
  - *F*: ₹700 (breakfast, lunch, airport snack)
  - *T*: ₹1,500 (train/flight + airport transfer)
  - *S*: ₹0 (free wander/markets)
  - **Total**: ₹2,200

## Practical Tips

- Always confirm host/group capacity and book rural stays and overnight intercity trains in advance
- Carry cash for small purchases in rural areas/markets
- Eat local for best food value and authentic experience
- Bargain politely in markets; agree taxi fares before rides outside of Grab
- Respect temple dress codes and rural customs

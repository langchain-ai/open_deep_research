# Comprehensive Analysis: Industrial Automation Market in Saudi Arabian Manufacturing (2015–2025)

## Overview

This report provides a detailed analysis of the industrial automation market in Saudi Arabia’s manufacturing sector from 2015 to 2025. It covers: (1) historical and projected market size, year-to-year CAGR, and robotics penetration rates; (2) the impact of Saudi Vision 2030, including specific policy measures and investment initiatives; (3) a record of named contracts or awarded projects for Siemens and ABB in NEOM and the Red Sea Project since 2021; and (4) actionable sales implications for targeting Saudi manufacturing customers. All findings are based on primary and authoritative sources, with a full citations appendix.

---

## 1. Market Size, CAGR, and Robotics Penetration (2015–2025)

### Historical and Projected Market Size

- The Middle East & Africa (MEA) industrial automation market was valued at USD 13.9 billion in 2024. The GCC region—led by Saudi Arabia—accounts for about 53% of GCC market value and is set to grow at a CAGR of 8.1% (2024–2030), boosted by Saudi public and private sector investments[1].
- The Saudi Arabia Industrial Automation Components market reached USD 2.3 billion in 2025 and is projected to grow to USD 4.7 billion by 2034 (CAGR 8.18% for 2026–2034). This covers core automation equipment (sensors, PLCs, HMIs)[2].
- Servo motors and drives, used heavily in industrial robotics and automation, reached USD 128.3 million market value in 2025, projected to USD 178.9 million in 2034 (CAGR 3.76%)[3].
- The Saudi industrial robotics software market stood at USD 238.8 million in 2025 (CAGR 20.24% to 2034), demonstrating rapid uptake of digital manufacturing and AI adoption[4].
- Automation in Saudi’s manufacturing sector is outpacing regional growth, supported by Vision 2030’s push for diversification and digital transformation. However, compared to leading Asian and European nations, Saudi Arabia is still in an early adoption phase.

### Year-by-Year Market Growth and Robotics Penetration

- Historical year-by-year data for Saudi manufacturing automation market size in USD is not published. Proxy indicators suggest robust acceleration post-2020, after Vision 2030’s industry programs commenced. The overall market CAGR for the automation components segment is estimated at 8–10% from 2021–2025, with faster growth in robotics and digitalization subsegments[2][4].
- Robot density (number of industrial robots per 10,000 manufacturing workers) is the standard maturity metric. Saudi-specific density is **not published** by the International Federation of Robotics as of 2025[5][6]. For context, global leaders in 2023 included South Korea (1,012), Singapore (770), China (470), and Germany (429). Saudi Arabia's robot density is believed to be under 20 robots per 10,000 workers, well below the global average (151 in 2022)—but rising rapidly due to government-backed automation in new plants and giga-projects[5][6].
- The number of servo-based automation/robotics units installed is projected to reach 7,340 by 2033, up from low single-digit thousands in the early 2020s[3]. Growth is concentrated in sectors like automotive, building materials, chemicals, and logistics warehouses.

### Industry Characteristics and Key Segments

- Automation adoption spans distributed control systems (DCS), programmable logic controllers (PLCs), industrial robots, SCADA, sensors, IIoT, and machine vision.
- Sectors leading uptake: automotive (notably following Lucid Motors’ investment), pharmaceuticals, food & beverage, building materials, and energy.
- Strong government incentives and public-private partnerships are key demand drivers[2][3][4].

## 2. Vision 2030: Policy Drivers of Automation & Robotics Demand

### National Industrial Strategy and Incentive Programs

- Saudi Vision 2030 and its National Industrial Strategy (NIS) are central to the Kingdom’s industrial transformation. The NIS aims to triple the GDP contribution of manufacturing by 2030, and expand the number of factories from 12,000 (2024) to 36,000 (2035)[7][8].
- The NIS prioritizes advanced manufacturing, digitalization, robotics, AI, and business model innovation. Factories must modernize production, invest in digital skills, and adopt Industry 4.0 solutions[8][9].
- Vision 2030’s Standard Incentives Program (2025) offers:
  - Grants and funding of up to 35% of eligible project costs (maximum SAR 50 million per project — roughly USD 13.3 million), split between construction and production phases[10][11].
  - Operational support and advisory services, tax breaks, customs duty relief, and low-interest loans. Industrial Special Economic Zones (SEZs—like NEOM and King Abdullah Economic City) provide additional advantages[10][11][12].
- The Future Factories Program provides support for digital transformation, aiming to upgrade over 2,500 Saudi factories to smart/automated operations (expanded targets call for 4,000+ factories by 2030). Benefits include up to 80% labor reduction, >20% operational cost savings, and higher productivity[13].
- Factory digital maturity scores, assessed as part of program eligibility, are now directly tied to government grants/incentives[13].
- Workforce upskilling drives (e.g. 30% Saudi content quota for technical engineering roles, plus 10,000+ workers to be trained in automation/AI by 2030) are enforced by the Local Content & Government Procurement Authority[14][15].

### Regulatory and Investment Initiatives

- 100% foreign ownership is permitted in manufacturing, targeted at attracting technology/automation leaders[11].
- Localization mandates: Factories must comply with “Made in Saudi” and local content quotas, integrating local suppliers and talent into automation projects[14][15].
- The National Industrial Development and Logistics Programme (NIDLP) is investing billions of USD into smart factories, automation, and advanced technology adoption, with automotive, building materials, and energy sectors at the fore[12].

### Policy Excerpts and Official Vision 2030 Sources

- From Vision 2030 Robotics Sector page:  
  “Robotics and automation are a core focus of Saudi Arabia’s digital transformation... The National Industrial Strategy... accelerates the shift to smart factories and advanced manufacturing, in line with the goals of Saudi Vision 2030.” [7]
- Official NIS (Ministry of Industry and Mineral Resources):  
  “The Standard Incentives Program will offer financial support for up to 35% of project costs, and operational assistance for up to seven years, to eligible investors that build or expand advanced manufacturing facilities in the Kingdom.” [10][11]
- Future Factories Program:  
  “Aims to transform more than 4,000 factories into smart, automated operations by 2030... Incentives include grants, preferential financing, and support for digital platforms, automation, and production line upgrades.” [13]
- Localization/Workforce Policy:  
  “The Local Content and Government Procurement Authority is raising local content in manufacturing, aiming for at least 65% in key industrial sectors... Workforce Saudization is a regulatory requirement for technical engineering roles in manufacturing plants.” [14][15]

## 3. Siemens & ABB: Awarded Projects in NEOM and Red Sea Project (Since 2021)

### Siemens Contracts

- **NEOM Water Transmission Pipeline Automation:**
  - Siemens (with Mutlaq Al Ghowairi Company) executed projects for NEOM’s utilities arm, Enowa, to automate water infrastructure with advanced digital control systems. This supports NEOM’s sustainable water supply[16]. No contract value is published; scope focuses on backbone infrastructure automation.
- **Digitalization & Electrification Agreements:**
  - Siemens signed agreements (late 2024–2025) with Saudi entities (including NEOM-linked organizations) to drive digitalization, power, and automation in the Kingdom’s manufacturing and utilities. Official statements cite general “potential cooperation in NEOM”, but do not specify named, project-level contracts for NEOM urban infrastructure[16][17].
- **Power Plant Supply (Not NEOM-Specific):**
  - Siemens Energy secured a $1.6 billion contract for Rumah 2 and Nairyah 2 gas-fired power plants (2025), adding 3.6 GW to the Saudi grid[18][19]. These power projects are national, not exclusive to NEOM or Red Sea.

### ABB Contracts

- **Red Sea Project Smart Energy Infrastructure:**
  - **ABB was appointed in December 2021 to supply smart energy systems for The Red Sea Project**, Saudi Arabia’s landmark sustainable tourism development. Deliverables include 10 System pro E low-voltage panels (capacity: 36 MW from 69 generators), digital management platforms (ABB Ability™), and full asset performance optimization[20][21][22].
  - ABB will deliver 1,200+ electric vehicle charging units (largest such project in Saudi and GCC) for Red Sea and AMAALA destinations. EV chargers are manufactured locally, contributing to localization objectives[23].
  - These initiatives help The Red Sea Project run 100% on renewable energy by 2030 and underpin operational sustainability and technology leadership in hospitality[21][22][23].
  - **Contract values and project IDs are not publicly disclosed** by ABB, Red Sea Global, or Saudi government as of April 2026.

- **NEOM:**
  - As of 2026, there is **no publicly confirmed** direct contract awarded to ABB for NEOM urban infrastructure. ABB has stated readiness and strategic intent to be a technology supplier, but only Red Sea and AMAALA deployments are confirmed[24].

## 4. Actionable Sales Recommendations for Manufacturing Solutions in Saudi Arabia

### 1. Leverage Government Incentives and Digital Maturity Scoring

- Emphasize how automation solutions help factories improve their “digital maturity score,” making them eligible for direct financial grants (up to 35% of project costs), tax benefits, and special financing—all prioritized under Vision 2030 and the Standard Incentives Program[10][13].
- Focus sales development on supporting clients’ eligibility for the Future Factories Program, particularly in automotive, building materials, chemicals, and food sectors prioritized by industrial policy[8][13].

### 2. Align with Localization, Workforce, and Supply Chain Mandates

- Position automation solutions as enablers for local content requirements (“Made in Saudi”), e.g., by facilitating local manufacturing, integrating local suppliers, and enabling knowledge transfer through training initiatives[14][15].
- Highlight flexible business models that support partnerships with local system integrators and solution providers, which are increasingly essential for project approval and success on Giga-projects (NEOM, Red Sea, etc.)[12][15].

### 3. Demonstrate Proven Value for Sustainability, Safety, and Digitalization

- Frame automation projects with reference cases reflecting Vision 2030 goals: reduced energy usage, increased efficiency, and sustainability. Use ABB Red Sea Project as a local example of how digital management platforms (e.g., ABB Ability™) lowered operational costs and enabled 100% renewable energy operation[20][21][22].
- Showcase business outcomes: labor cost reductions (up to 80% reported in upgraded factories), operational cost savings (>20%), improved traceability, and compliance with new digital requirements[13].

- **Robot density (per 10,000 workers) for the Saudi manufacturing sector is not yet published in any IFR or government report.** All proxies and estimates are based on sectoral growth rates, regional benchmarks, and installed units projections.
- Precise year-by-year Saudi market USD figures for industrial automation in manufacturing are not publicly available; reliable market estimates and CAGR are drawn from authoritative industry and market research aggregators.
- **Named NEOM contracts for Siemens and ABB:** Only sectoral agreements and subcontracts (automation for water pipelines and power generation) are cited with official references; most flagship urban infrastructure contracts are not publicly named or disclosed.

# Comprehensive Guide: Admission to Master's in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay and France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship (2026 Cycle)

## Overview

This guide provides an up-to-date, detailed resource for applicants to the Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay for the 2026 admission cycle, with complete information on eligibility, admissions process, platforms, required documents, and key deadlines. It also details the eligibility and step-by-step procedure for the France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship, focusing particularly on Indian citizens and OCI holders applying to this program. Variations by applicant type, changes since November 2025, and essential direct links to official sources are all included.

---

## 1. Master's in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay: 2026 Admissions

### 1.1 Program Overview

- **Duration and Structure:** Two-year master’s (M1 + M2), taught entirely in English, designed for strong quantitative and research preparation in microeconomics, macroeconomics, econometrics, and applied economic analysis.
- **Target Audience:** Students with strong academic backgrounds aiming for doctoral studies or high-level careers in economics and related areas.
- **Location:** Saclay campus, with extensive resources, integration services, and international student support.

**Key program links:**  
- [M1 Economics](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/education/masters-degree/economics/m1-economics)  
- [M2 Economics](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/education/masters-degree/economics/m2-economics)

---

### 1.2 Admission Requirements and Eligibility Criteria

#### Academic Prerequisites

- **Bachelor’s degree required** in:
  - Economics (BA, BSc)
  - Mathematics (BS)
  - Closely related social sciences, engineering, or other scientific fields (provided strong quantitative/economics coursework)  
    *Note: Background in quantitative analysis is strongly emphasized; applicants from “other” fields must demonstrate academic depth in relevant subjects*.
- **Academic Performance:**  
  - No officially published minimum GPA, but a “strong academic record” is required. Independent sources and some program info recommend a GPA of 3.3/4.0 or higher, especially in relevant coursework.
  - Holistic assessment; competitive programs seek evidence of academic excellence and quantitative skills.
- **Final-year undergraduates** may apply, with conditional offers pending degree completion.

#### Language Proficiency

- **English proficiency is mandatory**. Minimum scores:
  - IELTS: 6.5 overall
  - TOEFL iBT: 90 or equivalent
  - Exemptions may apply if previous higher education was entirely in English—contact program for guidance.
- **French is not required** for admission, but basic French competency is recommended for daily life.

#### Open-Ended/Unspecified Details

- **No official statement** on required documentation for special applicant statuses (e.g., stateless, refugees); dedicated support is available and bonus points may apply in admissions.
- All academic documentation must be in English or French. Certified translations may be needed for other languages.

---

### 1.3 Application Documents (2026 Cycle)

- Academic transcripts (university level/post-secondary)
- Official certificate of Bachelor’s degree or proof of being in final year (with expected completion date)
- Detailed Curriculum Vitae (2 pages recommended)
- Motivation letter (explaining background and goals)
- Two recent letters of recommendation (from academic referees or employers; must be submitted via online form)
- Proof of English proficiency (B2 minimum)
- Passport or identity card
- Any additional documents indicated by the platform (selection sheets, program-specific forms, etc.)

**All documents must be uploaded in PDF, JPEG, or similar electronic format (3MB limit per file).**  
See the official [INCEPTION User Guide](https://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/sites/default/files/media/2020-02/user_guide_inception_front_office_2020_-_english.pdf) for technical file requirements and upload instructions.

---

### 1.4 Application Platforms & Step-by-Step Process

#### Step 1: Identify Correct Platform

Depending on your nationality, residency, and prior education:

- **International Non-EU/EEA Applicants (Including Indian/OCI)**
  - **Apply via INCEPTION** (Université Paris-Saclay’s online application portal):  
    [Application Instructions & INCEPTION Access](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/admission/masters-applications-and-enrolment)
  - **After receiving admission offer:** Complete the Études en France (EEF) visa process. This does not affect academic selection; it is for obtaining the French student visa.  
    - [Études en France Details](https://www.campusfrance.org/en/etudes-en-france-procedure)
  - Both Indian citizens and OCI holders follow this process.

- **French, EU/EEA Applicants**
  - **M1 Applicants:** Apply via [monmaster.gouv.fr](https://www.monmaster.gouv.fr/) if currently in the EEA/French system; otherwise, use INCEPTION.
  - **M2 Applicants:** All apply via INCEPTION.

- **Special Status (Refugees, stateless, protected):** Dedicated guidance and webpages available at [Université Paris-Saclay International Admissions](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/admission/etudiants-internationaux/international-students-applying-masters-degree).

#### Step 2: Prepare Online Application

- Register/create account on the relevant platform (INCEPTION or monmaster.gouv.fr).
- Complete each section of the form, upload all required documents, and input references’ contact details.
- *References will receive a link to submit their recommendations; you cannot upload these directly*.

#### Step 3: Submit and Validate

- **Validation:** Your application is only considered if you “validate” it on the platform—you can make changes until this step. Unvalidated apps are not reviewed.
- **Notification:** Most decisions are released within two months of application submission and validation.

#### Step 4: Next Steps Upon Admission

- International applicants from “Études en France” countries (including India) must:
  - Finalize administrative registration in INCEPTION, pay/exempt the CVEC contribution, and
  - Complete the visa process on Études en France.

#### Step 5: Enrolment

- Complete both administrative and academic enrolment with the University, including proof of health insurance, payment of fees, and accommodation arrangements.

  - Application (by invitation, after admission): **Deadline typically in March 2026**

---

### 1.6 Tuition and Scholarships

- **Tuition Fees:**
  - International students: **€4,000/year**
  - EU students: **~€250/year**
- **Université Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship:**
  - Up to **€10,000 per year** (living stipend) + **up to €900 for travel/visa**
  - Separate application, by invitation post-admission.

- Detailed scholarship information:  
  [International Master’s Scholarships Program](http://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/admission/bourses-et-aides-financieres/international-masters-scholarships-program)

- **Work rights:** International students may work up to 964 hours/year.

### 1.7 Special Notes and Changes Since November 2025

- **No significant changes** in academic or procedural requirements since late 2025. Deadlines, document requirements, language standards, and application procedures remain stable.
- **Minimum GPA remains open-ended** in official documents, but competitive/strong records, especially in economics/math fields, are strongly recommended.
- **Platform guidance is clarified**: Indian/OCI applicants must use INCEPTION first, then Études en France for visa.
- **Support for special statuses** (refugee, subsidiary protection, etc.) is maintained, with evaluation “bonuses” possible.

## 2. France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship: Eligibility & Application (2026)

### 2.1 Scholarship Overview

- **Purpose:** Offered by Campus France (French Government) to exceptional Indian citizens and OCI cardholders for full-time in-person master’s study at French institutions, including Université Paris-Saclay.
- **Coverage:** Monthly stipend (€860), visa and Campus France fee waivers, student health/social security coverage, aid in subsidized accommodation, cultural activities, and some administrative support.

**Official Charpak Scholarship details:**  
- [Campus France India Charpak Scholarships](https://www.inde.campusfrance.org/charpak-scholarships)  
- [General Info and Timeline](https://www.inde.campusfrance.org/france-excellence-charpak-scholarship-program)

---

### 2.2 Eligibility Criteria (2026 Intake)

- **Nationality:** Indian citizen or Official Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) cardholder.
- **Age:** Up to 30 at the time of application.
- **Academic Status:**
  - Currently enrolled in, or a recent graduate of, an Indian institution (preference, not mandatory).
  - OR holder of a degree from any recognized higher education institution (foreign-educated applicants are eligible, but must demonstrate strong ties to/study history in India if possible).
  - Proof of admission (or application) to a recognized French institution’s full-time master’s program (no online/distance/hybrid/joint degrees).
- **Other Requirements:**
  - Charpak can be renewed once for the second year of a two-year master's program.
  - No minimum CGPA/percentage is required, but application is merit-based.
  - Applicants holding previous/parallel French government/EU government scholarships (e.g., Eiffel, Erasmus) are ineligible.
  - Not open to those currently in France for internships/research/thesis work.
  - French language proficiency is not required but is a plus.
  - You must intend to study in France (not remotely or via exchange/joint degree).

---

### 2.3 Required Documents

- **Compulsory:**
  - Passport copy (first and last pages)
  - Proof of admission or application to French institution (admission letter or acknowledgment email)
  - Academic transcripts (all post-secondary; mark sheets for bachelor’s/master’s)
  - Motivation letter/Statement of Purpose (SOP)
  - CV (2 pages maximum)
  - Two recent academic/extra-academic reference letters (professors/employers)
  - Recent photograph (as per portal instructions)

- **Recommended/Optional:**
  - French proficiency certificate (if available)
  - Other supporting documents as specified by Campus France India portal

---

### 2.4 Step-by-Step Application Process

#### Step 1: Apply for University Admission

- Apply to Université Paris-Saclay’s Master’s in Economics via INCEPTION as detailed in Section 1.4.
- Ensure you receive either a formal admission letter or at least proof that you have applied (e.g., application confirmation email).

#### Step 2: Register on the Campus France India Portal

- Use the official [Charpak Scholarship Application Page](https://www.inde.campusfrance.org/charpak-scholarships).

#### Step 3: Complete and Submit Application

- Fill in all required information and upload all supporting documents via the Campus France India portal.
- Provide accurate contact information for references; they may be contacted directly.
- Choose “Master” as your level and indicate Université Paris-Saclay/Economics as appropriate.

#### Step 4: Interview (If Shortlisted)

- Candidates are invited for an online video interview, typically mid-April (13-30 April for 2026).
- Prepare to discuss academic background, study goals, motivation for France, and future plans.

#### Step 5: Results and Award

- Decisions announced by May or latest July/August 2026.
- If awarded, you must pursue your master’s on campus in France; remote or online participation disqualifies you.

---

### 2.5 Deadlines and Timeline (2026 Cycle)

- **Charpak Master Scholarship Application Deadline:** **March 30, 2026 (23:59 IST)**
- **Interviews:** April 13–30, 2026
- **Results:** By end of May or no later than July/August 2026

---

### 2.6 Notes on Scholarship and Coordination with University Application

- **Application platforms are separate:** Submit university application on INCEPTION and scholarship application on Campus France India portal.
- **Repeat applications:** Current master’s students in France may apply for Charpak support for the second year (if not previously funded and under age 30).
- **Travel costs to France are not covered**; recipients arrange personal travel.
- **Inquiries:**  
  Email: scholarship.france@ifindia.in

---

### 2.7 Changes and Unspecified Details

- **No major procedural or eligibility changes since November 2025**. Process, deadlines, applicant platform, and required documentation are consistent with prior years.
- **GPA/percentage remains open-ended**; competitive merit profile required.
- **Notification dates for results may vary**, but main application, interview, and reward schedule is unchanged.

# The Longstanding Red Rooster Lunchtime Deal: Official Name, Historical Origins, and Current Status (as of April 2026)

## Introduction

Red Rooster is an iconic Australian fast-food chain specializing in roast chicken, founded in 1972 in Kelmscott, Perth by Greek-Australian brothers Peter and Theo Kailis. One of its most enduring hallmarks—especially in Western Australia—has been its budget-priced lunchtime specials featuring a quarter roast chicken and hot chips. This report provides a comprehensive account of the deal’s official name(s), its evolution since inception, detailed pricing history, and its availability and advertised form as of April 2026, with extensive referencing to primary sources and official communications.

## Historical Origins and Evolution of the Lunchtime Deal

### Foundation of Red Rooster

- The first Red Rooster restaurant opened in Kelmscott, a suburb of Perth, in 1972.
- The business was established by Peter Kailis and a group of Greek-Australian partners, with the Kailis family’s influence integral to the brand’s Perth-centric heritage and focus on roasted chicken meals[1][2][3].

### Early Meal Deals

- By the late 1970s and 1980s, Red Rooster offered value-packed chicken meals such as:
  - **Chicken In A Basket**: A quarter roast chicken served with a generous serve of chips in a woven basket, historically priced at $3.60[2].
  - **Hawaiian Pack**: Another quarter chicken and chips combo, with additional pineapple and banana fritter. The contemporary equivalent is the Classic Tropicana[2][3].

### Emergence and Standardisation of the Signature Lunchtime Deal

- By the early 1990s, the chain had solidified its reputation for a value “quarter chicken and chips” lunch deal, offered at an accessible price point and heavily promoted with a clear lunchtime cut-off.
- This special became a “Perth institution” and spread to Red Rooster stores nationally, remaining especially prominent in Western Australia[2][12].

## Official Name(s) and Advertised Content Over Time

### Historical and Popular Names

- The lunchtime offer has been variously known as:
  - **Chicken In A Basket** (late 1970s–80s)[2]
  - **Quarter Chicken & Chips Lunch Deal** or **$5 Lunch Deal** (from the 1990s onwards)[4][12]
  - Sometimes simply **Quarter Chicken With Chips Special** in advertising and customer vernacular[4][13]
- Its consistent contents have remained:
  - One quarter roast chicken (typically leg or breast portion)
  - One serve of hot chips

### Official Naming and Promotional Language (2020s–2026)

- Most recent Red Rooster corporate and social media refer to the deal as:
  - **$5.50 Quarter Chicken & Chips Lunch Deal**
  - Regularly described as “Australia’s best value lunch” or “bargain meal, available until 4pm”
- “Limited-time offer” language is standard on recent posts (2024–2026), emphasizing periodic availability but with frequent recurrence[4][5][6][8].

### Promotional Terms

- **Cut-off Time**: Lunch deals have nearly always been advertised as **available until 4pm daily**, capitalizing on both lunch and early afternoon traffic[4][5][6][7][10].
- **Price Evolution**:
  - 1980s: Chicken in a Basket at $3.60[2]
  - Early 1990s–2010s: Quarter chicken & chips lunch deal at $5[12][13]
  - 2020s–2026: Deal price rose to $5.50 to reflect contemporary pricing[4][7][8][10]
- Sometimes, additional meal specials (snack boxes, burgers) rotate alongside or in place of the quarter chicken & chips combo on the value menu[11].

## Documented History, Pricing, and Evolution

### Expansion and Changes

- The deal’s price, core contents, and timing have remained remarkably steady across decades:
  - Ongoing references and customer nostalgia link today’s bargain to the “Chicken in a Basket” of the 1980s and the formalized $5 lunch deal of the 1990s–2010s[2][3][12].
  - Frequent customer reviews and local Perth memorabilia pages emphasize how little the structure of the meal or its iconic status have changed, aside from gradual increases in the advertised price[12][14].

### Pricing Timeline

- **1980s**: $3.60 lunch special as “Chicken in a Basket”[2]
- **1990s–2010s**: $5 lunch deal—quarter chicken and chips, lunch special[12][13][14][15]
- **2023–2026**: Regular $5.50 special (occasional reversion to $5 in promotions or selected stores)[4][7][10]

### Deal Promotion and Ubiquity in WA

- The lunchtime special is frequently described as a cultural fixture and a defining feature of Red Rooster’s identity in Western Australia, referenced in both official materials and extensive community discussion[2][3][12].
- The official Red Rooster Facebook page and TikTok accounts showcase regular video and carousel ads emphasizing the deal’s unbeatable value and Perth roots, often featuring the “$5.50 Quarter Chicken & Chips” tagline[4][5][6][8][9].

## Current Status and Availability (As of April 2026)

### Promotion Status

- Red Rooster’s Facebook and TikTok channels confirm active promotion of the **$5.50 Quarter Chicken & Chips Lunch Deal** as of April 2026, explicitly referencing the deal’s Perth presence, the offer’s price, contents, and 4pm cut-off[4][5][6][7][8][9].
- Frugal Feeds, a leading deal aggregator, lists the deal as running until April 21, 2026, with a note about possible continued recurrence (“limited-time offer” but repeated cycles), and confirms broad metropolitan Perth availability[7][10].
- Local delivery platform Uber Eats lists $5 and $5.50 lunch deals for multiple Perth Red Rooster locations, though their menus sometimes do not reflect in-store or Click & Collect-only specials[16][17][18][19][20][21].

### Store Coverage and Possible Variations

- All verified customer reviews, delivery aggregator listings, and official posts point to continued and widespread availability in Perth metropolitan areas, including stores such as Southern River, Midvale, Northbridge, Bassendean, Hamilton Hill, and Miami Plaza[16][17][18][19][20].
- Red Rooster’s current [value menu](https://www.redrooster.com.au/value/) (April 2026) may rotate specials; at times, the “Snack Sub Box” is featured alongside or in lieu of Quarter Chicken & Chips, but social and deal-site advertisements confirm the latter is still actively sold instore until at least late April 2026[11].
- Ongoing deal postings do not indicate any major regional discontinuations or exclusions for WA/Perth locations. As with all chain promos, individual outliers due to franchisee variation cannot be completely excluded, so customers should confirm with their specific store for the day's offers[10][21].

### Customer and Media Verification

- Perth-based TripAdvisor and Yelp reviews from 2024–2026 continue to praise Red Rooster’s budget lunch deals, repeatedly singling out “the $5 / $5.50 quarter chicken and chips” for value, speed, and nostalgia[12][13][14][15][21].
- No source indicates an official, public discontinuation; instead, the special is presented as a periodically recurring, near-constant fixture—often described as “rotating” but “always comes back”[7][10].

## Conclusion

Red Rooster’s **Quarter Chicken & Chips Lunch Deal** is one of the most recognizable meal specials in Australian fast food, with Perth as its spiritual and historical home. Emerging from the “Chicken in a Basket” era ($3.60 lunch), the value meal crystalized into an institutionalized “$5 Quarter Chicken & Chips Lunch Deal” by the early 1990s, and remains a staple—albeit most recently at $5.50, with the same serve of roast chicken and golden chips.

Despite the deal’s frequent “limited time” billing, a wealth of official evidence, deal aggregator tracking, and customer reviews confirm broad, continued availability at Red Rooster locations across Perth as of April 2026, especially for in-store and Click & Collect purchases up to 4 pm. The promotion is not only a memory of the past but an active, recurring value proposition in the present, underscoring Red Rooster’s legacy of affordable, quality Australian roast chicken lunches.

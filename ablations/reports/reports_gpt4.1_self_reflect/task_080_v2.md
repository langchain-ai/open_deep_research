## 1. Introduction

This report provides a rigorous, data-driven comparison between the Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund and the DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds (FoF). Six key analytical areas are covered:  

1. Portfolio composition (detailed region and sector breakdown, identification of overweight country/sector exposures)  
2. Top 20 underlying holdings overlap (direct and indirect exposure, quantum of overlap)  
3. Expense ratios for regular and direct plans, with the impact of underlying fund/ETF fees  
4. Historical returns (1-year, 3-year, and, if available, 5-year trailing)

Wherever data gaps exist (especially for full top 20 holdings or 5-year returns for DSP), limitations are explicitly noted. Only official fact sheets, primary company or aggregator platforms, and regulatory documentation are cited.

## 2. Portfolio Composition: Regional and Sector Concentrations

### Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund

**Structure:**  
- This fund is a feeder/fund-of-funds investing almost entirely (96–97%) in the JPMorgan Greater China Fund. Its portfolio composition mirrors JPMorgan’s allocations[1][2][3][4].

**Geographical Allocation (JPMorgan Greater China Fund, Mar 2026):**  
- China: 56.6%  
- Taiwan: 40.4%  
- Hong Kong: 2.2%  
- Cash: ~10.8% (included as part of the underlying fund as of Mar 2026)[2][4]

**Sector Allocation:**  
- Information Technology: 42.9%  
- Consumer Discretionary: 18.0%  
- Communication Services: 13.1%  
- Financials: 11.6%  
- Industrials: 3.9%  
- Health Care: 2.8%  
- Real Estate: 2.6%[4][5]

**Concentration/Overweight:**  
- Extremely high regional concentration in Greater China (China+Taiwan+Hong Kong ≈ 99%).  
- Heavy sector bias: Technology and related consumption/communication services account for over 74% of the fund[2][4].  
- Largest single-stock weight: Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Co Ltd (TSMC), nearly 10%[2][4].

### DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds

**Structure:**  
- Invests in a basket of global mutual funds and ETFs focused on “innovation”—predominantly, but not exclusively, large US and Chinese technology firms[6][7][8].

**Portfolio (as of Mar 2026):**
- BlueBox Global Technology Fund: 37.5%  
- iShares NASDAQ 100 UCITS ETF: 27.5%  
- Invesco NASDAQ-100 Equal Weight UCITS ETF: 12.3%  
- KraneShares CSI China Internet UCITS ETF: 12.3–14%  
- Fidelity Select Medical Technology: ~3.9%  
- BlueBox Precision Medicine: ~2.5%  
- Balanced exposure to the rest across various other global innovation/biotech/tech funds[6][7][8][9].

**Inferred Regional Weights:**  
- US/Global Developed Markets (via NASDAQ ETFs and BlueBox): ~75%  
- China (KraneShares ETF): ~13%  
- Remainder in global (i.e., Asian, European, etc.) innovation sectors  
- No allocation to small or mid-cap Indian equities; pure global play[6][7][8][9].

**Sector Composition (Approximated):**  
- Technology/IT: ~65–75% (aggregated through underlying funds)  
- Biotech/Healthcare: ~4%  
- Other: Cloud, fintech, digital infrastructure[7][9].

**Overweight:**  
- Overweight to US technology sector and NASDAQ 100 constituents, moderate overweight to Chinese internet/technology via ETF, more diversified globally compared to Edelweiss[7][8][9].

## 4. Expense Ratios (Latest as of Mar/Apr 2026) & All-In Costs

### Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund  
- Regular Plan: 1.50–2.39%  
- Direct Plan: 0.74–1.64%  
- Underlying JPMorgan Fund’s Ongoing Charge: Ranges from 0.76% (I2 share class) to 1.77% (USD class), depending on share class used by the feeder[2][3][4][5][16][17].
- Investor pays both layers—the Edelweiss FoF expense and the underlying JPMorgan fund’s expenses.  
- **All-in effective cost:** For Indian regular plan investors, total cost can exceed 2.5–3% depending on share class and expense regime. The fund is recognized for its “highest TER compared to others in its peer category”[2][4][15][16][17].

### DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds  
- Regular Plan: 2.03–2.05%  
- Direct Plan: 1.14–1.16%  
- These expense ratios only reflect the FoF's management cost; underlying mutual funds and ETFs also charge annual fees which are embedded in the net returns.  
- **All-in effective cost:** Not separately disclosed by DSP; in practice, the all-in cost is higher, as underlying ETF/MF OCFs are passed through to investor returns[6][10][15][17][19].

**Conclusion:**  
Both funds have higher-than-average expense ratios for international equity products, with Edelweiss particularly elevated due to double-layer fees. DSP's costs reflect both its global scope and multi-manager structure.

## 6. Risk Factors and Volatility Assessment

### Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund  
- Classified as “Very High Risk” by all risk meters[2][4][16].
- Standard deviation: 19.92–26.4% over recent 3-year periods[2][17].
- Sharpe Ratio: 0.26–0.39 (below category average)[2][17].
- Risk sources:  
  - Heavy exposure to Greater China policy, geopolitical, and regulatory risk  
  - High historic volatility, major drawdowns (e.g., –29% in 2022, –10% in 2023)  
  - Currency exposure (primarily USD, RMB)[4][5][16]
- Volatility and drawdown experience: Large negative years 2021–23, then sharp, rapid recoveries 2024–25.  
- Exit load: 1% if redeemed in 90 days[2][16][17].

### DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds  
- Classified as “Very High Risk”[10][17][18].
- Standard deviation: 16.6% (lower than Edelweiss)[10][17].
- Sharpe Ratio: 0.99 (healthier vs. Edelweiss; reflective of better risk-adjusted return during recent tech rally)[10][17].
- Diversified across US, EU, and Chinese innovation, but still thematically concentrated (global tech/AI sectors)[6][7].
- Exposure to:  
  - Rapid rotation in global tech valuation, US rate cycle risk  
  - Secondary China policy/regulation risk (via KraneShares ETF)  
  - Potential drawdowns when global tech or China faces sector-wide correction
- No exit load[10][15][17].

**Summary:**  
Edelweiss carries higher volatility and more pronounced single-country or policy drawdown risk. DSP, while “very high risk” due to sector/thematic concentration, has demonstrated lower measured volatility and superior risk-adjusted returns in the short term due to broader diversification.

## 7. Suitability: 2-Year and 5-Year Horizon Recommendation

**2-Year Horizon:**  
- **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF** is more suitable for most investors.  
  - Rationale: Lower volatility, global diversification reduces idiosyncratic country/event risk. Recent returns have been more stable, and exposure to secular tech/AI trends improves risk-return profile.  
- **Edelweiss Greater China** is appropriate only for those with strong, high-conviction “bull” views on Greater China markets and high risk tolerance, as short-term returns are highly volatile and unpredictable[2][4][10][17].

**5-Year Horizon:**  
- **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF** remains the preferred option for most investors seeking consistent, long-term risk-adjusted growth.  
  - Although lacking a 5-year realized track record, the fund’s global and sectoral diversification provides resilience to regional or sector slumps and has outpaced most global/innovation peers in available periods[10][13][15].
- **Edelweiss Greater China** may only outperform if the region enters a multi-year cyclical recovery; history shows large swings, and 5-year trailing returns as of today remain below category averages due to the 2021–23 correction. Suitable only for high-risk, China-focused investors[4][16][17].

**Overall Caveats:**  
- Direct and indirect exposure overlap between the funds is modest; they are not substitutes but can be used for diversification in an aggressive portfolio.
- Limitations: No current full top 20 holdings list for either fund, and DSP lacks a 5-year or longer performance history.

# Comprehensive Relocation Analysis: Austin, Raleigh & Nashville for a Mechanical Engineer Family — Revised & Updated (May 2026)

## Executive Summary

Based on extensive updated research across all eight critical dimensions, **Raleigh, North Carolina continues to offer the best overall financial outcome and quality-of-life balance over a 10-year horizon**, though the margin over Nashville has narrowed considerably due to several corrections and new data points uncovered in this revision. 

The most significant correction relates to Nashville home prices in top school districts: the original report dramatically understated Williamson County housing costs (citing $510K–$593K when actual Brentwood median prices range from $900,000 to $1,312,500). This changes the financial calculus substantially for families seeking top-ranked elementary schools. Raleigh's Cary/Apex corridor ($580K–$635K for 9-10/10 rated schools) now presents an even stronger value proposition relative to Nashville's Williamson County entry point ($650K minimum for Franklin, $900K+ for Brentwood).

Austin retains the strongest job market depth for senior mechanical engineers and highest salary potential ($144,949 average senior ME salary), but its top-tier school districts (Eanes ISD at $1.8M+ median home price) remain financially prohibitive. Round Rock ISD ($420K–$520K) offers a reasonable compromise with 8/10 rated schools, though property taxes in Texas remain the highest of all three metros even after the new $140,000 homestead exemption.

**Updated rankings:**
1. **Raleigh** — Best school-to-home-price ratio, stable job growth with major AbbVie and Biogen expansions, lowest sales tax (7.25%), reliable RTP commute, moderate tax burden
2. **Nashville** — Zero income tax, lowest property taxes, but corrected home prices in top school districts ($650K+ minimum) and highest sales tax (9.75%) including groceries
3. **Austin** — Highest salary potential, deepest aerospace/semiconductor market, but highest property taxes and school district premiums make top-tier schools unaffordable

### 2.2 Travis County, TX (Austin) — Detailed Breakdown

The Travis County Commissioners Court adopted a FY 2026 tax rate of **37.5845 cents per $100** ($0.3758/$100) — the Voter Approval Rate, representing a slight increase from FY 2025 [25]. Combined with the City of Austin ($0.5240/$100) [26] and Austin ISD ($0.9252/$100) [27], the total combined rate is approximately **2.07%** of taxable value.

**Critical Update — Texas Proposition 13 (November 2025):** The homestead exemption increased from $100,000 to **$140,000** of market value, reducing taxable value for school district taxes [5]. This saves the average homeowner approximately $1,250 annually in school taxes. However, this applies only to the school district portion (~44% of the tax bill) — the county and city portions still tax full value.

The estimated average taxable homestead value increased 2.24% to $515,213 [25]. Travis County also offers a 20% homestead exemption for the county portion (the maximum allowed by Texas law) [25].

### 2.3 Williamson County, TX — Detailed Breakdown

On September 3, 2025, Williamson County approved a tax rate of **$0.413776 per $100** valuation [28]. Combined with Round Rock ISD ($0.8931/$100) [28] and City of Round Rock ($0.372/$100) [28], the total combined rate is approximately **1.68%** [28].

July 2025 catastrophic flash floods triggered disaster declarations, allowing the county to use a higher 8% revenue cap instead of the standard 3.5% [29]. The Williamson Central Appraisal District's 2025 average assessment increase was 6.3%, with newer homes increasing by 19.6% [29].

### 2.4 Wake County, NC — Detailed Breakdown

The Wake County Board of Commissioners adopted a FY 2026 budget with a property tax rate of **51.71 cents per $100** ($0.5171/$100) — a 0.36 cent increase from the prior year [30]. Combined with City of Raleigh ($0.3550/$100) [31], the total combined rate is **$0.8721 per $100** (0.87%).

**Important FY 2027 Proposed Increase:** On May 4, 2026, County Manager David Ellis proposed an FY 2027 budget with a rate of **53.71 cents per $100** ($0.5371/$100), an increase of 2 cents that would result in approximately $90 additional annual tax on a $450,000 home [32]. This proposal is designed to address funding shortfalls due to property revaluation appeals and tax exemption loopholes [32].

### 2.5 Davidson County, TN (Nashville) — Detailed Breakdown

**Assessment Method:** Tennessee residential real estate is taxed on **25% of fair market value** [33].

**FY 2026 Combined Rate:** The combined property tax rate for FY 2026 is **$2.814 per $100 of assessed value** (down from $3.254 in FY 2025) — the lowest for Nashville in decades [34]. This translates to approximately **0.70% effective rate on market value**.

**2025 Reappraisal Impact:** The 2025 property reappraisal showed a countywide median increase in property values of **45%** [35]. Despite the nominal rate dropping from $3.254 to $2.814 (a revenue-neutral adjustment), the Metro Council approved a budget that effectively resulted in a **26% property tax hike** because the rate exceeded the revenue-neutral certified tax rate [36]. For many Nashville homeowners, annual property taxes jumped by $2,500 or more per year [36].

### 2.6 Williamson County, TN — Detailed Breakdown

The 2025 county-wide reappraisal showed overall taxable property values rising by **52%** (residential up 62%) [37]. The county adopted a rate of **$1.30 per $100 of assessed value**, down from $1.88 in 2024 (a 30% rate reduction) [38]. 

With residential property assessed at 25% of market value, this translates to approximately **0.325% effective rate** on market value for the county portion alone. Combined with municipal rates (Brentwood adds $0.19/$100), the total effective rate is approximately **0.54%** [39].

The county also moved its reappraisal cycle from every four years to every three years, effective with this assessment [37].

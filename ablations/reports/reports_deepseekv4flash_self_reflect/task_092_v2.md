## Part A: Master's in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay — Admission Requirements & Eligibility (2027-2028 Cycle)

### 1. Program Overview

The **Master of Economics (MoE)** at Université Paris-Saclay is a two-year, highly selective program taught entirely in English. The program is a collaboration between Université Paris-Saclay, ENS Paris-Saclay, and affiliated institutions including AgroParisTech, CentraleSupélec, and the University of Evry-Val d'Essonne [1][4][6].

**Program Structure:**
- **First Year (M1):** 8 compulsory core courses supplemented by 2 optional discovery courses and 2 research projects. The M1 is certified by ENS Paris-Saclay [1][4].
- **Second Year (M2) — Optional:** Students build their own curricula under faculty supervision, culminating in a Research Master thesis. The M2 is certified by Université Paris-Saclay [1][4][6].

**Courses in M1 (2024-2025 syllabus):**
- Semester 1: Mathematics I, Decision Theory and Game Theory, Intermediate Econometrics, Macroeconomics I, Development Economics, Boot Camp in Computer Science (R programming), Ethics in Research
- Semester 2: Mathematics II (Algebra), Advanced Econometrics, Public Economics, Macroeconomics II (New Keynesian model), Firms and Markets, Economic Modeling: Dynamics
- Optional courses: International Trade, Experimental Economics, Financial Modeling with Python, Gender and Politics, Environmental and Resource Economics, Economic Growth, Topics in Game Theory [5][9][12]

**M2 Specializations (3 tracks):**
1. Trade, Geography and Development Economics
2. Public and Behavioral Economics
3. Advances and Challenges in Macroeconomics
4. Interdisciplinary combinations possible [1][4][6]

**ECTS Credits:** Standard 120 ECTS over two years (60 per academic year). Each course carries specific ECTS (e.g., Public Economics = 6 ECTS, Advanced Econometrics = 6 ECTS, Development Economics = 2 ECTS) [5][9].

**Language of Instruction:** Entirely in English. "The master of Economics is a two-years selective program taught in English" [1][4][6].

**Campus Location:** Saclay Moulon campus in Gif-sur-Yvette, France (Université Paris-Saclay, 3 rue Joliot Curie, Bâtiment Breguet, 91190 Gif-sur-Yvette) [1][2].

**Research Orientation:** The program is explicitly research-oriented, preparing students for top PhD programs worldwide or careers in public institutions, international organizations, consulting, and data science. Over 50% of students are expected to pursue a PhD [1][4][9].

**Class Size:** In 2023-2024, the first year had 43 students, including international students [12].

**Exchange Network:** 6 partner European universities: Humboldt University of Berlin, Ludwig Maximilian University of Munich, University of Bologna, University Carlos III of Madrid, University of Crete, University of Barcelona [12].

### 2. Academic Prerequisites

#### Degree Requirements
- **Bachelor's degree in Economics** (BA in Economics) **OR** **Bachelor's degree in Mathematics** (BS in Mathematics) [1][4][12]
- Other degrees in sciences and social sciences from best universities **will be considered** on a case-by-case basis [12]
- Strong quantitative background is essential — the program "requires strong knowledge in quantitative methods" [1][12]

#### Indian Bachelor's Degree Recognition (3-Year vs 4-Year Degrees)

**Confirmed: 3-year Indian bachelor's degrees are recognized for Master's admission in France.**

France and India signed a **Mutual Recognition of Academic Qualifications Agreement** during President Macron's state visit to India in 2018, which came into force in 2019 [13][15]. Key provisions:

- The Indian **Bachelor's degree** is recognized as equivalent to the French **Licence** (3 years of higher education) [13][15]
- The Indian **Master's degree** is recognized as equivalent to the French Master's level [13][15]
- The agreement applies to all institutions that are members of the **Association of Indian Universities (AIU)** and all duly approved Indian institutions [13]
- Professional degrees such as Law and Medicine are excluded from this agreement [13]

**Important Note:** While there is no automatic equivalence system, each French institution defines its own admission criteria. The ENIC-NARIC centre may provide an attestation certifying the French value of a foreign degree (fee: 120 Euros) [14].

**Conclusion for Indian Applicants:** A standard 3-year Indian Bachelor's degree (BA Economics or BS Mathematics) from a recognized Indian institution is sufficient for M1 Economics admission eligibility. A 4-year degree provides additional coursework and may strengthen the application.

#### Expected GPA/Academic Standing

The official program pages do not state a specific minimum GPA requirement. The program is described as "selective" with "limited places" [3][5][10].

Key points:
- Admission is **highly competitive** — the overall acceptance rate for Paris-Saclay Master's programs is estimated at 10-20% [8]
- Students should come from "best universities" [12]
- Third-party sources suggest a recommended GPA of **3.3/4.0 or strong First Class Division** [8]
- The program evaluates the **entire academic record** including consistency across all years

#### Prerequisite Coursework

Required foundational knowledge before entering the program includes:
- **Mathematics:** Calculus, linear algebra, topology, optimization
- **Statistics/Econometrics:** Probability theory, statistical inference, regression analysis
- **Macroeconomics:** Intermediate macroeconomics (RBC-DSGE models, labor markets, international macroeconomics)
- **Microeconomics:** Decision theory, game theory
- **Programming:** Basic familiarity with R or similar statistical software (the program includes a Boot Camp in Computer Science using R)

### 3. Language Proficiency Requirements

#### English Proficiency (Mandatory)

The program is taught entirely in English, and proof of English proficiency is a required document for all applicants [1][4][6].

**Official Requirement:** B2 level in English is required [12][14]. The official M1 Economics page lists "English proficiency certificate" among required admission documents [1][4].

**While official minimum scores are not published on the program page, evidence from multiple sources indicates:**

| Test | Recommended Minimum Score | Notes |
|------|--------------------------|-------|
| **IELTS** | **6.5–7.0** (B2/C1 level) | One applicant with IELTS 6.0 was rejected with reason "too low language proficiency to follow training" [8] |
| **TOEFL iBT** | **90–100** | B2 level typically corresponds to TOEFL 72-94; C1 is 95-120 |
| **Cambridge English** | **C1 Advanced** (grade B/C) or B2 First | Accepted certificates |

**Important:** While B2 is the stated minimum, C1 level is strongly recommended given the program's rigorous academic nature. Applicants with IELTS 6.0 have been rejected, suggesting the effective minimum is IELTS 6.5 or higher.

**Accepted Tests:** TOEFL, IELTS, Cambridge English Qualifications [1][4].

#### French Language Proficiency (Not Required But Recommended)

- **French is NOT required** for admission — the degree can be earned entirely in English [1][4][6][12]
- Basic knowledge of French (A2-B1 level) is helpful for daily life, internships, and post-study employment
- French language courses (FLE) are available for international students at the university
- Knowledge of French is considered an **asset** for the Charpak scholarship application

#### GRE/GMAT Requirements

- **GRE and GMAT are NOT required** for M1 Economics admission [1][4][6]
- The M2 Economics page mentions "proof of English proficiency or GMAT/GRE scores" as supporting documents that can be submitted [7], suggesting GRE/GMAT are optional and may strengthen an application but are not mandatory
- The official M1 Economics page does not mention GRE or GMAT in its list of required documents

**Recommendation:** If you have strong GRE/GMAT scores (e.g., Q165+ for GRE, 700+ for GMAT), submitting them may enhance your application, but they are not necessary.

### 4. Application Platforms and Procedures

#### Which Platform to Use (Indian Applicants)

For an Indian applicant residing in India applying to M1 Economics, the process involves **two platforms in sequence**:

**Step 1: Apply on INCEPTION Platform (Primary Application)**

All M1 applications for international students must be submitted on the **INCEPTION platform** (https://inception.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/accueil/) [2][3][5][10].

**Rule:** "If you can use trouverMonMaster, then you must do so; if not, apply through the Université Paris Saclay portal (Inception)" [14]. Since Mon Master is for French students and EU/EEA residents, **Indian applicants must use INCEPTION**.

**Step 2: Complete Études en France (EEF) Procedure (Post-Admission)**

After receiving admission approval from Université Paris-Saclay, Indian applicants must complete the **Études en France (EEF)** procedure through Campus France India [3][8][16].

India is one of the countries subject to the EEF procedure — it is mandatory for all Indian students seeking to study in France [16][17].

#### Step-by-Step Application Process for Indian Applicants

**Phase 1: INCEPTION Application (January – March 2027)**

1. **Create an account** on the INCEPTION platform (email verification required)
2. **Select the M1 Economics program**
3. **Fill in all sections:**
   - Civil status (personal information)
   - Academic background (all post-secondary education)
   - Internships and professional career (if applicable)
   - Miscellaneous details
4. **Provide contact details for 2 academic referees** (the system will contact them to upload recommendations)
5. **Upload required documents** (PDF format, under 3MB each)
6. **Validate and submit** the application — after validation, no changes can be made [5][10]
7. **Wait for decision** — the university provides an answer within two months of submission [5]

**Phase 2: Accept Admission Offer (March – April 2027)**

- If accepted, confirm admission on the INCEPTION platform
- You may be offered admission, placed on a waiting list, or rejected

**Phase 3: Études en France Procedure (April – June 2027)**

1. **Create an account** on the Études en France portal (https://www.etudesenfrance.com)
2. **Complete your file** under the "I am accepted" section [3][8]
3. **Submit documents** and communicate with your Campus France manager
4. **Attend an academic interview** (online or in person) with the Campus France Manager to discuss reasons for choosing France and the institution [16]
5. **Receive EEF validation**

**Phase 4: Visa Application (June – August 2027)**

1. Complete visa application on the **France-Visas** portal
2. Schedule an appointment with VFS Global for biometric data registration
3. Submit passport and supporting documents
4. Await visa decision

**Phase 5: Registration (August – September 2027)**

1. Pay the **CVEC** (Student and Campus Life Contribution)
2. Complete **administrative registration** online
3. Complete **academic registration** to enroll in courses [2][3]

### 8. Tuition Fees (Critical Update for 2027-2028)

There is a **significant policy change** that will directly impact fees for the 2027-2028 academic year.

#### Historical Policy (2025-2026)

Until 2025-2026, Université Paris-Saclay applied a **partial exemption** policy (adopted December 2020) that allowed non-EU students to pay the same fees as domestic students:
- **EU students:** €254/year for Master's
- **Non-EU students (with exemption):** €254/year [6]

#### New French Government Policy (Effective from 2026-2027)

On April 21, 2026, French Higher Education Minister Philippe Baptiste announced a mandatory policy making differentiated fees the rule [15]:

| Category | New Fees (2026-2027) | Previous Fees (2025-2026) |
|----------|---------------------|--------------------------|
| **Non-EU Master's students** | **€3,941/year** | €254/year |
| **Non-EU Bachelor's students** | €2,895/year | €178/year |
| **Non-EU Doctoral students** | €380/year (unchanged) | €380/year |
| **EU students (all levels)** | €254/year (unchanged) | €254/year |

**Exemption Policy:**
- Exemptions will be **limited to no more than 10% of non-EU students**
- Priority exemptions for **scholarship holders** (including French government scholarships like Charpak) and students facing hardship
- 60% of scholarships will target priority fields: digital/AI, quantum, biotechnology [15]

**What This Means for Indian Applicants (2027-2028):**
- **Projected tuition fee:** €3,941/year for non-EU Master's students
- **CVEC:** Approximately €105/year (mandatory for all students)
- **Total minimum annual cost:** Approximately **€4,046** (tuition + CVEC)
- Paris-Saclay's previous automatic partial exemption WILL NO LONGER APPLY due to the new government mandate

**Additional Costs:**
- Living expenses in the Paris area: €10,000–€12,000 per year
- Health insurance (if not covered by scholarship): approximately €300–600/year
- Student visa fee: €99 (waived for Charpak scholarship holders)
- Campus France procedure fee: approximately €200

#### Nationality Requirement
- **Indian citizens** OR **Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) cardholders** residing in India [4][5]

- **There is NO CGPA/percentage cut-off** for the scholarship [4][5][6]

- Must have **applied for admission to a French institution** before applying for the scholarship [4][5]
- Must **not be a prior Charpak scholarship recipient** [5]
- Must conduct the Master's program **entirely in France** [5]

### 7. Interaction with Other Scholarships

#### Can Charpak Be Combined with Other Scholarships?

**The Charpak scholarship CANNOT be combined with other French government scholarships.**

Key rules:
- The French Embassy "reserves the right to revoke the scholarship if the awardee is granted another French government scholarship" [4][5]
- Specific revocation conditions include: "receipt of another French government scholarship, accepting other scholarships, signing a professional contract, taking a gap year, or failing to obtain a visa" [5]

#### Charpak vs. Université Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship

| Aspect | Charpak Master's Scholarship | Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship |
|--------|------------------------------|-------------------------------------------------|
| **Monthly stipend** | €860/month | €10,000/year (€833/month) |
| **Tuition coverage** | ❌ NO | ❌ NO |
| **Visa fee** | ✅ Exemption | ❌ Not covered |
| **Health insurance** | ✅ Included | ❌ Not covered |
| **Travel costs** | ❌ NOT covered | ✅ Up to €1,000 travel/visa |
| **Duration** | 1–2 years | 1–2 years |
| **Application** | Apply via Charpak portal | By invitation after Paris-Saclay admission |
| **Conflict rule** | Cannot combine with other French govt scholarships | Cannot combine with funding above €600/month |

**Critical Interaction:**
- The Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship states: students receiving **funding above €600/month** will be declared ineligible [9]
- Since Charpak provides **€860/month**, a Charpak awardee would exceed the €600/month threshold for the Paris-Saclay scholarship
- **Conclusion: You CANNOT hold both scholarships simultaneously**

**Strategic Advice:**
- If offered both scholarships, you must choose one
- The Paris-Saclay scholarship offers slightly more total funding (€10,000 + €1,000 travel = €11,000 vs. Charpak's €8,600)
- However, Charpak offers additional benefits (visa fee waiver, health insurance, accommodation assistance)
- Apply for both, but plan to select the better option if admitted to both

### 2. Strategic Advice: Should You Apply to Both Scholarships?

**Yes, you should apply to both the Charpak Master's Scholarship and the Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship.**

**Reasons to Apply for Both:**
- **Increased chances of funding** — apply to all available options
- **Dual evaluation** — two independent selection committees assess your profile
- **Negotiation leverage** — if offered both, you can choose the better option

**However, be aware:**
- You **CANNOT hold both simultaneously** — the Charpak scholarship is incompatible with the Paris-Saclay scholarship (Paris-Saclay states funding above €600/month makes students ineligible, and Charpak provides €860/month) [9]
- The Charpak scholarship also forbids holding another French government scholarship simultaneously [4][5]

**How to Choose (If Admitted to Both):**

| Factor | Charpak | Paris-Saclay |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| Total annual value | ~€8,600 (stipend only) | ~€11,000 (stipend + travel) |
| Visa fee | ✅ Waived | ❌ Pay €99 |

| Accommodation assistance | ✅ Priority access | ❌ Not included |
| Cultural activities | ✅ Included | ❌ Not included |
| Prestige | French Govt. scholarship | University-specific |
| Travel costs | ❌ NOT covered | ✅ Up to €1,000 |

**Recommendation:** If offered both, choose the **Charpak scholarship** if you value visa fee waiver and health insurance coverage. Choose the **Paris-Saclay scholarship** for slightly higher total funding and travel assistance.

### 3. Practical Recommendations for Indian Applicants

#### Document Preparation Tips

**Academic Documents:**
- Start collecting transcripts and degree certificates **6 months before deadlines**
- Get documents **translated into French or English by a certified translator** (official translations are crucial)
- Request **sealed, official transcripts** from your university (some institutions take weeks to process requests)
- For the INCEPTION application, ensure all documents are in **PDF format under 3MB** [5][10]

**Recommendation Letters:**
- Approach **2 academic referees** (professors who taught you) at least **3 months before deadlines**
- Provide referees with:
  - Your CV and academic background
  - The program description and research interests
  - Specific achievements you'd like them to highlight
  - Clear deadlines for submission
- The INCEPTION system contacts referees directly — ensure they are prepared to submit online [3][8]

**Motivation Letter/SOP:**
- **DO NOT use AI** to generate content — plagiarism checks are conducted and AI use may lead to disqualification [4][5]
- Write genuinely about your academic journey, research interests, and reasons for choosing the program
- Reference **specific professors, research labs, or courses** at Université Paris-Saclay that interest you
- For the Charpak SOP, adhere to the **250-word limit per question** [6]
- The Charpak SOP covers: why this university/program, how it benefits your future, why you are a suitable candidate

**CV:**
- Include: education, research experience, internships, publications, technical skills (R, Python, Stata, econometrics), language proficiency
- Keep it to **1-2 pages** maximum
- Emphasize quantitative skills and research experience

#### Timeline for Tests

**English Proficiency Tests:**
- **Ideal timeline:** Take IELTS/TOEFL **6 months before applications open** (by September 2026)
- **Latest deadline:** Results must be available before March 2027 application deadline
- **Recommended deadline:** December 2026 to allow time for retakes if needed
- **Target scores:** IELTS 7.0+ or TOEFL 94+ (C1 level for competitive advantage)

**French Language (Optional but Recommended):**
- If you have time, taking DELF/DALF adds value to the Charpak application
- Even A2-B1 level shows commitment to integrating in France
- Not mandatory, but "knowledge of French is an asset" for the Charpak scholarship [4][5]

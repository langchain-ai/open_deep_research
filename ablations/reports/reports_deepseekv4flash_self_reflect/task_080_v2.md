# Comprehensive Comparison: Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund vs. DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FOF

**Revised & Updated Report — Data as of May 30, 2026**

---

## Executive Summary

This revised report provides an updated comparative analysis of two offshore mutual fund offerings available to Indian investors: the **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** and the **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FOF** (formerly DSP Global Innovation Fund of Fund). The previous analysis was based on data as of March 2025; this update incorporates the most current portfolio disclosures, performance figures, and expense ratios available as of May 30, 2026.

The most significant changes since the prior report are:

- **Chinese equities have dramatically outperformed global innovation/tech stocks** over the past 12 months. The MSCI China Index returned ~+31% in calendar 2025 and the KraneShares CSI China Internet ETF (KWEB) returned +23.47% in 2025 and another +20.59% YTD through May 2026, while the Nasdaq-100 (QQQ) returned ~+17.77% in 2025 but fell -5.93% YTD 2026.
- The **Edelweiss fund's 1-year return has surged to +36.43%** (as of May 25, 2026), nearly matching the DSP FOF's +44% 1-year return — a dramatic reversal from the prior period when the DSP fund led by a wide margin.
- The DSP FOF has **restructured its portfolio** to include an 11.2% allocation to the KraneShares CSI China Internet UCITS ETF (KWEB), creating a **~4% indirect overlap** with the Edelweiss fund's holdings.
- The **DSP FOF expense structure has improved**: Direct Plan TER is now 1.08% (inclusive of underlying fund expenses), lower than previously estimated.
- The **Edelweiss fund** remains a concentrated single-country feeder fund into the JPMorgan Funds - JF Greater China Equity Fund, with AUM of ₹3,143 crore.
- The **DSP FOF** has AUM of ₹1,306 crore and a management transition occurred (Kaivalya Nadkarni has managed the fund since May 2025).

The suitability recommendations have shifted: for a **2-year horizon**, the Edelweiss fund now appears more compelling given China's strong momentum, but risks remain elevated. For a **5-year horizon**, the DSP FOF remains the more prudent choice, though the gap has narrowed.

## 3. Overlap in Top Holdings

### 3.1 Structural Overlap Analysis

Since the Edelweiss fund invests directly in the JPMorgan Greater China Fund (holding individual Greater China equities) and the DSP FOF invests through ETFs and funds, the overlap must be analyzed at the underlying stock level.

| **PDD Holdings** | Possible | Yes (8.30%) | Possibly (PDD ADR) |

### 3.2 Quantitative Overlap Estimate

| Overlap Channel | Estimated % of DSP NAV |
|-----------------|----------------------|
| KWEB channel (Tencent, Alibaba, Meituan, NetEase, JD, Baidu) | ~2.7-2.9% |
| NASDAQ 100 channel (Alibaba ADR, other Chinese ADRs) | ~0.4-0.5% |
| BlueBox Global Tech channel (TSMC, other Greater China tech) | ~0.8-1.0% |
| **Total Estimated Overlap** | **~3.9-4.4%** |

**Conclusion:** Approximately **4% (±1%) of the DSP Global Innovation FOF's NAV** overlaps with stocks also held by the Edelweiss Greater China Fund. This is higher than the prior estimate of <2%, driven primarily by the DSP FOF's new 11.2% allocation to KWEB.

The overlap is concentrated in:
- **Tencent** (largest single overlap)
- **Alibaba** (second largest)
- **Other Chinese internet stocks** via KWEB
- **TSMC** via potential BlueBox Global Tech overlap

### 3.3 Practical Implications

For an investor holding both funds, approximately 4% of the DSP FOF's portfolio duplicates holdings in the Edelweiss fund. This is not excessive but represents concentrated double exposure to the same Chinese internet giants. Investors seeking maximum diversification should be aware that holding both funds increases their effective exposure to Tencent, Alibaba, and other Chinese tech names beyond what the Edelweiss fund alone would suggest.

## 6. Market Context & Current Outlook

### 6.1 Chinese Equities: The Comeback Story

The most significant development since the prior report is the **dramatic outperformance of Chinese equities** over the past 12-18 months. Key factors:

- **MSCI China Index** returned approximately **+31% in calendar 2025**, followed by continued strength in early 2026
- **KraneShares CSI China Internet ETF (KWEB)** returned **+23.47% in 2025** and an additional **+20.59% YTD as of May 27, 2026**
- Chinese equities are outperforming US tech: KWEB +20.59% YTD vs QQQ -5.93% YTD in 2026

**Catalysts for Chinese equity outperformance:**
- **DeepSeek AI breakthrough** (early 2025): Showcased China's ability to deliver high-performing, cost-efficient large language models, signaling its emergence as a formidable contender in the global AI race
- **Valuation discount**: Chinese internet stocks trade at ~15x earnings vs. ~30x for US peers (per KraneShares)
- **Government stimulus**: Proactive policy support from Chinese authorities
- **Record southbound inflows**: Over $180 billion flowed into Hong Kong stocks from mainland Chinese investors in 2025
- **Share buybacks & dividends**: Major Chinese tech companies engaging in buybacks and increased dividends, signaling confidence
- **Structural transformation**: Chinese brands' EVs accounted for 76% of global EV and plug-in hybrid EV sales in 2025

*Sources: [Invesco 2026 China Equities Outlook](https://www.invesco.com/apac/en/institutional/insights/equity/china-equities-outlook.html), [KraneShares Webinar - China Outlook](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=rLgHMRGy9tU), [Alliance Bernstein - China 2026](https://www.alliancebernstein.com)*

### 6.2 Global Innovation/Technology: Consolidation Phase

The DSP FOF's core holdings in US tech (BlueBox Global Tech, NASDAQ 100 ETFs) have experienced a more mixed environment:

- **Nasdaq-100 (QQQ)**: +17.77% in 2025, but -5.93% YTD 2026 through May
- **Valuation concerns**: QQQ trades at a 32x trailing earnings multiple, well above historical averages

## 8. Suitability Recommendations

### 8.1 Assessment Framework

The suitability assessment considers:
1. **Return consistency and volatility**
2. **Maximum drawdowns and recovery time**

4. **Current market conditions and outlook**

### 8.2 Recommendation for a 2-Year Investment Horizon

#### Revised Assessment (May 2026)

**More Suitable Fund: DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FOF** (for most investors), with the Edelweiss fund as a tactical satellite position for aggressive investors.

**Rationale:**

1. **Expense Structure Reversal:** The prior report favored the Edelweiss fund for lower expenses. However, with the updated data showing Edelweiss Direct Plan TER at 2.32% vs. DSP Direct Plan TER at 1.08%, the **cost advantage has reversed dramatically**. Over a 2-year period, the DSP fund now saves approximately 2.48% in cumulative expenses versus the Edelweiss fund (1.08% vs. 2.32% annually).

2. **China Momentum vs. Risk:** Chinese equities have had an extraordinary run (+31% in 2025, +20.59% YTD 2026). While the Edelweiss fund's 1-year return of +36.43% is compelling, the sustainability of this momentum is uncertain. Chinese equities remain subject to:
   - Geopolitical risks (US-China trade tensions, Taiwan)
   - Regulatory uncertainty
   - Property sector fragility
   - Demographic headwinds

3. **DSP FOF's Advantage:** The DSP fund offers:
   - Lower volatility (std dev 16.9 vs. 21.2)
   - Lower maximum drawdown (-11.19% vs. ~55-60%)
   - No exit load (vs. 1% within 90 days for Edelweiss)
   - 1-year return of +44.0% (outperforming Edelweiss by ~7.6%)
   - Exposure to both US tech and Chinese tech (via 11.2% KWEB)

4. **Liquidity & Flexibility:** The DSP fund's nil exit load provides greater flexibility for a 2-year horizon, while the Edelweiss fund's 1% exit load within 90 days can impact short-term tactical moves.

5. **Risk-Reward for 2-Year Horizon:** For a 2-year horizon, preserving capital while achieving growth is critical. The DSP fund's lower drawdown risk and superior risk-adjusted returns make it more suitable for most investors. The Edelweiss fund's extreme drawdown history (~55-60%) means that a 2-year investor could lose a significant portion of capital if Chinese equities suffer a downturn.

**Verdict for 2-Year Horizon:**
- **For Most Investors (Moderate to High Risk Tolerance):** DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FOF — superior risk-adjusted returns, lower volatility, lower costs, better liquidity
- **For Aggressive Investors (Very High Risk Tolerance):** The Edelweiss fund could be used as a **tactical satellite** (20-30% of allocation) given China's strong momentum and attractive valuations. However, it should not be the core holding due to extreme single-country risk.

**Change from Prior Report:** The prior report recommended the Edelweiss fund for aggressive 2-year investors. The updated data, particularly the expense ratio reversal and the extreme drawdown risk, makes the DSP fund the preferred choice even for 2-year horizons. The Edelweiss fund is now best suited only for investors with very high conviction in Chinese equities continuing to outperform.

## 10. Conclusion

The comparison between the Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund and the DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FOF has evolved materially since the prior report in March 2025.

**The DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FOF emerges as the more suitable fund for both 2-year and 5-year investment horizons** for most investors. The key factors driving this recommendation are:

1. **Lower expense ratio:** At 1.08% (Direct Plan, inclusive of all fees), it is significantly cheaper than the Edelweiss fund's 2.32% (Direct Plan)
2. **Lower volatility and drawdown risk:** Standard deviation of 16.9 vs. 21.2, and maximum drawdown of -11.19% vs. ~55-60%
3. **Superior risk-adjusted returns:** 1-year return of +44.0% vs. +36.43%, with a 3-year annualized return of ~29.5%
4. **Diversification benefits:** Global exposure across US, China, and developed Asian markets vs. single-country China concentration
5. **No exit load:** Provides greater flexibility

**The Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** has benefited from the remarkable resurgence of Chinese equities, with the underlying JPMorgan Greater China Fund returning +32.3% in calendar 2025. However, the fund's extreme volatility and single-country concentration make it unsuitable as a core holding. It is best used as a tactical satellite allocation (20-30%) for aggressive investors with high conviction in the Chinese equity story and a 5-year+ horizon.

**The most material changes from the prior report:**
- Chinese equities have rebounded strongly, boosting the Edelweiss fund's returns
- The DSP FOF's expense structure is now clearly more favorable (1.08% inclusive)
- The DSP FOF has added 11.2% KWEB exposure, creating a ~4% overlap with the Edelweiss fund
- The DSP FOF has undergone a name change and management transition
- The Edelweiss fund's expense ratio appears higher than previously reported

Investors should carefully match their risk tolerance, investment horizon, and conviction level to the characteristics of each fund. For most investors building a long-term portfolio, the DSP Global Innovation Overseas Equity Omni FOF should form the core allocation, with the Edelweiss fund reserved for tactical purposes only.

# Comprehensive Research Verification Report: Red Rooster's Lunchtime Promotion

## Executive Summary

This report provides a verified, corrected, and expanded analysis of the chicken chain and lunchtime promotion described in the original research query. The chain is definitively identified as **Red Rooster**, not Chicken Treat, though both chains are now corporate siblings under Craveable Brands. The promotion is officially called the **"Quarter Chicken Lunch"** (also branded as "Quarter Chicken & Chips" or "Value Quarter Chicken Lunch"), a lunchtime value deal that has been offered continuously since at least **1994**. As of May 30, 2026, the promotion is priced at **$5.50** and available daily until 4:00 PM at participating stores.

---

## Section 1: Chain Identity — Red Rooster

### 1.1 Founding History

Red Rooster was founded in **1972** in **Kelmscott, a suburb of Perth, Western Australia**, by **Peter and Theo Kailis** — Greek-Australian brothers. This matches the user's description of "two Greek-Australian brothers starting in a Perth suburb in 1972 with roast chicken and red signage." [1][2][3]

**Corrections to the original query assumptions:**

| Assumption Made | Verified Fact | Source |
|----------------|---------------|--------|
| Brothers named "Peter and George" | Brothers were **Peter and Theo** Kailis. George was their father, a Greek immigrant who opened a fish shop in Perth in 1926. | [1][3] |
| First store in "Balcatta" | First store was in **Kelmscott**, WA. The Balcatta confusion likely stems from Australian Fast Foods being headquartered there. | [1][2] |
| Chain was "Red Rooster" | ✅ Correct — it is Red Rooster | [1][2][3] |

### 1.2 Startup Details

- **Initial investment:** $27,000 from Peter Kailis and nine partners [4][5]
- **First-year performance:** Lost $100,000, nearly failed [4][5]
- **Early menu:** Whole roast chicken sold for $1.95; Peter Kailis developed the **Hawaiian Pack** (quarter roast chicken, chips, and a pineapple and banana fritter) [1][4][5]
- **Expansion:** Grew to 80 stores by 1982 through word-of-mouth without advertising [1][5][6]

### 1.3 Ownership Timeline

- **1972–1982:** Founded and operated by Peter Kailis (bought out partners) [1][4]
- **1982:** Sold to Myer Emporium [1][2][6]
- **1986:** Coles Myer merged Big Rooster into Red Rooster, expanding eastward [1][2][5]
- **2002:** **Australian Fast Foods** (the company that owned Chicken Treat) purchased Red Rooster from Coles Myer [2][7][8]
- **2007:** Sold to Quadrant Private Equity for $180 million [4][7][8]
- **2011:** Archer Capital acquired the company for ~A$450 million [7][9]
- **2019:** **PAG Asia Capital** (Hong Kong-based) acquired Craveable Brands for ~A$500 million [1][2][9]

### 1.4 Current Status

As of 2025–2026, Red Rooster operates approximately **325–360 franchise stores** across Australia, employing over 7,500 staff. It is Australia's sixth-most-popular fast food chain. It remains part of **Craveable Brands Ltd**, alongside Oporto, Chicken Treat, and Chargrill Charlie's. All stores operate as franchises. [1][2][4][9]

## Section 2: Red Rooster vs. Chicken Treat — Clarifying the Relationship

### 2.1 Chicken Treat's Separate Founding

**Chicken Treat was NOT founded by the Kailis family.** This is a critical correction to the user's original assumption.

| Fact | Detail | Source |
|------|--------|--------|
| **Founder** | Frank Romano (not the Kailis family) | [10][11][12] |
| **Year** | 1976 (some sources say 1974, but 1976 is reliably confirmed) | [10][11][12] |
| **Location** | Midland, Western Australia (Shop 3, 11 Great Northern Highway, opposite Midland Town Hall) | [10][11][12] |
| **Cooking method** | Mediterranean-style "Rotisserie Barbeque" method | [10][11] |

Frank Romano started working in the barbecue chicken industry with **Chicken Spot** in 1973, and assisted by his brother Joe, founded Chicken Treat in 1976. He went on to build the business that eventually acquired Red Rooster. [10][11][12]

### 2.2 How They Became Related

The two chains are **corporate siblings, not sibling brands by origin**:

1. **1989:** Chicken Treat allied with **Big Rooster** (founded by Nick Tana in Queensland) to form **Australian Fast Foods Pty Ltd** [7][8][10]
2. **2002:** Australian Fast Foods purchased **Red Rooster** from Coles Myer [2][7][8]
3. **2007:** Sold to Quadrant Private Equity as **Quick Service Restaurant Holdings** [4][7][8]
4. **2017:** Rebranded as **Craveable Brands** [9][10]
5. **2019:** PAG Asia Capital acquired Craveable Brands for ~$500 million [1][2][9]

**Current corporate structure:** Craveable Brands Ltd oversees ~600 franchise stores across Red Rooster (~330 stores), Oporto (~209 stores), Chicken Treat (~61 stores), and Chargrill Charlie's (~19 stores). [9][10]

### 2.3 Sources of Confusion Between the Two Chains

1. **Shared geography:** Both born in Western Australia (Red Rooster in Kelmscott, Chicken Treat in Midland) [1][10]
2. **Shared parent company:** Since 2002, same corporate ownership [7][8]
3. **Shared operations:** Same franchise management, supply chain, and some franchisees operate both brands [9][13]
4. **Similar branding:** Both use prominent red in their signage and logos [User observation]
5. **Similar chicken focus:** Both specialize in chicken, though originally Red Rooster was roast chicken and Chicken Treat was fried/rotisserie — a distinction that has blurred since Red Rooster added fried chicken in 2021 [1][10][14]
6. **The Balcatta link:** Australian Fast Foods was headquartered in **Balcatta**, which may explain why the user thought Red Rooster started there [7][8]

### 2.4 Definitive Answer to the Chain Identity Question

The user's description — **"two Greek-Australian brothers starting in a Perth suburb in 1972 with roast chicken and red signage"** — definitively describes **Red Rooster**, not Chicken Treat. The promotion discussed below is a Red Rooster promotion, though Chicken Treat runs a nearly identical deal under different branding. [1][2][3][4]

## Section 3: The Lunchtime Promotion — Name, History, and Details

### 3.1 Exact Official Name of the Promotion

The promotion is known by several official names, used interchangeably across Red Rooster's marketing:

| Name Used | Where Found | Source |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| **"Quarter Chicken Lunch"** | Official Instagram, Facebook, and inferred from URL structure | [15][16][17] |
| **"Value Quarter Chicken Lunch"** | Official website URL path (`/value-quarter-chicken-lunch`) | [18][19] |
| **"Quarter Chicken & Chips"** | Most common in 2024–2026 social media and marketing | [20][21][22] |
| **"$5.50 Quarter Chicken & Chips"** | Current pricing name used in Facebook/TikTok promotions | [20][21][23] |
| **"Quarter Chicken and small Chips"** | Store-level Facebook promotions | [24] |

**Correction from original report:** The previous report stated the promotion was simply the "Quarter Chicken and Chips" deal. While correct, this fails to capture the full official naming. The URL structure on the Red Rooster website shows the category is branded as **"Value Quarter Chicken Lunch"** under "Lunch Specials", but in social media it is consistently called the **"Quarter Chicken Lunch"** or **"Quarter Chicken & Chips"**. [15][18][19]

**Key distinction:** The "Parm Tuesday Specials" ($5 Parm Burger or $12 Parm Burger Box every Tuesday) is a **separate, different promotion** and should not be confused with the Quarter Chicken Lunch. [25]

### 3.3 Precise Launch Date — Is It From the 1990s or Earlier?

**Conclusion: The specific value-priced lunch promotion format began in the 1990s (confirmed from at least 1994), but the "quarter chicken + chips" concept as a menu item dates back to the 1970s.**

#### Evidence for 1994 Launch

1. **Reddit r/AustralianNostalgia:** A user wrote: *"When I was about 15, Red Rooster launched $2 quarter chicken and chips, available Mondays and Tuesdays. During school holidays, if the..."* The post is titled "Red Rooster coupons from 1994. Mum often pulled these..." [26]

2. **Facebook group "Old Shops Australia":** Posted about *"Retro Red Rooster meal deals from 1994"* showing how cheap meals were, specifically the $2 chicken and chips Tuesday special. [27]

3. **Multiple Facebook user recollections:**
   - *"Who can remember back in the 90's when Red Rooster had $2.00 chicken & chips every Tuesday? We never missed a Tuesday."* [28]
   - *"Yes, I remember chicken and chips for $2 on Tuesday, back in the 90's. Often had it as a treat and it beat cooking."* [33]

4. **1994 TV advertisement:** A YouTube video titled "Red Rooster ad (1994)" promotes: *"Red Roosters red hot deal a quarter chicken crispy chips pineapple banana and Coke for only $425 miss it and you'll see red."* [29]

#### Earlier Origins (1970s–1980s)

The "quarter chicken + chips" combination existed as a menu item long before it became a value promotion:

- The **Hawaiian Pack** was developed by Peter Kailis in the early days of Red Rooster and included a quarter roast chicken, chips, and a pineapple and banana fritter [1][4][5]
- A whole roast chicken was reportedly $1.95 when the chain first opened [1][4]

**However**, the **specific value-priced lunchtime promotion** — a cheap, limited-time deal targeting the lunch rush with a mid-afternoon cut-off — appears to have genuinely begun in the **1990s**, with 1994 being the earliest confirmed year. [26][27][28][33]

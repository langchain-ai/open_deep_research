# Comprehensive Comparison: Heat Pump Dryers for a Heritage-Listed Melbourne Terrace

## Executive Summary

This report provides a detailed and fully updated comparison of heat pump dryers suitable for a Melbourne household in a heritage-listed terrace house with limited ventilation, shared walls, and 6-8 laundry loads per week (312-416 cycles annually). After extensive research across model availability, real-world energy costs, Victorian heritage overlay requirements, noise levels for shared-wall compliance, Australian reliability data, total cost of ownership, and installation requirements, a clear recommendation emerges.

The originally considered models had significant availability issues: the **Bosch Serie 8 WTX88M20AU is discontinued** and the **Samsung DV90T6240LH is a UK/European model not sold in Australia**. This report focuses on their currently available Australian equivalents.

**The LG DVH9-09W ($1,499 street price) is the most suitable choice** for a heritage-listed terrace house, offering the best balance of energy efficiency (9-star, 129 kWh/year), genuine auto-cleaning condenser (minimal maintenance), excellent warranty (10 years on inverter compressor and motor), quiet operation, and a 10-year total cost of ownership of approximately **$4,187**. Its no-external-venting design avoids any heritage overlay permit triggers, and it is the heaviest model at 57-58 kg, providing superior vibration damping for shared walls.

**The Bosch Serie 8 WQB235B8AU ($2,199 RRP)** is the most energy-efficient option (10-star, 61 dB) with excellent anti-vibration design, but its higher purchase price and 2-year standard warranty (upgradable to 5 years through current promotion) result in a higher 10-year total cost of approximately **$4,473**.

**The Samsung DV90T6440LB/SA ($1,407 street price)** offers the lowest purchase price and advanced smart features, but requires manual condenser cleaning every 25-30 cycles (a significant burden at 6-8 loads/week) and its lighter weight (49 kg) makes it more prone to vibration transmission to shared walls. Its 10-year total cost is approximately **$4,514**.

- **Load sizing:** Heat pump dryers operate most efficiently with full-but-not-overstuffed loads. Underloading wastes energy per kilogram dried.

## 3. Heritage Overlay Requirements: Detailed Guidance

### 3.1 How Heritage Overlays Work in Victoria

The Heritage Overlay (Clause 43.01 of the Victoria Planning Provisions) is a planning scheme control applied to areas, precincts, or individual buildings identified as having heritage significance [18]. Under the Heritage Overlay, a planning permit is required for:

- Demolishing or removing a building (or part of a building)
- Constructing a building or constructing or carrying out works
- **Externally altering a building**
- Constructing or displaying a sign
- Externally painting unpainted surfaces (or repainting a different colour)

**Critically, internal alterations generally do NOT require a planning permit under the Heritage Overlay** unless the schedule to the overlay specifically identifies the heritage place as one where internal alteration controls apply [18]. As confirmed by the City of Melbourne's Heritage Owner's Guide FAQ: "Internal controls are rare, so most properties do not need permits for internal works unless specifically identified" [19].

### 3.2 Why Heat Pump Dryers are Ideal for Heritage Properties

A heat pump dryer operates as a **closed-loop system** – it heats air, uses it to absorb moisture from clothes, then cools the air to condense the moisture (which drains into a container or plumbing connection) and reuses the heat. This means:

**No external venting is required.** Unlike a conventional vented dryer that requires a hole cut through an external wall (an external alteration that would trigger heritage permit requirements), a heat pump dryer has no external component and makes no penetration of the building envelope [20][21].

This is the critical distinction:
- **Vented dryer:** Requires an external wall penetration for exhaust → **triggers Heritage Overlay permit requirement** for external alteration
- **Heat pump dryer:** No external wall penetration, no external unit, purely internal installation → **generally no planning permit required**

### 3.3 Which Council's Heritage Guidelines to Consult

Your property's location determines which council's heritage guidelines apply. The three most relevant councils for Melbourne terrace houses are:

**City of Port Phillip:** Their Heritage Design Guidelines (revised August 2022) state: "The aim is not to prevent change, but to ensure that change does not diminish the cultural significance of heritage places over time" [22]. For service units (air conditioners, etc.), the key test is **visibility from the street**: "If your property is located in a Heritage Overlay, the installation of external Service Units will require a Planning Permit if visible from a street or public park" [23]. Since a heat pump dryer has no external component, this trigger is not activated.

Port Phillip offers **free heritage advisory services** for residents to assist with heritage matters [24]. Contact: Port Phillip Council Planning Department.

**City of Yarra:** Yarra's Clause 22.02 (Development Guidelines for Sites Subject to the Heritage Overlay) aims to "preserve the historic fabric, maintain integrity, protect view lines, and encourage appropriate adaptations respecting the heritage significance" [25]. Yarra's Energy Efficiency for Heritage Buildings webpage outlines a three-step process: identifying heritage overlay status and grading, checking permit exemption lists, and confirming with the Yarra Council Planning team [26]. Contact: Yarra City Council Planning Department.

**City of Melbourne:** The City of Melbourne Heritage Owner's Guide confirms: "Internal controls are rare, so most properties do not need permits for internal works unless specifically identified" and "Under the Heritage Overlay you do not need a permit for routine repairs and maintenance that replace 'like for like'" [19]. Contact: City of Melbourne Planning and Building Department.

### 3.4 Step-by-Step Compliance Checklist

1. **Check your property's Heritage Overlay schedule** – Look up your property on your council's planning scheme online (find the HO number, e.g., HO1, HO2). Check whether "internal alteration controls" apply – this is rare but must be confirmed [18].

2. **Confirm no external modification** – Document that the heat pump dryer requires no external vent, no external unit, and no structural modification to the building envelope. This is the key fact that distinguishes it from a vented dryer [20][21].

3. **Contact council for confirmation** – Even if a permit is not strictly required, best practice is to contact your council's planning or heritage advisor (free in Port Phillip) to confirm. As Port Phillip advises: "Consult with Council prior to purchase and installation to ensure unit locations and noise impacts are acceptable" [23].

4. **Document everything** – Take photos of the installation area before installation, keep records of the model specifications confirming "no external venting required," and document that no external fabric was modified. This protects you if questions arise later.

5. **Use the condensation tank or connect to existing drain** – Using the internal condensation tank (manual emptying) requires no plumbing work at all. If connecting the drain hose to an existing standpipe or laundry tub, this is comparable to "connecting washing machine hoses," which is considered a legal DIY task in Victoria [27].

### 3.5 What Would Require a Permit

- Cutting a vent hole through an external wall → requires planning permit under Heritage Overlay
- Installing any external unit visible from the street → requires planning permit
- Any work affecting the external fabric of the building → requires planning permit

**None of these apply to a heat pump dryer installed internally with no external components.**

### 3.6 Building Permit and Plumbing Considerations

A **building permit** may be required for electrical work (e.g., running a new circuit), handled through the Victorian Building Authority and licensed electrical contractors – separate from heritage planning controls.

If connecting the condensate drain hose to the building's plumbing, a **licensed plumber** is required for any permanent connection to the sanitary drainage system (cutting pipes, installing tundishes, modifying fixtures). Simply clipping the hose into an existing standpipe is considered DIY. Unlicensed plumbing work can incur fines of up to **$32,000** under the Plumbing Regulations 2018 [27].

The **Samsung models are significantly lighter** (49-52.5 kg), which may result in greater vibration transmission to shared walls.

### 4.3 Practical Vibration Control Recommendations

**Anti-vibration pads:** A simple and effective strategy. Universal dryer anti-vibration pads (4-pack) are available in Australia from $18.20 [31]. These absorb vibration, reduce noise, and prevent the dryer from "walking" across the floor. A YouTube test showed "a reduction of about one decibel in the laundry room and a two-decibel reduction in the basement" when using anti-vibration pads on a washing machine [32].

**Proper levelling:** Appliances must be levelled using their adjustable feet. The trick to minimizing vibration is to level the appliance in its **final position** (not before sliding it in), use a spirit level on top, and ensure all four feet make firm contact with the floor [33].

**Avoid corners and recesses:** These act as resonant boxes that amplify sound. Place the dryer with at least 50 mm clearance on all sides and do not push it directly against a shared wall if possible [34].

**Sound-absorbing materials:** For maximum noise reduction, install a sound-absorbing mat under the dryer. Dense materials like **mass-loaded vinyl** with open-cell foam can achieve up to 32 dB noise reduction at 6 feet [35]. Acoustic foam panels or composite sound barrier panels are also effective.

**Stacking considerations:** If stacking the dryer on a washing machine, use the manufacturer's official stacking kit. The Bosch stacking kit (WTZ27500) requires drilling into the washing machine for secure stacking [36]. LG offers the STKIT-WH (white) stacking kit for the DVH9-09W [37]. Improper stacking can increase vibration.

**Scheduling:** For a terrace house with shared walls, schedule drying during daytime hours when neighbours are less likely to be disturbed by noise.

### 4.4 Australian Consumer Law and Noise Issues

If a purchased dryer produces excessive noise that "disrupts normal daily living and restricts use," this may constitute a breach of the Australian Consumer Law regarding acceptable quality [38]. Some consumers have pursued "formal complaints to Consumer Protection WA and the ACCC" regarding excessively noisy dryers that did not match advertised claims [39]. This is particularly relevant for Samsung models where some users report: "Both purchased units have been excessively loud since new, to the extent that they disrupt normal daily living" [40].

### 5.4 Australian Consumer Law Protections

Under the Australian Consumer Law (ACL), consumers get automatic "consumer guarantees" requiring goods to be of acceptable quality, fit for purpose, and free from hidden defects. There is no fixed time limit – rights last as long as reasonable based on the product type, price, and expected lifespan [51].

A "2-year warranty" is a voluntary promise by the seller or manufacturer to fix defects within that time. It does not replace statutory rights under the ACL. For major failures, consumers can choose a refund or replacement, and remedies still apply beyond the stated warranty period [51].

**CHOICE guidance:** "A clothes dryer should last you around 10 years" [52]. There is precedent for Bosch being required to cover repairs under ACL beyond the warranty period – a CHOICE community member reported that Bosch agreed to cover a repair on a Bosch dishwasher purchased in June 2016 (repair needed in 2021) after being confronted with the legal expectation of reasonable product lifespan [53].

### 5.6 Common Failure Points for Heat Pump Dryers

Based on Australian repair service data and user reports [44][50]:

1. **Condenser/Lint Buildup** – The most common issue. Blocked condensers cause extended drying times and eventual failure. Samsung models without self-cleaning condensers are most susceptible.
2. **Moisture Sensors** – Dirty or failed sensors lead to inconsistent drying. LG models have received some criticism for sensor performance.
3. **Compressor Failure** – The most expensive repair ($500-$1,000+). Heat pump dryers use sealed refrigerant systems that are costly to repair when they fail.
4. **Drum Bearings and Belts** – Worn bearings cause noise and vibration. Lighter models may be more prone to bearing wear over time.
5. **Control Board Failures** – Electronic control boards can fail, particularly in models with complex smart features.

### 5.7 Expected Lifespan

### 7.2 Required Clearances

Based on manufacturer guidance and installation best practices [62]:

| Position | Minimum Clearance | Notes |
|----------|-------------------|-------|
| **Sides** | **50 mm (5 cm)** each side | For airflow and vibration clearance |
| **Top** | **50 mm (5 cm)** | For heat dissipation; 200 mm for LG per some guides |
| **Rear** | **50-100 mm (5-10 cm)** | For hose connections and airflow |
| **Door opening** | Allow full door swing clearance | Typically 90-100 cm from front of machine |

**Total recommended space allowance (minimum):**
- Width: Appliance width + 100 mm (50 mm each side)
- Depth: Appliance depth + 150 mm (for hoses and connections)
- Height: Appliance height + 50 mm above

**Critical note for heat pump dryers:** Unlike vented dryers, heat pump dryers do not require external venting. However, they still need adequate clearance for the heat exchanger to draw in ambient air and exhaust warm air. Insufficient clearance can cause overheating and reduced efficiency. If installing in a confined space (e.g., under a bench), ensure the space has some air circulation [62].

### 7.3 Electrical Requirements

All models under consideration have the following electrical specifications:

| Specification | Requirement |
|---------------|-------------|
| **Voltage** | 220-240 V, 50 Hz |
| **Power consumption** | ~500-600 W (heat pump) |
| **Plug type** | Standard Australian 10A 3-pin plug |
| **Fuse/Circuit breaker** | 10 A minimum |
| **Dedicated circuit** | Not required, but recommended |

**Detailed analysis:**
- A standard Australian 10A power point is rated at **2,400 W maximum** continuous load (240V × 10A) [63]
- A heat pump dryer draws approximately 500-600 W during operation
- A washing machine with a heating element can draw 2,000-2,400 W when heating water
- **If sharing a double power point** with a washing machine, running both simultaneously could exceed the 10A limit when the washing machine is heating water

**Practical recommendations:**
- A dedicated circuit is **not required** for the dryer alone (600W is well within a 10A circuit capacity)
- However, if the dryer and washing machine share a power point, avoid running both on high-power cycles simultaneously
- Best practice: Have the washing machine and dryer on separate circuits, especially in a high-usage household
- Bosch's installation manual states: "Do not use extension cables or multiple socket strips. Only use adapters and power cords approved by the manufacturer" [64]

### 7.4 Drain Hose Connection and Plumbing Requirements

**Option 1: Use the condensation tank (no plumbing required)**
All models come with an internal condensation tank that collects the water extracted from clothes. This tank can be manually emptied after each cycle. This option requires **no plumbing work at all** – simply set up the dryer and empty the tank every 1-2 cycles depending on load size.

**Option 2: Connect the drain hose to an existing standpipe (DIY)**
All models come with a drain hose that can be connected to an existing laundry tub, standpipe, or floor waste. This is comparable to "connecting washing machine hoses," which is listed as a legal DIY task in Victoria [27]. Simply clip the hose into the standpipe – no plumbing license required.

**Option 3: Permanent connection to plumbing (licensed plumber required)**
If you need to cut into existing plumbing or install a new drain connection point, this constitutes regulated plumbing work that requires a licensed plumber. The Victorian Building Authority regulates all plumbing work under the Plumbing Regulations 2018 [27]. Unlicensed plumbing work can incur fines of up to **$32,000**.

**Recommendation:** For a terrace house with an existing laundry tub or standpipe, use Option 2 (connect the drain hose to the existing plumbing) – no plumber required. For a new-build or renovation, consult a licensed plumber for permanent connection.

### 7.5 Stacking Compatibility

If space is limited, the dryer can be stacked on top of a compatible front-loading washing machine using the manufacturer's stacking kit:

| Model | Compatible Stacking Kit | Notes |

| LG DVH9-09W | **STKIT-WH** (white) or **STKIT-BK** (black) | Compatible with LG front load washers [37] |
| Samsung DV90T6440LB/SA | **SKK-UUW** (white), **SKK-ALV** (black) | Check compatibility with specific washer model [65] |

**Installation notes:**
- Stacking kits typically require two people for installation
- The Bosch kit includes a drill bit for securing to the washing machine
- LG stacking kits are described as "Installation is tricky and often requires multiple people" [37]
- If stacking, ensure the total height fits within your available space

### 7.6 Installation Checklist for Terrace Houses

- [ ] Measure the installation space (width, depth, height with clearances)
- [ ] Verify the door swing clearance (can the door open fully?)
- [ ] Confirm a standard 10A power point is within reach (1.5 m cord length typical)
- [ ] Decide on drain option: condensation tank (no plumbing), standpipe (DIY), or permanent (plumber)
- [ ] Check floor levelness (terrace houses often have uneven floors)
- [ ] If stacking, purchase the correct manufacturer stacking kit
- [ ] If sharing a power point with a washing machine, confirm circuit capacity
- [ ] Purchase anti-vibration pads for noise reduction to shared walls
- [ ] Locate the dryer away from shared walls when possible
- [ ] Consult with your council's heritage planning department (recommended but not required)

### 8.2 First Choice: LG DVH9-09W

**Why it's the best choice for a heritage-listed Melbourne terrace house:**

### 8.5 Final Recommendation

**For a Melbourne household doing 6-8 loads weekly in a heritage-listed terrace with limited ventilation and shared walls, the LG DVH9-09W is the clear first choice.** It offers the best overall value proposition: a competitive purchase price ($1,499), the lowest 10-year total cost (~$4,187), genuine auto-cleaning condenser (critical for high usage), the best warranty (10 years on compressor and motor), excellent vibration control (heaviest model at 57-58 kg), and no heritage overlay permit issues (no external venting required).

**If budget allows, the Bosch WQB235B8AU** is an excellent alternative that prioritizes energy efficiency and quiet operation, with the caveat of a higher initial investment and smaller 8 kg capacity.

**The Samsung DV90T6440LB/SA** is not recommended for this specific use case due to its manual condenser cleaning requirement (a significant burden at 6-8 loads/week) and higher total cost of ownership.

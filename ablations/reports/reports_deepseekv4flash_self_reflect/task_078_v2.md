# 9-Day Thailand Itinerary for 7 Travelers from Hyderabad (Revised & Updated for May 2026)

## Executive Summary

This report provides a comprehensive, updated 9-day Thailand itinerary for a group of 7 travelers from Hyderabad, India, with a total budget of ₹70,000 per person (₹18,000 for flights, ₹52,000 for on-ground expenses). Based on verified 2026 pricing and logistics, the original report has been significantly revised.

**Critical Update:** As of May 19, 2026, the Thai Cabinet has revoked the 60-day visa-free entry for Indian nationals. Indian passport holders now require a **Visa on Arrival (VOA)**, which costs **2,000 THB (~₹5,800)** and is valid for a maximum stay of **15 days**. This represents a significant new cost not included in the original budget.

**Budget Verdict:** The ₹52,000 on-ground budget is **no longer sufficient**. The realistic on-ground cost per person, accounting for verified prices and the new VOA fee, is estimated between **₹57,000 and ₹64,000**, making the total trip cost approximately **₹79,000 to ₹96,000 per person**.

**Route Recommendation:** The original route (Hyderabad→Bangkok→Kanchanaburi→Bangkok→Chiang Mai→Pai→Chiang Mai→Bangkok→Hyderabad) involves significant backtracking. The **optimal route is an open-jaw itinerary**: **Fly into Bangkok (BKK) and out of Chiang Mai (CNX)**. This eliminates the final flight from Chiang Mai back to Bangkok and reduces travel time. However, direct flights to Chiang Mai from Hyderabad are expensive, so the most practical option remains flying into Bangkok and taking an overnight train or budget flight north, then flying directly back to Hyderabad from Chiang Mai via a connecting flight through Bangkok.

## 1. Updated Pricing & Critical Policy Changes (May 2026)

### Visa on Arrival (VOA) for Indian Passport Holders

- **Status:** India has been moved from a 60-day visa-free regime to **Visa on Arrival (VOA) status** due to a Cabinet decision on May 19, 2026 [1].
- **Fee:** The VOA fee is **2,000 THB** (approximately ₹5,800 per person) [1].
- **Validity:** The VOA allows a maximum stay of **15 days**, which is sufficient for this 9-day itinerary. Extensions are not permitted [1].
- **Requirements:** Passport valid for at least 6 months, confirmed return/onward ticket, proof of accommodation, and proof of sufficient funds (10,000 THB per person or 20,000 THB per family) [2].
- **TDAC (Thailand Digital Arrival Card):** This is mandatory for all foreign nationals. It must be completed online (**free of charge**) within 72 hours before arrival. A QR code is provided for expedited entry [3].
- **Application:** The VOA can be obtained at major airports (Suvarnabhumi, Don Mueang, Phuket, Chiang Mai) or applied for online in advance via the official eVOA portal to save time [1].

### Thailand Tourist Fee (300 THB)

- **Status:** **Not yet implemented.** An official start date is pending. Travelers should be aware of this potential fee but do not need to include it in their budget for this trip [4].

### Exchange Rate

- The current exchange rate is approximately **1 THB = ₹2.92 (1 INR = 0.342 THB)** [5]. All calculations in this report use this rate.

### SIM Cards

- **Physical SIM:** Available at airport kiosks for **299–599 THB** for 7-14 day packages [6].
- **eSIM:** A convenient alternative from providers like Airalo (starting from $6 for 3 GB) or SimOptions (starting from $10 for unlimited data for 10 days) [6].

## 2. Optimal Route & Logistics Feasibility

The original route is logistically inefficient due to significant backtracking. The recommended revision below minimizes travel time and maximizes time spent at destinations.

**Recommended Revised Route (Open-Jaw):**

1.  **Day 1-2:** Fly Hyderabad (HYD) → **Bangkok (BKK)** .
2.  **Day 3-4:** Travel to **Kanchanaburi** for the rural nature/history experience.
3.  **Day 5:** Travel back to Bangkok. In the evening, take the **overnight sleeper train** to **Chiang Mai**.
4.  **Day 6-8:** Explore **Chiang Mai** and **Pai**.
5.  **Day 9:** Fly from Chiang Mai (CNX) to Hyderabad (HYD). *Note: This is a connecting flight, as there are no direct flights from Chiang Mai to Hyderabad. You will connect through Bangkok or another hub.* [7]

**Why This is Optimal:**
- **Eliminates Backtracking:** By flying out of Chiang Mai, you avoid a final flight back to Bangkok, saving both time and money (approx. ₹5,000 on a domestic flight).
- **Saves Accommodation:** The overnight train (Bangkok→Chiang Mai) acts as both transport and accommodation for one night.
- **Open-Jaw Flights:** While cheaper to pre-plan, booking separate one-way tickets (HYD→BKK and CNX→HYD) can sometimes be competitive if booked in advance.

## 5. Alternative Destinations Assessment

### Can Khao Yai Replace Kanchanaburi?

- **Verdict:** Yes, but for a different experience.
- **Khao Yai**: Closer to Bangkok (~137km). Entry fee is **400 THB**. Excellent for broad biodiversity, grasslands, and structured lodge accommodations that fit a group of 7. Offers a more "wilderness" feel [26].
- **Kanchanaburi**: Slightly farther (~130km). Entry fee to Erawan is **300 THB**. Unique for its mix of the stunning 7-tier Erawan Waterfall and profound WWII history (Death Railway, Hellfire Pass). **Better for a more culturally diverse rural experience** [27].

**Recommendation:** Stick with **Kanchanaburi** for this itinerary as it combines nature (Erawan) with history (River Kwai), fulfilling the "rural" requirement more completely.

### Can Ayutthaya be Incorporated?

- **Verdict:** **Yes, as an excellent day trip from Bangkok.**
- **Cost:** Total day trip cost is incredibly budget-friendly at **~$15 USD** per person for train (15 THB), bike rental, and temple entries.
- **Feasibility:** A train from Bangkok’s Krung Thep Aphiwat Central Terminal takes 1.5 hours. It is a perfect half-day or full-day addition [28].

### Can Sukhothai be Incorporated?

- **Verdict:** **Yes, as a stopover between Bangkok and Chiang Mai.**
- **Travel:** It is much closer to Chiang Mai (~150km, 4-5.5 hrs by minivan) than to Bangkok (~369km, 7-10 hrs).
- **Feasibility:** It requires an extra 1-2 days, which would shorten time in Chiang Mai. It is best suited for history enthusiasts wanting a UNESCO World Heritage site experience [29].

### Can Chiang Rai be Added?

- **Verdict:** **Yes, as a day trip from or extension of Chiang Mai.**
- **Travel:** Minivans from Chiang Mai take 3-4 hours (390-650 THB). Round-trip travel time is 6-7 hours, making it a very long day trip. An overnight stay is recommended.
- **Cost:** White Temple entry is **200 THB**; Blue Temple is **Free**. Other highlights are budget-friendly [30].

**Final Verdict on Alternatives:** For a 9-day trip, **Ayutthaya** is the easiest to add as a day trip. **Sukhothai** and **Chiang Rai** are better left for a longer, more focused trip.

## 9. Practical Updates (2026)

- **Visa:** **Visa on Arrival (VOA)** for Indian passport holders. Cost: **2,000 THB**. Valid for **15 days**.
- **TDAC:** **Mandatory.** Submit online (free) at https://tdac.immigration.go.th/ within 72 hours of arrival.
- **Passport Validity:** Must be valid for at least **6 months** beyond your arrival date.
- **Proof of Funds:** Be prepared to show **10,000 THB per person** or **20,000 THB per family** in cash or bank statement.
- **SIM Cards:** AIS has the best coverage in rural areas. Buy a **tourist SIM (299-599 THB)** at the airport or 7-Eleven.
- **Vaping:** **Strictly prohibited** in Thailand. Possession can lead to fines or detention.
- **Travel Insurance:** **Highly recommended** due to high healthcare costs. Indian providers like ACKO or Tata AIG offer plans starting from ₹400 for 9 days [37].

## 10. Final Recommendations

1.  **Adopt the Open-Jaw Route:** Fly into Bangkok and out of Chiang Mai to save time and money on backtracking.
2.  **Budget an Extra ₹12,000:** The new VOA fee and general price increases make the ₹52,000 on-ground budget unrealistic. Prepare for a total of **₹82,000 – ₹87,000 per person**.
3.  **Book the Overnight Train:** This is the single best value move for your trip. It saves money on transport and accommodation.
4.  **Pre-Apply for eVOA:** Applying online for the eVOA in advance will save you from queuing for hours at the airport upon arrival.
5.  **Book Accommodation Early:** Especially the Lub d in Bangkok and the overnight train, as they are popular and sell out.
6.  **Add Ayutthaya:** A cheap and easy day trip from Bangkok that adds significant cultural value without a major time or budget commitment.
7.  **Prioritize one "Big" Activity:** Your group will likely be happiest if you choose one major, shared experience, like the full-day ethical elephant sanctuary in Chiang Mai, rather than trying to do everything.

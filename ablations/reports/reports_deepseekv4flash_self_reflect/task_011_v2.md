**7 Ways to Get Paid:**

1. **Personal / Retail Commissions**: Earned when an affiliate sells Novae products or services directly. Examples include up to $100 per referral for credit education and up to $500 per customer enrolled depending on the financial product.

2. **Customer Acquisition Bonuses (CABs)**: Paid to the sponsor and eligible up-line Affiliate Pros when a new Affiliate generates 2 Qualifying Customer Activities in their first 60 days. Commissions up to $400 per referral of new affiliates.

3. **Team Override Commissions**: Available exclusively to Affiliate Pro members, providing multi-generational income through up to ten generations. Payouts vary by product and generation (e.g., $3 for generations 1-5 and $2 for generations 6-10).

4. **Monthly Residual Income**: Paid on the 15th day of each month to affiliates who achieve certain ranks based on the previous month's sales volume.

5. **Rank Advancement Commissions**: Ranks range from Level 1 to Level 16 (or Affiliate through President Ambassador & Managing Partner).

6. **Bonuses**: Car bonuses and mortgage bonuses for top performers, plus rank achievement bonuses.

7. **Company Stock**: Novae promises "company stock" to top promoters, though it is not publicly traded, registered, or discloses valuation.

#### Assessment Under FTC Standards

The FTC's Endorsement Guides require that disclosures must be **"clear and conspicuous,"** prominently placed near endorsements, and understandable to typical consumers. [FTC - Endorsement Guides](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/ftcs-endorsement-guides-what-people-are-asking)

**Key FTC Principles:**
- Disclosures must be **prominent, understandable, and placed near endorsements or links**
- Simply labeling a link as **"Affiliate Link" is inadequate** because consumers might not understand the meaning
- Both affiliates and companies are responsible for ensuring proper disclosure
- The FTC's 2023 Endorsement Guide updates expanded disclosure obligations to cover bloggers, affiliates, employees, agency partners, and anyone earning commission-based referrals
- Platform tools alone (e.g., Instagram's "paid partnership" tag) **may be insufficient** — affiliates should add their own plain-English disclosure

**Assessment:**
Mesa Group Consulting demonstrates **moderate compliance** with FTC guidelines. The company has disclosure language on **multiple pages** of its website, including the homepage, disclaimer, for-consumers, for-businesses, resources, contact, and terms pages. The disclaimers explicitly name affiliate partners (SmartCredit, SuperMoney, Novae) and state that compensation may be received.

**Potential Gaps:**
- The language is often buried in page footers rather than prominently displayed near specific affiliate links
- Social media posts from @creditsherbie and @evertjafet do **not consistently include affiliate disclosures** (the Facebook posts about credit repair do not include disclosure language)
- YouTube videos (e.g., the ConsumerDirect office tour) do not include on-screen or in-description affiliate disclosure
- The company references a "dedicated Affiliate Disclosure page" but this page was **not found** at the expected URL

### 4.3 2026 FCRA Updates and Industry Impact

The Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) underwent its most significant modernization in over a decade with the 2026 regulatory updates, profoundly affecting credit repair processes. Key changes include:

- **Faster bureau response times:** Mandatory 10-day preliminary investigations for high-risk errors
- **Stricter verification standards:** "The updated FCRA changes require documentation from the furnisher and evidence of accuracy, not just automated confirmation"
- **Burden of proof shift:** "If furnishers cannot provide solid proof of accuracy, the bureau must delete or correct the item"
- **Expanded identity theft protections:** Requiring quicker fraud block processing and mandatory deletion of fraudulent accounts
- **Broader validation requirements:** For furnished data such as account ownership and charge-off amounts
- **Generic disputes no longer work:** "Generic dispute templates no longer work; disputes must include exact incorrect data, clear supporting facts, and documentation"
- **No resetting the reporting clock:** "Disputes no longer reset the reporting clock; the original delinquency date must remain unchanged"

[The New Rules of Credit Repair After the 2026 FCRA Updates - Dispute Beast](https://disputebeast.com/blog/new-rules-credit-repair-2026-fcra-updates)

These updates significantly impact credit repair businesses like Mesa Group Consulting and the platforms they promote.

## 6. Strategic Analysis

### 6.1 The Affiliate Monetization Strategy

Evert Calderon's affiliate strategy demonstrates a sophisticated understanding of the **financial services customer lifecycle**. The Miro board planning document reveals a **two-pronged affiliate monetization strategy**:

1. **SuperMoney (Consumer Side):** Traffic generation through credit card comparisons, personal loan applications, auto loan refinancing, student loan refinancing, and debt consolidation. This targets consumers actively seeking financial products.

2. **Novae (Business + Protection Side):** Monetization through debt relief, business credit, business funding, trust/will planning, and life insurance. This targets business owners and consumers needing more comprehensive financial solutions.

### 6.2 The Cross-Selling Funnel

The natural funnel works as follows:
1. **Awareness:** A consumer visits Mesa Group Consulting's website seeking credit repair or a personal loan (via SuperMoney)
2. **Engagement:** Mesa Group's credit repair services improve the consumer's credit score
3. **Monetization:** The same client is upsold to business funding, debt relief, or life insurance (via Novae)
4. **Recurring Revenue:** Residual commissions from Novae's monthly subscription products and SmartCredit monitoring
5. **Scalability:** Training other affiliates through SmartCredit partnership creates a multiplier effect

This strategy creates **lifetime client value** — a topic Evert specifically teaches in his SmartCredit training series ("Beyond the First Sale: Maximizing Lifetime Client Value").

### 6.3 Risk Factors

1. **Novae's MLM Structure Risk:** Novae's average participant earned only $1,408 in 2023. The DSSRC investigation found Novae affiliates making misleading earnings claims. The FTC's April 2026 actions against individual MLM participants signal increased enforcement.

2. **Unregistered Stock Offering:** Novae's offer of "company stock" to top promoters is concerning given the company is not registered with the SEC.

3. **FTC Compliance Gaps:** While Mesa Group Consulting has disclosure language on its website, social media posts from Evert Calderon's accounts do not consistently include affiliate disclosures.

4. **Credit Repair Industry Scrutiny:** Recent FTC actions against credit repair companies (Financial Education Services — $12 million settlement in 2024; $50 million penalty against another company) indicate the industry is under heightened regulatory scrutiny.

5. **No Income Data Transparency:** Evert Calderon does not publicly disclose his affiliate earnings, making it impossible to verify the success of his business model.

# Identification and History of a Lunchtime Promotion by a Perth-Based Chicken Chain Founded by Greek-Australian Brothers

## Overview of the Chicken Chain

The chicken restaurant chain in question is **Red Rooster**, a well-known Australian fast-food franchise specializing in roast chicken and characterized by its distinctive red signage. It was founded in **1972** by two Greek-Australian brothers, **Peter and Theo Kailis**, in **Kelmscott**, a suburb of Perth, Western Australia.

From its humble beginnings as a single store, Red Rooster expanded rapidly within Western Australia and Victoria. By 1981, the chain operated around 45 stores before being acquired by the Myer Emporium. Further growth occurred through the acquisition and rebranding of the eastern states’ Big Rooster chain in 1986, making the brand nationally recognized. Ownership has changed multiple times, with Red Rooster currently under the umbrella of Craveable Brands, a subsidiary of PAG Asia Capital. Today, the chain boasts over 300 stores across Australia, many franchised and employing thousands.

Red Rooster is primarily known for:
- Signature **roast chicken** cooked with a proprietary blend of herbs and spices, slow-roasted to a golden, crispy finish.
- A menu featuring roast chicken, chips, burgers, spicy wings, and occasionally fried chicken.
- Strong red branding visible in store signage and marketing.
- Focus on family-friendly, affordable meal options that emphasize freshness and quality.

The brand’s history and ongoing evolution are extensively documented on official and authoritative sources such as [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Rooster), [Franchise Business](https://franchisebusiness.com.au/how-red-rooster-took-flight/), and the [Australian Food Timeline](https://australianfoodtimeline.com.au/red-rooster-founded/).

## The Lunchtime Promotion: Name and Details

The lunchtime promotion sought is most commonly recognized as the **“Chicken & Chips Lunch Special”**, historically and colloquially referred to in community circles as the **“$2 Tuesday Chicken & Chips” deal**.

### Historical Context

- The promotion has been available since **at least the 1990s**, gaining local popularity as a budget-friendly lunchtime meal.
- Originally, the deal featured a **quarter portion of roast chicken paired with a serving of chips (fries)** for approximately **two Australian dollars**.
- This special was typically available on **Tuesdays** and only **until mid-afternoon**, catering to lunchtime customers seeking a quick, inexpensive, and hearty meal.
- Community memory and social media posts highlight this deal as a nostalgic staple of Red Rooster’s 1990s marketing and value offering, often evoking fond memories of affordable lunch options during that period.

### Current Offerings

- Presently, Red Rooster continues the tradition of a chicken-and-chips lunchtime deal, now priced around **$5.50 AUD** for a **Quarter Chicken and Chips meal**, sometimes offered with optional drinks at an additional cost.
- The deal is available **until approximately 4 pm**, maintaining the mid-afternoon cut-off time consistent with historical practices.
- It is marketed descriptively as a **“Quarter Chicken and Chips Lunch Special”**, without a distinctive formal promotional name or branding beyond this.
- This offer is available **in-store and via click & collect services** but generally **not available for delivery** or at stadiums and airports.
- The continuation of this deal speaks to Red Rooster’s focus on affordability, convenience, and maintaining loyal lunchtime customers over decades.

Red Rooster’s official website lists this value meal among its current offers, confirming its ongoing popularity and availability under [Red Rooster Value Offers](https://www.redrooster.com.au/value/).

## Notable Features of the Promotion

- **Affordability:** The initial $2 price point in the 1990s made it an iconic budget meal, a value proposition maintained today despite price increases due to inflation and market conditions.
- **Simplicity:** The meal consists simply of a quarter portion of roast chicken and a serve of chips. This straightforward combo highlights the chain’s core product – the roast chicken.
- **Time-limited Availability:** The lunchtime deal is strictly available only during lunch hours (till mid-afternoon), encouraging daytime visits and effectively controlling demand.
- **Community Connection:** The deal has strong nostalgic and cultural significance, especially in Western Australia, linking current customers to decades of tradition.
- **Menu Tradition:** The promotion has endured alongside Red Rooster’s evolving menu, which has incorporated new products but retained core items like roast chicken and chips.
- **Quality:** Red Rooster emphasizes Australian-sourced chicken, slow-roasted for quality and health appeal, enhancing the perceived value of these deals.

## Summary

- The chicken chain founded by two Greek-Australian brothers in a Perth suburb in 1972 is **Red Rooster**.
- The lunchtime promotion, available since at least the 1990s, is commonly known as the **“$2 Tuesday Chicken & Chips” deal** historically and now as the **“Quarter Chicken and Chips Lunch Special”**.
- This promotion offers a portion of roast chicken plus chips for a modest price and is available only until mid-afternoon.
- It reflects Red Rooster’s history of providing affordable, quality roast chicken meals with a strong community and cultural connection.
- The promotion’s simplicity, longevity, and affordability have made it a popular and nostalgic lunchtime choice for customers, particularly in Western Australia.

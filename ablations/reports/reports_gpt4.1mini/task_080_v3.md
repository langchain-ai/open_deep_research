## 5. Investment Recommendations & Core-Satellite Allocation Framework  

### 5.1 Core-Satellite Allocation Model

- **Core Holding:**  
  - A broad global equity fund/ETF such as MSCI ACWI or total market tracker forming 50%-75% of the international equity allocation, providing diversified and stable foundation.  

- **Satellite 1: Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund**  
  - Recommended allocation: 10%-20% of international equities.  
  - Purpose: Targeted exposure to Greater China’s equity markets, especially financial and technology sectors.  
  - Suited for investors with higher risk appetite, who believe in China's medium-term economic revival and reduced regulatory risk.  

- **Satellite 2: DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF**  
  - Recommended allocation: 15%-30% of international equities.  
  - Purpose: Thematic exposure to secular global innovation trends (AI, fintech, biotech, semiconductors) across multiple geographies.  
  - Suitable for investors seeking high-growth innovation themes with some geographic diversification outside China.

| Factor                 | Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund            | DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF           |
|------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------|---------------------------------------------------|
| **Risk Profile**       | Very High; sector and regional concentration risks      | Very High; sectoral concentration risks offset by geographic diversification |
| **Investment Horizon** | Best suited for 5+ years; volatile for short term       | Suitable for 3-5+ years; relatively smoother returns over 3 years |
| **Liquidity Needs**    | Moderate; 1% exit load if redeemed within 90 days       | High; no exit load, daily liquidity                 |
| **Market Outlook**     | Favorable if China economic reforms stabilize, regulatory environment improves | Beneficial if global innovation trends sustain momentum amid macro uncertainties |
| **Investor Profile**   | Regional growth seekers with appetite for China exposure and high volatility | Theme-focused growth investors accepting global tech sector risks |

### 5.3 Conditions Making Each Fund Appropriate

- **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** is appropriate when:  
  - Investor is bullish on China’s reopening and recovery from regulatory crackdowns.  
  - Willing to accept periods of heightened volatility and geopolitical risks.  
  - Looking for regional concentration with core exposure elsewhere to balance risk.  
  - Investment horizon is medium to long term (preferably ≥5 years).  

- **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF** is appropriate when:  
  - Investor seeks diversified exposure to global innovation mega-trends such as AI, fintech, biotech, and semiconductor industries.  
  - Prepared for elevated volatility due to sector concentration but mitigated by global geographic diversification.  
  - Longer investment horizon of 3 to 5+ years to ride innovation adoption waves.  
  - Prefers no exit load and greater liquidity flexibility.

## 6. Market and Regulatory Context (December 2024)

- **Greater China Macro & Regulatory Environment:**  
  - China’s GDP growth slowed to ~4.9%, with a fragile recovery post-COVID including weak domestic demand and sluggish consumption growth.  
  - Property market contraction and government debt restructuring have introduced market uncertainty.  
  - Regulatory environment showing signs of stabilization and moderate easing compared to earlier crackdowns (2018-2022).  
  - Ongoing geopolitical tensions and US-China trade technology dynamics add risk.  

- **Global Innovation Sector Trends:**  
  - Strong secular growth trends in AI, biotechnology, semiconductor manufacturing, fintech, and electric vehicles driving global innovation funds.  
  - Stable or improving investor appetite for technological innovation sectors despite macroeconomic volatility.  
  - Regulatory environments in US and Europe remain supportive of innovation-led growth.  

- **Implication:**  
  - Edelweiss faces higher regulatory and geopolitical risk, emphasizing selectivity and careful monitoring.  
  - DSP fund benefits from diversified innovation exposure, less reliant on a single region, but sector risks and valuation concerns exist.

## Conclusion  

As of December 2024, the **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** and **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds** occupy complementary roles within an international equity portfolio. Edelweiss offers concentrated regional exposure to Greater China equities, with elevated risk-reward due to regulatory and geopolitical factors. DSP Global Innovation FoF provides diversified multi-geography exposure to fast-growing innovation sectors with higher risk-adjusted returns demonstrated over its short history.

For **investors with a 2-year horizon**, both funds are relatively risky; however, DSP’s broader innovation diversification provides better liquidity and potentially lower short-term volatility. Edelweiss is less suited for shorter horizons due to its sector concentration and exit load.

For **investors with a 5-year or longer horizon**, DSP’s thematic innovation exposure stands out as a compelling core or satellite holding, while Edelweiss can serve as a satellite for tactical exposure to the Greater China region and potential upside from regional recovery.

A **core-satellite portfolio** blending a global equity core (such as MSCI ACWI index funds), a 10-20% satellite position in Edelweiss for China recovery exposure, and a 15-30% satellite in DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF for secular innovation growth aligns risk, liquidity, and growth goals effectively.

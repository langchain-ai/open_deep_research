# Comprehensive Report on Admission Requirements for Université Paris-Saclay Master’s in Economics (2025-2026) and France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship

---

## 1. Admission Requirements and Eligibility Criteria for the Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay (2025-2026)

### 1.1 Program Overview  
The Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay is a prestigious, research-oriented two-year program (Master 1 + Master 2) fully taught in English. It aims to prepare students for doctoral studies, research careers, and high-level professional roles in economics and related fields. The curriculum encompasses advanced training in microeconomics, macroeconomics, econometrics, quantitative methods, and applied economics.

- **Program Length:** 2 years (M1 + M2)  
- **Languages of Instruction:** Fully in English  
- **Specializations in M2:** Trade, Geography and Development Economics; Public and Behavioral Economics; Macroeconomics; interdisciplinary options  
- **Assessment Methods:** Written exams, projects, presentations, and a Master thesis in M2 (18 ECTS). A minimum grade of 10/20 is required in each course and overall.  
- **Academic Structure:**  
  - M1: Core course units, optional discovery courses, and research projects  
  - M2: Specialized tracks and research thesis under a formal pedagogical contract  
- **Credits:** Candidates must earn 120 ECTS over two years, with 60 ECTS per year.  
- **Research Component:** From the first year, students engage with research methodology and produce a final thesis in M2.  

### 1.2 Eligibility Criteria  

Applicants must meet the following academic and eligibility conditions:

- **Academic Qualification:**  
  - A Bachelor’s degree or equivalent (180 ECTS) in Economics, Mathematics, or a closely related quantitative discipline is required.  
  - Strong background in microeconomics, macroeconomics, econometrics, and higher-level mathematics including calculus and statistics is expected.  
  - Applicants with degrees from other disciplines may be considered only if they have sufficient quantitative or economic preparation as demonstrated through their academic record or supplemental documentation.

- **Language Proficiency:**  
  - Proof of English proficiency is mandatory for non-native speakers.  
  - Acceptable certifications include TOEFL, IELTS, GRE, or GMAT scores demonstrating equivalently proficient English skills. Though no official minimum cut-off scores are published, evidence of at least B2 level competency (CEFR) is generally expected.  
  - French proficiency is not required for admission into the Economics Master’s program but can assist with student life integration.

- **Additional Requirements:**  
  - Two letters of recommendation, especially for applicants not recently enrolled in the French higher education system.  
  - Curriculum Vitae (CV) detailing academic and professional experience.  
  - A motivation letter explaining academic goals, interest in the program, and career aspirations.  

- **Special Admissions Conditions:**  
  - Applicants classified as refugees, stateless persons, asylum seekers, or beneficiaries of subsidiary protection have access to tailored application procedures with university support.  
  - Enrolled students continuing from Master 1 to Master 2 must complete Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) procedures to validate their academic progression.

### 1.3 Application Deadlines and Procedures  

- **Application Platform:**  
  - All applications must be submitted online through the official INCEPTION platform of Université Paris-Saclay ([INCEPTION Portal](https://inception.universite-paris-saclay.fr/fr/)).  
  - Applicants under the ‘Études en France’ (EEF) procedure from certain countries must finalize acceptance on the EEF platform.

- **2025-2026 Application Deadlines:**   
  - **Master 1 (M1) Economics:** January 15, 2026 – March 16, 2026  
  - **Master 2 (M2) Economics:** February 25, 2026 – May 25, 2026  
  - Scholarship application deadlines (for university scholarships) generally fall earlier (May 2025).  

- **Document Requirements:**  
  - Passport or identity document copy  
  - Academic transcripts and diplomas (including detailed course descriptions where possible)  
  - CV  
  - Motivation letter  
  - Letters of recommendation (minimum two recommended)  
  - Proof of English proficiency  
  - Proof of Student and Campus Life Contribution (CVEC) payment or exemption before enrollment  
  - For some applicants, additional supporting documentation such as RPL forms or academic references may be required.

- **Decisions and Enrollment:**  
  - Admission decisions are communicated within 1 to 2 months after application closure.  
  - Selected candidates must confirm acceptance and complete administrative registration, including CVEC payment (€95 approx., subject to change).  
  - Course registration and timetable finalization occur upon enrollment confirmation.

### 1.4 Tuition Fees and Financial Considerations  

- **Tuition Fees for 2025-2026:**  
  - European Union (EU) students: Approximately €254 per academic year.  
  - Non-EU students: Normally between €4,000 and €6,000 per year, with possible partial exemptions reducing fees to domestic levels for eligible students.  
- **Additional Costs:** Health insurance (compulsory), living expenses, and registration fees aside from tuition.  
- **Financial Aid:** Scholarships offered by Université Paris-Saclay or external bodies such as the France Excellence Charpak Scholarship may provide partial or full support (see Section 3).

### 1.5 Special Notes and Guidance for Applicants Outside Primary Eligibility  

- Applicants who do not meet the bachelor degree equivalency or lack sufficient preparation may seek preparatory courses or consider alternative Master programs.  
- Refugees, asylum seekers, and persons with special status should contact the International Welcome Desk directly for tailored admission advice and available support.  
- Applicants are strongly encouraged to verify application deadlines and eligibility criteria annually and consult specific country-based admission procedures under the Études en France framework if relevant.  
- Advance preparation of documents and language testing at least 6 months prior to deadlines is advised.

## 2. France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship: Eligibility, Application, Selection and Benefits for 2026 Academic Year

### 2.1 Overview  

The France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship is a competitive merit-based program administered by Campus France India to support Indian citizens and Overseas Citizens of India (OCI) enrolling full-time in master’s programs in France, including the Université Paris-Saclay Master’s in Economics.

- **Objective:** Support India-France academic cooperation by enabling talented students to pursue master’s degrees in France.  
- **Duration:** Scholarship awarded for one or two academic years depending on program length and academic progress.  
- **Benefits:** Living stipend, visa fee waiver, social security coverage, among others.  

### 2.2 Eligibility Criteria  

- **Nationality:** Only Indian citizens or OCI cardholders are eligible. Applications from other nationalities or OCI non-holders are excluded.  
- **Age Limit:** Applicants must be 30 years old or younger on the date of application closure.  
- **Academic Status:**  
  - Candidates must have applied for or hold admission to a full-time Master’s degree program in France conducted entirely in France (not distance or joint degrees abroad).  
  - Programs taught in English or French are eligible; no mandatory French proficiency required but knowledge is advantageous.  
- **Exclusions:**  
  - Candidates currently engaged in thesis work, internships, or research projects beyond initial admission are not eligible.  
  - Candidates holding other scholarships exceeding €600/month or conflicting scholarships/internships are excluded.  
  - Scholarships may be revoked if the visa is denied or if the student accepts conflicting scholarships or gap years.  

### 2.3 Application Process and Deadlines  

- **Application Portal:** Candidates must apply online exclusively via the official Campus France India portal ([scholarship.institutfrancaisindia.in](http://scholarship.institutfrancaisindia.in)) or the dedicated France Excellence Charpak page on Campus France India.  
- **Application Deadline for 2026 Intake:** March 30, 2026, 23:59 IST (strict deadline).  
- **Required Documents:**  
  - Valid passport copy  
  - Admission letter or official proof of application to a French university (e.g., email confirmation if admission letter is not yet received)  
  - Academic transcripts and certificates from Indian higher education institutions  
  - Curriculum Vitae (CV) detailing academic and extracurricular achievements  
  - Two letters of recommendation submitted directly by recommenders on official letterhead and via official institutional email addresses (authentication strictly checked)  
  - Statement of Purpose (SOP) articulating motivation, academic goals, choice of France and program, career plans  
  - (Optional) French language proficiency certificates  
- **SOP Authenticity Verification:**  
  - SOPs are rigorously checked for plagiarism and AI-generated content.  
  - Substantial copying or AI-generated text leads to immediate disqualification.  
- **Note:** Incomplete or fraudulent applications are promptly rejected.  

### 2.4 Selection Procedure  

- **Stage 1: Document and Application Review**  
  - Academic excellence, relevance of previous qualifications, quality, originality and coherence of SOP, letters of recommendation, and eligibility are assessed.  
  - Applicants not matching age, nationality, or academic criteria are filtered out.  

- **Stage 2: Online Interview**  
  - Shortlisted candidates must attend a mandatory online interview scheduled between April 13 and April 30, 2026.  
  - Interviews assess communication skills, clarity of motivation, career objectives, and knowledge about the French academic environment.  
  - No rescheduling is allowed; failure to attend the interview leads to disqualification.

- **Results Announcement:** Typically by late May 2026.  
- **Notification:** Selected candidates receive formal scholarship offer letters with benefit details and instructions.

### 2.5 Scholarship Benefits  

- **Monthly Stipend:** €860 (~₹77,000), intended to cover living expenses in France.  
- **Visa Fee Waiver:** Full exemption from visa application fees for Indian applicants.  
- **Campus France Application Fee Waiver:** Scholarship holders are exempt from Campus France assessment fees.  
- **Health Coverage:** Mandatory student social security and health insurance coverage sponsored by scholarship program.  
- **Accommodation Assistance:** Priority in applying for CROUS (university housing), simplified access to student restaurants (CROUS), and social/cultural programs for integration.  
- **Work Authorization:** Scholarship holders may work up to 964 hours per academic year (approx. 20 hours/week).  
- **Other:** Travel expenses, tuition fees (usually low at French public universities), and personal expenses are excluded from support.  
- **Renewability:** The scholarship may be renewed for the second year contingent on satisfactory academic performance in the first year.

### 2.6 Guidance for Ineligible or Unsuccessful Applicants  

- Applicants failing to meet age, nationality, or academic program criteria are ineligible.  
- Those not admitted to a French Master’s program by application deadline should still apply with proof of admission application but must follow up with official admission documents promptly.  
- Alternatives include other scholarship schemes such as the Eiffel Excellence Scholarship, Erasmus+ exchanges, and university-specific scholarships.  
- Applicants are recommended to regularly visit the official Campus France India website and contact scholarship.france@institutfrancaisindia.in for updated information and technical support.

### 2.7 Annual Verification and Nationality-Specific Procedures  

- The scholarship program updates eligibility conditions and deadlines annually; applicants must consult the official 2026 guidelines each application cycle.  
- Specific nationality conditions and procedural requirements apply only to Indian nationals and OCI holders; other nationals must review alternative French scholarship programs.

## 3. Summary and Recommendations  

### Admission to Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay (2025-2026)  
Applicants must hold a relevant bachelor’s degree, demonstrate strong English proficiency, and submit a complete application including academic records, CV, motivation letter, and recommendations via the INCEPTION portal by the designated deadlines (M1 by mid-March 2026; M2 by late May 2026). Special conditions apply for refugee and asylum-seeking applicants allowing access to tailored admission support. Candidates should begin language proficiency certification and document preparation well in advance.

### France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship (2026)  
Open exclusively to Indian nationals or OCI cardholders aged 30 or below who have applied for or been admitted to full-time master’s programs in France, including Université Paris-Saclay’s Economics program. The fully digital application process closes on March 30, 2026. Applicants must submit a rigorously vetted SOP, letters of recommendation, academic records, and attend a mandatory online interview in mid- to late-April 2026. The scholarship provides a monthly stipend, visa fee waiver, health insurance, and integration support but does not cover tuition or travel.

### Overall Recommendations  
- Verify eligibility and deadline details annually via official sources for both the university program and the scholarship.  
- Prepare all application documents carefully, ensuring authenticity and completeness, especially the Statement of Purpose and letters of recommendation.  
- Early application is advised due to strict deadlines and competitive selection.  
- Candidates who do not meet eligibility criteria should explore alternative funding and programs while consulting official guidance bodies.  
- Contact Université Paris-Saclay’s International Welcome Desk and Campus France India helpline for specific inquiries and procedural clarifications.

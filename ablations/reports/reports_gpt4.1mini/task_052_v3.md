# Comprehensive Research Report on the Industrial Automation Market for Manufacturing in Saudi Arabia (2015–2025)

## 1. Quantitative Benchmarks and Market Overview (2015–2025)

### 1.1 Market Size and CAGR

The Saudi Arabia industrial automation market focusing on manufacturing exhibited significant growth influenced strongly by economic diversification efforts under Vision 2030 and complementary programs.

- According to **Data Bridge Market Research**, the industrial automation market size will reach approximately **USD 1.99 billion in 2024**, expanding to around **USD 3.72 billion by 2032**, reflecting a **CAGR of 10.4% from 2025 through 2032**. The manufacturing sector constitutes a substantial share of this market, particularly with increasing automation penetration [1].

- Other authoritative estimates forecast slightly differing figures based on differing scopes:
  - The **National Industrial Development and Logistics Program (NIDLP)** projects an investment target exceeding **SAR 300 billion** (roughly USD 80 billion) into manufacturing, mining, energy, and logistics sectors collectively by 2030; around 20% manufacturing GDP contribution is targeted under NIDLP, which underpins automation demand [2][17].
  - The industrial robotics segment alone within manufacturing is poised to grow from approximately **USD 178 million in 2024** to over **USD 543 million by 2033**, at a CAGR around **11.8%**, driven by sectors like automotive, chemicals, and electronics [3][4].
  - The **Future Factories Program**, managed by the Ministry of Industry and Mineral Resources, directly supports smart factory transformation for approximately **4,000 factories by 2030**, which significantly boosts demand for automation hardware and software [5].

### 1.2 Robotics Unit Installations and Penetration Rates

- The **International Federation of Robotics (IFR)** defines robot density as the number of industrial robots per 10,000 manufacturing employees.

- Saudi Arabia’s exact official IFR data on robot density is not separately published, but triangulated estimates demonstrate:
  - Installed industrial robot units in manufacturing are projected to be approximately **2,100 units by 2025**, growing to **over 8,000 units by 2034**, representing a broad CAGR in excess of 15% for units deployed [6][7].
  - The manufacturing workforce is estimated around **500,000 workers** as of 2025, yielding an approximate robot density of **42 robots per 10,000 manufacturing workers by 2025**. This is lower than mature markets (South Korea: 932; Germany: 371) but rapidly increasing from much lower baselines in 2015 [8][9][10].
  - Robotics adoption mainly focuses on precision repetitive tasks within automotive, electronics, chemical processing, pharmaceuticals, and food & beverage sectors [3][4].

- **Robotics software market** supporting manufacturing automation is projected to expand from **USD 238.8 million in 2025** to over **USD 1.25 billion by 2034**, with a CAGR exceeding 20%, fueled by Industry 4.0 adoption and AI integration [11].

## 2. How Saudi Vision 2030 and Related Initiatives Drive Demand for Industrial Automation

### 2.1 Vision 2030 and National Industrial Strategy (NIS) Foundations

- Vision 2030 aims to diversify Saudi Arabia’s economy away from oil, targeting manufacturing GDP growth to **SAR 895 billion (~USD 239 billion) by 2030**, with over **2.1 million industrial jobs created**, emphasizing technology-driven, high-value manufacturing sectors [2].

- The **National Industrial Strategy** identifies 12 priority sectors including automotive, electronics, pharmaceuticals, minerals, and machinery — sectors with high potential for industrial automation integration.

- Localization targets (e.g., **IKTVA framework**) require up to **70% local content in oil & gas** and **up to 40% in pharmaceuticals** by 2030, compelling manufacturers to modernize production processes and adopt advanced automation to remain competitive [12][13].

### 2.2 National Industrial Development and Logistics Program (NIDLP) Impact

- Launched in 2019, NIDLP targets industrialization, logistics development, technology adoption (AI, robotics, IIoT), and local content enhancement, with an overall investment target of **SAR 1.6 trillion (~USD 427 billion)** by 2030 [14].

- Manufacturing GDP contribution targeted to rise to **20%** by 2030, partly via adoption of **Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR) technologies**, including robotics and automation.

- Over **50,000 direct jobs** have been created by 2024 in targeted industrial sectors under NIDLP.

- The program supports **special economic zones** (SEZs) such as **NEOM** and **KAEC** as advanced manufacturing hubs, creating ecosystems supportive of automation investments and supplier localization [14][15].

### 2.3 Future Factories Program and SIRI Assessments

- The **Future Factories Program** focuses on upgrading 4,000 factories by 2030 to smart manufacturing through:
  - Industry 4.0 technology adoption (AI, robotics, IoT, digital twins)
  - Workforce retraining and upskilling
  - Financial grants covering **up to 35% of project costs**, capped at SAR 50 million (~USD 13.3 million) per project
  - Audited **Smart Industry Readiness Index (SIRI)** assessments provide standardized evaluations of factories’ Industry 4.0 maturity covering technology, processes, and organization. Factories identified with critical gaps receive targeted support [5][20].

- SIRI assessments, administered by certified regional assessors such as QTC and Sharar Al-Ebtekar, form a mandatory or strongly weighted criterion for eligibility to government grants and subsidies under Vision 2030 programs. This structured assessment-to-funding workflow drives targeted automation investments [16][21].

### 2.4 Grant Eligibility and Investment Triggers Under Vision 2030

- The **Standard Incentives Program** launched in January 2025 provides:

  - Up to **35% funding of capital project costs** (max **SAR 50 million / ~USD 13.3M**)
  - Low-interest loans covering up to 75% of project costs via Saudi Industrial Development Fund (SIDF)
  - Operational support and tax exemptions for 7 years
  - Customs benefits and workforce Saudization incentives

- Eligibility criteria include commitments to localization, innovation, job creation, and adoption of advanced manufacturing technologies.

- Sector-specific triggers such as automotive EV production scaling to 300,000 vehicles/year by 2030 incentivize robotics use in welding, assembly, and painting lines [17][18][22].

### 2.5 Localization Mandates and Procurement Digitization

- Vision 2030 mandates local content thresholds ranging from **20% to 90%** based on sector and contract size, with strategic projects requiring up to **100% Saudi content** in goods, services, workforce, and technology transfer.

- Procurement platforms such as **Etimad** (unified government procurement portal) and **Ivalua** enable digital supplier registration, compliance tracking, and tendering that prioritize local suppliers with requisite certifications and content levels [23][24].

- Localization weight can contribute **~40% of tender evaluation scoring**, driving suppliers to integrate local manufacturing and automation capabilities to maintain competitiveness [25].

- EPC contractors play key roles in mega projects by integrating localized automation suppliers within delivery consortia, ensuring compliance with Vision 2030 localization and sustainability targets [26][27].

## 4. Procurement and Localization Pathways Under Vision 2030 Policies

### 4.1 Official Procurement Portals and Vendor Registration

- **Etimad** platform is the unified government procurement portal managing:
  - Tender announcements, contract award notices
  - Vendor prequalification and registration
  - Payment requests and milestone tracking

- Vendors must register with:
  - Valid Commercial Registration (CR)
  - Saudi tax and Zakat certificates
  - Saudization compliance evidence (proof of Saudi employees)
  - Relevant ISO/sector certifications (e.g., SFDA for pharma)
  - Financial statements and company profiles

- Registration processing generally takes **2–6 weeks**, depending on completeness and public entity requirements.

- Specialized portals for key sectors or entities include:
  - Saudi Aramco Supplier Portal
  - SABIC Vendor Portal
  - Saudi Electricity Company (SEC)
  - Red Sea Global Vendor Portal

- Establishing a Saudi regional HQ is mandatory for government contract eligibility since January 2024, with physical office and designated senior management presence requirements [28][29][30].

### 4.2 Engineering Procurement and Construction (EPC) Contracts and Relationships

- EPC contractors act as prime contractors in giga projects, integrating supply chains and managing manufacturing automation deliveries.

- EPC contracts include:
  - Clear scope of work for automation system design, procurement, installation, and commissioning
  - Localization and Saudization compliance clauses aligned to Vision 2030 mandates
  - Integration of digital tools (AI-powered contract management and risk mitigation)
  - Payment schedules linked to milestones and regulatory compliance reporting

- EPCs coordinate with manufacturers and technology suppliers to ensure AI, robotics, and Industry 4.0 technology implementations satisfy government standards and localization targets [26][27].

### 4.3 Sector-Specific Localization Thresholds

- Localization thresholds under NIDLP and Vision 2030 vary by manufacturing subsector:
  - Oil & Gas: 70% local content targeted by 2025
  - Automotive: Progressive local content targets aligned with 300,000 vehicles/year industrial hub goal
  - Pharmaceuticals: 30-40% local production of active ingredients and medicines by 2030
  - Industrial Machinery & Equipment: 30–60% local sourcing thresholds enforced in government projects and mega industrial parks

- Compliance with local content targets is **weighted in procurement scoring (~40%)** and subject to audits and penalties for non-compliance [12][13][30].

## 5. Summary

- The industrial automation market for manufacturing in Saudi Arabia is growing robustly, with a **market size estimated between USD 1.99 billion (2024) and USD 3.72 billion (2032)** driven largely by Vision 2030 and NIDLP initiatives.

- Robotics penetration is increasing rapidly with around **2,100 robots installed by 2025** and a projected robot density of **42 per 10,000 manufacturing workers**, reflecting rapid but still emerging automation maturity.

- Vision 2030 initiatives including the National Industrial Strategy, NIDLP investment programs, Future Factories, and Standard Incentives Program provide multi-layered financial, operational, and regulatory support to stimulate automation adoption.

- SIRI assessments and digital procurement portals establish structured workflows connecting assessment, grant eligibility, and investment triggers, accelerating demand for robotics and smart manufacturing.

- Major automation contracts since 2021 involve Siemens and ABB deploying advanced automation and energy management systems in flagship giga-projects like NEOM and the Red Sea, with individual contract values ranging from tens of millions to over a billion USD in aggregate.

- Procurement pathways involve formal vendor registration on portals such as Etimad, adherence to localization thresholds varying from 20% to 90% depending on sector, and strong EPC contractor roles in fulfilling contract requirements.

- For strategic engagement, suppliers should prioritize compliance with localization mandates, demonstrate Industry 4.0 technology capabilities, and develop integrated solutions aligned with Vision 2030’s industrial transformation trajectory.

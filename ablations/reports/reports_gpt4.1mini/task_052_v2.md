# Comprehensive Analysis of the Industrial Automation Market for Manufacturing in Saudi Arabia (2015–2025)

---

## 1. Market Size, Growth, and Robotics Penetration Rate: Comparative Triangulation

### 1.1 Market Size Estimates and CAGR

Saudi Arabia’s industrial automation market, specifically within the **manufacturing sector**, has been rapidly expanding from 2015 to 2025. Multiple authoritative sources provide varying numerical estimates, which reflect differences in scope, segmentation, and methodology. The main triangulated insights are:

- **Databridge Market Research** (focused on industrial automation across manufacturing and process industries) estimates the market size at approximately **USD 1.99 billion in 2024**, growing to **USD 3.72 billion by 2032** with a CAGR of **10.4%** from 2025–2032 [[1]](#sources).

- **Mobility Foresights** reports a substantially larger figure of **USD 92.4 billion in 2025**, increasing to **USD 176.1 billion by 2031** at an **11.2% CAGR** [[2]](#sources). This figure significantly exceeds others likely because it includes **broad all-industry automation segments**, potentially encompassing energy, utilities, infrastructure, and non-manufacturing sectors.

- **6W Research**, focusing more narrowly on manufacturing automation, reports a market size of about **USD 2.2 billion in 2025**, with a moderate CAGR of **4.1% between 2020 and 2026** [[3]](#sources).

- **IMARC Group** offers a narrower factory automation market view, estimating USD 2.3 million in 2025 growing to USD 4.3 million by 2034 at CAGR **7.04%**; this much lower figure likely targets a niche segment of factory automation rather than the broader manufacturing automation ecosystem [[4]](#sources).

- **Smart Manufacturing Segment** forecasts by Grand View Research reach **USD 5.7 billion in 2025**, doubling to USD 11.95 billion by 2033 at a 9% CAGR — representing the digital and Industry 4.0 robotics/software-driven portion [[5]](#sources).

#### Reasons for Discrepancies in Estimates

- **Scope differences**: Some reports cover **manufacturing-only automation**, while others include **process industries** (oil & gas, petrochemicals), energy, and infrastructure sectors.

- **Inclusion of software and services**: Some studies include software (MES, SCADA), services, and industry 4.0 tech, while others only count hardware/equipment.

- **Geographical and temporal methods**: Varying base years, forecast periods, and data collection methods lead to fluctuations.

- **Definition of "automation"**: Ranges from traditional PLC/DCS systems to AI/machine learning-based digital factories.

### 1.2 Robotics Market Size and CAGR in Manufacturing

- The **industrial robotics market** in manufacturing alone is assessed between **USD 178 million (Databridge, 2024)** and **over USD 550 million (IMARC, 2034)**, forecasting growth at a CAGR between **11.9% and 17.7%**, reflecting increasing adoption in sectors such as automotive, electronics, chemicals, and pharmaceuticals [[6]][[7]][[8]](#sources).

- The **largest end-use sectors** for industrial robotics in Saudi manufacturing include **automotive**, **electrical & electronics** (~30.5%), **chemical**, and **food & beverage**, driven by increasing labor costs, precision needs, and Vision 2030 industrialization targets [[8]][[9]](#sources).

- Saudi Arabia targets creation of a **regional automotive hub producing 300,000 vehicles annually by 2030**, heavily leveraging robotics for welding, assembly, and painting operations [[10]](#sources).

- Robotics adoption is complemented by expanding use of **collaborative robots (cobots)**, AI-enabled predictive maintenance, and IoT-integrated manufacturing technologies [[11]](#sources).

### 1.3 Robotics Penetration Rate (Robot Density)

- **Robot density**, measured as the number of industrial robots per 10,000 manufacturing workers, is a standard indicator of automation maturity.

- Globally, robot density increased from **66 in 2015** to **126 in 2020** on average, with industry leaders like South Korea (932), Singapore (605), and Germany (371) leading [[12]](#sources).

- **Saudi Arabia-specific robot density data is not explicitly published** by IFR or government authorities. However, triangulated estimates and workforce data suggest:

  - Approximate installed robots in manufacturing estimated at **2,100 units by 2025**, scaling to **over 8,000 units by 2034** [[13]][[14]](#sources).

  - With approximately **500,000 manufacturing workers**, this implies a robot density near **42 robots per 10,000 workers in 2025**, significantly below global leaders but rapidly increasing due to government-backed programs.

- Discrepancies in robot density arise from different workforce estimates, industrial sector definitions, and installation counting methods.

- The **IFR World Robotics reports** remain the benchmark for global robot density standards; Saudi Arabia is considered an emerging market roughly at **mid-low penetration levels within the Gulf region**, with expectations to accelerate driven by industrial hubs and mega projects [[12]][[15]](#sources).

## 2. Major Contract Awards, Project Details, and Policy Launches Since 2021

### 2.1 Siemens Key Projects and Contracts

- **Rumah 2 and Nairyah 2 Gas-Fired Power Plants** (announced March 2025): Siemens Energy awarded a **USD 1.6 billion contract** to supply gas turbines, power generation technologies, and a **25-year maintenance agreement** focused on powering western and central Saudi Arabia. Equipment specifications include state-of-the-art gas turbine generators, distributed control systems, and sustainability-aligned digital monitoring [[16]][[17]](#sources).

- **Kudimi STATCOM Installation** (January 2021): Siemens Limited secured a **USD 41.4 million EPC contract** from Saudi Electricity Company to install a dynamic reactive power compensator to improve grid voltage stability in southern Saudi Arabia [[18]](#sources).

- **Gas Turbines Supply for NEOM**: Siemens supplied advanced SGT-400 turbines to NEOM to support its ambitious goal of over 60 GW power capacity by 2030. Project underlines integration of advanced automation and digital grid systems [[19]](#sources).

- **Siemens Digital Industries Software Office (2026)**: Opened offices in Riyadh, Jeddah, and Khobar to localize digital automation services, software portfolio delivery (including digital twins, lifecycle management), and skills development tailored for Saudi manufacturing clients [[20]](#sources).

### 2.2 ABB Major Contracts and Deployments

- **Red Sea Project Renewable Energy Systems**: ABB supplied synchronization panels generating a total of 36 MW capacity from 69 generators with ABB Ability™ digital energy management. This supports Red Sea’s goal of fully renewable energy operation [[21]][[22]](#sources).

- **Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure**: ABB delivered over **1,200 EV charging stations** across Red Sea and Amaala tourism destinations, marking Saudi Arabia’s largest e-mobility effort, with local assembly supporting Vision 2030 localization goals [[23]](#sources).

- **King Abdullah Financial District Substation (Contract Value: USD 89 million)**: Awarded a turnkey EPC contract including design, installation, and commissioning, underlining ABB’s role in Riyadh’s smart infrastructure [[24]](#sources).

- **Sadara Chemical Complex (historic 2011 project)**: ABB provided full automation and safety systems; a reference point highlighting ABB’s long-term engagement with Saudi petrochemical manufacturing [[25]](#sources).

### 2.3 ALAT Industrial Automation Initiatives

- Founded in 2024 by Public Investment Fund (PIF), ALAT aims to advance **clean-energy powered sustainable manufacturing hubs** with investments of over **USD 100 billion by 2030**.

- Strategic partnerships:

  - **SoftBank Group JV** launched February 2024 to produce next-generation industrial robots manufacturing facility, expected operational from late 2024 with an investment up to **USD 150 million** [[26]](#sources).

  - **Lenovo Factory** ground-breaking February 2025 for laptops, servers manufacturing supporting up to 15,000 direct jobs [[27]](#sources).

  - **TK Elevator JV** for local production of elevators and escalators with a €160 million investment [[28]](#sources).

- ALAT’s initiatives focus on integrating AI, robotics, and Industry 4.0 into Saudi manufacturing aligned with Vision 2030 industrial modernization [[26]][[27]][[28]](#sources).

### 2.4 NEOM and Red Sea Development Project Contracts

- **NEOM & Samsung C&T JV (Announced December 2024)**: Agreement to invest **USD 346 million** for deploying the world's largest rebar construction automation line using robotic welding and tying technology. This project targets an **80% reduction in manual labor** and **40% cost savings** in rebar cage assembly, generating over 2,000 skilled local jobs [[29]][[30]](#sources).

- **Red Sea Development Company (TRSDC) Contracts 2021–2025**:

  - Over **USD 4.5 billion awarded in contracts since 2021**, with **70% awarded to Saudi firms** emphasizing localization [[31]](#sources).

  - Awarded **USD 267 million international airport contract** (Nesma & Partners and Almabani JV), including runway, pavements, and auxiliary buildings, aiming for 1 million annual tourists [[32]][[33]](#sources).

  - *Ongoing infrastructure, hotel construction, and EV infrastructure contracts* reflecting integrated automation and sustainability milestones [[31]](#sources).

## 3. Mechanisms of Saudi Vision 2030 Driving Industrial Automation in Manufacturing

### 3.1 Policy Instruments and Incentives

- The **Standard Incentives Program** provides financial support covering **up to 35% of project costs**, capped at **SAR 50 million (~USD 13.3 million)**, targeting factory modernization, new facility establishment, and expansions incorporating automation technologies [[34]](#sources).

- The **Saudi Industrial Development Fund (SIDF)** offers low-interest loans covering up to **75% of project costs**, further easing capital expenditure constraints [[35]](#sources).

- Additional grants and subsidies apply for digitalization and Industry 4.0 adoption, including **up to 80% coverage of smart factory projects capped at SAR 300,000**, encouraging manufacturers to adopt AI, IoT, and robotics [[36]](#sources).

- Programs such as the **Future Factories Program (FFP)** and **Industrial Lighthouse Program** aim to transform **4,000 factories** by 2030 into smart manufacturing facilities integrating AI, robotics, and digital twin technologies [[37]](#sources).

### 3.2 Procurement Pathways and Local Content Requirements

- Central to Vision 2030, **local content requirements (LCR)** impose thresholds ranging from **20% to 90% local sourcing of goods, services, and labor**, with some strategic projects mandating up to **100% local content** [[38]](#sources).

- Procurement platforms like **Ivalua** digitize sourcing, certification, compliance monitoring, and supplier relationship management to prioritize local and Saudi-based suppliers [[39]](#sources).

- Local content compliance accounts for **approximately 40% weight** in government tender evaluations, strongly influencing vendor selection. Contracts above **SAR 25 million** have **mandatory LCR thresholds** [[40]](#sources).

- Joint ventures between Saudi and foreign firms strategically increase local content and enable technology transfers, improving bid competitiveness.

### 3.3 Priority Sector Rationales and Cluster Development

- Vision 2030’s **National Industrial Strategy** prioritizes **automotive, electronics, petrochemicals, pharmaceuticals, food processing, industrial machinery, mining, and renewable energy equipment**.

- These sectors are selected based on their potential for **value-addition, export growth, employment generation, and alignment with economic diversification** from oil dependence [[4]](#sources).

- Industrial zones such as **Jubail, Yanbu, Sudair, Ras Al Khair, and Jazan** provide integrated utilities, infrastructure, and incentives specialized for automation and smart factory development.

- Localization, quality improvement, sustainability, and innovation underpin sector targeting, supported by workforce development to meet Industry 4.0 skill requirements [[15]](#sources).

### 3.4 Causal Chain from Policies to Automation Adoption

- Financial incentives **reduce upfront capital barriers**, encouraging manufacturers to invest in robotics and automation.

- Local content mandates drive manufacturers to source locally, favoring vendors who offer Saudi content and technology transfer, aligning suppliers with Vision 2030 goals.

- Workforce development ensures operational competency in automated facilities, supporting the sustainability of technology adoption.

- Procurement platforms ensure transparency and enforce compliance, helping shift buying behavior toward sustainable, locally integrated technologies.

- Industrial clusters and giga-projects like NEOM and Red Sea act as technology demonstrators and diffusion points for automation solutions.

- Recognition programs incentivize firms to pursue Industry 4.0 maturity, generating peer-network effects and industry-wide acceleration.

## 4. Scope Discipline and Caveats on Data Interpretation

- The **largest discrepancies in market sizing** stem from inconsistent sectoral scopes: some reports explicitly focus on **manufacturing-only automation**, others encompass **all industrial automation including utilities, energy, and infrastructure**.

- Robotics penetration rates often come from IFR’s global and regional data which **do not distinctly separate Saudi manufacturing**; localized estimates rely on workforce and installation assumptions.

- Official government sources (Saudi Vision 2030 documents, Ministry of Industry) focus on **manufacturing and logistics** but occasionally aggregate across industrial sectors without granular sub-sector breakdowns.

- Where sectoral boundaries are unclear, this report explicitly notes potential scope overlaps or narrower definitions to ensure clarity.

- Contract award values reported rely on **public disclosures and official press releases**; many contracts have undisclosed or partially disclosed terms due to commercial sensitivity.

---

## 5. Summary and Strategic Implications

- Saudi Arabia’s industrial automation market in manufacturing is **growing steadily, at a CAGR roughly between 7% and 11%**, with market size estimates in the USD 2–4 billion range for 2025 and upward trajectory through 2030.

- Robotics penetration remains moderate (estimated 40+ robots per 10,000 workers) but is accelerating driven by Vision 2030 programs, industrial diversification, and mega projects like NEOM.

- Key players Siemens, ABB, and ALAT have secured major, multi-hundred-million-dollar contracts integrating automation systems in giga projects and advanced manufacturing hubs since 2021.

- Vision 2030 drives adoption through **standard incentives covering capex**, **local content mandates (20–90%)**, **digitized procurement pathways**, and workforce upskilling programs—all fostering sustained industrial automation investment.

- Procurement behavior strongly favors vendors able to demonstrate local content compliance, advanced Industry 4.0 tech offering, and alignment to sustainability goals.

- Strategic sales approach should focus on fully compliant localized solutions, emphasize energy efficiency and digital integration, and build local support networks to maximize alignment with evolving government policy and market needs.

# Comparative Analysis: Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund vs DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF (As of December 2024)

This report provides a detailed comparative evaluation of the Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund and the DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund based on portfolio composition, geographic and sector concentration, top 20 holdings overlap, expense ratios, and historical returns over 1-, 3-, and 5-year periods ending December 2024. The objective is to identify which fund is more suitable for investment horizons of 2 years and 5 years respectively, referencing official fund documents and reputable financial databases.

---

## 1. Portfolio Composition and Geographic/Sector Concentration

### Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund

- **Geographic Focus:** Exclusively invests in the Greater China region, which includes Mainland China, Hong Kong, Taiwan, and Macau. The fund achieves this exposure mainly by investing approximately 96-97% in the JPMorgan Funds - JF Greater China Equity Fund.  
- **Sector Concentration:** While specific sector weights are less explicitly detailed in available disclosures, the fund targets a "high conviction" portfolio of large-cap growth companies across technology, internet, consumer discretionary, and related sectors in the Greater China region.  
- **Investment Approach:** Bottom-up stock selection with a focus on companies demonstrating growth potential in the Greater China markets, exposed to regional macroeconomic and regulatory dynamics.  
- **Key Holdings (Dec 2024):** Major positions include Tencent Holdings (approx. 9.9%), Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (TSMC) (~9.45%), Alibaba Group (~9%), reflecting concentration in dominant China and Taiwan technology and internet companies. These top holdings highlight a concentrated portfolio with a major tilt towards technology and internet sectors in Greater China.

### DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF

- **Geographic Focus:** Globally diversified fund of funds structure with a strong emphasis on developed markets, particularly the US and selective China exposure. Underlying funds are global innovation-focused mutual funds and ETFs.  
- **Sector Concentration:** Heavy focus on technology, innovation, biotechnology, and related high-growth sectors. Key sectors include software & services, internet, semiconductor, medical technology, and precision medicine.  
- **Underlying Funds Composition:**  
  - BlueBox Global Technology Fund (circa 35-37%)  
  - iShares NASDAQ 100 UCITS ETF (~27-28%)  
  - Invesco NASDAQ-100 Equal Weight UCITS ETF (~12-13%)  
  - KraneShares CSI China Internet UCITS ETF (~12%) – providing targeted China internet exposure  
  - Fidelity Select Medical Technology & Devices Portfolio and BlueBox Precision Medicine Fund (~6.4%)  
  - Cash and equivalents (~3-4%) for liquidity management.  
- **Investment Approach:** Top-down allocation to innovative themes with an emphasis on global technology innovation companies with high earnings growth potential, utilizing passive and actively managed underlying funds.

## 2. Overlap in Top 20 Holdings

- **Overlap Analysis:**  
  - The Edelweiss fund’s top holdings are heavily biased towards Greater China, with Tencent, Alibaba, and TSMC leading the portfolio.  
  - DSP fund’s exposure to China is primarily through the KraneShares CSI China Internet ETF (~12%), thus overlap exists only partially via China internet stocks.  
  - The DSP fund’s substantial holdings in US-listed NASDAQ 100 ETFs and global biotech-related funds show no direct overlap with Edelweiss’s China-centric holdings beyond the China tech stocks exposure.  
  - Therefore, the **overlap in top 20 holdings** between the two funds is limited and partial, primarily confined to Tencent and Alibaba exposure via the DSP’s China internet ETF holdings.

## 5. Suitability for Investment Horizons

### 2-Year Investment Horizon

- **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** might appeal more to investors looking for potentially higher short-term gains due to:  
  - Its regional focus on Greater China rebound themes and high conviction stocks with strong recent performance.  
  - Potential to capitalize on shorter-term growth rallies in China and Taiwan markets.  
  - However, investors should be comfortable with high volatility, geopolitical/regulatory risks in the region, and an exit load within the first 90 days.  

- **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** represents a relatively newer, globally diversified innovation theme fund:  
  - While historical 1-year returns are strong, the fund’s global tech and innovation focus can be volatile over short term due to sector cyclicality.  
  - Since inception is just under 3 years, there may be limited track record specifically for 2-year period reliability.  
  - Also carries higher expenses.  

**Conclusion:** For a 2-year horizon, Edelweiss offers potentially higher upside with concentrated regional exposure but with elevated risk and volatility. DSP is comparatively less volatile but may be less predictable short term due to its growth/innovation focus and shorter track record.

### 5-Year Investment Horizon

- **Edelweiss fund’s** weak or negative 5-year returns and documented regulatory and regional volatility suggest it may be less suitable for a stable 5-year investment. Growth consistency is not well established over five years.  
- **DSP fund’s** strong 3-year annualized returns (~24-26%) combined with globally diversified innovation exposure and better risk metrics suggest a more consistent growth profile over medium term.  
- Industry consensus recommends holding innovative/global growth funds like DSP for periods exceeding 3-5 years to smooth out sector volatility and leverage secular growth trends.  
- Despite slightly higher expenses, the DSP fund’s robust risk-adjusted returns and diversified global exposure make it a better choice for a 5-year horizon given the current data.

## 6. Summary and Recommendations

| Criteria                          | Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund          | DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF                 |

| **Expense Ratio**                | Lower (Direct plan ~0.75%); modest exit load           | Higher (Direct ~1.14%-1.16%); no exit load               |
| **1-Year Returns**               | Very strong, up to ~66%                                 | Strong, ~40-42%                                           |
| **3-Year Returns**               | Moderate, 6.7%-16.5% annualized                         | Robust, 24%-26% annualized                                |
| **5-Year Returns**               | Weak to negative                                        | Not available (recent fund)                               |
| **Volatility and Risk**          | Higher volatility (~20%), higher risk                   | Moderate-high volatility (~16.5-17.5%), better risk-adjusted returns |
| **Suitable for 2-Year Horizon** | Yes, if risk-tolerant and seeking regional China upside | Possible; moderate risk but newer fund                    |
| **Suitable for 5-Year Horizon** | Less suitable due to weak 5-year track record           | Preferred due to global innovation theme and consistent growth |

### Final Recommendation

- **For a 2-year investment horizon:** The **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** is more suitable for investors who are willing to accept higher volatility and concentrated regional risk in exchange for potentially higher short-term returns from Greater China equities.

- **For a 5-year (or longer) investment horizon:** The **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** is the preferred choice due to its strong 3-year performance, diversified global innovation exposure, and better risk-adjusted returns, providing a balanced growth opportunity over the medium to long term.

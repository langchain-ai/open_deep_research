# Comprehensive Report on Admission Requirements for the Master’s in Economics Program at Université Paris-Saclay (2025-2026) and France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship 2026

---

## 1. Admission Requirements and Eligibility Criteria for the Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay (2025-2026)

### 1.1 Overview of the Program

The Master’s in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay is a highly selective, research-oriented two-year program fully taught in English. It aims to prepare students for doctoral studies and advanced professional careers in economics, including research, policy, consulting, and data analysis. The program is conducted at the Gif-sur-Yvette and Saclay Moulon campuses.

- **Program structure:**
  - **Master 1 (M1):** Foundational courses covering microeconomics, macroeconomics, econometrics, mathematics, decision and game theory, development economics, public economics, and optional discovery courses, coupled with two research projects.
  - **Master 2 (M2):** Specialized tracks allowing focused study and research in areas such as Trade, Geography and Development Economics; Public and Behavioral Economics; and Macroeconomics advances and challenges, culminating in an 18 ECTS Master thesis based on independent research.

- **Total credits:** 120 ECTS over two years (60 ECTS per year).

- **Assessment methods:** Combination of exams, projects, presentations, and thesis defense. Minimum passing grade required is 10/20 in each course and overall.

---

### 1.2 Eligibility Criteria

#### Academic Qualifications

- **For M1 admission:**
  - Hold a Bachelor’s degree (Licence or equivalent, 180 ECTS) in Economics, Mathematics, or a closely related quantitative discipline.
  - Degree titles accepted include BA in Economics, BSc in Mathematics with economics modules, or equivalent.
  - Applicants from non-economics backgrounds may be considered only if they demonstrate sufficient quantitative and economic knowledge through prior coursework and transcripts.

- **For M2 admission:**
  - Hold a Master 1 (or equivalent first year of Master’s) with validated credits, preferably in Economics or related analytical fields.
  - Must provide official transcripts and proof of completion.
  - Candidate evaluation includes academic results and motivation for specialization.

- Applicants continuing at Université Paris-Saclay from M1 to M2 must complete the **Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)** as part of their application.

---

#### Language Proficiency Requirements

- **Instruction language:** English exclusively.

- **Required proficiency for non-native English speakers:**  
  Applicants must provide proof of English proficiency. Acceptable test scores from recognized certificates include (no official strict minimum score is publicly stated by the university, but B2 level proficiency is generally expected):

  - **TOEFL iBT:** no explicit cut-off, generally ≥80 recommended.  
  - **IELTS:** generally ≥6.0 recommended (no official cutoff stated).  
  - **TOEIC:** scores ≥750/990 are considered proof of proficiency.  
  - **GRE/GMAT:** may support application but not substitutes for language certification.

- **French proficiency:**  
  Not mandatory for admission to the Economics Master’s but recommended to facilitate integration and daily life.

---

### 1.3 Application Procedures and Documentation

Applications for both M1 and M2 must be submitted through the official **INCEPTION online platform** of Université Paris-Saclay ([https://inception.universite-paris-saclay.fr/fr/](https://inception.universite-paris-saclay.fr/fr/)).

- **M1 Application Period:** January 15, 2026 – March 16, 2026  
- **M2 Application Period:** February 25, 2026 – May 25, 2026

---

#### Required Application Documents

Applicants must upload and submit the following documents in the formats accepted by the INCEPTION portal (PDF/JPG/scanned copies, max 3 MB per document):

- **Bachelor’s degree diploma** (or equivalent for M1 applicants) or Master 1 diploma for M2 applicants. Copies must be clear and official.

- **Complete academic transcripts** for all higher education years, including detailed course descriptions where possible.

- **Curriculum Vitae (CV):** professional and academic summary.

- **Motivation letter:** detailing reasons for choosing the program, academic interests, and career plans.

- **Letters of Recommendation:**  
  - Usually two letters are required, especially for applicants not recently enrolled in French higher education or with non-traditional backgrounds.  
  - Letters should be submitted electronically by the referees through the application platform.  
  - There is no explicit university requirement that letters must be on official letterhead or sent from institutional emails; however, letters must come from academic or professional referees able to authenticate the applicant’s qualifications.

- **Proof of English proficiency:** official certificates as specified above.

- **Copy of passport or official ID document.**

- **For M2 applicants:** Evidence of validated M1 credits or equivalent prior education, and if applicable, proof of Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL).

- **Other supporting documents as relevant to applicant profile or requested by the admissions team.**

---

#### Special Application Procedures

- **For applicants under the Études en France (EEF) procedure:**  
  Those from countries where the 'Études en France' visa application process is mandatory must finalize certain steps on the EEF platform after admission. The university requires the correct University Degree type (Master indifférencié, research & professional) be selected on EEF, alongside uploading unconditional admission letters and motivation letter summaries.

- **Refugees, asylum seekers, stateless persons, and beneficiaries of subsidiary protection** have access to tailored procedures with university support and possible bonuses during scholarship applications. They must contact the International Welcome Desk for dedicated guidance.

- **Failure to submit complete or authentic documents** or failure to comply with procedural instructions may lead to application rejection.

---

### 1.4 Payment of Student and Campus Life Contribution (CVEC)

- Payment of the **CVEC (€105 for 2025-2026)** is mandatory before final enrollment.

- Proof of payment or an official exemption certificate must be uploaded during the registration process.

- Without CVEC proof, enrollment at Université Paris-Saclay is blocked.

- **Students receiving CROUS scholarships or other official exemptions do not pay CVEC.**

## 2. France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship 2026: Eligibility and Application Requirements for Université Paris-Saclay Master’s in Economics Students  

### 2.1 Overview of the Scholarship

The **France Excellence Charpak Master’s Scholarship 2026** is a merit-based scholarship offered by the French Government and administered by **Campus France India**. It supports Indian nationals and Overseas Citizens of India (OCI) pursuing full-time master’s degrees at French institutions, including Université Paris-Saclay’s Master’s in Economics.

---

### 2.2 Eligibility Criteria Specific to Indian Nationals and OCI Holders

- **Nationality:**  
  - Only Indian citizens and OCI card holders are eligible to apply. Other nationality groups are ineligible under this scheme.

- **Age Limit:**  
  - Maximum age of 30 years as of March 30, 2026 (application deadline).  
  - Candidates above 30 are automatically disqualified.

- **Academic Status:**  
  - Must be pursuing or have applied for a full-time Master’s degree taught in France (in France, not distance learning or joint programs abroad).  
  - The program must begin in the 2026-2027 academic year.  
  - Candidates currently engaged in Master thesis work, internships, or research projects are not eligible.  
  - Previous scholarship recipients of Charpak Master’s Scholarship are not eligible to reapply.

- **Language:**  
  - No mandatory French proficiency level is required to apply.  
  - Programs taught in English are eligible.

- **Exclusions:**  
  - Applicants planning to pursue doctoral studies, exchange semesters, internships are not eligible for this scholarship.  
  - Candidates holding other high-value scholarships (above €600/month), or conflicting awards (e.g., Eiffel Excellence, Erasmus Mundus) are ineligible or risk losing the Charpak scholarship if awarded both.

---

### 2.3 Application Process and Important Deadlines

- **Application platform:**  
  Applications must be submitted **online** exclusively via the official Campus France India portal:  
  [https://scholarship.institutfrancaisindia.in/](https://scholarship.institutfrancaisindia.in/)

- **Application deadline:**  
  - March 30, 2026 (23:59 IST) - Late or incomplete applications will NOT be accepted.

- **Interview scheduling:**  
  - Shortlisted candidates are required to attend a **mandatory online interview** scheduled between **April 13 and April 30, 2026**.  
  - Non-attendance or rescheduling requests are not permitted and result in automatic disqualification.

- **Result announcement:**  
  - Expected by late May 2026.

---

### 2.4 Required Documents for Scholarship Application and Format Requirements

Applicants must upload scanned copies as per Campus France India portal guidelines in PDF or JPG formats (clear, unencrypted, max 3 MB per file). The required documents include:

- **Valid passport copy:** colour scan of personal data page.

- **Proof of Indian citizenship or OCI card:** scanned copy.

- **Admission letter from the French university (Université Paris-Saclay) OR proof of application/confirmation email from the institution if admission letter is pending.**  
  - The letter or email should be unconditional (regular master’s admission).  
  - If only a confirmation email is available, official admission must be provided later.

- **Academic transcripts and certificates:**  
  - Full transcripts of all higher education years completed in India.  
  - Certificates of degrees/diplomas obtained.  
  - Must be clear and official scans.

- **Curriculum Vitae (CV):**  
  - Detailed and professional format comprising academic background, extracurricular achievements, internships, and projects.

- **Statement of Purpose (SOP):**  
  - Must clearly describe applicant academic motivations, program choice, career goals, and reasons for choosing France and Université Paris-Saclay.  
  - SOP is subject to rigorous plagiarism and AI-generated content checks; plagiarism or AI-generated text leads to immediate disqualification.

- **Letters of Recommendation (1 or 2):**  
  - Must be on official institutional letterhead, include full contact information, and uploaded by referees through the portal or provided scanned originals.  
  - Must include referee institutional email addresses for authentication purposes.  
  - Letters from referees without official institutional affiliation or non-verifiable formats will be rejected.

- **Passport-sized photograph:** recent, clear, professional.

- **Any additional documents as requested by the scholarship portal or university.**

---

### 2.5 Scholarship Benefits

Selected laureates (Boursiers du Gouvernement Français) receive:

- **Monthly living allowance:** €860 per month (~₹77,000, variable with exchange rates), intended to cover accommodation, food, and personal expenses.

- **Visa fee waiver:** Full exemption of the student visa application fee for Indian nationals.

- **Campus France application fee waiver:** Recipients are exempted from Campus France evaluation fees.

- **Health and social security coverage:** Mandatory student social security and health insurance is covered.

- **Accommodation assistance:** Priority access to CROUS university residences (subject to availability); facilitations in booking student restaurants (meals approx. €1).

- **Social and cultural integration:** Access to Campus France-organized activities.

- **Work authorization:** Scholarship holders may legally work up to the legal limit in France (~964 hours/year).

---

### 2.6 Scholarship Exclusions and Disqualifications

- Scholarship is **not renewable beyond two consecutive years**, and renewal depends on academic performance.

- The scholarship **does not cover**:
  - Tuition fees (generally low at French public universities),
  - Travel expenses to/from France, and
  - Personal incidental costs.

- Scholarship is revoked or applications disqualified for reasons including:

  - Failure to attend the interview within April 13 – April 30, 2026 window.
  - Plagiarism or AI-generated content in the SOP.
  - Holding concurrent scholarships or financial aids exceeding €600/month.
  - Undertaking internships, academic exchanges, or gap year activities inconsistent with full-time study.
  - Visa denial by French authorities.

---

### 2.7 Procedural Rules Specific to Indian Nationals and OCI Holders

- Applicants must strictly **verify and follow the online application steps as published on Campus France India portal**.

- Letters of recommendation must come from referees in Indian academic institutions or professional bodies recognizable in India, on official letterhead with institutional emails.

- Candidates holding **OCI cards** have the same eligibility and application procedures as Indian citizens.

- Applicants must verify documentation authenticity and upload **clear, official, and unaltered copies/scans**.

- All documents must be uploaded before the deadline; late uploads or incomplete files lead to automatic rejection.

- Applicants should be reachable and prepared for the interview in the scheduled time frame without exceptions.

---

### 2.8 Importance of Annual Verification of Requirements

- Both Université Paris-Saclay admission and Charpak Scholarship processes and requirements **undergo annual updates**.

- Applicants must consult the **official Université Paris-Saclay websites and Campus France India portals** every year before applying to confirm eligibility criteria, deadlines, required documents, and procedures.

- Failure to comply with the latest official instructions risks disqualification and loss of funding opportunities.

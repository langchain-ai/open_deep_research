# Industrial Automation Market for Manufacturing in Saudi Arabia (2015–2025)

## 1. Market Size, CAGR, and Robotics Penetration Rate

### Market Size and Growth

The industrial automation market in Saudi Arabia has experienced significant growth between 2015 and 2025, underpinned by increasing demand for modernization, productivity improvements, and diversification away from oil dependence. Key data points include:

- **Market Size (USD):**  
  - The broader industrial automation market was valued at approximately **USD 1.99 billion in 2024**, with expectations to reach **USD 3.72 billion by 2032**, growing at a **compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of about 10.4%** from 2025 to 2032.  
  - The smart manufacturing segment (a subset of industrial automation) reached **USD 5.7 billion in 2025**, expected to more than double to about **USD 11.95 billion by 2033**, reflecting a **9% CAGR** during 2026–2033.  
  - The **servo motors and drives market**, critical for robotics and automation, stood at **USD 128.3 million in 2025**, projected to hit **USD 178.9 million by 2034** with a CAGR of 3.76%.  
  - The **industrial robotics market** alone is estimated at **USD 178 million in 2025**, with projections of rapid growth at a CAGR between 11% and 17%, depending on the study and segment, fueled by automotive, electronics, and chemical sectors adopting robotics extensively.

- **Key Drivers:**  
  Growth is reinforced by government programs under Saudi Vision 2030, rising labor costs, technological innovation (Industry 4.0), and large-scale infrastructure projects like NEOM and Red Sea Development. Increasing adoption of AI, IoT, collaborative robots, and automation software support sustained expansion.

- **Industrial Automation Market Components:**  
  The market encompasses hardware (PLCs, drives, sensors, robotics), software (SCADA, MES, analytics), and services (system integration, maintenance). Systems such as Distributed Control Systems (DCS) and Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition (SCADA) dominate specific segments, while robotics leads in precision, flexibility, and speed for manufacturing lines.

### Robotics Penetration Rate

- Robotics penetration is commonly measured as the number of installed industrial robots per 10,000 manufacturing workers—a metric known as **robot density**.  
- Globally, robot density reached an average of 162 units per 10,000 manufacturing employees in 2023, with industrial leaders like South Korea exceeding 1,000 units.  
- Saudi Arabia’s **robot density metric is not explicitly published** by authoritative sources such as the International Federation of Robotics (IFR) or the Ministry of Industry and Mineral Resources. However, available data allows an estimation:  
  - Approximately **2,100 industrial robots** were installed in manufacturing by 2025 in Saudi Arabia, projected to reach **8,000 units by 2034**, with an estimated CAGR of 15.9% for robotics installations.  
  - Given Saudi’s manufacturing employment base (~500,000 workers estimated), robot density likely remains lower than mature markets but is rapidly increasing as automation intensifies in key sectors.

- Robotics adoption focuses on automotive, electrical & electronics, chemicals, food & beverage, and pharmaceuticals, using articulated robots, SCARA, and emerging collaborative robot technologies.

## 2. Impact of Saudi Vision 2030 on Robotics Demand

Saudi Vision 2030 is the Kingdom’s ambitious strategy to diversify its economy, reduce oil dependence, and promote industrial modernization. Several **specific policy measures**, investment targets, and regulatory updates have directly stimulated robotics and industrial automation demand:

### Policy Measures and Programs

- **Future Factories Program:**  
  A flagship initiative under Saudi Vision 2030 and the Ministry of Industry and Mineral Resources aims to transform approximately **4,000 factories** to Industry 4.0 maturity levels by introducing robotics, AI, IoT, and digital twin technologies. The program facilitates technology transfer, training, and digital infrastructure upgrades.  
  A strategic partnership with DBR77 (technology provider) focuses on mapping digitization roadmaps, robotics integration, and building Saudi competency in automation.

- **National Industrial Development and Logistics Program (NIDLP):**  
  Facilitates investment and infrastructure enhancement worth over **USD 400 billion by 2030**, fostering smart factories, robotics deployment in manufacturing supply chains, and logistics modernization.

- **Advanced Manufacturing Hub Strategy:**  
  Envisions creation of industrial hubs offering more than **800 investment opportunities valued at USD 273 billion**, emphasizing robotics and automation as critical components for competitiveness.

- **Localization and Industrial Sovereignty:**  
  Vision 2030 strongly promotes domestic manufacturing capabilities, supported by Public Investment Fund (PIF)-backed entities like Alat, which partners with global robotics makers and manufacturers, enhancing local capabilities and robotics production.

### Investment Targets and Regulatory Changes

- Robotics and AI investments have dramatically increased, with the AI robotics market expected to reach **USD 1.2 billion by 2030**, catalyzed by government incentives, research partnerships (e.g., KAUST robotics hubs), and private sector participation.

- Regulations now emphasize **energy efficiency and safety** in automated manufacturing. For example, the Saudi Energy Efficiency Program mandates adoption of high-efficiency servo motors and drives integral to robotic systems, supporting sustainability goals.

- The Ministry of Industry and Mineral Resources oversees certification and safety rules aligned with **SASO standards** for machinery and robotics safety, ensuring compliance with global best practices.

### Mega Projects as Catalysts

- **NEOM, Red Sea Development, Madar Smart City** and other giga projects are integrating advanced automation and robotics extensively into their smart manufacturing and construction processes, reinforcing local demand and creating reference ecosystems for Industry 4.0 technologies.

## 3. Named Project Awards to Siemens and ABB in NEOM and Red Sea Projects (Since 2021)

### Siemens Project Awards

- **Rumah 2 and Nairyah 2 Gas-Fired Power Plants:**  
  Siemens Energy was awarded a **USD 1.6 billion contract** for supplying core technologies and equipment to these plants, adding 3.6 GW power capacity to serve western and central Saudi Arabia, along with a **25-year maintenance agreement**. Local manufacturing at Siemens’ Dammam Hub supports key sustainability goals under Vision 2030.  
  [Source 12, 13, 14, 15]

- **Kudimi Substation Dynamic Reactive Power Compensator (STATCOM):**  
  Siemens Limited secured a **$41.4 million EPC contract** with Saudi Electricity Company for STATCOM installation improving voltage stability in southern Saudi Arabia.  
  [Source 2, 3]

- **Gas Turbines for NEOM Power Supply:**  
  USP&E supplied Siemens SGT-400 gas turbines to NEOM, aligning with plans to reach over 60 GW power capacity by 2030.  
  [Source 25]

### ABB Project Awards

- **Red Sea Project Renewable Energy System:**  
  ABB supplied synchronization panels (total 36 MW from 69 generators) featuring ABB Ability™ Energy and Asset Manager for digital monitoring and energy optimization, advancing the Red Sea Project’s goal of operating on 100% renewable energy.  
  [Source 16, 17, 18, 19]

- **Electric Vehicle Charging Infrastructure:**  
  ABB delivered over **1,200 EV charging stations** to Red Sea and AMAALA destinations, marking Saudi Arabia’s largest e-mobility initiative aligned with Vision 2030’s sustainability goals. Local manufacturing of chargers supports localization objectives.  
  [Source 20]

- **King Abdullah Financial District Substation (Riyadh):**  
  ABB won a **$89 million turnkey contract** to build a substation including design, installation, and commissioning.  
  [Source 25]

### Other Relevant Contract Awards

- NEOM awarded a **$2 billion contract** for the Oxagon-The Line rail connector (contractors not disclosed), plus tunneling and renewable energy infrastructure contracts.

- Red Sea Development Company awarded **over $4.5 billion in contracts** since 2021, with more than 70% to Saudi firms.

- Contract awards for accommodations and infrastructure include a **$266 million** contract to Al-Ayuni Investment for Amaala’s Triple Bay infrastructure.

The involvement of Siemens and ABB in these giga projects demonstrates their critical role in underpinning Saudi Arabia's industrial automation and sustainable infrastructure goals.

## 4. Practical Implications and Strategies for Sales Representatives Targeting Manufacturing Firms in Saudi Arabia

### 1. Align Offerings with Vision 2030 and Industrial Policy Priorities

Sales strategies should emphasize how automation solutions contribute directly to Saudi Arabia’s Vision 2030 goals. Demonstrate:

- Compliance with localization goals and the Future Factories Program’s Industry 4.0 objectives.  
- Contribution to sustainability targets such as energy efficiency and emissions reduction.  
- Ability to improve productivity, process optimization, and digital transformation, citing local success stories.

Highlight regulatory compliance (SASO standards, energy efficiency) and potential incentives for adopting advanced automation technologies.

### 2. Cultivate Personal Relationships and Provide Local Support

Saudi business culture values trust, personal interactions, and long-term partnerships:

- Invest in local presence, including technical support teams and training programs to build customer confidence.  
- Offer workforce upskilling aligned with technical roles in robotics and automation, addressing skill shortages.  
- Support through lifecycle services beyond initial sale, including maintenance, upgrades, and digital transformation consulting.

Fostering relationships through face-to-face meetings and demonstrating cultural sensitivity increases sales success probability.

### 3. Focus on Flexible, Scalable, and Integrated Solutions

Manufacturing firms seek solutions that offer:

- Scalability to evolve from partial to full automation as maturity grows.  
- Integration with AI, IoT, and ERP/MES systems for predictive maintenance and operational transparency.  
- Collaborative robots (cobots) for safety and flexibility in mixed human-robot environments.  
- Proven return on investment via cost savings, quality improvements, and downtime reduction.

Tailor product demonstrations and proposals to sector-specific needs (e.g., automotive, food processing, chemicals) and underline success metrics like throughput increases and energy savings.

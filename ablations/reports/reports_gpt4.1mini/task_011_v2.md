# Comprehensive Analysis of the Relationships and Business Strategies Between Evert Calderon (Mesa Group Consulting), SuperMoney, and Novae

This report presents a detailed examination of the partnerships and business strategies involving Evert Calderon’s Mesa Group Consulting and its affiliate relationships with SuperMoney and Novae. It clarifies each entity’s business model, financial service offerings, and the explicit mapping of Mesa Group Consulting’s website content with these partners. The report further distinguishes direct services Mesa delivers versus affiliate/referral-based services, analyzes the operational mechanics and revenue streams from these partnerships, and deepens the regulatory compliance and risk management discussion, explaining why Mesa strategically shifts licensing and liability to its partners.

## 1. Overview of Mesa Group Consulting and Its Business Model

Mesa Group Consulting, led by Evert Jafet Calderon, operates primarily as a business consulting and credit services firm based in Bakersfield, California. The company offers a blend of direct client services and affiliate marketing-driven referral offerings, emphasizing credit improvement, business funding, and financial education.

### Services Directly Delivered by Mesa Group Consulting

- **Credit Repair:** Mesa Group offers direct credit restoration services through its proprietary Mesa360 Credit System™, including:
  - Dispute management across all three credit bureaus  
  - Legal support for Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA) violations  
  - 1-on-1 coaching and strategic credit building programs  
  - DIY credit restoration software with AI-driven dispute tools  

- **Debt Relief Negotiation:** Mesa directly negotiates unsecured debt to reduce monthly payments and free up cash flow.

- **Business and Financial Consulting:** Provides strategic business planning, marketing, bookkeeping, sales training, tax preparation support, and payroll services.

### Referral and Affiliate-Delivered Services

Mesa Group Consulting leverages affiliate partnerships to extend its service offerings without underwriting or direct financing. Key external relationships include:

- **SuperMoney:** Facilitates consumer financial product referrals including credit cards, personal loans, auto loan refinancing, student loan refinancing, and debt consolidation loans.

- **Novae LLC:** Enables access to a broader fintech product suite including debt relief, trust and will estate planning, life insurance, business credit, and business debt relief programs.

This blended model allows Mesa Group to monetize web traffic and client referrals while focusing internal resources on credit repair and consulting services.

## 3. SuperMoney: Business Model, Financial Services, and Affiliate Partnership Mechanics

### Business Model and Financial Services

SuperMoney is a California-based fintech marketplace (founded 2013) connecting consumers to multiple lenders and financial products through a single application interface:

- Consumer products include personal loans ($500–$100,000), auto loans and refinancing, student loan refinancing, debt consolidation loans, credit cards, mortgages, and insurance comparisons.
- The platform protects user data with SOC 2 Type II compliance, PCI DSS security, and encrypted US-based data infrastructure.
- It performs soft credit pulls for pre-qualification, avoiding adverse credit impacts unless a user proceeds with a lender’s hard pull.
- SuperMoney does not underwrite loans but acts as a market facilitator and comparison service.
- Its reputation is supported by repeated Inc. 5000 listings, Fintech Breakthrough awards, and inclusion on Deloitte’s Technology Fast 500.

### Affiliate Partnership Program

- The affiliate model is primarily CPA-based, paying Mesa Group Consulting per qualified lead or application (e.g., $15 for credit reports, up to $750 per funded personal loan).
- Affiliates receive turnkey tools: embeddable offer engines, co-branded loan marketplaces, and pre-qualification forms.
- SuperMoney’s program supports multiple verticals aligning with Mesa’s core markets: credit monitoring, credit cards, personal and auto loans, student loan refinancing.
- Lenders integrated within SuperMoney bear all required licensing, underwriting, and compliance responsibility.

### Operational Roles and User Experience

- Mesa Group integrates SuperMoney’s tools to allow website visitors to compare offers seamlessly without leaving the site environment, thus optimizing conversion.
- User flows involve visiting Mesa’s site, selecting a category (e.g., personal loans), completing a single application form embedded via SuperMoney, and receiving real-time loan offers from multiple lenders.
- Funding occurs offsite via lending partners, with SuperMoney serving as an intermediary and affiliate management platform.

### Compliance and Regulatory Risk Management

- SuperMoney holds regulatory responsibility for proper licensing of lending partners and compliance with financial services regulations.
- All lending underwriting, credit decisioning, licensing, and bonding are managed by SuperMoney’s vetted lender network.
- Mesa Group functions as an affiliate publisher, legally protected by appending disclaimers that it does not make lending decisions, provide financing, or handle client funds.
- SuperMoney’s SOC 2 Type II compliance and privacy standards provide an added layer of risk mitigation protecting Mesa from customer data liability.
- Contractual agreements with affiliates include indemnity clauses limiting Mesa’s exposure for regulatory infractions related to lending or data breaches.

This regulatory risk transfer is strategic for Mesa, enabling it to focus solely on client acquisition and education rather than the complex, costly compliance responsibilities of a licensed financial services provider.

## 4. Novae LLC: Business Model, Financial Services, Affiliate Programs, and Strategic Partnership Details

### Novae Business Model and Financial Service Offerings

- Founded in 2014 in Georgia, Novae specializes in fintech-driven financial inclusion for underserved consumers and small businesses.
- Their offerings span personal and business financial services, including:
  - Debt relief programs reducing monthly payments and accelerating payoff  
  - Trust and will estate planning, with integrations of digital estate planning platforms offering downloadable, attorney-supported wills and related documents  
  - Life insurance shopping through networked carriers  
  - Business credit building programs and business funding ranging up to $10 million, including SBA and CDFI loans, and invoice factoring  
  - Tax preparation software platform targeting entrepreneurs and tax preparers, offering branded apps and year-round financial services access  
  - No-interest payday loans and cash advances up to $7,000 for consumers  

### Affiliate, White-Label, and Co-Brand Programs

- Novae operates a multi-tiered Affiliate Program allowing individuals and businesses to earn commissions and residual income through direct sales and team-building.
- Affiliate types include:
  - **Affiliate Basic:** Entry-level marketing and product referral program.  
  - **Affiliate Pro:** Includes credit monitoring, dispute tools, team-building, and residual benefits.
- Novae also offers White Label Partner Programs, enabling entrepreneurs and professionals (attorneys, financial advisors, loan officers) to license Novae’s fintech platform and operate under their branding with comprehensive support: websites, billing, merchant accounts, marketing, and training.
- Co-Brand Programs provide turnkey fintech websites with marketing automation to partners, expanding product reach.
- Church-based partnership programs leverage community outreach without jeopardizing 501(c)(3) status.
  
### Operational Mechanics of Partner Relationships with Mesa Group Consulting

- Mesa Group likely operates as a Novae affiliate or co-brand partner, promoting Novae’s offerings via website pages aligned with Novae’s product categories (debt relief, trust & wills, life insurance, business credit).
- User journeys on Mesa’s site lead to Novae-powered fintech portals or enrollment pages that are either white-labeled or directly affiliate-marketed, funneling clients into Novae’s platform for service delivery.
- Digital estate planning services offered by Novae reflect partnerships with established digital will and trust document platforms, providing users with attorney-backed legal documents electronically delivered and optionally notarized/shipped.
- Business funding offers integrate Novae’s multi-lender financial services platform using proprietary software and waterfall approval algorithms to boost approval rates and credit access for clients.
- Mesa benefits financially from upfront commissions and residual income via revenue-sharing agreements intrinsic to Novae’s multi-level compensation plan.
  
### Regulatory and Compliance Risk Management

- Novae is a Direct Selling Association (DSA) member, complying with stringent ethics, marketing, and operational standards; affiliates must adhere to robust policies against deceptive marketing and unauthorized income claims.
- Licensing, bonding (where applicable), and regulatory oversight for financial products remain Novae’s responsibility and/or their lending partners.
- Novae’s Affiliate Policies strictly define affiliate conduct, require identity verification, independent contractor status affirmations, and specify indemnification protecting Novae and partners from affiliate-related liabilities.
- White Label and co-brand partners undergo vetting processes ensuring compliance readiness, with Novae providing operational compliance infrastructure.
- This approach strategically shields Mesa Group from direct licensure, bonding requirements, and liability tied to lending, insurance, or debt relief services, limiting Mesa’s risk while allowing revenue from affiliate commissions.
- Novae’s use of compliant fintech platforms and standardized contracts supports legally sound business expansion through third-party partners like Mesa.

## 7. Regulatory Compliance and Risk Mitigation Analysis

### Licensure, Bonding, and Liability Shifts

- Mesa Group Consulting acts as a non-licensed affiliate marketer and advisor, explicitly avoiding direct lending, underwriting, or financial product issuance.
- SuperMoney and Novae each maintain necessary licenses or ensure their lending and insurance partners hold required state and federal licenses.
- Licensing and bonding obligations related to lending and insurance products rest exclusively with SuperMoney, Novae, or their respective underwriting institutions.
- Mesa Group’s contractual affiliate agreements include indemnity clauses that transfer liability for compliance breaches, underwriting errors, or consumer disputes back to the partner companies.
- This liability framework protects Mesa from regulatory fines, consumer financial liability, and bond claims, mitigating business risk.
- Both partners have documented compliance programs, security certifications (e.g., SOC 2 Type II for SuperMoney), and internal controls (marketing standards for Novae) to solidify this risk transfer.

### Strategic Importance for Mesa Group Consulting

- By eschewing licensure and licensing responsibilities, Mesa preserves flexible operational capacity, reduces legal and capital expenditures, and prevents reputational risk from regulatory violations.
- The affiliate/referral model allows Mesa to scale product offerings rapidly and diversify revenue sources with minimal compliance overhead.
- Trusted risk management and compliance practices of partners ensure Mesa's compliance with FTC affiliate marketing disclosures and financial services law.
- This strategic setup makes Mesa Group Consulting primarily a platform and marketing hub rather than a regulated financial services provider, enabling focus on growth and client acquisition.

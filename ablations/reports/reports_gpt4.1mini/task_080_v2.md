## 1. Breakdown of Fund Structure, Underlying Holdings, and Expense Ratios

### 1.1 Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund

- **Fund Structure & Domicile:**
  - An Indian-domiciled open-ended equity mutual fund launched on August 26, 2009.
  - Primarily invests approximately 96.9% in the underlying **JPMorgan Funds - JF Greater China Fund**.
  - The **JPMorgan Greater China Fund** is domiciled in **Luxembourg** and registered across markets including Hong Kong, Singapore, UK, Germany, and France.
  - The underlying JPMorgan fund offers multiple share classes, notably the A (acc) USD share class launched in 2005, denominated in USD, HKD, EUR, and GBP.

- **Share Class Allocations:**
  - Edelweiss offers Growth and Direct Growth plans; the direct plan typically invests in the “A (acc) USD” share class of JPMorgan Greater China Fund.
  - Minimum investment: ₹100; exit load of 1% if redeemed within 90 days.

- **Expense Ratios:**
  - Total Expense Ratio (TER) for Edelweiss GMC Offshore Fund:
    - **Regular Plan:** ~2.40% (includes underlying fund expenses).
    - **Direct Plan:** ~1.51%.
  - The embedded expense ratios of the JPMorgan underlying fund range between 0.91% and 1.76%, with the actual pass-through portion in Edelweiss approximately 0.76%.
  - The overall fees include management fees, operational costs, and GST.

- **Portfolio Concentration & Holdings:**
  - Dominantly invested in Financial sector (~98.47%) as an indirect consequence of JPMorgan Greater China Fund’s allocation.
  - Top underlying holdings include Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing Company (~9.8%), Tencent (~9.2%), Alibaba (~6.4%), and Xiaomi (~4.5%).
  - Sector tilt is predominantly Technology, Consumer Discretionary, and Communication Services at the underlying level.
  - Fund managers: Bhavesh Jain (since Sept 2019) and Bharat Lahoti (since Oct 2021).

## 3. Investment Recommendations Anchored in Analytical Frameworks

### 3.1 Allocation Strategies: Core-Satellite Approach

- **Core:**  
  Use a broad global equity index fund (e.g., MSCI ACWI ETF) as the portfolio foundation to gain diversified exposure and stability.

- **Satellite 1 (Regional Focus):**  
  Allocate 10-20% to Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund as a satellite bet on Greater China equity recovery and growth potential, exposing portfolio to regional/financial sector themes.

- **Satellite 2 (Thematic Innovation Focus):**  
  Allocate 10-20% to DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF to capture high growth from disruptive global sectors like AI, biotech, semiconductor, and fintech.

- **Rationale:**  
  This splits risk across geographic and thematic dimensions — regional concentration risk (Edelweiss) and sectoral innovation risk (DSP), while maintaining diversified core stability.

### 3.2 Historical Performance attributions

- **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund:**  
  - Short-term rallies tied to China's 2025-2026 easing of regulatory crackdowns and fiscal stimulus (e.g., slow bull market momentum post-2025 regulatory adjustments).  
  - Longer term underperformance due to episodic regulatory tightening (e.g., 2018-2022 periods of market uncertainty, trade war impacts, tech sector clampdowns).  
  - Risk amplified by geopolitical tensions and macroeconomic slowdowns in Greater China.

- **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF:**  
  - Benefited from strong secular trends in global innovation: AI adoption, biotechnology breakthroughs, semiconductor demand, fintech innovation.  
  - Performance shaped by global investor enthusiasm in innovation and growth sectors, partially tempered by supply chain disruptions and geopolitical tech conflicts.  
  - Emphasized regions include US and China innovation hubs, with resilience from geographic diversification mitigating isolated regional risks.

## Conclusion

The **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** offers a focused regional exposure through the JPMorgan Greater China Fund with a financial sector tilt. It features moderately lower expenses in direct plans but carries high sector concentration risk and regulatory volatility. Its short-term performance is strong but longer-term returns are inconsistent.

In contrast, the **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni Fund of Funds** delivers diversified thematic global exposure focused on innovation sectors such as AI, technology, biotechnology, and fintech. With a higher TER reflecting multi-layered underlying fees, it offers stronger trailing 3-year returns and better risk-adjusted metrics but with limited long-term history.

**For shorter investment horizons (~2 years), investors with high risk tolerance and a focus on Greater China growth themes may find Edelweiss more appropriate, albeit with meaningful volatility and exit load considerations. For medium to long-term horizons (5+ years), the DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FoF, with its broad thematic diversification and superior 3-year performance, is the preferred choice for investors seeking sustained participation in global innovation trends.**

Implementing a **core-satellite allocation strategy**, combining broad global equity funds as core with these two funds as satellite allocations—Edelweiss for regional growth and DSP fund for thematic innovation—allows investors to balance risk and pursue diversified growth avenues.

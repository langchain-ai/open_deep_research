# 9-Day Thailand Itinerary for 7 Travelers from Hyderabad (Budget: ₹70,000 per Person)

## Executive Summary

This comprehensive itinerary provides a detailed 9-day Thailand trip for a group of 7 travelers from Hyderabad, India, with a total budget of ₹70,000 per person (₹18,000 for flights, ₹52,000 remaining for on-ground expenses). Based on extensive research of current 2026 prices, this itinerary is logistically feasible, includes at least 2 days in rural/countryside areas, offers a mix of cultural, adventure, and nightlife experiences, and fits comfortably within the ₹52,000 per person budget (with an estimated ₹39,200 per person on-ground cost, leaving a ₹12,800 buffer).

The recommended route is: **Hyderabad → Bangkok (2 nights) → Kanchanaburi countryside (2 nights) → Chiang Mai (2 nights) → Chiang Mai countryside/Pai (2 nights) → Bangkok → Hyderabad**, with domestic flights connecting Bangkok to Chiang Mai.

---

## 1. Flight Options: Hyderabad ↔ Thailand (Round Trip Under ₹18,000)

### Primary Recommendation: Hyderabad to Bangkok Direct Flights

The most cost-effective strategy is to fly directly from Hyderabad (HYD) to Bangkok (BKK or DMK), as this is the only route within the ₹18,000 round-trip budget.

| Airline | Route | Estimated Round-Trip Price | Flight Duration | Notes |
|---------|-------|---------------------------|-----------------|-------|
| **IndiGo (6E)** | HYD → BKK (Direct) | **₹15,000 – ₹17,500** | 3 hours 35 minutes | Best value, reliable budget carrier |
| **Air India (AI)** | HYD → BKK (Direct) | **₹15,946 – ₹19,010** | 3 hours 30 minutes | Full service, meals included |
| **Thai AirAsia (FD)** | HYD → DMK (Direct) | **₹14,000 – ₹16,000** | ~3 hours 45 minutes | Lowest base fare; baggage costs extra |
| **Air India Express** | HYD → BKK (Direct) | **₹16,000 – ₹18,000** | ~3 hours 40 minutes | 30 kg free checked baggage on international |

**Key Finding:** Direct flights from Hyderabad to Bangkok are available and fit within the ₹18,000 budget when booked 3–6 weeks in advance [1][2][9].

**Booking Tips:**
- Book 3–6 weeks before departure for best prices; the absolute cheapest fares are found 12 weeks in advance [4]
- Fly on **Tuesday, Wednesday, or Thursday** for savings of 15–20% over weekend departures [1][5]
- Choose **evening/night departures** — these are ₹1,500–₹2,000 cheaper than morning flights [1][5]
- Best months for lowest fares: **June, July, September** (off-peak season) [10]
- Avoid Indian holiday periods (Diwali, Dussehra, Christmas) when prices spike

**Thai AirAsia (for domestic flights):** 7 kg cabin only in base fare; 20 kg checked baggage purchased separately for approximately ₹500–₹1,000 extra [11][12].

#### Day 1: Arrive Bangkok (HYD → BKK)

**Morning/Afternoon:** Take direct flight from Hyderabad (HYD) to Bangkok (BKK). IndiGo flights depart around 05:40 and arrive 10:55 (3h 45m) or evening departures [24].

- **3BR Airbnb near BTS Nana** (Sukhumvit Soi 8): Sleeps 7, ~3,500–5,000 THB/night with pool access, 3 minutes walk to BTS [1]

#### Day 2: Bangkok — Temples & Culture

**Morning (8:00 AM – 12:00 PM):**
- **Grand Palace & Wat Phra Kaew** (Emerald Buddha): 500 THB per person. Open 8:30 AM – 3:30 PM. Dress code: shoulders and knees must be covered [12]
- **Wat Pho** (Reclining Buddha): 300 THB per person. Located 10-minute walk from Grand Palace. Known for the 46-meter-long reclining Buddha and traditional Thai massage school [12]

**Afternoon (1:00 PM – 4:00 PM):**
- **Wat Arun** (Temple of Dawn): 200 THB per person. Take a short ferry across the Chao Phraya River (15 THB) [12]
- **Lunch at street food stalls** near Tha Tien Pier: 60–100 THB for boat noodles, pad thai, or som tam

**Evening (6:00 PM – 11:00 PM):**
- **Asiatique Night Market** — riverside market with shopping, dining, and live entertainment (free entry, take BTS to Saphan Taksin then free shuttle boat)
- Alternative: **Chinatown (Yaowarat)** for the best street food in Bangkok — grilled seafood, dim sum, mango sticky rice (40–120 THB per dish)

**Optional Nightlife:**
- **RCA (Royal City Avenue)** — Route 66 Club: 300 THB entrance includes 200 THB drink coupon; three sections (K-pop, EDM, hip-hop) [4]
- **Eagle Nest Bar at Sala Arun** — premium rooftop with Wat Arun sunset views; cocktails 300–500 THB [1]

#### Day 4: Kanchanaburi Countryside (Rural Day 2) — Erawan National Park

**Full-Day Adventure (7:00 AM – 5:00 PM):**

**Option A: Erawan Waterfall (Recommended)**
- **Erawan National Park**: 7-tier waterfall with turquoise emerald pools perfect for swimming
- **Entry fee**: 300 THB per person (foreigner rate) [3]
- **Transport**: Take local bus from Kanchanaburi bus station to Erawan (50 THB per person, ~2 hours), OR hire private songthaew/taxi for the group (~1,200–1,800 THB total, split 7 ways = ~170–250 THB each) [3]
- **Hiking**: 2 km trail through jungle to the 7th tier. Wear comfortable shoes (not sandals) — steeper after tier 5
- **Swimming**: Tiers 2, 3, and 5 are best for swimming. Bring swimwear
- **Lunch**: Food available at park entrance — Thai dishes ~200 THB per person [3]
- **Return**: Last bus back to Kanchanaburi at 5:00 PM. Bring cash — no ATMs in the park [3]

**Option B: Death Railway & Hellfire Pass (Historical)**
- Ride the historic **Death Railway train** through scenic countryside along the River Kwai (100 THB for the scenic stretch)
- Visit **Hellfire Pass Museum** (free entry) and walking trail — sobering WWII history
- Duration: Half-day, combine with afternoon visit to **Somboon Legacy Elephant Sanctuary** (ethical, hands-off)

**Evening (6:00 PM – 9:00 PM):**
- Return to Kanchanaburi town
- Dinner at **night market** near River Kwai Bridge (seasonal) or **JJ Night Market**
- Try local specialties: grilled river fish, tom yam, mango sticky rice

## 8. Practical Information & Tips

### Thailand Entry Requirements (2026)

- **Visa**: Indian passport holders enjoy **visa-free entry for up to 60 days** in 2026 [7]
- **Thailand Digital Arrival Card (TDAC)**: Must be completed online within 72 hours before departure — **free of charge** [4][7]
- **Passport validity**: Required to be valid for at least 6 months beyond arrival date [6]
- **Proof of funds**: Be prepared to show 10,000–20,000 THB upon entry [7]
- **New Tourist Entry Fee**: 300 THB for air arrivals (expected Q2–Q3 2026) [13]

### SIM Cards & Connectivity

| Provider | Plan | Price | Validity | Where to Buy |
|----------|------|-------|----------|-------------|
| **AIS** | 15 GB + calls | **249 THB** | 7 days | Airport, 7-Eleven [6] |
| **DTAC** | 10 GB + calls | **199 THB** | 7 days | Airport, 7-Eleven [6] |
| **True Move H** | 8 GB + calls | **199 THB** | 7 days | Airport, 7-Eleven [6] |
| **AIS (budget)** | 2 GB | **49 THB** | 7 days | 7-Eleven [7] |

**Tip:** AIS has the best coverage in rural areas (Kanchanaburi, Pai) [6]. Passport required for purchase.

### Health & Safety

- **Drinking water**: Bottled water from 7-Eleven (7–15 THB). Do not drink tap water
- **Motion sickness**: Bring medication for the Pai minivan ride (762 curves)
- **Mosquito repellent**: Essential in Kanchanaburi and rural areas — carry DEET-based repellent
- **Sunscreen**: Strong sun in Thailand, especially at Erawan Waterfall
- **Travel insurance**: Highly recommended — budget 300–500 THB per person for 9 days
- **Pharmacies**: Available everywhere, 7-Elevens stock basic medications

### Packing List Essentials

| Item | Reason |
|------|--------|
| Modest clothing (shoulders/knees covered) | Temple dress code enforced |
| Swimsuit | Erawan Waterfall, hot springs, hotel pools |
| Comfortable walking shoes | Grand Palace, Erawan hiking, temple visits |
| Flip-flops/sandals | Casual wear, beach, hostels |
| Rain jacket/umbrella | June–October is rainy season |
| Power bank | Long travel days |
| Reusable water bottle | Stay hydrated, fill at filtered water stations |
| Motion sickness medication | Pai minivan journey |
| Passport copies | Backup for visa/immigration purposes |

### Money Matters

- **ATM fees**: Thai ATMs charge **220 THB per withdrawal** — withdraw large amounts to minimize fees [1]
- **Exchange rate**: 1 THB ≈ ₹2.4 (as of mid-2026)
- **Cash vs card**: Street food, night markets, and local transport require cash. Cards accepted at larger restaurants and shops
- **Carry enough cash**: Erawan Waterfall has no ATMs — bring cash for entry and food [3]
- **Tipping**: Not mandatory but appreciated — small change or 20–50 THB for good service

### Best Time to Travel

| Season | Months | Conditions | Impact on Budget |
|--------|--------|------------|------------------|
| **High season** | Nov–Feb | Cool, dry, pleasant | Prices 30–50% higher |
| **Shoulder season** | Mar–May | Hot, some smog | Moderate prices |
| **Low season** | **Jun–Oct** | Rainy, but manageable | **Best prices: 30–50% cheaper** |

**Recommendation:** June–September offers the best combination of low prices, fewer crowds, and still-pleasant conditions (rain is usually short afternoon showers). October is the cheapest month to fly from Hyderabad to Bangkok [1].

## 10. Group Travel Tips for 7 People

### Booking Strategies

1. **Book group activities together** — Cooking classes, elephant sanctuaries, and tours often have per-person pricing but can offer group discounts
2. **Private transport is often cheaper than 7 individual tickets** — For Bangkok→Kanchanaburi, a private minivan (1,500–2,500 THB) splits to 215–360 THB per person — comparable to bus (150–350 THB) but more convenient
3. **Use 12Go Asia** for booking buses, trains, and minivans in advance — especially for popular routes like Chiang Mai→Pai
4. **Hotel/hostel booking tips**: 
   - Airbnb ≥3 bedrooms is ideal for 7 people — book one property instead of multiple rooms
   - Hostels with private 8-bed dorms allow the entire group to stay together
   - Book the first 2–3 nights in advance, then remain flexible for later nights [4]

### Communication & Coordination

- **Create a shared WhatsApp/Telegram group** for the trip
- **Use Grab/Bolt apps** for taxis — fare is set, no negotiation needed, split easily
- **Carry a portable WiFi hotspot** (rent at Suvarnabhumi Airport, ~200 THB/day) — keeps all 7 connected
- **Designate a "budget keeper"** who tracks spending and ensures the group stays within per-person limits

### Safety for Groups

- **Keep photocopies of passports** — store separately from originals
- **Store luggage collectively** when moving between cities — use luggage storage services in Bangkok/Chiang Mai (100–200 THB per bag per day)
- **Stay together in nightlife areas** — especially Khao San Road and RCA (pickpocketing can occur)
- **Drink responsibly** — bucket drinks on Khao San Road are potent; space out consumption

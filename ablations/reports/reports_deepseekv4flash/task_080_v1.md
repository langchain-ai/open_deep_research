# Comprehensive Comparison: Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund vs. DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF

## Executive Summary

This report provides a detailed comparative analysis of two distinct offshore mutual fund offerings available to Indian investors: the **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** and the **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF**. While both funds provide international diversification, they differ significantly in their investment mandate, geographic focus, sector allocation, risk profile, and cost structure. The Edelweiss fund is a concentrated, single-country equity fund focused on China and Hong Kong markets, whereas the DSP fund is a globally diversified fund-of-funds with a thematic tilt toward innovation and technology. The data for this analysis is based on the most recent available fund factsheets and disclosures as of March 2025 (the latest complete quarter-end), with historical return data extended to capture the closest available periods.

## 1. Portfolio Composition & Concentration

### 1.1 Geographic Allocation (Country/Region Exposure)

**Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund**

The fund is exclusively focused on the Greater China region. As per its most recent portfolio disclosure (February 2025), the geographic allocation is heavily concentrated:

- **Hong Kong:** ~60–70% of the portfolio (primary listing for most Chinese companies)
- **China (A-shares via Stock Connect):** ~20–25%
- **Taiwan:** ~5–10%
- **Cash & Equivalents:** ~2–5%

The fund invests in equities of companies that are incorporated, listed, or have their principal business activities in China, Hong Kong, or Taiwan. There is no exposure to any other geographic region.

**DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF**

This fund-of-funds invests predominantly in overseas mutual funds and ETFs, providing broad global diversification. Based on its March 2025 portfolio disclosure:

- **United States:** ~55–65% (dominant exposure, primarily through the underlying innovation-focused funds)
- **Developed Asia (Japan, South Korea, Taiwan):** ~15–20%
- **Europe (Germany, Netherlands, UK):** ~10–15%
- **Emerging Markets (China, India, Brazil):** ~5–10%
- **Cash & Other Assets:** ~3–5%

The geographic spread is significantly wider than the Edelweiss fund, reflecting the global mandate of the underlying FOF structure.

### 1.2 Sector Allocation (Industry Breakdown)

**Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund**

The sector allocation reflects the composition of the Chinese equity market, with a heavy tilt toward:

- **Internet & Technology:** ~35–40% (Tencent, Alibaba, Meituan, Baidu)
- **Financials (Banks & Insurance):** ~15–20% (China Construction Bank, ICBC, Ping An)
- **Consumer Discretionary:** ~10–15% (Li Ning, JD.com, NIO)
- **Healthcare/Pharmaceuticals:** ~5–10%
- **Real Estate:** ~3–5%
- **Industrials/Manufacturing:** ~3–5%
- **Energy & Materials:** ~2–3%

The fund is predominantly growth-oriented given its tech exposure, but maintains significant allocation to value-oriented state-owned banks and financials.

**DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF**

The sector allocation is determined by the underlying global innovation-oriented funds and ETFs. Based on the latest available portfolio:

- **Technology/Semiconductors:** ~30–35% (NVIDIA, TSMC, ASML, Apple)
- **Healthcare/Biotech:** ~15–20%
- **Consumer Discretionary (E-commerce, Digital Media):** ~10–15% (Amazon, Meta, Netflix)
- **Industrials (Automation, Robotics):** ~8–12%
- **Financials (Fintech):** ~5–8%
- **Communication Services:** ~5–8%
- **Clean Energy & Renewables:** ~3–5%
- **Other (Defense, Aerospace):** ~2–3%

The DSP fund has a much stronger technology and innovation bias compared to the Edelweiss fund, which is more broadly structured around the Chinese economy.

## 2. Overlap in Top 20 Holdings

### 2.1 Direct Overlap Analysis

**Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** holds individual Chinese equities (Tencent, Alibaba, Meituan, etc.) directly.

**DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** does **not** hold individual equities directly. Instead, it holds units of other mutual funds and ETFs. Therefore, at the highest level of the portfolio, there is **zero overlap** between the top 20 holdings of the two funds.

### 2.2 Indirect Overlap Through Underlying Funds

While the DSP fund does not directly hold the same stocks as the Edelweiss fund, some of the **underlying ETFs and funds within the DSP FOF** may hold shares of Chinese companies like Tencent and Alibaba. For example:

- The **iShares Global Tech ETF** (top holding in DSP FOF) holds ~2–3% in Tencent and ~1.5–2% in Alibaba.
- The **Invesco QQQ Trust** holds a small allocation to Chinese ADRs (though these have been reduced in recent years).
- The **ARK Innovation ETF** holds a small position in certain Chinese tech companies.

However, these indirect holdings represent a very small fraction of the DSP fund's total portfolio (estimated at 1–3% of total AUM). **There is no significant overlap between the two portfolios.**

### 2.3 Overlap Percentage Calculation

**Direct Overlap (Top 20 Holdings Level):** 0%
**Indirect Overlap (Estimated at Portfolio Level):** <2%

For practical investment purposes, the two funds can be considered **non-overlapping** portfolios. An investor holding both funds would have complementary, rather than duplicative, exposure.

## 3. Expense Ratios & Historical Returns

### 3.1 Expense Ratios

**Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund**

| Fee Component | Rate |
|--------------|------|
| Total Expense Ratio (TER) – Regular Plan | ~1.75–2.10% per annum |
| Total Expense Ratio (TER) – Direct Plan | ~1.00–1.20% per annum |
| Management Fees | Included in TER |
| Exit Load | 1% if redeemed within 3 months; Nil after 3 months |

*Note: Actual TER varies slightly based on fund size and asset levels. The figures above reflect the most recent disclosures (February 2025 factsheet).*

**DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF**

| Fee Component | Rate |
|--------------|------|
| Total Expense Ratio (TER) – Regular Plan | ~2.25–2.65% per annum |
| Total Expense Ratio (TER) – Direct Plan | ~1.30–1.55% per annum |
| Management Fees | Included in TER |
| Expense of Underlying Funds (estimated) | ~0.50–0.75% additional (embedded in underlying fund NAVs) |
| Exit Load | 1% if redeemed within 3 months; Nil after 3 months |

**Fee Comparison Summary:**
- The **DSP FOF has a higher expense ratio** than the Edelweiss fund by roughly 0.35–0.60% on a stated TER basis.
- Furthermore, the DSP FOF incurs **additional embedded expenses** from the underlying funds/ETFs it holds (typically 0.50–0.75% p.a.), which are not captured in the stated TER. These expenses reduce the net returns available to investors.
- The **effective total cost** for the DSP fund is estimated at **1.80–2.30% p.a. for Direct Plan** and **2.75–3.40% p.a. for Regular Plan**.
- The Edelweiss fund is a **direct equity fund** with no layered fee structure, making it **cheaper overall**.

**Expense Drag Impact:**
- Over a 2-year period, the DSP fund's higher fees could erode an additional **1.5–2.5% of total returns** compared to the Edelweiss fund.
- Over a 5-year period, the compounding effect of higher fees could reduce returns by **4–7% cumulatively**.

## 4. Suitability Recommendation

### 4.1 Assessment Framework

To recommend suitability for different investment horizons, we evaluate each fund on:
1. **Return consistency** – Can the investor rely on positive returns?
2. **Volatility / Risk** – What is the potential for drawdowns?
3. **Liquidity** – Are there lock-in periods or exit loads?
4. **Expense drag** – How much do costs eat into returns?
5. **Horizon-specific factors** – Does the fund's strategy align with the time frame?

### 4.2 Recommendation for a 2-Year Investment Horizon

**More Suitable Fund: Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund**

**Rationale:**

1. **Lower Total Expense (Direct Plan):** At ~1.00–1.20% TER (Direct), the Edelweiss fund has significantly lower expenses than the DSP FOF's effective cost of ~1.80–2.30%. Over a short 2-year period, this expense differential directly impacts net returns.

2. **No Layered Fees:** The Edelweiss fund avoids the double-layer fee structure inherent in FOFs (fund management fees + underlying fund expenses). For a 2-year horizon, fee optimization is critical because the compounding benefit of lower fees is less impactful over short periods, but the absolute drag is still meaningful.

3. **Potential for Cyclical Recovery:** Chinese equities have been severely depressed since 2021. If the Chinese government's stimulus measures (announced in late 2024/early 2025) lead to a sustained recovery, the Edelweiss fund could deliver outsized returns in a 2-year window. This is a **higher-risk, higher-reward** trade-off that may appeal to aggressive investors.

4. **Short-Term Exit Flexibility:** The exit load structure (1% if redeemed within 3 months, Nil after) is favorable for a 2-year holding period.

**However, significant risks remain:**
- Chinese regulatory and geopolitical risks are elevated.
- The fund's single-country concentration means any negative China-specific event (e.g., Taiwan tensions, further regulatory crackdowns, property sector contagion) could cause sharp losses in a 2-year timeframe.
- The fund is unsuitable for risk-averse investors with a 2-year horizon.

**Verdict for 2-Year Horizon:**
- **For Aggressive Investors:** Edelweiss Greater China Fund (higher potential upside, lower fees, but higher risk)
- **For Conservative/Moderate Investors:** Neither fund is ideal for a 2-year horizon due to equity market volatility. A short-term debt fund or balanced advantage fund would be more appropriate.

### 4.3 Recommendation for a 5-Year Investment Horizon

**More Suitable Fund: DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF**

**Rationale:**

1. **Superior Long-Term Return Track Record:** The DSP fund's 5-year annualized return of +12.3% (Direct Plan) versus the Edelweiss fund's +2.4% is a substantial difference. The DSP fund has demonstrated the ability to compound wealth consistently over medium-term horizons.

2. **Diversification Benefits:** Over 5 years, the DSP fund's global diversification across geographies (US, Europe, Asia) and sectors (tech, healthcare, industrials) provides a more resilient portfolio. The Edelweiss fund's single-country concentration introduces uncompensated risk over longer periods.

3. **Return Consistency:** The DSP fund has delivered positive returns in 4 out of the last 5 calendar years (only negative year was 2022, which was a global tech downturn). The Edelweiss fund has seen multiple negative years due to China-specific headwinds.

4. **Innovation Theme Alignment:** The 5-year horizon aligns well with the structural growth trends in global innovation (AI, cloud computing, biotechnology, renewable energy). These themes are expected to unfold over 3–7 years, making the DSP fund's thematic focus appropriate for a 5-year holding period.

5. **Volatility Management:** While both funds invest in equities, the DSP fund has lower maximum drawdowns (~25–30%) compared to the Edelweiss fund (~55–60%). For a 5-year horizon, recovering from a 60% drawdown is mathematically challenging and could leave the investor with subpar returns.

6. **Expense Consideration:** Although the DSP fund has higher fees, the outperformance over 5 years (+12.3% vs +2.4%) more than compensates for the expense drag. The higher fee is justified by superior net returns.

**Potential Concerns for 5-Year Horizon with DSP FOF:**
- Elevated technology sector valuations (historically high P/E ratios in US tech stocks)
- Potential for a global recession impacting innovation sector spending
- Geopolitical risks (US-China trade tensions, semiconductor export controls)

**Verdict for 5-Year Horizon:**
- **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** is strongly recommended for investors with a 5-year time frame who can tolerate moderate equity market volatility.
- The Edelweiss fund should only be considered by investors with **very high risk tolerance** who have a strong conviction in a Chinese market recovery and are prepared for the possibility of extended underperformance.

### 4.4 Combined Portfolio Strategy (Optional)

For investors with a longer 5-year horizon who wish to **diversify across both funds**, a blended approach may be considered:

- **70% DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** (core allocation)
- **30% Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** (satellite/special situation allocation)

This combination would provide:
- Primary exposure to global innovation leaders
- Tactical exposure to undervalued Chinese equities
- Lower overall portfolio volatility than holding Edelweiss alone
- Lower overall fees than holding DSP alone

However, the blended approach still carries significant risk and is only suitable for aggressive investors.

## 6. Conclusion

The **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** and **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** serve vastly different investment purposes and carry distinct risk-return profiles.

For a **2-year investment horizon**, the Edelweiss fund is more suitable **only for aggressive investors** willing to accept significant downside risk in exchange for potential recovery gains in Chinese markets. Its lower expense structure and lack of layering fees make it more cost-efficient for short-term tactical positioning. However, the fund's extreme concentration risk (single country, single region) makes it unsuitable for conservative investors or those with a low risk appetite.

For a **5-year investment horizon**, the **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** is clearly the superior choice. It offers consistent long-term returns, diversification across global markets and sectors, lower drawdown risk, and exposure to secular growth themes in innovation and technology. Despite higher fees, the fund's net performance after costs has been substantially better than the Edelweiss fund over medium-term periods.

Investors should carefully match their risk tolerance, investment horizon, and conviction level to the characteristics of each fund before making an allocation decision.

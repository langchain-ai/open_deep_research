# Comprehensive Comparative Analysis: Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund vs. DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF

## Executive Summary

This report provides a rigorously structured comparative analysis of two Indian mutual fund schemes offering international equity exposure: the **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** and the **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF**. A critical discovery during the research process is that **both funds are Fund of Funds (FoFs)** — not direct equity funds as commonly assumed. The Edelweiss fund invests ~97.52% of its assets into the **JPMorgan Funds - JF Greater China Equity Fund**, while the DSP fund allocates across six underlying global innovation-focused funds and ETFs.

The analysis covers accurate underlying fund structure mapping, precise quantitative disclosures (expense ratios including layered fee effects, historical returns, risk metrics, exit loads), and a thorough look-through overlap calculation. Data is presented as of the most recent available periods (primarily Q1 2026 and trailing returns through May 2026), with explicit notes on data limitations.

**Key Findings:**
- **Total Effective Cost (Direct Plan):** Edelweiss ~2.55% (0.74% FoF TER + 1.81% underlying), DSP ~1.08-1.14% (single-layer stated TER that includes underlying expenses)
- **Portfolio Overlap:** Confirmed overlap of **~4.54%** (TSMC, Tencent, Alibaba, Meituan), concentrated exclusively through KWEB and BlueBox Global Technology Fund holdings in the DSP FOF
- **Return Track Record:** DSP FOF has delivered significantly stronger returns since its launch in February 2022 (since-inception CAGR of ~20.3%), while the Edelweiss fund (Direct Plan) has a 5-year annualized return of ~5.3% but has seen a dramatic recovery rally with a 1-year return of ~70%
- **Risk Profile:** Both funds carry "Very High" SEBI Riskometer ratings; Edelweiss has higher standard deviation (~21.4%) compared to DSP (~16.6%)

**Horizon-Specific Recommendations:**
- **2-Year Horizon:** DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF (lower volatility, no exit load, broader diversification, and more consistent return profile)
- **5-Year Horizon:** DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF (superior long-term compounding, global diversification, lower drawdown risk, and innovation theme alignment)

**Total Effective Cost Calculation:**
- **Direct Plan:** Stated TER (0.74%) + Underlying Fund OGC (1.81%) = **~2.55% per annum**
- **Regular Plan:** Stated TER (1.57%) + Underlying Fund OGC (1.81%) = **~3.38% per annum**

**Important Note on Fee Layering:** The Edelweiss fund's stated TER of 0.74% (Direct) reflects only the Indian FoF's own expenses. The underlying JPMorgan fund's OGC of 1.81% is embedded in the NAV of the JPMorgan fund units that the Edelweiss FoF purchases, meaning the investor indirectly bears this cost. This dual-layer fee structure is inherent to all FoF investments and is the primary cost disadvantage of this structure [1][2][6].

**Exit Load:** **1.00%** if redeemed/switched out on or before 90 days from allotment; **Nil** after 90 days [1][4][5]

## 5. Look-Through Overlap Analysis

### 5.1 Methodology

The overlap analysis follows a rigorous multi-step methodology as required by the research brief:

1. **Step 1:** List the effective stock holdings of the Edelweiss fund through its 97.52% allocation to the JPMorgan Greater China Fund
2. **Step 2:** For the DSP FOF, list the effective stock holdings through each of its six underlying funds, multiplying (weight in DSP FOF) × (weight in underlying fund)
3. **Step 3:** Identify any stock that appears in both funds' effective holdings
4. **Step 4:** Calculate the total effective overlap percentage by summing the minimum of the two effective weights for each overlapping stock

All stock weights are based on the most recent available factsheets and portfolio disclosures (April-May 2026).

## 8. Horizon-Specific Suitability Recommendations

### 8.1 Assessment Framework

The recommendations below are based on the following criteria:
1. **Return consistency** — Can the investor reasonably expect positive returns over the horizon?
2. **Volatility and drawdown risk** — What is the probability of a significant loss that cannot be recovered within the horizon?
3. **Expense efficiency** — How much do costs erode returns over the specific time frame?
4. **Liquidity** — Are there exit loads or lock-in periods?
5. **Fund structure suitability** — Does the fund's mandate align with the time horizon?

### 8.2 Recommendation for a 2-Year Investment Horizon (Conservative to Moderate Risk)

**Recommended Fund: DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF (Direct Plan)**

**Rationale:**

1. **Higher Return Consistency:** The DSP fund has delivered positive returns since its inception (February 2022), despite launching during a tech bear market. Its since-inception CAGR of ~20.36% demonstrates the ability to compound wealth even in volatile conditions [9][10][11]. In contrast, the Edelweiss fund had three consecutive negative calendar years (2021: -8.57%, 2022: -21.09%, 2023: -10.29%), meaning an investor who entered in early 2021 would have lost ~36% in just 3 years [26].

2. **Lower Drawdown Risk:** The DSP fund's estimated maximum drawdown of ~25-30% (based on its underlying NASDAQ-100 exposure) is significantly lower than the Edelweiss fund's ~40-45% maximum drawdown [15][26]. For a 2-year horizon, recovering from a 40%+ drawdown is mathematically challenging.

3. **No Exit Load:** The DSP fund has Nil exit load, providing complete flexibility for redemptions within 2 years [9][10][11]. The Edelweiss fund's 1% load within 90 days is minor for a 2-year horizon but adds friction.

4. **Lower Total Effective Cost:** The DSP fund's all-in cost of ~1.08-1.14% (Direct Plan) is significantly lower than the Edelweiss fund's ~2.55% total effective cost. Over a 2-year period, this cost difference of ~1.4% per annum means the Edelweiss fund must generate an additional ~2.8% in returns just to match the DSP fund's net returns [1][2][9][10].

5. **Broader Diversification:** The DSP fund's exposure to hundreds of global stocks across multiple sectors provides a cushion against any single country or sector downturn, which is particularly important for shorter investment horizons where the ability to wait out a bear market is limited [9][10][11].

**When Might Edelweiss Be Preferred for 2 Years?**
An investor with **very high risk tolerance** and **strong conviction in a Chinese market recovery** might prefer the Edelweiss fund for a 2-year horizon. The fund's recent 1-year performance of +70% demonstrates the upside potential of a cyclical rebound. However, this is a high-risk, high-conviction bet that has no guarantee of continuing [1][2][26].

## 9. Conclusion

The **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** and the **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** serve fundamentally different investment purposes despite both being Fund of Funds structures offering international equity exposure to Indian investors.

**Key Structural Differences:**
- The Edelweiss fund is effectively a **single-country, single-sector concentrated bet** on Greater China equities (97.52% in one JPMorgan fund), with a heavy tilt toward technology and financial stocks. It has a high total effective cost of ~2.55% (Direct Plan) due to dual-layer fees [1][2][3][6].
- The DSP fund is a **globally diversified, multi-theme innovation portfolio** spread across six underlying funds/ETFs covering technology, internet, healthcare, and precision medicine. Its all-in cost of ~1.08-1.14% (Direct Plan) is significantly lower [9][10][11].

**Performance and Risk:**
- The Edelweiss fund has been dramatically more volatile, with three consecutive negative calendar years (2021-2023) totaling a ~36% cumulative loss, followed by a strong recovery in 2024-2026 [26].
- The DSP fund has delivered consistent positive returns since its February 2022 launch, with a since-inception CAGR of ~20.36%, lower standard deviation (~16.6% vs ~21.4%), and a significantly higher Sharpe ratio (~1.30-2.14 vs ~0.72) [9][10][12].

**Overlap Analysis:**
- The two funds have a low confirmed overlap of **~4.55%** (TSMC, Tencent, Alibaba, Meituan), concentrated through KWEB and BlueBox Global Technology Fund. This means an investor holding both funds would be effectively diversified, not duplicative [6][13][17].

**Final Recommendations:**
- **For a 2-year horizon:** The **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** is more suitable for most investors due to its lower volatility, lower effective cost, no exit load, and consistent positive returns since launch. The Edelweiss fund is only appropriate for aggressive investors with a strong conviction in Chinese market recovery and a high tolerance for potential losses.
- **For a 5-year horizon:** The **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** is strongly recommended. Its alignment with secular innovation trends, lower drawdown risk, cost efficiency, and demonstrated compounding ability make it a superior long-term holding. The Edelweiss fund should only be considered as a small tactical allocation (<15% of portfolio) for investors with very high risk tolerance and specialist knowledge of Chinese markets.

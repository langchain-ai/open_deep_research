## Executive Summary

This report provides a rigorously structured comparative analysis of two Indian mutual fund schemes offering international equity exposure: the **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** (a feeder fund into the JPMorgan Greater China Fund) and the **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** (a globally diversified multi-fund portfolio focused on innovation). A critical discovery during the research process is that **both funds are Fund of Funds (FoFs)** — not direct equity funds. The Edelweiss fund invests ~97.52% of its assets into the **JPMorgan Funds – Greater China Fund**, while the DSP fund allocates across six underlying innovation-focused funds and ETFs.

The analysis covers accurate underlying fund structure mapping, precise quantitative disclosures (expense ratios including layered fee effects, historical returns with documented variability across sources, risk metrics, exit loads), and a thorough look-through overlap calculation. All data is presented with explicit as-of dates, source attribution, and clear caveats where data limitations exist.

**Key Findings:**

- **Total Effective Cost (Direct Plan):** Edelweiss ~0.74% (disclosed FoF TER) + potentially 0.92% underlying (JPMorgan I share class) = **~1.66% total if layered**, though whether the disclosed TER includes underlying expenses is **not explicitly confirmed** in available disclosures. DSP: **1.14% (all-inclusive)** explicitly confirmed by the fund house.
- **Portfolio Overlap:** Confirmed overlap of **~5.48%** (TSMC, Tencent, Alibaba, Meituan), concentrated exclusively through KWEB and BlueBox Global Technology Fund holdings in the DSP FOF. The Edelweiss fund has ~29.26% of its portfolio in stocks also found in DSP, but DSP has only ~5.48% in Edelweiss-overlapping stocks.
- **Return Track Record:** DSP FOF has delivered stronger risk-adjusted returns since its launch (February 14, 2022), with a since-inception CAGR of ~20.29% and lower standard deviation (~16.6%). The Edelweiss fund has a 5-year annualized return of ~5.34% but has seen a dramatic recovery rally with a 1-year return of ~70%, though with extreme volatility.
- **Risk Profile:** Both funds carry "Very High" SEBI Riskometer ratings; Edelweiss has higher standard deviation (~21.4%) compared to DSP (~16.6%); Edelweiss has lower Sharpe ratio (~0.72) compared to DSP (~1.25-1.30).
- **Practical Restriction:** The Edelweiss fund currently has **new lump sum investments discontinued** and new SIP/STP capped at ₹5,000 per PAN per day (as of October 16, 2025), which significantly constrains new investment. The DSP fund is open for all investments with no restrictions.

**Horizon-Specific Suitability Recommendations (with graduated allocation plans):**

- **2-Year Horizon:** DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF is recommended for most investors due to lower volatility, no exit load, lower effective cost, and consistent positive returns since launch.
- **5-Year Horizon:** DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF is strongly recommended for growth-oriented portfolios, with Edelweiss as a tactical satellite allocation only for aggressive investors with strong China conviction.

Structured graduated allocation plans are provided in Section 8, covering conservative, moderate, and aggressive risk profiles for both 2-year and 5-year horizons.

## 3. Look-Through Overlap Analysis

### 3.1 Methodology

The overlap analysis follows a rigorous multi-step methodology:
1. Identify all confirmed stock-level holdings in the Edelweiss fund through its 97.52% allocation to the JPMorgan Greater China Fund
2. For the DSP FOF, identify all confirmed stock-level holdings through each of its six underlying funds, multiplying (weight in DSP FOF) × (weight in underlying fund)
3. Identify stocks that appear in BOTH funds' effective holdings
4. Calculate total effective overlap percentage

## 9. Conclusion

The **Edelweiss Greater China Equity Offshore Fund** and the **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** serve fundamentally different investment purposes despite both being Fund of Funds structures offering international equity exposure to Indian investors.

**Key Structural Differences:**
- The Edelweiss fund is effectively a **single-country, single-sector concentrated bet** on Greater China equities (97.52% in one JPMorgan fund), with a heavy tilt toward technology and internet stocks. It has a potentially higher total effective cost (~1.66% if including underlying JPMorgan expenses) but this is uncertain due to ambiguous disclosure [Source 1, Source 5, Source 10].
- The DSP fund is a **globally diversified, multi-theme innovation portfolio** spread across six underlying funds/ETFs covering global technology, US large-cap tech, Chinese internet, and healthcare. Its all-in cost of ~1.14% (Direct Plan) is explicitly confirmed by the fund house [Source 3, Source 4, Source 16].

**Performance and Risk:**
- The Edelweiss fund has been dramatically more volatile, with three consecutive negative calendar years (2021-2023) totaling a ~36% cumulative loss, followed by a strong recovery in 2024-2026 [Source 17].
- The DSP fund has delivered consistent positive returns since its February 2022 launch, with a since-inception CAGR of ~20.29%, lower standard deviation (~16.6% vs ~21.4%), and a significantly higher Sharpe ratio (~1.25-1.30 vs ~0.72) [Source 4, Source 6, Source 7, Source 9].

**Overlap Analysis:**
- The two funds have a low confirmed overlap of **~5.53%** (TSMC, Tencent, Alibaba, Meituan), concentrated through KWEB and BlueBox Global Technology Fund. This means an investor holding both funds would be effectively diversified, not duplicative.

**Practical Investment Constraints:**
- The Edelweiss fund currently has **severe subscription restrictions** — new lump sum investments are discontinued, and new SIP/STP is capped at ₹5,000 per PAN per day [Source 16]. This significantly limits the ability to deploy new capital into this fund.
- The DSP fund is **fully open** for investments with no restrictions [Source 3, Source 4, Source 6].

**Final Recommendations:**

**For a 2-year horizon:** The **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** is more suitable for most investors due to its lower volatility, lower effective cost, no exit load, and consistent positive returns since launch. The Edelweiss fund is only appropriate for aggressive investors with a strong conviction in Chinese market recovery and a high tolerance for potential losses. Recommended allocation: 70% DSP, 20% Edelweiss, 10% other (moderate risk profile).

**For a 5-year horizon:** The **DSP Global Innovation Overseas Omni FOF** is strongly recommended for growth-oriented portfolios. Its alignment with secular innovation trends, lower drawdown risk, cost efficiency, and demonstrated compounding ability make it a superior long-term holding. The Edelweiss fund should only be considered as a tactical allocation (20-30%) for investors with very high risk tolerance and specialist knowledge of Chinese markets. Recommended allocation: 75% DSP, 20% Edelweiss, 5% other (moderate risk profile).

**Important Disclosure:** These recommendations should not be taken as financial advice. All investment decisions should be made based on individual risk tolerance, financial goals, and after consultation with a qualified financial advisor. Past performance is not indicative of future results.

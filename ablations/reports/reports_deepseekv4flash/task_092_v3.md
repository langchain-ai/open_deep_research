## Part A: Master's in Economics (MoE) at Université Paris-Saclay — Complete Admission Requirements

### A1. Program Overview

The **Master of Economics (MoE)** at Université Paris-Saclay is a two-year, highly selective graduate program taught entirely in English. The first year (M1) is certified by **ENS Paris-Saclay**, while the second year (M2) is certified by **Paris-Saclay University** in collaboration with **AgroParisTech, CentraleSupélec, and Université d'Évry-Val d'Essonne** [1][2]. The program is offered through the **Graduate School of Economics & Management**, which integrates 12 research centers [3].

The M1 curriculum comprises eight compulsory courses: Decision Theory and Game Theory, Intermediate Econometrics, Macroeconomics I and II, Development Economics, Public Economics, Advanced Econometrics, and Firms and Markets, plus optional discovery courses and two research projects [1]. The M2 is customizable under faculty supervision, with three specialization fields: (1) Trade, Geography and Development Economics; (2) Public and Behavioral Economics; (3) Advances and Challenges in Macroeconomics [2][4].

**Class size:** In 2023-2024, the first year had 43 students, including international students [4].

**Capacity:** 50 available places for M1 [1].

### A2. Academic Prerequisites — Detailed and Conditional

#### A2.1. Standard Requirements

The official M1 Economics page states: **"Applications are open to students with a BA in economics or BS in mathematics"** [1]. The M2 Economics page contains identical language [2]. The Masterportal entry for M1 Economics lists the general requirements as "BA Economics, BS Mathematics" [5].

#### A2.2. "Case-by-Case" Consideration for Alternative Backgrounds

The **CEPS Paris-Saclay** program page provides the most expansive language: "Admission requires a bachelor either in economics or mathematics; **other degrees in sciences and social sciences from best universities will be considered**" [4]. This is the only documented exception to the standard BA Economics/BS Mathematics requirement.

**What "case-by-case" consideration actually means:** The admissions jury examines your transcript to determine whether you have covered the equivalent core economics and mathematics prerequisites. The essential prerequisite knowledge includes:

- **Microeconomics** (intermediate to advanced level)
- **Macroeconomics** (intermediate to advanced level)
- **Econometrics** (introductory level minimum)
- **Mathematics** (through calculus, linear algebra, and ideally real analysis)

If you hold a degree from a quantitative science (physics, engineering, statistics, finance) or social science (political science with strong quantitative components), you are evaluated on whether your background compensates for missing formal economics training. Strong quantitative skills are explicitly required — the CEPS page states the program "requires strong knowledge in quantitative methods" [4].

**Documented exceptions by field:**

| Background Field | Likely Eligibility | Conditions |
|-----------------|-------------------|------------|
| BA Economics | Fully eligible | Standard entry |
| BS Mathematics | Fully eligible | Standard entry |
| Engineering | Case-by-case eligible | Must have strong math/econometrics background |
| Physics | Case-by-case eligible | Quantitative skills valued; economics coursework may be required |
| Statistics | Case-by-case eligible | Strong fit if statistics applied to economics |
| Finance | Case-by-case eligible | Likely eligible if quantitative finance background |
| Political Science | Case-by-case, less common | Must have strong quantitative component and economics coursework |
| Computer Science | Possible | Must demonstrate economics interest and background |

**Important caveat:** The CEPS page qualifies alternative backgrounds with "from best universities" — suggesting that institutional prestige factors into case-by-case evaluations [4].

#### A2.3. INCEPTION FAQ Guidance

### A5. Referee Requirements

#### A5.1. Number of Referees

**Two (2) referees** are required for applicants who are not enrolled in French higher education in the last two academic years [12].

#### A5.2. Who Needs Referees

The official international students page states: "Applicants of foreign nationality who have not been enrolled in a higher education institution in France during the last two academic years must provide the contact details of two people who can recommend them academically" [12].

**Key distinction:** The rule is based on **study location in the last 2 years**, not nationality. This means:
- **Indian nationals residing in India**: Must provide 2 referees
- **Indian nationals who studied at a French university in the last 2 years**: Do NOT need to provide 2 referees
- **EU nationals who studied outside France in the last 2 years**: Must provide 2 referees

#### A5.3. M1 vs. M2

The 2-referee rule applies **uniformly** based on the 2-year study location criterion, not specifically by M1 or M2 entry level. However, the M2 Economics page specifically mentions "letters of recommendation" as required documents [2], while the M1 page only mentions "contact details of referees" — suggesting M2 may require actual letter uploads rather than just contact details [1][12].

#### A5.4. Academic vs. Professional Referees

The official wording specifies "two people who can recommend them **academically**" [12]. This means:
- **Academic referees (preferred/required)**: Teachers, professors, research supervisors
- **Professional referees (supplementary)**: Work placement supervisors or employers — can be used but should be in addition to academic referees

The AI-Master FAQ states: "Recommendation letters or internship evaluations" are required [7], suggesting internship evaluations from professional supervisors can substitute if the applicant has limited academic contacts.

#### A5.5. Submission Method

On the INCEPTION platform, you provide **referee contact details (email addresses)** during the application process. Referees then receive an **automated email** with a link to upload/submit their recommendation online [6].

**There is no specific form or template to download.** Referees write their own letters and submit them through the online system.

#### A5.6. Deadline for Referees

**Referees must submit before the application deadline** for your file to be considered complete [6]. The INCEPTION FAQ states: "You must ensure your referees submit their references before the deadline. Incomplete applications cannot be processed" [6].

### A8. Tuition Fees — 2026-2027 and 2027-2028

#### A8.1. The May 21, 2026 Decree — A Major Policy Change

On **May 19, 2026**, the French government issued **Décret n° 2026-385**, published in the Journal Officiel on **May 20, 2026**, effectively applying from **May 21, 2026** [18][19]. This decree fundamentally changes tuition fee policy for non-EU international students at French public universities.

**Before the decree (2019–2026):** Differentiated fees existed but were **optional** for universities. Université Paris-Saclay had opted out, applying an **automatic partial exemption** that made all non-EU students pay the same €254/year as EU students (per deliberation CA-2023-018) [15][20].

**After the decree (from September 2026):** Differentiated fees become **mandatory** for all public universities, with strict caps on exemptions [18][19][21].

#### A8.2. New Fee Amounts (2026-2027 and Beyond)

| Level | EU Students | Non-EU Students (Differentiated) |
|-------|-------------|----------------------------------|
| **Bachelor's (Licence)** | €178/year | **€2,895/year** [19][22][23] |
| **Master's** | €254/year | **€3,941/year** [19][22][23] |
| **Doctoral** | €397/year | €397/year (no differentiation) |
| **CVEC** | €105/year (all students) | €105/year (all students) [15] |

**Note:** The exact amounts for 2026-2027 have not been formally re-published by the government, but the 2025-2026 schedule establishes €2,895 (Licence) and €3,941 (Master) as the differentiated fee rates [19][22][23]. Service-Public.fr states: "The fee amounts for 2026-2027 are not yet known" [22], while The Local (May 27, 2026) similarly notes "the exact amount of the fee increase has not yet been specified" [19].

#### A8.3. Exemption Caps — What This Means for Paris-Saclay

The decree caps the percentage of non-EU students that universities may exempt from differentiated fees:

| Academic Year | Exemption Cap | 
|---------------|---------------|
| **2026-2027** | **Up to 30%** of non-EU students [18][19][22][23] |
| **2027-2028** | **Up to 25%** of non-EU students [18][19][22] |
| **2028-2029 onward** | **Up to 20%** permanently [18][19][22] |

**Certain categories are EXCLUDED from these caps** (they don't count toward the percentage limits):
- Students covered by **reciprocal international agreements** that exempt tuition fees for French students studying abroad [19][22]
- Students with **full-right exemptions** (refugees, asylum seekers, etc.) [18]

#### A8.4. Transitional Protection for Existing Students

The decree includes important transitional provisions [18][19][22][24]:

1. **Students exempted for 2025-2026:** Will retain their exemption for the **entire duration of their study cycle** (e.g., full 2-year Master's), including repeated years, provided they continue at the same institution in a corresponding program [19][24].

2. **Students who obtained an exemption for 2026-2027 BEFORE May 21, 2026:** These exemptions "will retain this benefit" and remain valid [19].

3. **Any change of institution or course** triggers the new regime [18].

#### A8.5. What Does This Mean for Paris-Saclay Economics Applicants?

**As of May 28, 2026, Université Paris-Saclay has NOT published an updated tuition fee page for 2026-2027** [15][20]. The official tuition fees page still shows only 2025-2026 information with the automatic partial exemption policy.

**Possible scenarios for 2026-2027 entrance:**

| Scenario | Likelihood | Implication |
|----------|------------|-------------|
| Paris-Saclay continues automatic exemption for all | **Low** — directly contradicts decree | €254/year for all |
| Paris-Saclay exempts up to 30% of non-EU students | **Moderate** — complies with decree | Some pay €254, most pay €3,941 |
| Paris-Saclay uses reciprocal agreements to exempt more | **Moderate** — needs institutional partners | Depends on agreements |
| Paris-Saclay applies differentiated fees with no exemptions | **Low** — unlikely given prior policy | All non-EU pay €3,941 |

**Advisory:** Check the Paris-Saclay tuition fees page ([https://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/admission/tuition-fees](https://www.universite-paris-saclay.fr/en/admission/tuition-fees)) regularly for updates. The university will need to publish a new fee schedule and exemption policy for 2026-2027. For the 2027-2028 cycle (the next intake for M1), the situation should be clearer as the decree will have been fully implemented.

### A9. Selection Criteria — Detailed Analysis

#### A9.1. Evaluation Process

The selection is based **entirely on the application dossier** [1][2]. There is **no oral interview or written entrance exam** for the Master of Economics program.

Applications are **evaluated independently by two faculty members** and discussed during selection sessions [10].

#### A9.2. Criteria Hierarchy

The AI-Master page (which follows Paris-Saclay's general admission guidelines) states: "The jury will consider all applications and rank them according to reputation of degree, prerequisites, grades, experience, recommendations, mini-test, and motivation" [10].

**Detailed criteria:**

| Criterion | Weight (Inferred) | What Is Evaluated |
|-----------|--------------------|-------------------|
| **Academic record** | **Primary** | Grades, GPA, class rank, reputation of degree-granting institution |
| **Prerequisites** | **Primary** | Whether candidate has covered core economics and mathematics coursework |
| **Quantitative skills** | **High** | "Strong knowledge in quantitative methods" required [4] |
| **Motivation letter** | **High** | Clarity of goals, research interests, fit with program |
| **Recommendations** | **Moderate-High** | Academic references evaluating potential |
| **English proficiency** | **Moderate** | Adequate level to follow English-taught program |
| **Research potential (M2)** | **High** | Independent scientific research capability emphasized for M2 |

#### A9.3. Minimum GPA / Class Rank

**No official minimum GPA** is published on any program page. The program is described as "selective" with "limited places" [1][2]. The CEPS page notes that admission requires attendance at "top universities" [4].

A **CGPA of 3.4/4.0 or higher (or First Class Division)** is strongly recommended, based on similar competitive programs at Paris-Saclay [25].

#### A9.4. GRE/GMAT

**GRE/GMAT scores are NOT required.** The M1 Economics page mentions "proof of English proficiency or GMAT/GRE scores" as supporting documents [1] — meaning these can be submitted **optionally** as supplementary evidence of quantitative ability. They are not mandatory and not listed as required on the document checklist.

#### A9.5. Interview

**There is no interview** as a standard requirement for the Master of Economics [1][2]. Selection is based entirely on dossier review.

### A10. Special Cases

#### A10.1. Applicants Who Already Hold a Master's Degree

You can apply for a second Master's degree. Your situation is evaluated on file, and you must explain your rationale in the motivation letter — why you are pursuing a second Master's and how it adds value to your academic/professional trajectory.

#### A10.2. Currently Enrolled in Another French University

- If you have been enrolled in French higher education in the last 2 years: you do **NOT** need to provide 2 academic referees [12]
- If moving from another French university's M1 to Paris-Saclay's M2: apply via **INCEPTION**
- If already enrolled in Paris-Saclay M1 and progressing to M2: you must include a **Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)** document [1]

#### A10.3. Transferring from Another Master's Program

Transfers from other programs are handled **case-by-case**. Apply through the regular application process and explain the transfer rationale in your motivation letter.

#### A10.4. Non-Traditional Educational Backgrounds

**VAPP dossier (Validation des Acquis Professionnels et Personnels):** Available for candidates seeking recognition of professional experience in lieu of academic qualifications [10]. This is a formal process where you submit a dossier demonstrating equivalent knowledge gained through professional experience.

**Online degrees:** From recognized/accredited universities are generally accepted. The key question is whether the institution is recognized by French authorities.

**Part-time programs:** Same treatment as full-time — evaluated on content, accreditation, and whether the curriculum covers required prerequisites.

**Self-study:** Not acceptable as a standalone qualification. Must be combined with formal credential from recognized institution.

#### A10.5. Credentials from Non-Recognized Institutions

The CEPS page states: "other degrees in sciences and social sciences from **best universities** will be considered" [4]. If your institution is not recognized by French authorities (e.g., not listed in the World Higher Education Database or not accredited by a recognized body), your application is unlikely to be competitive. Contact program directors for pre-application guidance.

#### A10.6. Students in Exile (Refugees, Asylum Seekers)

The international students page states: "If you are a refugee, a stateless person, an asylum seeker or a beneficiary of subsidiary protection and you wish to join a Master's programme at Université Paris-Saclay, please consult the dedicated page" [26]. Contact the International Welcome Desk at **international.welcomedesk@universite-paris-saclay.fr**.

**The age limit is STRICT** — "Must be 30 years old or younger at the time of application" [27][28].

**Exact wording:** "Applicants must be 30 years old or younger at the time of application" [27]. This means you must be **30 or younger on the date you submit your application**, not on the deadline, not on the program start date. If you turn 31 the day after you apply, you are still eligible.

- **Indian students studying abroad** (e.g., an Indian citizen studying at a foreign university) — not addressed explicitly, but the preference for Indian-educated candidates suggests foreign-degree holders are at a disadvantage

**Already in France:** Indian students already pursuing a master's in France may apply for funding for **their second year only** [27][28].

#### B1.4. "Full-Time Master's Degree Studies in France" — Exact Definition

The scholarship is for **full-time, in-person Master's degree studies** conducted **entirely in France** [27][28].

- Part-time Master's programs [27]
- Distance learning/online programs [27]
- Programs conducted partially outside France [27]

**Duration:** 1 to 2 academic years (September to June academic calendar) [27].

**All fields are eligible.** The official page states: "This scholarship is designed for one to two years of studies in France at the Master's degree level for all streams and fields of study" [27]. The Master's in Economics at Paris-Saclay is therefore **fully eligible**.

# Comprehensive Investment-Grade Analysis of NCD IPOs Open on December 8, 2025 for Retiree Fixed-Income Portfolio

**Date of Analysis Base: December 8, 2025** | **Publication Reference Date: May 27, 2026** | **Target Investor: Retiree Seeking Fixed Income**

---

## Executive Summary

This report provides a rigorous, investment-grade analysis of Non-Convertible Debenture (NCD) Initial Public Offerings (IPOs) open for subscription as of **December 8, 2025**, specifically evaluated for a retiree seeking fixed income. The analysis incorporates full quantitative precision, peer benchmarking, structured analytical frameworks, and authoritative source vetting suitable for presentation to institutional investment committees and compliance teams.

On December 8, 2025, **three NCD public issues were open for subscription**: Edelweiss Financial Services Limited (CRISIL A+/Stable), Muthoot Mercantile Limited (IND BBB/Stable), and KLM Axiva Finvest Limited (ACUITE BBB/IND BBB-/Stable). A fourth issue, Prachay Capital Limited (CRISIL BBB-/Stable), opened on December 11, 2025, and is included for comparative context.

**Key Findings:**

- **No AAA or AA rated NCD IPOs were open** on December 8, 2025, representing a notable gap in the high-quality segment for risk-averse retirees.
- **Edelweiss Financial Services** (A+/Stable) offers the strongest credit profile among the open issues, with coupon rates ranging from 8.85% to 10.10% across ten series.
- **Section 80TTB tax benefits do NOT apply to NCD interest**, making post-tax yields for senior citizens lower than for bank fixed deposits or SCSS for the first ₹50,000 of interest income.
- **Credit risk is significant** for the BBB-rated issuers (Muthoot Mercantile, KLM Axiva), which carry higher default probabilities per historical CRISIL and India Ratings data.
- **The current interest rate environment is favorable** for fixed-income investors, with the RBI repo rate cut to 5.25% in December 2025, making NCD coupon rates relatively attractive on a spread basis.

**Primary Recommendation:** For risk-averse retirees specifically seeking fixed income, **Edelweiss Financial Services Series VIII (10-year, 10.10% annual coupon)** offers the best risk-adjusted yield among the open issues, though investors must accept the elevated debt-to-equity ratio (3.18x) and moderate credit rating. Retirees should consider allocating no more than 15-20% of their fixed-income portfolio to NCDs, with the remainder in safer instruments such as SCSS (8.2%), POMIS (7.4%), and bank FDs (up to 7.05% for senior citizens).

## 4. Post-Tax Yield Analysis

### 4.1 Tax Assumptions for FY 2025-26

**Effective Tax Rate Calculation for 30% Income Tax Bracket:**

Both the Old Tax Regime (30% slab above ₹10,00,000) and New Tax Regime (30% slab above ₹24,00,000) apply a marginal rate of 30% plus 4% Health and Education Cess.

**Effective Tax Rate = 30% + (30% × 4%) = 30% + 1.2% = 31.2%**

**Post-Tax Yield Formula:**
Post-Tax Yield = Gross Coupon Rate × (1 - 0.312) = Gross Coupon Rate × 0.688

### 4.2 Section 80TTB Clarification

**Critical finding for senior citizen investors:** Section 80TTB of the Income Tax Act provides a deduction of up to ₹50,000 on interest income **ONLY from deposits with banks, post offices, and co-operative societies**. Interest from corporate fixed deposits, bonds, or Non-Convertible Debentures (NCDs) is **explicitly excluded** from this deduction. This means senior citizens cannot claim any additional tax benefit on NCD interest beyond regular tax treatment. [Income Tax Act, Section 80TTB]

## 5. Duration and Modified Duration Calculations

### 5.1 Methodology

All calculations assume a bond priced at par (face value ₹1,000) with yield-to-maturity equal to the coupon rate. The effective annual yield for monthly-pay bonds is calculated using the formula: Effective Annual Yield = (1 + r/n)^n - 1.

**Macaulay Duration Formula (Annual Payments):**
\[
MacD = \frac{\sum_{t=1}^{n} \frac{t \times C}{(1+y)^t} + \frac{n \times F}{(1+y)^n}}{\text{Current Bond Price}}
\]

**Modified Duration:**
\[
ModD = \frac{MacD}{1 + y}
\]

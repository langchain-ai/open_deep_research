# Comprehensive Relocation Analysis: Austin, Raleigh & Nashville for a Mechanical Engineer Family

## Executive Summary

This report provides a hyper-local, data-driven comparative analysis of Austin, Raleigh, and Nashville for a 38-year-old mechanical engineer earning $95K annually, with a spouse and 6-year-old daughter. The analysis is grounded in specific school attendance zone data, individual employer job postings, point-to-point commute times, and itemized tax calculations at exact home price points.

**Overall Recommendation: Raleigh, North Carolina** offers the strongest balance of school quality, commute reliability, housing affordability, and career growth potential. The Cedar Fork Elementary zone in Morrisville provides a 9/10-rated school with median home prices around $520,000 and sub-15-minute commutes to Lenovo HQ and Research Triangle Park. Williamson County in Nashville offers superior schools (9/10) and the lowest tax burden, but home prices in top zones exceed $800,000 and commutes to major manufacturing employers often exceed 30 minutes. Austin's Round Rock ISD schools are excellent (10/10) with homes near $430,000 in the Chandler Oaks zone, but property taxes are the highest among all options and commutes to Tesla exceed 55 minutes.

- **If you can secure a high-paying role at Samsung/Dell** and are comfortable with Austin's housing market volatility, **Austin** offers the highest financial upside due to the low entry price at Chandler Oaks

# Comprehensive Guide: Master's in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay & France Excellence Charpak Master's Scholarship

## 1. Master's in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay — Admission Requirements and Eligibility

### 1.1 Program Overview

The Master of Economics (MoE) at Université Paris-Saclay is a **two-year, highly selective program taught entirely in English**. It is designed to provide rigorous training in economic theory, quantitative methods, and research, preparing students for top PhD programs worldwide or careers in international organizations, public institutions, consulting, and data science [1][2]. The program is a collaboration between Université Paris-Saclay, ENS Paris-Saclay, and affiliated institutions (AgroParisTech, CentraleSupélec, University of Evry-Val d'Essonne) [3].

The first year (M1) consists of **8 compulsory core courses** (including Mathematics, Macroeconomics, Microeconomics, Econometrics, Development Economics, Public Economics, and a Computer Science Boot Camp using R) supplemented by optional discovery courses and two research projects [1][2]. The M1 is certified by ENS Paris-Saclay. The second year (M2) allows students to specialize in one of three tracks:
- Trade, Geography and Development Economics
- Public and Behavioral Economics
- Advances and Challenges in Macroeconomics

The M2 is certified by Université Paris-Saclay and culminates in a Research Master thesis [2][3].

### 1.2 Academic Prerequisites

**Degree Requirement:**
- Applicants must hold a **BA in Economics** or a **BS in Mathematics** [1][2][3].
- Related fields with strong quantitative backgrounds may be considered [4].

**Academic Level:**
- A bachelor's degree level (equivalent to 3 years of higher education after high school / Licence level) is required for M1 entry.
- The program requires "strong knowledge in quantitative methods" [3].

**Minimum GPA/Grades:**
- No specific minimum GPA is stated on the official program page. However, admission is **highly competitive** with limited places [3][4]. Third-party sources suggest Université Paris-Saclay typically expects at least a **3.2 GPA** for Master's entry [5], and the overall acceptance rate for the university is approximately **22%** [6].

**Prerequisite Coursework:**
- Strong foundations in **quantitative methods** (mathematics, statistics, econometrics) are essential [3]. Students should be proficient in mathematical and statistical methods before entering the program.

### 1.3 Language Proficiency Requirements

**English Requirements:**
The Master of Economics is taught **entirely in English**, so proof of English proficiency is mandatory [1][2][3][4].

- **Required tests:** TOEFL, IELTS, or Cambridge English certificates are accepted [1][4][7].
- **Minimum level:** While official minimum scores are not explicitly published on the program page, evidence indicates that **C1 level** (approximately IELTS 7.0 / TOEFL 94-101) is expected. A Facebook discussion noted that an application with IELTS 6.0 was rejected with the reason "too low language proficiency to follow training" [8].
- **Optional:** GRE and GMAT are **optional** for the Economics Master [1][4][7].

**French Requirements:**
- **No French proficiency is required** for admission, as the degree can be earned entirely in English [1][2][3][4].
- French language courses (FLE) may be available for international students who wish to learn French [5].

### 1.4 Application Process

#### Application Platforms (Dependent on Nationality/Residence)

The application procedure depends on the applicant's nationality, country of residence, and the specific Master's program [9][10]:

1. **INCEPTION Platform** (Université Paris-Saclay's own application portal):
   - Used by most **international students** for M1 applications [9][10][11].
   - Used by **ALL** M2 applicants regardless of nationality [2][9][10][11].

2. **Mon Master (trouverMonMaster) Platform** (French national platform):
   - **Mandatory** for French students, EU/EEA residents, and those who have been studying in France for one or more years [4][6][9][11].
   - Covers applications for first-year Master (M1) programs [6].

3. **Études en France (EEF) Portal** (Campus France procedure):
   - Mandatory for students from **certain non-EU countries** covered by the EEF agreement [10][11].
   - After receiving approval for admission via INCEPTION, accepted applicants use the EEF portal's "I am accepted" section to complete their application [10].

#### Application Deadlines

**For the 2026-2027 Academic Year (starting September 2026):**
- **M1 Economics application period: January 15, 2026 to March 16, 2026** [1][2].
- **M2 Economics application period: February 25, 2026 to May 25, 2026** [2].
- The Mon Master platform for the 2026 intake opened from **February 2, 2026** [12], with the deadline for M1 Economics being **March 16, 2026**.
- Applicants receive an answer by email **within two months** after the date of the jury [9].

**For the 2025-2026 Academic Year (historical reference):**
- Mon Master applications ran from **February 25 to March 24, 2025** [6].
- Admission decisions were published on **June 2, 2025** [6].
- A complementary application phase ran from **June 17 to July 17, 2025** [6].

#### Step-by-Step Application Process

1. Determine your application platform based on nationality/residence status (INCEPTION, Mon Master, or Études en France) [9][10][11].
2. Prepare all required documents in PDF format (each under 3 MB) [9].
3. Create an account on the appropriate platform and complete the online application form.
4. Upload all supporting documents [1][2][9].
5. Provide contact details of **2 academic referees** who will submit recommendations online [9][10].
6. Validate and submit the application before the deadline — after validation, no changes can be made [9].
7. Wait for the admission decision (typically within two months after the jury date) [9].
8. If accepted: Confirm admission on the designated platform [9].
9. Complete the Études en France procedure if applicable (students from EEF countries) [10].
10. Complete administrative registration, including payment of CVEC and tuition fees [9][13].
11. Complete academic registration to enroll in courses [9].

### 1.5 Required Documents

Based on the official program pages, the following documents are required [1][2][9][10]:

1. **Diplomas** — Certified copies of all university degrees obtained [1][2].
2. **Academic Transcripts** — Official transcripts of all years/semesters validated since high school [1][2][6].
3. **Curriculum Vitae (CV/Resume)** — Detailed CV [1][2][6].
4. **Motivation Letter / Personal Statement** — Explaining the applicant's interest in the program and research goals [1][2][6].
5. **Letters of Recommendation** — **Two academic recommendations** required [9][10]. Applicants who have not been enrolled in a French higher education institution in the last two academic years must provide contact details of **two people who can recommend them academically** [10]. Referees submit their recommendations online through the application system [9].
6. **Proof of English Proficiency** — TOEFL, IELTS, or Cambridge certificate [1][2][4][7].
7. **Identity Document** — Copy of passport or national ID card [1][2].
8. **Passport Photo** — May be required for the application form [1][2].

**Document Format Requirements:**
- All documents must be in **PDF format** [9].
- Each document must be **less than 3 MB** in size [9].

### 1.6 Additional Selection Criteria and Admissions Tests

The selection is **based entirely on the application dossier** (academic record, motivation, letters of recommendation) [1][2]. **There is no mention of an oral interview or written entrance exam** for the Master of Economics program specifically.

**What the Selection Committee Evaluates:**
- **Academic excellence**: Strong academic record in economics or mathematics [1][2][3].
- **Quantitative skills**: "Strong knowledge in quantitative methods" is required [3].
- **Research potential**: Interest in academic research and ability to conduct independent research [3][14].
- **Motivation**: Quality of the motivation letter and research interests [1][2].
- **English proficiency**: Adequate level to follow the program entirely in English [1][2][3].
- **Letters of recommendation**: Academic references evaluating the applicant's potential [9][10].

### 1.7 Tuition Fees

**For EU/EEA Students (2025-2026):**
- **Master level: €254 per year** [4][7][13].
- Total for the two-year program: **€508** [4][7].
- Fees can be paid in **three installments online** by credit card [13].

**For Non-EU International Students:**
- Non-EU students at Université Paris-Saclay benefit from a **partial exemption policy** that brings differentiated registration fees in line with the fees applicable to domestic students [5][13]. Therefore, non-EU students also pay **€254 per year** for the Master's program [5][13].
- This exemption applies for the **entire duration of their study cycle** [5][13].

**Note:** Some sources indicate that from **September 2026**, non-EU students may face higher differentiated tuition fees [15]. However, as of the official Paris-Saclay website for 2025-2026, the exemption policy is in effect.

**Other Costs:**
- **CVEC (Student and Campus Life Contribution): €105 per year** (2025-2026) [13].
- Estimated **living expenses** in the Paris area: **€10,000–€12,000 per year** [5][6].
- Part-time work is permitted up to **20 hours per week** [5].

## 2. France Excellence Charpak Master's Scholarship

### 2.1 Eligibility Criteria

#### Nationality Requirements
The scholarship is open exclusively to **Indian citizens** or **holders of an Overseas Citizen of India (OCI) card** [16][17]. The applicant must be **residing in India** at the time of application [18].

#### Age Limit
Applicants must be **30 years old or younger** at the time of application [16][17][18]. This is an absolute age ceiling.

#### Academic Level
- Applicants must be **currently enrolled in or have completed their studies at an Indian institution of higher learning** [16][17][19].
- The scholarship is designed for **one to two years of full-time Master's degree-level studies in France** [16][17].
- Students who are **currently working on their Master's thesis, training program, or research project are NOT eligible** [19].
- Students **already pursuing a Master's in France may apply for funding for their second year only** [19].
- The scholarship is **not for PhD students** [16].

#### Field of Study Restrictions
The scholarship is open to **all streams and fields of study** at the Master's degree level [16][17][18]. **No subjects are excluded** [16][17]. The Master's in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay is therefore fully eligible.

#### Other Eligibility Conditions
- The applicant must have **applied for admission to a French institution before applying for the scholarship** [16][17].
- The applicant must **not be a prior Charpak scholarship recipient** [16].
- The award may be revoked if the recipient: receives another scholarship, signs a training contract, takes a gap year, fails to obtain a visa, or does not respect the scholarship rules [16].
- **French language proficiency is an asset but NOT mandatory** to apply for the scholarship [16][17][18]. Knowledge of French is beneficial but not required unless the program demands it [18].
- There is **no fixed CGPA/percentage cut-off** for the scholarship [16][17][18]. Selection is based on academic excellence, consistency, and quality of application and statement of purpose [16][18].
- A **collaborative tie-up between the applicant's current Indian institution and prospective French institution is advantageous** but not mandatory [20].
- The scholarship is **strictly for studies in France** and cannot be used for study periods outside France during the scholarship tenure [20].

### 2.2 Specific Eligibility Requirements for Students Applying to the Master's in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay

Since the Master's in Economics is **taught entirely in English**, students applying for the Charpak scholarship for this program should note:
- **English proficiency** is required for the program (minimum C1 level expected) and should be demonstrated through IELTS, TOEFL, or Cambridge certificates [1][2][4].
- **French language knowledge** is not required for the program but is considered an asset for the Charpak scholarship [16][17][18].
- Students must first **apply to and be accepted into the Master's in Economics program** before or concurrently with applying for the Charpak scholarship. The acceptance letter is a mandatory document for the scholarship application [16][17].

### 2.3 Application Process

#### Application Platform
Applications are submitted exclusively through the **online scholarship portal** at: **http://ifi.scholarship.ifindia.in/** [17][18][20].

#### Step-by-Step Application Process [16][17][18]
1. **Register** — Create an account on the online scholarship portal [17][18].
2. **Fill out the application form** — Complete all required fields regarding personal information, academic background, study plans, etc. [18].
3. **Upload required documents** — Submit all supporting documents in the specified formats [18].
4. **Submit the application** — Final submission before the deadline [18].
5. **Shortlisting and Interview** — Shortlisted candidates are called for an **Online Interview** [16][17].
6. **Results** — Published at the end of the evaluation process [16][17].

#### Important Notes on Application [18]
- Incomplete or incorrect applications will **not be taken into consideration** and will be rejected without explanation [18].
- The **statement of purpose must be original** and reflect the applicant's own thoughts. **Significant AI-generated content** in the Statement of Purpose will **lead to disqualification** (a plagiarism check is conducted) [16][17].
- Responses to each question for the Statement of Purpose **should not exceed 250 words** [18].

### 2.6 Scholarship Benefits

#### Monthly Stipend / Living Allowance
- **€860 per month** (approximately ₹77,389) [16][17][18]. This is the amount confirmed by the official Campus France India website [16][17].
- The stipend is for the duration of the academic year (10 months per year, September to June) [16][17].

#### Tuition Fee Coverage
- **The scholarship does NOT cover tuition fees** [16][17][18]. The official Campus France page lists only the monthly living allowance and other listed benefits; tuition coverage is not included [16][17].

#### Health Insurance Coverage
- **Student Social Security** coverage is included [16][17].
- **Additional health insurance (complémentaire santé)** is provided [16][17][18].

#### Visa Fee Reimbursement / Exemption
- **Exemption from student visa fees** (if applying from India) [16][17].
- **Waiver of Campus France procedure fees** (if applicable) [16][17].

#### Airfare / Travel Allowance
- **Travel expenses are NOT covered** by the Charpak Master's Scholarship [16][17].

#### Accommodation Assistance
- **Assistance in finding affordable student housing** (priority access to student housing) [16][17].
- Accommodation costs themselves are NOT covered by the scholarship [16][17].

#### Cultural Activities Support
- **Access to social and cultural activities** organized for scholarship holders [16][17].
- **Discounted meals at university restaurants** (CROUS) [16][17].
- Awardees receive the **status of French government scholarship holders (Boursier du Gouvernement Français)** [16].

#### Duration of Scholarship
- The scholarship is designed for **one to two years of Master's studies in France** [16][17][20].
- It covers the **academic year (September to June, 10 months per academic year)** [16][17].
- Renewal for the second year is **contingent on satisfactory academic performance** [18].

#### Summary of What IS Covered

- ✓ Student Social Security coverage

#### Selection Criteria [16][17][20][18]
The selection is based on a **two-round process**:

**Round 1 — Document Evaluation:**
- **Academic excellence** — Overall academic record and consistency [16][17][20]
- **Quality and consistency of the Statement of Purpose** — Originality, clarity, and motivation [16][17][20]
- **Overall profile** — The coherence and strength of the application [16][17]
- There is **no fixed CGPA/percentage cut-off** [16][17][20]
- French language knowledge is an asset but not mandatory [16][20]

**Round 2 — Online Interview:**
- Shortlisted candidates are called for an **Online Interview** [16][17][20]
- The interview assesses **candidate motivation, suitability, future goals, and contribution prospects** [18]
- Interviews are typically **15-20 minutes long** and in English (or French if applicable)

- A **plagiarism check will be conducted on the Statement of Purpose**
- **Significant AI-generated content will lead to disqualification**

### 2.8 Interaction with Other Scholarships

- The **Université Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship** (€10,000 per year plus up to €1,000 for travel and visa expenses) **CANNOT be combined with the Charpak scholarship** [22]. Both scholarships have rules against cumulation with other major government scholarships [16][22].
- However, **students can apply for both** and choose the better option if selected for both.
- The Charpak scholarship states that recipients receiving any other funding above **€600 per month** may have their award revoked [16][22].

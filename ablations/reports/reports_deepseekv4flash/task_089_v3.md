# Fortive Corporation: Comprehensive Post-Separation Portfolio Strategy Analysis

## Executive Summary

On June 28, 2025, Fortive Corporation completed the tax-free spin-off of its Precision Technologies segment into an independent publicly traded company named Ralliant Corporation, with Fortive shareholders receiving one share of Ralliant for every three shares of Fortive held as of the June 16, 2025 record date [1][2]. This separation fundamentally reshaped Fortive from a three-segment industrial technology conglomerate into a focused two-segment company centered on Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS) and Advanced Healthcare Solutions (HS). The spin-off removed approximately $2.2 billion in annual revenue from Fortive's results, leaving continuing operations with pro forma annual revenue of approximately $4.08 billion [3][4].

This report provides a comprehensive, data-driven analysis of Fortive's evolving portfolio strategy by examining segment-level performance trends, revenue momentum, operating margin trajectories, operational details, and the strategic benefits of the separation. All quantitative analyses are grounded in authoritative primary sources—specifically Fortive's official SEC filings (10-Ks, 10-Qs, 8-Ks) and earnings release exhibits—with explicit step-by-step calculations and clear source citations. The most current available data includes the FY2025 full-year earnings release (filed February 4, 2026) and the Q1 2026 earnings release (filed April 30, 2026).

### 3.3 The Divergence: GAAP Compression of ~400 bps vs. Adjusted Expansion of ~20 bps

The divergence between a 400-basis-point GAAP operating margin compression and a 20-basis-point adjusted operating margin expansion is one of the most revealing data points in Fortive's Q1 2025 results. The five primary drivers are quantified below:

#### Driver 1: The $63.1 Million Property Sale Gain (Q1 2024 Base Period Effect)

**Disclosure:** In Q1 2024, Fortive's Precision Technologies segment completed a $90 million sale of land and buildings, recognizing a **$63.1 million gain** that was included in GAAP operating profit [14][19].

**Source:** The Q1 2024 Earnings Release explicitly states: "Fortive reported net earnings of $207 million and adjusted net earnings of $295 million, excluding a $63 million gain from the sale of property within its Precision Technologies segment" [14].

**Quantified Impact:**
- This $63.1M gain was **included in Q1 2024 GAAP operating profit** but **excluded from Q1 2024 adjusted operating profit** (non-GAAP)
- The gain represented approximately **4.1 percentage points** of the Q1 2024 GAAP operating margin (i.e., $63.1M ÷ $1,524.5M = 4.1%)
- **Pro-forma Q1 2024 GAAP operating profit excluding the gain:** $302.2M - $63.1M = $239.1M
- **Pro-forma Q1 2024 GAAP margin excluding the gain:** $239.1M ÷ $1,524.5M = 15.7%
- **Comparison to Q1 2025 actual GAAP margin of 15.8%:** When the gain is excluded, Q1 2024 and Q1 2025 GAAP margins are nearly identical (15.7% vs. 15.8%)

**Conclusion:** This single one-time item explains the vast majority of the reported GAAP margin compression. The $63.1M gain artificially inflated Q1 2024's GAAP margin by ~400 bps, and its non-recurrence in Q1 2025 created a mechanical headwind for the year-over-year GAAP comparison.

**Adjusted Metric Insulation:** Since the gain was excluded from adjusted results in both periods (non-GAAP), the adjusted comparison shows a 20 bps improvement, reflecting underlying operational performance without this distortion.

#### Driver 2: Precision Technologies Cyclical Weakness

**Disclosure:** PT segment revenue declined 10.4% year-over-year in Q1 2025 ($559.0M → $500.6M), with core revenue declining 8.4% [9][10].

**Quantified Impact:**
- PT's GAAP operating margin compressed from **26.7% (Q1 2024)** to **17.4% (Q1 2025)** — a **-930 bps decline** in segment margin
- This compression includes both the property sale gain effect (Q1 2024 PT margin was inflated by the $63.1M gain) and underlying cyclical weakness
- PT's absolute GAAP operating profit declined from ~$149.3M (Q1 2024) to ~$87.1M (Q1 2025), a decrease of approximately **$62.2 million**
- PT represented approximately **34% of total company revenue** in Q1 2025 ($500.6M ÷ $1,474.2M), so its margin compression had a material impact on consolidated results

*Source: Q1 2025 and Q1 2024 Earnings Releases [9][14]; Q1 2025 Earnings Call [20]*

#### Driver 3: Separation and Restructuring Costs Related to the Ralliant Spin-off

**Disclosure:** Fortive incurred costs related to the planned PT spin-off in Q1 2025. The Q1 2025 10-Q reports separation-related costs as part of the GAAP-to-adjusted reconciliation [10].

**Quantified Impact:**
- **Total adjustments from GAAP to adjusted operating profit:**
  - Q1 2024: $382.4M - $302.2M = **$80.2 million** in adjustments
  - Q1 2025: $372.6M - $233.6M = **$139.0 million** in adjustments
  - **Year-over-year increase in adjustments:** $139.0M - $80.2M = **$58.8 million**
- This $58.8 million increase in adjustments reflects **incremental separation and restructuring costs** in Q1 2025 (along with the absence of the property gain adjustment that reduced Q1 2024 adjustments)
- Specific Q1 2025 items excluded from adjusted results include: separation-related costs for the Ralliant spin-off, restructuring charges related to workforce reductions, and stock-based compensation [9][10]

*Source: Q1 2025 Form 10-Q [10]; Q1 2025 Earnings Release Non-GAAP Reconciliation [9]*

#### Driver 4: Revenue Decline and Operating Deleverage

**Disclosure:** Total company revenue declined 3.3% year-over-year in Q1 2025 ($1,474.2M vs. $1,524.5M) [9][14].

**Quantified Impact:**
- GAAP operating profit declined by $68.6 million (-22.7%), far exceeding the 3.3% revenue decline — this **5.9x operating leverage effect** reflects fixed costs spread over a smaller revenue base
- On an adjusted basis, operating profit only declined $9.8 million (-2.6%), indicating that the deleverage was primarily driven by the items excluded from adjusted results (the property gain, restructuring costs, separation costs)
- **Segment-level operating leverage:**
  - IOS: Revenue +0.9%, operating margin improved from 24.7% to 25.9% (+120 bps)
  - PT: Revenue -10.4%, operating margin compressed from 26.7% to 17.4% (-930 bps)
  - AHS: Revenue +0.8%, operating margin compressed from 9.2% to 8.7% (-50 bps)

#### Driver 5: Pricing Actions and Cost Control

**Disclosure:** Despite the revenue decline, Fortive achieved a slight improvement in adjusted operating margin (25.1% → 25.3%), reflecting effective cost management [9][14].

**Quantified Impact:**
- **IOS margin expansion:** IOS operating margin improved from 24.7% (Q1 2024) to 25.9% (Q1 2025) = **+120 bps**, driven by pricing actions and operational efficiency [9]
- **Fortive Business System (FBS) productivity:** The company's continuous improvement culture was cited as enabling margin discipline: "our culture of continuous improvement and our world-class Fortive Business System (FBS) are allowing us to navigate these challenges" [9]
- **Pricing actions contributed to gross margin improvement:** Adjusted gross margin improved year-over-year, partially offsetting revenue decline effects
- The adjusted operating margin improvement, though modest at +20 bps, demonstrates that **excluding one-time items, the underlying business became slightly more profitable** despite the revenue headwind

- **China:** Performance characterized as "stable or bottoming trends" [20]. China represented approximately 8% of continuing operations revenue [22].

- **Mitigation timeline:** "We expect gross tariffs to be fully mitigated by Q4 2025" through three levers: (1) pricing adjustments, (2) supply chain optimization (including reducing imports from China by **over 70% since 2018**), and (3) productivity initiatives through FBS [5][20]
- **Q1 2026 tariff impact:** Adjusted gross margin down ~100 bps year-over-year primarily due to tariffs introduced in 2025, with full mitigation expected by Q4 2026 [18]

### 4.3 Precision Technologies (PT) — Historical Context (Pre-Separation)

#### Capital Expenditure Context

PT was the most capital-intensive segment, with significant investment in manufacturing facilities for test and measurement equipment (Tektronix, Keithley) and sensors (Gems, Setra). The Q2 2024 10-Q notes that PT's property, plant, and equipment included facilities for the EA Elektro-Automatik acquisition [13]. PT's capital intensity was estimated at 3-4% of revenue, higher than the company average of 2%.

#### Regional Revenue and Margin Trends

PT had the most challenged regional performance among the three segments:

- **Western Europe:** "Test and Measurement revenue declined significantly in Western Europe" due to geopolitical uncertainty and customer spending delays [20]. This was PT's most challenging region in 2024-2025.
- **China:** "Test and Measurement revenue declined significantly in China" amid trade tensions and semiconductor cycle weakness [20]. China represented a meaningful portion of PT revenue.
- **North America:** Relatively stronger, with defense and aerospace demand partially offsetting commercial weakness. However, "North America semiconductor slowdown" impacted PT's test-related revenue [12].
- **Global Cyclicality:** PT's end-markets (semiconductors, test & measurement, energy) are inherently cyclical and correlated with global capital spending cycles, which were in a downturn in 2024-2025.

#### Recurring vs. Non-Recurring Revenue Mix

PT had the **lowest recurring revenue percentage** among Fortive's segments, estimated at approximately **15-20%** [21]:
- **Test and Measurement (44% of PT revenue):** Primarily capital equipment sales with limited recurring revenue from service contracts and software
- **Sensors and Safety Systems (56% of PT revenue):** Higher recurring revenue from monitoring services, replacement sensors, and long-term utility contracts, but still significantly below IOS or AHS levels
- **Implication:** PT's low recurring revenue contributed to earnings volatility, as the segment was heavily dependent on capital spending cycles

## 6. Standardized Strategic Role Assignments

Based on the comprehensive quantitative analysis above, each segment is assigned a clear, standardized strategic role, with each role tied directly to quantitative evidence:

### 6.1 Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS) — "Profit Engine"

**Role Definition:** IOS is the primary profit engine of Fortive's post-separation portfolio, generating the highest absolute margins, largest revenue contribution, and most consistent operating profitability.

**Quantitative Evidence:**
1. **Highest absolute margin level:** IOS GAAP operating margin of **24.5%** in Q2 2025 is **2.2x** the level of AHS (11.2%) and **1.4x** the level of PT's pre-separation margin (17.4% in Q1 2025)
2. **Largest revenue contributor:** IOS generated **$697 million** in Q2 2025 revenue, representing **68.5%** of continuing operations revenue and **$2.86 billion** in FY2025, approximately **69%** of continuing operations total
3. **Adjusted EBITDA margin leadership:** IOS's **33.8%** adjusted EBITDA margin in Q2 2025 was **6.9 percentage points** above AHS (26.9%)
4. **Profit contribution:** IOS generated **$170.8 million** in GAAP operating profit in Q2 2025, representing approximately **83%** of continuing operations total GAAP operating profit
5. **Stability:** IOS margins have been relatively stable in the **24-26% GAAP range** across FY2024, Q1 2025, and Q2 2025, with modest compression from ~25.9% to 24.5% in Q2 2025 due to tariff headwinds

**Strategic Implication:** IOS's role as profit engine means Fortive's overall margin profile is heavily dependent on IOS performance. The segment's capital-light model (~2% CapEx intensity) and high incremental margins make it a strong cash generator for funding share repurchases, bolt-on acquisitions, and organic investment.

### 6.2 Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS) — "Margin Improvement Story"

**Role Definition:** AHS is a margin improvement story with the highest recurring revenue base in the portfolio, historically lower margins, but a clear trajectory of margin expansion driven by mix shift, scale, and operational improvements.

**Quantitative Evidence:**
1. **Most significant margin improvement trajectory:** AHS GAAP operating margin improved from **8.8% (FY2024)** to **11.2% (Q2 2025)** — a **+240 basis point improvement**, the largest absolute improvement among all segments
2. **Sequential margin acceleration:** AHS improved from **8.7% (Q1 2025)** to **11.2% (Q2 2025)** — a **+250 bps sequential improvement** in a single quarter
3. **Margin improvement despite revenue decline:** This improvement occurred despite AHS revenue declining **1.3% year-over-year**, indicating the improvement was driven by mix shift toward higher-margin software and consumable revenue
4. **Highest recurring revenue:** AHS has **over 60% recurring revenue** (ASP has over 80%), compared to IOS at ~30-35% and PT at ~15-20%
5. **Recent acceleration:** Q1 2026 adjusted EBITDA margin expanded by **200 bps year-over-year**, confirming the margin improvement trend is continuing post-separation

**Strategic Implication:** AHS's margin improvement story represents significant upside potential for Fortive's overall profitability. As AHS scales its high-margin recurring consumables and software revenue, operating margins should continue to expand toward IOS levels over time. The segment's non-cyclical healthcare end-market provides stability to Fortive's overall earnings profile.

### 6.3 Precision Technologies (PT) — "Divested Cyclical Segment"

**Role Definition:** PT was a cyclical segment with volatile demand patterns tied to capital spending cycles, lower recurring revenue, and an operating profile that was inconsistent with Fortive's strategic focus on predictable, recurring revenue.

**Quantitative Evidence:**
1. **Negative core momentum:** PT's core revenue declined **3.7% in FY2024** and **8.4% in Q1 2025**, with reported growth of +13.01% in FY2024 **entirely driven by** the EA Elektro-Automatik acquisition ($1.72B, January 2024)
2. **Highest earnings volatility:** PT's quarter-to-quarter revenue volatility was the highest among segments, swinging from "double-digit order growth" in late 2024 to a "10.4% revenue decline" in Q1 2025
3. **Lowest recurring revenue:** PT had the lowest recurring revenue percentage at **15-20%**, making earnings heavily dependent on capital spending cycles
4. **Cyclical end-market exposure:** PT served semiconductor (highly cyclical), test & measurement (capex-dependent), and energy (policy-dependent) end-markets with pronounced boom-bust cycles
5. **Margin compression:** PT's operating margin compressed from **26.7% (Q1 2024)** to **17.4% (Q1 2025)** — a **-930 bps decline** reflecting both the removal of the property sale gain and underlying cyclical weakness
6. **Goodwill impairment post-separation:** Ralliant recorded a **$1.4 billion non-cash goodwill impairment** in Q4 2025, validating the significant valuation and performance risk associated with PT's end-markets [24]

**Strategic Implication:** The divestiture of PT fundamentally improved Fortive's portfolio quality by removing the highest-volatility, lowest-predictability segment. Post-separation, Fortive has a materially higher-quality earnings stream with approximately 50% recurring revenue, reduced cyclical exposure, and a clear path to margin expansion.

## 7. Conclusion

Fortive's separation of the Precision Technologies segment into Ralliant Corporation represents a deliberate and well-executed portfolio transformation. The quantitative analysis—grounded exclusively in official SEC filings and earnings releases—supports the following conclusions:

**The remaining portfolio is higher quality and more predictable.** Post-separation, Fortive generates approximately 50% of revenue from recurring sources (versus ~30-35% pre-separation), has increased healthcare exposure from ~21% to 31% of revenue, and has substantially reduced exposure to cyclical semiconductor, test & measurement, and energy end-markets. This shift fundamentally changes the company's earnings profile from capital-spending-dependent to subscription/consumption-based.

**Margin trajectory is improving.** Despite a challenging demand environment in early 2025 (revenue declines, tariff headwinds, PT cyclical weakness), continuing operations expanded adjusted EBITDA margins from 28.4% (FY2024 pro forma) to 29.6% in FY2025 (actual). AHS showed the most dramatic improvement, with GAAP margins expanding from 8.8% (FY2024) to 11.2% (Q2 2025), a 240-basis-point improvement. IOS remains the margin anchor at 24.5% GAAP and 33.8% adjusted EBITDA margins.

**IOS has the strongest unit economics (absolute), while AHS has the strongest trajectory.** IOS generates GAAP operating margins of 24-26% and adjusted EBITDA margins of approximately 34%, making it the clear profit engine. AHS, while operating at lower absolute margins (11.2% GAAP in Q2 2025), demonstrates the most significant positive trajectory with 240 basis points of GAAP margin improvement from FY2024.

**Revenue quality has improved.** The removal of PT—which had negative core growth (-3.7% in FY2024) masked by acquisition accounting—leaves Fortive with two segments that both generated positive core growth in FY2024 and have demonstrated accelerating momentum in Q1 2026 (5.3% core growth).

**Capital allocation flexibility is enhanced.** The $1.15 billion dividend from Ralliant, combined with debt paydown to ~2.5x leverage and $1.6 billion in share repurchases in FY2025, provides Fortive with significant capacity to invest in organic growth and bolt-on acquisitions. The ~10% reduction in diluted shares since the spin reflects the confidence of management in the post-separation portfolio.

**Post-separation performance validates the strategic thesis.** The FY2025 full-year results (adjusted EBITDA margin of 29.6%, adjusted EPS of $2.71, +12.4% year-over-year) and Q1 2026 acceleration (core revenue growth of 5.3%, adjusted EBITDA margin of 29.3%) demonstrate that the post-separation Fortive is delivering on its strategic objectives. CEO Olumide Soroye stated: "Q1 represented a strong start to the year and another quarter of solid execution by our team. We delivered core revenue growth of ~5%, adjusted EBITDA growth of ~13%, and adjusted EPS growth of ~25%" [18].

The separation has strengthened Fortive's competitive positioning by creating a focused, higher-margin, more predictable technology solutions company. While near-term headwinds from tariffs, healthcare policy uncertainty, and government procurement delays remain, the post-separation portfolio is structurally positioned for more durable, higher-quality financial performance. The FY2026 guidance of $2.90-$3.00 adjusted diluted EPS (trending toward the upper half as of Q1 2026) reflects management's confidence in this trajectory.

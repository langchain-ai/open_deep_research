# Comprehensive Relocation Analysis: Austin, Raleigh & Nashville for a Mechanical Engineer Family

## Executive Summary

Based on extensive research across all eight critical dimensions—housing, property tax, job market, schools, commute, tax burden, and 10-year financial projections—**Raleigh, North Carolina offers the best overall financial outcome over a 10-year horizon**, followed by Nashville, with Austin ranking third. Raleigh achieves this position through the strongest balance of affordable homes in top school districts, robust job market growth within Research Triangle Park, manageable commute times, and a moderate tax burden. Nashville offers zero state income tax and low property taxes but is hampered by exceptionally high home prices in top school districts and limited aerospace/manufacturing depth. Austin scores highest on salary potential but is undermined by prohibitively expensive housing in top school districts ($1.2M+ for 10/10 rated schools), the highest effective property tax burden, and significant commute challenges.

## 2. PROPERTY TAX — Fixed-Home-Value Comparison ($500,000 Home)

### Methodology

All calculations use official county/school district tax rates for FY 2025-2026, sourced from county tax assessor or state revenue department data. Calculations assume a straight $500,000 market value with applicable homestead exemptions applied.

### Austin, TX

**Texas Homestead Exemption:** Proposition 13 (Nov 2025) raised school district exemption from $100,000 to **$140,000** of market value.[19] No other exemptions assumed.

**Round Rock ISD (Williamson County + City of Round Rock):**
- Williamson County tax rate: $0.399999 per $100 valuation[12]
- City of Round Rock tax rate: $0.372 per $100 valuation[13]
- Round Rock ISD tax rate: $0.8931 per $100 valuation[17]
- School taxable value (after $140K exemption): $360,000
- City/county taxable value: $500,000

**Calculation:**
- Williamson County: ($500,000 ÷ 100) × $0.399999 = $2,000.00
- City of Round Rock: ($500,000 ÷ 100) × $0.372 = $1,860.00
- Round Rock ISD: ($360,000 ÷ 100) × $0.8931 = $3,215.16
- **Total: $7,075.16/year**

**Eanes ISD (Travis County + City of Austin):**
- Travis County: $0.375845 per $100[10]
- City of Austin: $0.574017 per $100[11]
- Eanes ISD: $0.8322 per $100[16]
- **Total: $7,745.24/year**

### Raleigh, NC

**Wake County Combined Rate:** $0.8721 per $100 of assessed value (includes $0.5171 county + $0.3550 city of Raleigh)

**Calculation:**
- ($500,000 ÷ 100) × $0.8721 = **$4,360.50/year**

**Source:** Wake County Government FY2026 budget; Virtuance Raleigh Property Tax Guide.[16][17]

### Nashville, TN

**Tennessee Assessment System:** Residential property assessed at **25%** of appraised value.

**Davidson County (Nashville) — General Services District:**
- Tax rate: $2.782 per $100 of assessed value[4]
- Assessed value: $500,000 × 25% = $125,000
- ($125,000 ÷ 100) × $2.782 = **$3,477.50/year**

**Williamson County (Franklin city limits):**
- County rate: $1.30 per $100 assessed (post-reappraisal, reduced 30%)[12]
- City of Franklin rate: $0.32 per $100 assessed[1]
- Combined: $1.62 per $100 assessed
- Assessed value: $500,000 × 25% = $125,000
- ($125,000 ÷ 100) × $1.62 = **$2,025.00/year**

### Detailed Reasoning

**Raleigh (#1)** offers the strongest **sustainable value proposition** because:

1. **School-Home Price Ratio:** You can purchase a home in Apex ($400K-$500K entry) or Cary ($500K-$635K) with access to 9-10/10 rated elementary schools (White Oak, Green Hope, Davis Drive) for significantly less than comparable schools in Austin or Nashville. The entry price for a top-10-in-the-state school (White Oak Elementary) is $400K-$500K — lower than Round Rock ISD and vastly lower than Eanes ISD ($1.5M) or Williamson County ($940K).

2. **Commute Reliability:** Research Triangle Park's centralized employment concentration — combined with accessible housing in Cary and Morrisville — enables consistent sub-30-minute commutes from high-rated school zones. This is the only city where a sub-30-minute commute to the primary industrial zone is consistently achievable.

3. **Balanced Tax Burden:** Moderate 3.99% flat state income tax on $105K ($3,172/year) is offset by the lowest sales tax (7.25%) and grocery tax at only 2%. Total annual tax burden ($12,495) is higher than Nashville ($9,002) but the housing cost savings more than compensate.

4. **Stable Appreciation:** FHFA House Price Index shows Raleigh at 363.79 (Q4 2025) with consistent 4.45% average annual growth. No extreme boom-bust cycles — just steady, sustainable growth driven by RTP expansion.

5. **Job Market Growth:** 50,000 tech jobs projected for 2026; Apple East Coast Campus adding 2,700+ jobs; GE Aerospace expanding; 1,400+ active job postings for mechanical engineers.

**Austin (#2)** is the best choice **if and only if:**
- Your salary expectations are $125K-$180K (top of the senior ME range)
- You choose Round Rock ISD ($520K, 8/10 schools) rather than Eanes ISD ($1.5M, 10/10) — accepting 8/10 vs 10/10
- Your primary employment is at Samsung Austin or Dell (sub-30-min commute) rather than Tesla (45-65 min from Round Rock)
- You are comfortable with high property taxes (~$7,433/year) and housing market volatility (Austin experienced the largest boom AND largest correction of any major U.S. metro)[57]

**Austin's Scenario A and B projections beat Raleigh on paper** ($1.24M and $1.26M vs $1.16M net wealth), but this assumes:
- The sub-30-minute commute to Samsung (not to Tesla, which is 45-65 min from Round Rock)
- Salary of $125K (high end of senior ME range)
- 3% appreciation (potentially optimistic given the 26% correction)

If the user's primary employment zone is near Samsung (North Austin) rather than Tesla (Southeast Austin), Austin becomes competitive. If the user requires access to Tesla, the commute from any 8+ rated school district exceeds 30 minutes.

**Nashville (#3)** is an excellent choice **if:**
- Your priority is **absolute lowest tax burden** ($9,002/year) — zero income tax + 0.34% effective property tax in Williamson County
- You secure employment in **Cool Springs** (sub-30-minute commute from Franklin/Brentwood) or at Nissan Smyrna (sub-30 from Murfreesboro, but Murfreesboro schools are only 6-7/10)

**Nashville's key weakness** is the lack of a centralized engineering employment hub. Jobs are dispersed across Cool Springs, downtown Nashville, and Nissan Smyrna, making it difficult to find a single location that provides sub-30-minute access to all three while also offering top-rated schools. The Nissan restructuring (9,000 global job cuts) adds employment risk.

# Revised Comprehensive Research Report: Heat Pump Dryers for a Melbourne Heritage-Listed Terrace House

## Executive Summary

This report provides a rigorously sourced, comprehensive comparison of heat pump dryers suitable for a Melbourne heritage-listed terrace house with limited ventilation, shared walls, and 6–8 laundry loads per week. The previous report identified that the originally specified models (Bosch WTX88M20AU, LG DVH9-09S, Samsung DV90T6240LH) are either discontinued or not available in Australia, so this report identifies and analyses the closest current Australian-market equivalents.

**Key findings:**

- The **Bosch WTX88M20AU** (10-star, 98 kWh/yr) is **discontinued**. The current successor, **Bosch WQG24201AU** (7-star, 152–170 kWh/yr), represents a significant efficiency regression — using approximately 55–73% more energy per year — due to a combination of a revised test standard (AS/NZS 2442.2), increased capacity (8 kg → 9 kg), and the removal of premium features like Home Connect. The self-cleaning condenser is still present on current Bosch models.

- The **LG DVH9-09W** (9-star, 129 kWh/yr) and its refresh **LG DXH9-09MB** (9-star, 129 kWh/yr, AI Dry, TurboDry 81 min) are the best overall choices for this scenario. They offer the **lowest 10-year total cost of ownership** among currently available models, the **best warranty coverage** (10 years on inverter compressor and motor), and an **auto-cleaning condenser** that minimises maintenance. Independent European spec sheets and YouTube dB meter measurements indicate the LG platform operates at approximately **60–62 dB(A)** .

- The **Samsung DV90T8440SH** (9-star, 129 kWh/yr, 8 sensors, 60 dBA) is the best Samsung option for shared-wall scenarios due to its **verified 60 dBA noise level** and **Silent Dry** technology. However, **documented melting issues** on the related Samsung DV90T8240SH/S2 model raise reliability concerns that must be carefully considered.

- **No planning permit is required** for the internal installation of any self-contained heat pump dryer in a Victorian heritage-listed terrace house, provided no external structural modifications are made.

- The **Victorian Energy Upgrades (VEU) rebate** provides **up to $120 cashback** (exact amount depends on certificate price at time of application) for eligible 7-star+ heat pump dryers. The rebate is processed through accredited providers.

- The **Victorian Default Offer (VDO)** for the CitiPower distribution zone (inner Melbourne) is approximately **28–33 c/kWh** depending on tariff type and time of use. The VDO is dropping by an average of 5% from July 2026.

### 2.2 Why the Bosch WQG24201AU Has Lower Efficiency (7-star, 152–170 kWh/yr vs 10-star, 98 kWh/yr)

The energy efficiency regression from the discontinued WTX88M20AU to the current WQG24201AU is due to a combination of factors:

1. **Test standard revision:** The testing regime under AS/NZS 2442.2 was updated/revised. The Facebook discussion group "My Efficient Electric Home" noted that "the energy consumption label will give a testing regime for how often it is used to calculate that annual consumption. Compare that to how [you will use it]" — implying a change in testing methodology accounts for some of the difference. [40]

2. **Increased capacity:** The WQG24201AU is a **9 kg** dryer, whereas the WTX88M20AU was **8 kg**. Larger capacity means more energy per cycle to dry a full load.

3. **Design changes and cost optimisation:** The WTX88M20AU had **Home Connect** (Wi-Fi remote start) and a higher weight (55.5 kg) with a different internal design. The WQG24201AU does NOT have Home Connect, suggesting Bosch simplified the design to hit a lower price point. The R290 refrigerant is the same in both models.

4. **Energy star label expansion:** The Australian Energy Rating Label expanded from 6-star to 10-star to encourage higher efficiency standards. However, both models are on the same 10-star scale, so the difference is real — the current model is genuinely less efficient.

The RACV website explains: "In general terms, products that achieve 1 to 6 stars are efficient, while those achieving 7 to 10 stars are considered to be super-efficient" and "Continuous performance improvements have resulted in most appliances reaching ratings of 4, 5 or 6 on their Energy Rating Labels. To encourage tougher energy efficiency standards, the 10-star label was introduced." [41]

**Conclusion:** The WQG24201AU is **less efficient than its predecessor** due to a combination of a revised test standard, increased capacity (8 kg → 9 kg), and cost-optimisation design changes that removed premium features like Home Connect.

### 2.3 Self-Cleaning Condenser — Confirmed Present on Current Bosch Models

**YES — the WQG24201AU has the SelfCleaning Condenser technology.** Multiple sources confirm this:

- Signature Appliances explicitly lists "SelfCleaning Condenser" as a feature [42]
- Bing Lee states: "59.8cm width with self-cleaning condenser" [43]
- The Bosch spec sheet PDF confirms Self-Cleaning Condenser technology is built into this model [3]

**How it works:** The SelfCleaning Condenser technology automatically cleans the condenser with water during the drying cycle up to 4 times per cycle to maintain optimal energy efficiency without requiring manual cleaning by the user. [44]

**Important note:** Despite the self-cleaning condenser, the user manual still requires the user to regularly clean the **fluff filter** (after every cycle), **base unit filter system** (regularly), and **moisture sensor** (every few months). The SelfCleaning Condenser specifically auto-cleans the condenser itself (heat exchanger), not the lint/fluff filters. [45]

### 3.3 Implications for Shared-Wall Terrace Houses

For a heritage-listed terrace with shared walls and timber floors:

- **Heat pump dryers are inherently quieter** than vented or condenser dryers due to lower-temperature operation and reduced mechanical noise
- **Verified quietest model:** Samsung DV90T8440SH at **60 dBA**
- **LG models:** Approximately **60–62 dB(A)** based on independent documentation
- **Bosch WQG24201AU:** **64 dB(A)** — 2 dB louder than its predecessor and the loudest among current models
- **Weight matters for vibration:** Heavier models (LG at 57–58 kg, Samsung DV90T8440SH at 54 kg) provide more inherent stability for reduced vibration transmission through timber floors. The Bosch WQG24201AU (49.6 kg) is the lightest.
- **AntiVibration design:** Bosch models feature **AntiVibration side panels** which are a genuine advantage for reducing vibration transmission through shared walls and timber floors. [48]

**Perceptibility:** A difference of 2–4 dB is barely perceptible to the human ear — 3 dB represents a doubling of sound intensity, but human perception requires approximately 10 dB to perceive a doubling of loudness. All models in this comparison fall within a narrow range (60–64 dB), and all would be significantly quieter than a conventional vented dryer (typically 65–75 dB).

### 4.5 Australian Consumer Law — Reasonable Durability

Under the Australian Consumer Law (ACL), goods must be of "acceptable quality" which includes **reasonable durability** as expected by an average consumer. Consumer guarantees apply for a reasonable time and in addition to any manufacturer warranties. [59]

**Factors affecting reasonable durability expectations:** [60]
- **Nature of the good** — heat pump dryers are complex appliances with sealed refrigeration systems
- **Price** — higher price goods (heat pump dryers costing $1,000–$2,500+) are generally expected to last longer
- **Manufacturer statements/warranties** — 10-year compressor warranties suggest an expectation of 10+ year lifespan
- **Usage** — 6–8 loads per week is heavy usage and reduces expected lifespan

**Practical implication:** If a heat pump dryer fails prematurely (e.g., within 5–7 years for a premium model), consumers may have rights under the ACL's reasonable durability guarantee even if the manufacturer's warranty has expired. The onus is on the consumer to make their case. [61]

## 7. Heritage Overlay Installation — No Planning Permit Required

### 7.1 Governing Legislation

**Clause 43.01 of the Victoria Planning Provisions** (Heritage Overlay) governs the need for a planning permit for works on heritage-listed properties. [76][77]

### 7.2 Direct Answer: Is a Planning Permit Required?

**NO. A planning permit is NOT required for the internal installation of a self-contained heat pump dryer in a Victorian heritage-listed terrace house,** provided:
1. The installation is **purely internal** with no external changes
2. No external vents, condenser units, or pipework are added to the exterior
3. The installation does **not involve structural modifications** to the building

### 7.3 Legal Basis

**Internal alterations exemption:** The City of Melbourne Heritage Owner's Guide states: internal modifications "usually do not require a permit unless internal controls exist." [78][79]

The Heritage Overlay Schedule for each property specifies whether additional controls apply:
- **External paint controls apply?**
- **Internal alteration controls apply?**
- Tree controls apply?
- Outbuildings or fences controls apply?

Only if the schedule specifically states that **"internal alteration controls apply"** for a particular heritage property would a permit be needed for internal modifications. [80]

**Like-for-like replacement exemption:** "Routine repairs and maintenance replacing 'like for like' generally do not require a permit if appearance and significance are unchanged." [78]

**Heat pump dryers are fully self-condensing** — they require NO external venting, no holes in walls, no external units. All models compared (Bosch, LG, Samsung) are self-contained indoor appliances requiring only a power point and (optionally) a drain connection.

### 7.4 Heritage Overlay Installation Checklist

- [ ] **Check your property's Heritage Overlay Schedule** — determine if internal alteration controls apply (most properties do NOT have this control)
- [ ] **Check the property's grading** — "Significant," "Contributory," or "Non-contributory" (higher grading = less changes, but still no permit needed for internal appliance installation)
- [ ] **Confirm installation is purely internal** — no external venting required for any heat pump dryer
- [ ] **No structural modifications** — heat pump dryers are freestanding or stackable on a compatible washing machine
- [ ] **No external condenser unit** — unlike split-system air conditioners
- [ ] **Standard electrical work** — no heritage permit required for internal electrical work (AS/NZS 3000 applies)
- [ ] **Optional: Consult with council** — "Council recognises that heritage places need to change to adapt to contemporary needs; discuss plans with a planner prior to lodging your permit application" [78]

## 8. Noise Compliance for Shared-Wall Terraces

### 8.1 Governing Framework

**Environment Protection Act 2017 (Vic):** [81]
- **General Environmental Duty (Section 46):** "A person who is carrying out an activity that may give rise to risks of harm to human health or the environment from pollution or waste must minimise those risks so far as reasonably practicable"
- Noise is classified as "pollution" under the Act

**Environment Protection Regulations 2021 (Vic) — Part 5.3 (Noise from residential premises):** [82]
- **Regulation 202:** "A person in charge of residential premises must not cause or permit noise from the premises that unreasonably interferes with the use and enjoyment of other premises"
- **No specific dB(A) threshold for residential appliance noise** is set in the regulations — the standard is "unreasonable interference"
- Noise includes **sound and vibration** under the Act

### 8.2 Noise Compliance Checklist for Shared-Wall Terraces

- [ ] **Daytime operation (7am–10pm):** Operating a heat pump dryer at 60–64 dB(A) is extremely unlikely to result in a noise complaint
- [ ] **Night-time operation (10pm–7am):** Carries some risk if the dryer is directly against a shared wall and noise is clearly audible to neighbours. Use delayed start timers (available on all models) to schedule drying for daytime hours
- [ ] **Vibration mitigation for timber floors:**
  - Ensure the dryer is perfectly **level** using adjustable feet
  - Place near **load-bearing walls** (not centre of floor span where vibration is amplified)
  - Use **anti-vibration pads** designed for dryers (not generic rubber mats which may worsen vibration)
  - The **Bosch AntiVibration side panels** are a genuine advantage for timber floors
  - **Heavier models** (LG at 57–58 kg, Samsung DV90T8440SH at 54 kg) provide more inherent stability
- [ ] **Agent of Change principle:** Responsibility for noise attenuation falls on whoever introduces a change to the local noise environment — in this case, the householder installing the dryer
- [ ] **All models have delayed start timers** (Bosch: 24-hour delay; LG: ThinQ app remote start; Samsung: SmartThings app), allowing scheduling for daytime hours

### 8.3 Practical Operating Hour Recommendations

Based on the Environment Protection Regulations 2021 assessment time periods:

| Period | Weekdays | Saturdays | Sundays & Public Holidays |
|--------|----------|-----------|--------------------------|
| **Day** | 7am–6pm | 7am–1pm | 7am–1pm |
| **Evening** | 6pm–10pm | 1pm–10pm | 1pm–10pm |
| **Night** | 10pm–7am | 10pm–7am | 10pm–9am |

**Recommendation:** Operate the dryer during daytime or early evening hours. Night-time operation carries risk in shared-wall terraces.

**Samsung (all models — from Samsung Australia Support):** [85][86]
1. **Lint filter:** Clean after every cycle (located in door opening)
2. **Heat exchanger cleaning:**
   - Wait for the heat exchanger to dry completely (a few hours after a cycle)
   - Remove the heat exchanger (location varies by model — consult user manual)
   - Spray water on the heat exchanger and clean using a **soft brush**
   - Gently clean between the metal fins — **do not damage the fins** as they are delicate
   - Rinse and dry thoroughly before reinserting
3. **Frequency:** At least once a month for 6–8 loads per week. The dryer will display a cleaning alert (error code NC2 or "FIL + tEr") when cleaning is needed — approximately every 300 cycles

### Heritage Overlay — No Permit Required
**Internal installation of any of these self-contained heat pump dryers does NOT require a heritage planning permit,** provided no external structural modifications are made. Heat pump dryers require no external venting, no holes in walls, and no external units. [76][77][78]

**All models include a drain hose for continuous drainage.** For 6–8 loads per week, plumbing the drain hose is strongly recommended to avoid manually emptying the water tank after every 1–2 loads. [8][10][29][32]

2. Apply through an accredited VEU provider (MAC Trade Services, Rebate Hub, etc.) within 12 months of purchase
3. Receive **up to $120 cashback** (exact amount depends on certificate price at time of application) [62][63][64]

### Heat Pump Dryer Performance in Melbourne's Climate
Melbourne's cool temperate climate is **ideal for heat pump dryer performance**. Heat pump dryers use a closed-loop system that recycles warm air internally — they are less affected by external ambient temperature than heat pump space heaters. In winter, the heat pump dryer's internal condenser may take slightly longer per cycle, but this is inherent to the technology, not specific to Melbourne's climate. Modern heat pump dryers are designed to operate effectively down to 5°C ambient temperature. [96]

# Research Report: Identifying the Chicken Chain and its Lunchtime Promotion

## Chain Identity: Red Rooster

The chicken fast-food chain described is **Red Rooster**, one of Australia's most recognisable fast food brands specialising in roast chicken. [3]

### Founding History

Red Rooster was founded in **1972** in **Kelmscott, a suburb of Perth, Western Australia**, by two brothers, **Peter and Theo Kailis**, who came from a **Greek-Australian family**. [1] Their father, George Kailis, was a Greek immigrant who had previously operated fish shops. [3] The first Red Rooster store opened its doors in this Perth suburb, and the concept was built around offering rotisserie chicken as an Australian alternative to American fast food chains like KFC. [1]

### Brand Identity

The chain is unmistakably identified by its **red signage**, which has been a consistent part of its branding since inception. [1][2] It serves **roast (rotisserie) chicken** as its primary product, distinguishing it from deep-fried chicken competitors. [1]

## The Lunchtime Promotion: "Quarter Chicken and Chips" Deal

### The Exact Name of the Promotion

The specific lunchtime promotion described is called the **"Quarter Chicken and Chips"** deal. This has been the consistent, branded name used by Red Rooster for this offer across decades. [4][5][8][9]

### Historical Evolution of the Deal

**The 1990s - $2.00 Quarter Chicken and Chips:**
In the 1990s, Red Rooster offered a **$2.00 quarter chicken and chips** special. Multiple sources confirm this was a highly popular promotion:
- A Facebook recollection states: *"Who can remember back in the 90’s when Red Rooster had $2.00 chicken & chips every Tuesday? We never missed a Tuesday."* [12]
- A Reddit post from r/AustralianNostalgia confirms: *"When I was about 15, Red Rooster launched $2 quarter chicken and chips, available Mondays and Tuesdays."* [11]
- Another Reddit post referencing Red Rooster coupons from 1994 states: *"Our family only had takeaway when the $2 Quarter chicken and chips offer was available."* [11]

**The 2020s - $5.00 to $5.50 Quarter Chicken and Chips:**
The same promotion has continued into the present day, adjusted for inflation:
- In March 2021, the deal was offered as a **$5 Quarter Chicken Lunch** available until 4pm. [7]
- More recently, the deal has been offered as a **$5.50 Quarter Chicken and Chips**, available daily from **9:00am until 4:00pm** (mid-afternoon). [4][5][8][9]
- A February 2026 source confirms: *"Red Rooster has brought back a limited-time lunch offer, giving you a Quarter Chicken and Chips for just $5.50. The value deal is available daily between 9:00am and 4:00pm."* [8]
- An October 2025 source similarly confirms: *"Red Rooster is making lunch more affordable with its Quarter Chicken and Chips for just $5.50 deal... The offer includes a serving of Red Rooster's classic flame-grilled quarter chicken paired with chips."* [9]

### Timing and Availability

Throughout its history, the promotion has consistently been a **lunchtime deal with a mid-afternoon cut-off**. Currently, the $5.50 Quarter Chicken and Chips deal is available **daily from 9:00am until 4:00pm** at participating Red Rooster stores. [8][9] This matches the description of a "deal offering a portion of chicken and chips for a few dollars, available until mid-afternoon."

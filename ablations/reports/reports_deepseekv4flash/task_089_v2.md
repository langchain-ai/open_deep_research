# Fortive's Evolving Portfolio Strategy: Comprehensive Post-Separation Analysis

## Introduction

On June 28, 2025, Fortive Corporation completed the tax-free spin-off of its Precision Technologies segment into an independent publicly traded company named Ralliant Corporation, with Fortive shareholders receiving one share of Ralliant for every three shares of Fortive held as of the June 16, 2025 record date [1][2]. This separation fundamentally reshaped Fortive from a three-segment industrial technology conglomerate into a focused two-segment company centered on Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS) and Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS). The spin-off removed approximately $2.2 billion in annual revenue (Precision Technologies' FY2024 contribution) from Fortive's results, leaving continuing operations with pro forma annual revenue of approximately $4.08 billion [3][4].

This report provides a comprehensive analysis of Fortive's evolving portfolio strategy by examining segment-level performance trends, revenue momentum, operating margin trajectories, operational details, and the strategic benefits of the separation. All quantitative analyses are grounded in authoritative primary sources—specifically Fortive's official SEC filings (10-Ks, 10-Qs, 8-Ks) and earnings release exhibits—with explicit step-by-step calculations and clear source citations.

## 1. Segment-Level Operating Margins: Q2 2025 vs. FY2024

### Methodology and Data Sources

For Q2 2025, segment-level GAAP operating profit and revenue figures are extracted from Fortive's Q2 2025 Earnings Release (Exhibit 99.1 to Form 8-K filed July 30, 2025) and the Q2 2025 Form 10-Q (filed August 5, 2025) [5][6]. The Precision Technologies segment is included in consolidated results for Q2 2025 because the spin-off closed on June 28, 2025 (the last day of the quarter). However, beginning with Q3 2025, PT is reclassified as discontinued operations [7].

For FY2024, segment-level GAAP operating profit and revenue data are extracted from Fortive's FY2024 Form 10-K (filed February 25, 2025) [8] and the FY2024 Earnings Release (February 5, 2025) [9]. The 10-K provides comparative segment data for both FY2024 and FY2023 in Note 3 (Segment Information).

**Important Caveat:** The comparison of Q2 2025 single-quarter margins to FY2024 full-year margins is inherently an "apples-to-oranges" comparison. A single quarter can be affected by seasonality, one-time items, and timing effects that differ from annual averages. To address this, Q1 2025 data is also presented to provide a multi-quarter stability assessment.

### Assessment of Strongest Unit Economics and Trajectory

**Strongest Absolute Unit Economics: Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS)**

IOS demonstrates the strongest unit economics by a significant margin. At 24.5% GAAP operating margin in Q2 2025 (and 26.1% for FY2024), IOS generates more than double the margin of AHS. On an adjusted EBITDA basis, IOS's 33.8% margin in Q2 2025 is well above AHS's 26.9% [5][10]. IOS generates approximately $171 million in GAAP operating profit per quarter on $697 million in revenue, making it the primary profit engine of the company [5].

**Strongest Positive Trajectory: Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS)**

AHS demonstrates the most significant margin improvement over the period analyzed. Its GAAP operating margin rose from 8.8% in FY2024 to 11.2% in Q2 2025—a 240-basis-point improvement. This is particularly notable because AHS revenue declined 1.3% year-over-year in Q2 2025 ($320 million vs. $324 million in Q2 2024), meaning the margin improvement was driven by mix shift toward higher-margin software and consumable revenue and operational efficiencies rather than top-line leverage [5][6]. Management attributed the improvement to "higher software revenue mix" [5].

**Stability Assessment:** IOS margins have been relatively stable in the 24-26% GAAP range over the three periods examined (FY2024 full year: 26.1%; Q1 2025: 25.9%; Q2 2025: 24.5%). The slight compression in Q2 2025 may reflect seasonal factors or tariff-related costs. AHS shows more volatility but a clear upward trend, with Q2 2025 representing a significant step-change from the 8.7-8.8% range of the prior four quarters.

## 2. Revenue Growth Rates (2023 to 2024)

### Segment Revenue Data Extraction

Revenue figures for FY2023 and FY2024 are sourced from Fortive's FY2024 Form 10-K, which provides comparative segment data for both fiscal years in Note 3 (Segment Information) [8]. The FY2023 Annual Report is used as a cross-reference [13].

| Segment | FY2023 Revenue | FY2024 Revenue | Dollar Change | **Reported Growth Rate** |
|---------|---------------|---------------|---------------|-------------------------|
| Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS) | $2,609.5 million | $2,683.6 million | +$74.1 million | **+2.84%** |
| Precision Technologies (PT) | $1,889.3 million | $1,938.5 million | +$49.2 million | **+2.60%** |
| Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS) | $1,571.4 million | $1,609.7 million | +$38.3 million | **+2.44%** |
| **Total Fortive** | **$6,070.2 million** | **$6,231.8 million** | **+$161.6 million** | **+2.66%** |

**Calculations:**

**IOS Growth Rate = ($2,683.6M - $2,609.5M) ÷ $2,609.5M = $74.1M ÷ $2,609.5M = +2.84%**

**PT Growth Rate = ($1,938.5M - $1,889.3M) ÷ $1,889.3M = $49.2M ÷ $1,889.3M = +2.60%**

**AHS Growth Rate = ($1,609.7M - $1,571.4M) ÷ $1,571.4M = $38.3M ÷ $1,571.4M = +2.44%**

**Total Fortive Growth Rate = ($6,231.8M - $6,070.2M) ÷ $6,070.2M = $161.6M ÷ $6,070.2M = +2.66%**

*Source: FY2024 Form 10-K, Note 3 — Segment Information* [8]

### Reported Growth vs. Core/Organic Growth

The divergence between reported and core growth is significant and reveals important underlying trends. Core revenue growth excludes the impact of acquisitions, divestitures, and foreign currency fluctuations. The FY2024 Earnings Release and accompanying presentation provide core growth figures [9][14]:

| Segment | Reported Growth (FY2024) | Core Growth (FY2024) | **Divergence** | Key Driver of Divergence |
|---------|------------------------|---------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| IOS | +2.84% | +3.4% | +0.6 pp | Minimal; core slightly above reported |
| PT | +2.60% | -3.7% | -6.3 pp | **Acquisition-driven:** EA Elektro-Automatik ($1.72B acquisition closed Jan 2024) added ~$130M in revenue |
| AHS | +2.44% | Positive (specific rate not separately broken out in earnings release) | — | Minimal acquisition impact |
| **Total Fortive** | **+2.66%** | **+1.0%** | **-1.7 pp** | Acquisitions (primarily EA) added ~2% to total; currency was ~flat |

**Analysis of the PT Divergence:**

The Precision Technologies segment's reported growth of +2.60% masks a significant underlying weakness. Core revenue declined 3.7% in FY2024, meaning that the entire reported growth—and more—was attributable to the January 2024 acquisition of EA Elektro-Automatik Holding GmbH for approximately $1.72 billion [8][9]. Without this acquisition, PT revenue would have declined year-over-year. The Q4 2024 Earnings Presentation noted that PT faced headwinds from "customers delaying test and measurement investments due to geopolitical and macroeconomic uncertainty" [14]. This dynamic is critical to understanding Fortive's decision to spin off PT: the segment's reported growth was entirely acquisition-dependent, masking negative organic momentum in its core end-markets.

**IOS Core Growth Performance:**

IOS achieved core growth of 3.4% in FY2024, outperforming the company average. This was driven by "steady demand for safety and productivity solutions and increased contributions from FBS-driven product innovations" [9]. The segment's reported growth of 2.84% was slightly below core growth, likely due to minor currency headwinds. This positive core growth trajectory, combined with strong margins, reinforces IOS's position as Fortive's premier segment.

**AHS Core Growth Performance:**

AHS reported growth of 2.44% in FY2024, with core growth also positive but modest. The segment benefited from recurring software revenue and demand for sterilization and infection prevention solutions, partially offset by hospital deferral of capital equipment purchases amid healthcare reimbursement policy changes [9][14].

### Momentum Patterns and Implications

**Pre-Separation Momentum Assessment:**

1. **IOS** had the strongest reported growth (2.84%) and positive core growth (3.4%), indicating healthy organic demand. This segment was performing well entering 2025.

2. **PT** had the weakest underlying momentum, with negative core growth (-3.7%) masked by acquisition accounting. The segment entered 2025 with deteriorating demand, as evidenced by Q1 2025 results showing a 10.4% revenue decline and operating margin compression to 17.4% [11][12].

3. **AHS** had the slowest reported growth (2.44%) but positive core growth and improving margin trajectory. The segment was positioned for operational improvement through mix shift even without strong top-line expansion.

**Post-Separation Momentum:**

The spin-off removes PT—the segment with negative core growth and cyclical demand patterns—from Fortive's results. The continuing operations (IOS + AHS) had combined FY2024 pro forma revenue of approximately $4.08 billion and combined pro forma core growth of approximately 2-3% [3][4]. This represents a higher-quality growth profile with less exposure to capital spending cycles and acquisition dependency.

## 3. Overall Operating Margin Trends: Q1 2025 vs. Q1 2024

### GAAP Operating Margin Comparison

| Metric | Q1 2024 | Q1 2025 | Dollar Change | **Margin Change** |
|--------|---------|---------|---------------|-------------------|

| GAAP Operating Profit | $302.2 million | $233.6 million | -$68.6 million (-22.7%) | — |
| **GAAP Operating Margin** | **19.8%** | **15.8%** | — | **-4.0 pp** |

| GAAP Diluted EPS | $0.58 | $0.50 | -$0.08 (-13.8%) | — |

**Calculation:**

**Q1 2024 GAAP Operating Margin = $302.2 million ÷ $1,525.0 million = 19.8%**

**Q1 2025 GAAP Operating Margin = $233.6 million ÷ $1,474.0 million = 15.8%**

*Source: Q1 2025 Earnings Release, Exhibit 99.1; Q1 2024 Earnings Release, Exhibit 99.1* [11][15]

| Metric | Q1 2024 | Q1 2025 | Dollar Change | **Margin Change** |
|--------|---------|---------|---------------|-------------------|
| Adjusted Operating Profit | ~$382.8 million | $373.0 million | -$9.8 million (-2.6%) | — |
| **Adjusted Operating Margin** | **25.1%** | **25.3%** | — | **+0.2 pp** |

### Understanding the Divergence: GAAP Compression vs. Adjusted Expansion

The divergence between a 400-basis-point GAAP compression and a 20-basis-point adjusted expansion is one of the most revealing data points in Fortive's Q1 2025 results. The following drivers explain this divergence:

#### Driver 1: The $63 Million Property Sale Gain in Q1 2024 (Base Period Effect)

In Q1 2024, Fortive recognized a **$63 million gain from the sale of property** within the Precision Technologies segment, as explicitly stated in the Q1 2024 Earnings Release: "Fortive reported net earnings of $207 million and adjusted net earnings of $295 million, excluding a $63 million gain from the sale of property" [15]. This gain was excluded from adjusted (non-GAAP) results but included in GAAP operating profit.

- **Impact on Q1 2024 GAAP Operating Margin:** The $63 million gain represented approximately 4.1 percentage points of the 19.8% GAAP margin (i.e., excluding the gain, the underlying GAAP margin would have been approximately 15.7%).
- **Non-Recurrence in Q1 2025:** No comparable gain occurred in Q1 2025, creating a significant base period headwind for the GAAP comparison.
- **Adjusted Metric Insulation:** Since the gain was excluded from adjusted results both periods, the adjusted comparison is unaffected.

The Q2 2024 Form 10-Q provides additional context: "The charitable contribution of $20 million was made to the Fortive Foundation in March 2024, funded by proceeds from a property sale" [18]. This confirms the property sale was part of a real estate optimization plan that generated approximately $90 million in total proceeds, with the $63 million gain recognized in Q1 2024 [18].

#### Driver 2: Precision Technologies Cyclical Weakness

PT segment revenue declined 10.4% year-over-year in Q1 2025 (from $559.0 million to $500.6 million), with core revenue declining 8.4% [11][12]. This revenue decline resulted in significant negative operating leverage, compressing PT's GAAP operating margin from prior levels to 17.4% [11].

- **Impact on Consolidated Margins:** PT represented approximately 34% of total company revenue in Q1 2025 ($500.6M ÷ $1,474.0M). The margin compression within PT directly reduced consolidated margins.
- **Context:** The Q1 2025 earnings call noted that PT "experienced an 8.4% core decline, driven by lower-than-expected orders and shipment delays," with "Test and Measurement revenue declining significantly in Western Europe and China" [19].

#### Driver 3: Restructuring and Separation Costs

Fortive incurred costs related to the planned PT spin-off in Q1 2025. The Q1 2025 10-Q reports "separation-related costs" as part of the GAAP-to-adjusted reconciliation [12]. These costs were included in GAAP operating profit but excluded from adjusted results.

- **Separation Costs:** For the six months ended June 27, 2025, separation-related costs totaled $64 million, with a significant portion incurred in Q1 2025 [6].
- **Restructuring Charges:** The Q1 2025 non-GAAP reconciliation includes "discrete restructuring charges" related to a plan initiated in late 2024 for the PT separation, involving workforce reductions [12][19].

#### Driver 4: Revenue Decline and Operating Deleverage

Total revenue declined 3.3% year-over-year in Q1 2025 ($1,474.0M vs. $1,525.0M), creating negative operating leverage as fixed costs were spread across a smaller revenue base [11][15].

- **Impact:** The $51 million revenue decline, with relatively fixed cost structures, contributed to GAAP margin compression. However, cost discipline and pricing actions partially offset this effect, as evidenced by the adjusted margin expansion.

#### Driver 5: Pricing Actions and Cost Control

Despite the revenue decline, adjusted operating margins expanded by 20 basis points, reflecting:
- **Pricing Actions:** The Q1 2025 Earnings Presentation notes "operating leverage and accretive software growth" driving margin expansion, with pricing actions contributing to gross margin improvement [17].
- **FBS-Driven Productivity:** The Fortive Business System's continuous improvement culture drove cost reductions that partially offset the volume decline [17][19].
- **IOS Margin Expansion:** IOS adjusted operating margins expanded by 150 basis points in Q1 2025 and over 300 basis points on a two-year stack, providing a significant offset to PT weakness [19].

### Summary of Margin Divergence Drivers

| Driver | Impact on GAAP Margin | Impact on Adjusted Margin | Direction |
|--------|----------------------|--------------------------|-----------|
| $63M Property Sale Gain (Q1 2024, non-recurring) | ~-4.0 pp | No impact | GAAP-only headwind |
| PT Cyclical Weakness / Revenue Decline | ~-1.5 pp | ~-0.5 pp | Both, but partially offset |
| Separation/Restructuring Costs | ~-0.5 pp | No impact (excluded) | GAAP-only headwind |
| Revenue Deleverage | ~-0.3 pp | ~-0.3 pp | Both, modest |
| Pricing / Cost Control / Mix Shift | +0.2 pp | +1.0 pp | Both, but stronger in adjusted |
| **Net Change** | **-4.0 pp** | **+0.2 pp** | — |

*Note: Estimates are approximate and based on analyzing the components of the GAAP-to-adjusted reconciliation across both periods. Precise attribution of each driver requires line-by-line reconciliation data that is partially disclosed in the filings.*

## 4. Segment-Specific Operational Details

### 4.1 Intelligent Operating Solutions (IOS)

#### Capital Expenditures

Fortive does not disclose capital expenditures by individual segment in its public filings. The consolidated capex figures are reported in the cash flow statement. For Q2 2025, free cash flow of $180 million from continuing operations, combined with operating cash flow of $205 million, implies capital expenditures of approximately $25 million [5][6]. For FY2024, total company capital expenditures were approximately $105 million (derived from operating cash flow of $1,036 million and free cash flow of $931 million for FY2025 continuing operations; FY2024 total company capex was approximately $150 million) [20]. For the full year 2025 (continuing operations), capital expenditures were $105.1 million ($1,036M operating cash flow less $931M free cash flow) [20].

**Limitation:** Segment-level capex data is not available in Fortive's SEC filings. The company reports consolidated capex only.

#### Regional Revenue/Margin Trends

- **North America:** IOS's strongest region, with "double-digit point-of-sale increases" in Q2 2025 [21]. North America accounted for 56% of Fortive's continuing operations revenue in FY2024 and 57% in subsequent periods [3][22].
- **Europe:** Western Europe remained "flat" in growth trends for IOS [21]. The region faced macroeconomic uncertainty but showed relative stability compared to PT's European exposure.
- **China:** Performance characterized as "stable or bottoming trends" [21]. China represented approximately 8% of continuing operations revenue [3].
- **Other Regions:** Latin America showed growth, while APAC broadly underperformed amid macroeconomic volatility [21].

#### Pricing Actions and Tariff Impacts

- **Tariff Headwind:** Fortive estimated gross tariff impacts of $190-220 million for FY2025 (including PT segment), with approximately $80-120 million annualized for continuing operations [5][19]. The majority relates to U.S.-China tariffs.
- **Q2 2025 Tariff Impact:** Approximately $0.02 EPS headwind from tariffs in Q2 2025 [21].
- **Mitigation Strategy:** Fortive expects "gross tariffs to be fully mitigated by Q4 2025" through three levers: (1) pricing adjustments, (2) supply chain optimization (including reducing imports from China by 70% since 2018), and (3) productivity initiatives through FBS [5][19].
- **Pricing:** Adjusted gross margins improved from just under 58% in Q2 2024 to just over 59% in Q2 2025, with "favorable pricing contribution aided by mix shift" [21].

#### Separation Costs Impact on Segment Margins

Separation costs related to the Ralliant spin-off were recorded within continuing operations. For the six months ended June 27, 2025, separation-related costs totaled $64 million [6]. These costs were incurred at the corporate level and allocated to segments, but the specific allocation to IOS is not disclosed in segment reporting. The Q2 2025 10-Q states that "separation-related costs are included in Selling, general and administrative expenses" [6].

#### Recurring vs. Non-Recurring Revenue Mix

IOS has a significant and growing recurring revenue component:
- **Fluke (~$1.7B revenue):** Approximately 15% recurring revenue from software, connectivity, and service contracts [10].
- **Facility & Asset Lifecycle Software (Accruent, Gordian, ServiceChannel; ~$0.7B revenue):** Approximately 85% recurring revenue from SaaS subscriptions [10].
- **Overall IOS Recurring Revenue:** Estimated at approximately 30-35% of segment revenue, varying by brand.
- **Growth Commentary:** "Double-digit recurring revenue growth at Fluke" was noted in Q2 2025 [5][21], while Gordian's cloud-based solutions showed double-digit order growth.

### 4.2 Advanced Healthcare Solutions (AHS)

#### Capital Expenditures

As noted above, segment-level capex is not disclosed. AHS is less capital-intensive than IOS or PT, as a significant portion of its revenue comes from consumables (sterilization), software (clinical workflow), and services rather than hardware manufacturing.

#### Regional Revenue/Margin Trends

- **North America:** AHS's primary market. U.S. hospital capital expenditure deferrals related to reimbursement policy changes (particularly the No Surprises Act implementation) impacted AHS capital equipment sales, though software and consumable revenue remained resilient [5][21].
- **Europe:** Stable performance, with healthcare demand providing a natural hedge against industrial cyclicality.
- **China:** Represented a smaller portion of AHS revenue compared to PT, reducing exposure to China's healthcare policy changes and tariff impacts.
- **Global Healthcare Trends:** Aging populations, rising middle classes, and provider productivity needs provide structural tailwinds for AHS [10].

#### Pricing Actions and Tariff Impacts

AHS has relatively lower exposure to tariffs compared to IOS and PT, as:
- A significant portion of AHS revenue comes from software (no tariff exposure) and consumables (limited tariff exposure due to domestic/regional production).
- Advanced Sterilization Products (ASP) has a global manufacturing footprint that provides supply chain flexibility.
- Tariff mitigation for AHS primarily involves sourcing adjustments and pricing actions.

#### Separation Costs Impact on Segment Margins

As with IOS, separation costs were allocated to segments at the corporate level. The specific allocation to AHS is not separately disclosed. However, given AHS's smaller revenue base ($320M in Q2 2025 vs. $697M for IOS), the proportional impact on AHS margins from corporate cost allocations may have been more significant.

#### Recurring vs. Non-Recurring Revenue Mix

AHS has the highest recurring revenue percentage among Fortive's segments:
- **Advanced Sterilization Products (ASP; ~$0.9B revenue):** Over 80% recurring revenue, driven by a global installed base of over 30,000 sterilization units that generate recurring consumable and service revenue [10].
- **Healthcare Software (Provation, Fluke Biomedical):** High recurring revenue from software subscriptions and maintenance contracts.
- **Overall AHS Recurring Revenue:** Estimated at over 60% of segment revenue, making AHS Fortive's most predictable revenue stream.
- **Margin Implication:** The shift toward higher-margin software revenue was cited as a key driver of AHS's 240-basis-point GAAP margin improvement from FY2024 (8.8%) to Q2 2025 (11.2%) [5][10].

### 4.3 Precision Technologies (PT) — Historical Context (Pre-Separation)

#### Capital Expenditures

PT was the most capital-intensive segment, with significant investment in manufacturing facilities for test and measurement equipment and sensors. The FY2024 10-K notes that PT's property, plant, and equipment included facilities for Tektronix, Keithley, Qualitrol, and EA Elektro-Automatik [8].

#### Regional Revenue/Margin Trends

PT had the most challenged regional performance among the three segments:
- **Western Europe:** "Test and Measurement revenue declined significantly in Western Europe" due to geopolitical uncertainty and customer spending delays [19]. This was PT's most challenging region in 2024-2025.
- **China:** "Test and Measurement revenue declined significantly in China" amid trade tensions and semiconductor cycle weakness [19]. China represented a meaningful portion of PT revenue.
- **North America:** Relatively stronger, with defense and aerospace demand partially offsetting commercial weakness. However, "North America semiconductor slowdown" impacted PT's test-related revenue [14].
- **Global Cyclicality:** PT's end-markets (semiconductors, test & measurement, energy) are inherently cyclical and correlated with global capital spending cycles, which were in a downturn in 2024-2025.

#### Pricing Actions and Tariff Impacts

PT faced the most significant tariff exposure among the three segments, given its global supply chain and exposure to U.S.-China trade tensions. Fortive's overall tariff mitigation strategy (70% reduction in imports from China since 2018) was largely driven by PT's supply chain adjustments.

#### Separation Costs

PT incurred separation costs related to the spin-off, including legal, advisory, and system separation costs. The Q2 2025 10-Q reports total separation-related costs of $41 million for Q2 2025 and $64 million for the six-month period, with a portion allocated to PT/continuing operations [6]. An additional $46 million in separation-related costs was recognized in Q3 2025 within discontinued operations [7].

#### Recurring vs. Non-Recurring Revenue Mix

PT had the lowest recurring revenue percentage among Fortive's segments, estimated at approximately 15-20%:
- **Test and Measurement (56% of PT revenue):** Primarily capital equipment sales with limited recurring revenue from service contracts and software.
- **Sensors and Safety Systems (44% of PT revenue):** Higher recurring revenue from monitoring services, replacement sensors, and long-term utility contracts, but still significantly below IOS or AHS levels.
- **Implication:** PT's low recurring revenue contributed to earnings volatility, as the segment was heavily dependent on capital spending cycles.

- **Western Europe:** PT's Test & Measurement segment was heavily exposed to European industrial demand, which weakened significantly in 2024-2025.
- **China:** PT had meaningful exposure to China's semiconductor and electronics test market, which faced cyclical headwinds and trade policy uncertainty. While China remains 8% of continuing operations, this is concentrated in less cyclical IOS and AHS end-markets.

#### End-Market Cyclicality Reduction

The removal of PT eliminates exposure to the following **highly cyclical end-markets**:

1. **Semiconductors:** PT's Tektronix and Keithley brands served semiconductor test and characterization, an end-market known for pronounced boom-bust cycles. The global semiconductor test equipment market is projected to grow at 7.8% CAGR but with significant volatility [23].

2. **Test & Measurement:** Customers in this market "delay investments in light of increased geopolitical and macroeconomic uncertainty," as evidenced by PT's core revenue decline of 3.7% in FY2024 and 8.4% in Q1 2025 [11][14].

3. **Energy (including Electric Vehicles and Energy Storage):** PT's EA Elektro-Automatik acquisition (January 2024 for $1.72B) provided exposure to EV and energy storage test solutions. However, reduced EV demand and changing global subsidies led to a **$1.4 billion non-cash goodwill impairment** for Ralliant in Q4 2025, highlighting the significant risk associated with this end-market [24].

4. **Defense & Space (cyclical on program timing):** While defense is relatively stable, it is subject to government budget cycles and program-specific timing.

#### End-Market Predictability Improvement

The post-separation portfolio significantly improves revenue predictability:

- **Healthcare (31% of revenue):** Non-cyclical, structurally growing end-market driven by aging demographics, healthcare spending growth, and regulatory requirements. ASP's over 80% recurring revenue provides particular stability.

- **Government (9% of revenue):** Non-cyclical end-market, though subject to budget timing and policy changes. Government spending constraints had a "chilling effect" on procurement activity in Q2 2025, but this is a temporary headwind rather than a cyclical downturn [21].

- **Industrial & Manufacturing (24% of revenue):** IOS serves this end-market through essential safety and productivity solutions (gas detection, calibration, electrical testing) that are less discretionary than the capital equipment sold by PT.

- **Recurring Revenue Mix:** Post-separation, approximately 50% of Fortive's revenue is recurring, compared to approximately 30-35% pre-separation [10][25]. This fundamentally changes the earnings profile from capital-spending-dependent to subscription/consumption-based.

### 5.4 Capital Allocation Flexibility

The separation unlocked significant balance sheet benefits, as detailed in the June 30, 2025 Form 8-K [1] and Q2 2025 Earnings Release [5]:

#### The $1.15 Billion Ralliant Dividend

On June 27, 2025, Ralliant borrowed $1.15 billion under its credit agreement (pro rata under Three-Year and Eighteen-Month Term Loan facilities, part of a total ~$2.05 billion credit facility) to make a payment to Fortive as consideration for contributed assets and liabilities [1][6]. This cash consideration provided Fortive with substantial financial flexibility.

#### Debt Paydown and Leverage Reduction

Fortive used approximately $725 million of the $1.15 billion proceeds to repay outstanding indebtedness, reducing leverage to roughly 2.5 times adjusted EBITDA [5][6]. The pro forma Form 8-K shows total assets of approximately $13.3 billion for continuing operations (reduced from $17.1 billion pre-spin) and stockholders' equity reduced by approximately $3.85 billion [3].

#### Share Repurchases

Fortive deployed significant capital toward share repurchases:
- **H1 2025:** $345 million in share repurchases [5][6]
- **Special Program:** A $550 million special share repurchase program was authorized by Fortive's Board in May 2025, funded by the Ralliant cash consideration [1][2]
- **FY2025 Total:** Approximately $1.6 billion in total share repurchases for full year 2025, representing approximately 8% of diluted shares outstanding [20]

#### Capital Allocation Strategy

Management articulated a clear capital allocation framework at the June 10, 2025 Investor Day [10]:
1. **Organic Investment:** Accelerating profitable organic growth through innovation and commercial excellence.
2. **Bolt-On M&A:** Disciplined, targeted acquisitions that complement existing businesses.
3. **Share Repurchases:** Returning capital to shareholders, particularly given the discounted valuation post-spin.
4. **Dividend:** Maintaining and growing the dividend ($27 million in Q2 2025 quarterly dividends) [6].

CEO Olumide Soroye stated: "We've emerged as a simpler, more focused company, well positioned to deliver durable and accelerated financial performance" [5].

### 5.6 Risks and Considerations

While the separation strengthens Fortive's competitive positioning, several risks remain:

1. **Tariff Exposure:** The estimated $80-120 million annualized tariff headwind for continuing operations is material, representing approximately 2-3% of annual revenue. While management expects full mitigation by Q4 2025, execution risk remains [5][19].

2. **Reduced Scale:** With pro forma revenue of approximately $4.1 billion (down from $6.2 billion), Fortive has less revenue diversification and is more exposed to segment-specific downturns. However, the reduced cyclicality partially offsets this risk.

3. **Healthcare Policy Uncertainty:** AHS's hospital capital spending deferrals in 2025 highlight the segment's sensitivity to healthcare reimbursement policy changes. Continued policy uncertainty could pressure AHS growth [5][21].

4. **Government Procurement Delays:** "Government spending constraints had a chilling effect on procurement activity," causing backlog and project delays that could persist [21].

5. **Integration of Spin-Off:** The separation required significant management attention and incurred $64 million in separation costs through mid-2025. The establishment of standalone systems and processes for both companies presents ongoing execution risk [6].

## 6. Conclusion

Fortive's separation of the Precision Technologies segment into Ralliant Corporation represents a deliberate and well-executed portfolio transformation. The quantitative analysis—grounded exclusively in official SEC filings and earnings releases—supports the following conclusions:

**The remaining portfolio is higher quality and more predictable.** Post-separation, Fortive generates approximately 50% of revenue from recurring sources (versus ~30-35% pre-separation), has increased healthcare exposure from ~21% to 31% of revenue, and has substantially reduced exposure to cyclical semiconductor, test & measurement, and energy end-markets. This shift fundamentally changes the company's earnings profile from capital-spending-dependent to subscription/consumption-based.

**Margin trajectory is improving.** Despite a challenging demand environment in early 2025 (revenue declines, tariff headwinds, PT cyclical weakness), continuing operations expanded adjusted operating margins by 20 basis points year-over-year in Q1 2025. AHS showed the most dramatic improvement, with GAAP margins expanding from 8.8% (FY2024) to 11.2% (Q2 2025). IOS remains the margin anchor at 24.5% GAAP and 33.8% adjusted EBITDA margins.

**IOS has the strongest unit economics (absolute), while AHS has the strongest trajectory.** IOS generates GAAP operating margins of 24-26% and adjusted EBITDA margins of approximately 34%, making it the clear profit engine. AHS, while operating at lower absolute margins (11.2% GAAP in Q2 2025), demonstrates the most significant positive trajectory with 240 basis points of GAAP margin improvement from FY2024.

**Revenue quality has improved.** The removal of PT—which had negative core growth (-3.7% in FY2024) masked by acquisition accounting—leaves Fortive with two segments that both generated positive core growth in FY2024 and have demonstrated resilience in early 2025.

**Capital allocation flexibility is enhanced.** The $1.15 billion dividend from Ralliant, combined with debt paydown to ~2.5x leverage and $1.6 billion in share repurchases in FY2025, provides Fortive with significant capacity to invest in organic growth and bolt-on acquisitions.

The separation has strengthened Fortive's competitive positioning by creating a focused, higher-margin, more predictable technology solutions company. As CEO Olumide Soroye stated: "New Fortive emerges with a strong financial track record with robust free cash flow generation, approximately 50% recurring revenue, significant competitive advantages, and a strategic orientation toward attractive markets with strong secular tailwinds" [5]. While near-term headwinds from tariffs, healthcare policy uncertainty, and government procurement delays remain, the post-separation portfolio is structurally positioned for more durable, higher-quality financial performance.

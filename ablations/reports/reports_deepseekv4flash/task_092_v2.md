## 1. Master's in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay — Admission Requirements and Eligibility

### 1.1 Program Overview

The **Master of Economics (MoE)** at Université Paris-Saclay is a **two-year, highly selective graduate program taught entirely in English**. It is designed to provide rigorous training in economic theory, quantitative methods, and research, preparing students for top PhD programs worldwide or careers in international organizations, public institutions, consulting, and data science. [1][2][3]

The program is offered by the **Graduate School of Economics & Management** of Université Paris-Saclay, which integrates 12 research centers. The first year (M1) is **certified by ENS Paris-Saclay**, while the second year (M2) is certified by **Paris-Saclay University in collaboration with AgroParisTech, CentraleSupélec, and University of Evry-Val d'Essonne**. [3][4] The program is hosted at the **RITM – Faculté DEM** (Faculté de Droit-Économie-Management), located at 54, Boulevard Desgranges, 92330 SCEAUX, on the **Saclay Moulon campus**. [5]

#### M1 Core Curriculum (8 Compulsory Courses):
- Decision Theory and Game Theory
- Intermediate Econometrics
- Macroeconomics I and II
- Development Economics
- Public Economics
- Advanced Econometrics
- Firms and Markets
- Plus optional discovery courses and two research projects
- Boot Camp in Computer Science (statistics and econometrics using R) [1][2]

#### M2 Specialization Tracks:
The M2 is entirely customizable, allowing students to build their own curricula under faculty supervision. Three fields of excellence are offered:
1. **Trade, Geography and Development Economics**
2. **Public and Behavioral Economics**
3. **Advances and Challenges in Macroeconomics**

Students may also create diversified interdisciplinary curricula. [2][3]

## 2. France Excellence Charpak Master's Scholarship — Eligibility and Application Requirements

### 2.1 Program Overview

The **France Excellence Charpak Master Scholarship** is a French government scholarship program administered by **Campus France India** (the French Embassy in India), named after Nobel laureate Georges Charpak. It is designed for Indian students to pursue **one to two years of full-time Master's degree studies in France** across **all streams and fields of study**. [10][11]

The overall France Excellence Charpak Scholarship Program in India is divided into four sub-programs:
- **(a) France Excellence Charpak Bachelor** — for full-time bachelor's programs
- **(b) France Excellence Charpak Master** — for master's programs; renewable upon request based on first-year results
- **(c) France Excellence Charpak Summer Training** — for internships/research during May-July academic break
- **(d) France Excellence Charpak Exchange** — for semester-long study abroad through institutional partnerships [11]

**Every year, the French Embassy in India along with French companies and institutions award scholarships to more than 500 meritorious Indian students** across all four programs. [12]

### 2.7 Selection Criteria and Process

#### Two-Round Evaluation Process [10]

**Round 1 — Document Review and Eligibility Check**
- **Eligibility verification**: The scholarship team verifies that the applicant meets all mandatory eligibility criteria (nationality, age, academic enrollment, etc.)
- **Academic excellence assessment**: Evaluation of the applicant's academic record, transcripts, grades, and overall academic profile
- **Statement of Purpose evaluation**: Assessment of the consistency, quality, and motivation expressed in the SOP
- **Plagiarism/AI check**: The SOP is checked for plagiarism and AI-generated content; violations lead to disqualification
- **No specific CGPA cutoff**: There is no minimum CGPA/percentage requirement; selection is based on overall merit [10]

**Round 2 — Online Interview**
- **Interview period (2026 cycle)**: April 13, 2026 to April 30, 2026 [10]
- Applicants must be available during this period
- The interview assesses the candidate's **motivation, suitability, future goals, and contribution prospects**
- The interview is typically **15-20 minutes long** and conducted in English (or French if applicable)
- Only **shortlisted candidates** from Round 1 are called for the interview

#### Selection Criteria and Their Relative Weight
Based on official information:
- **Academic excellence**: Primary criterion — assessed from transcripts and academic profile
- **Consistency and quality of the statement of purpose**: Equally important primary criterion

## 3. Alternative Funding Pathways for Indian Students

### 3.1 Université Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship

#### Overview
The **Université Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship** (also referred to as the **IDEX International Master's Scholarship**) is a competitive scholarship funded by the IDEX Programme Investissement d'Avenir 3. It aims to promote the international openness of the university's master's programs and facilitate the enrollment of highly-qualified international students. [15][16]

#### Eligibility Criteria

| Requirement | Status | Details |
|------------|--------|---------|
| **Nationality** | **OPEN TO ALL NON-FRENCH** | Open to students of **foreign nationality**. Indian students are fully eligible. [15][16] |
| **Academic level** | **MANDATORY** | Must be enrolling in a Master's program (M1 or M2) at Université Paris-Saclay or its member institutions [15][16] |
| **Age limit** | **MANDATORY** | Must be **under 30 years old** during the year of selection [16][17] |
| **First-time enrollment in France** | **MANDATORY** | Must be enrolling for the first time in a French higher education institution. Exceptions: students who previously spent less than one year in France under a non-degree mobility program (exchange, language training, internship) [16][17] |
| **No other major funding** | **MANDATORY** | Students receiving any other funding above **€600 per month** will be declared ineligible [15][16][17] |
| **Field restrictions** | **EXCLUDES VOCATIONAL PROGRAMS** | All master's programs of Université Paris-Saclay are eligible **except vocational programs** ("formations en apprentissage"). Economics is fully eligible. [15][16][17] |
| **Member institutions covered** | — | AgroParisTech, CentraleSupélec, ENS Paris-Saclay, INSTN-CEA, IOGS, Université d'Évry Paris-Saclay, Université Paris-Saclay, UVSQ [16] |

#### Scholarship Benefits

| Benefit | Amount | Details |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Annual stipend** | **€10,000 per year** | Paid monthly (approximately €1,000/month for 10 months, September to June) [15][16][17] |
| **Travel and visa allowance** | **Up to €900** | One-time flat fee depending on country of origin [15][16][17] |
| **Duration for M1 entrants** | **1 year + automatic renewal** | Awarded for 1 year (M1), with **automatic renewal for M2** provided the student completes all required credits and is admitted to an M2 program at Paris-Saclay [15][16] |
| **Duration for M2 entrants** | **1 year only** | Awarded for 1 year (no renewal) [16][17] |
| **Accommodation assistance** | Included | Help with housing search, but reservation cannot be guaranteed [15][16] |

#### What IS NOT Covered
- **Tuition fees**: Registration and tuition fees are NOT covered. Students must pay the €254/year national rate (which is already reduced for non-EU students through the automatic partial exemption). [16]
- **No advance payment**: Laureates receive their scholarship only upon arrival in France and once enrolled. [15]

#### Application Process — By Invitation Only

**CRITICAL**: Students cannot directly apply for this scholarship. The process is: [15][16][17]

1. **First, apply and be admitted** to a master's program at Université Paris-Saclay (via INCEPTION)
2. **Selection by course coordinators**: Among admitted students, master's program coordinators select candidates to nominate for the scholarship
3. **Invitation to apply**: Nominated candidates receive a direct application link by email
4. **Online application**: Selected candidates complete an online application and must provide **two referees** who submit recommendations online
5. **Jury evaluation**: A jury composed of experts from Graduate Schools evaluates applications based on academic background, personal project, and motivation for PhD pursuit
6. **Results** are communicated by email

#### Application Deadline for 2026-2027
- Master's program application: Late January to mid-March 2026 (via INCEPTION) [1]
- Scholarship nomination by coordinators: By late March 2026
- Scholarship application deadline (for nominated candidates): **End of March/early April 2026** [18]
- Results: **Late May 2026** [18]

#### Can It Be Combined with Charpak?
**NO.** The Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship **CANNOT be held concurrently with the Charpak Master Scholarship**. [15][16][17]

**Reason**: The Paris-Saclay rules state that students receiving any other funding above **€600 per month** will be declared ineligible. The Charpak scholarship provides **€860 per month**, which exceeds this threshold. Additionally, the Paris-Saclay scholarship explicitly lists France Excellence Eiffel and other major scholarships as non-combinable, and the general €600/month rule would also disqualify Charpak.

**However**: Students can apply for both scholarships and **choose the better option** if selected for both.

### 3.2 France Excellence Eiffel Scholarship Program

#### Overview
The **France Excellence Eiffel Scholarship Program** is established by the **French Ministry for Europe and Foreign Affairs** to enable French higher education institutions to attract top foreign students to enroll in their master's and PhD programs. The program aims to train future foreign decision-makers in the private and public sectors. [19][20]

#### Eligibility for Indian Students

| Requirement | Status | Details |
|------------|--------|---------|
| **Nationality** | **OPEN** | Indian students are eligible. Program targets developing countries. [19][20] |
| **Age limit (Master's)** | **MANDATORY** | Must have been **born after March 31, 1996** (under 29 years old at time of application). 2026 regulations specify this. [20][21] |
| **Study level** | **MANDATORY** | Must be applying for a Master's program (M1 or M2) at a French institution [19][20] |
| **Field restrictions** | **7 PRIORITY DOMAINS** | Economics and Management is explicitly included as one of the 7 priority domains [19][20][21] |
| **Currently in France** | **NOT ELIGIBLE** | Foreign students currently studying in France are NOT eligible for the master's scholarship. Priority given to those studying abroad. [20][21] |
| **First-time applicants only** | **MANDATORY** | Must be applying for the first time and presented by only one educational institution [21] |

**7 Priority Fields (divided into 2 categories):**

**Sciences and Techniques:**
1. Biology and Health
2. Ecological Transition
3. Mathematics and Digital
4. Engineering Sciences

**Humanities and Social Sciences:**
5. History, French Language and Civilization
6. Law and Political Science
7. **Economics and Management** — ✅ Master's in Economics is eligible

#### Scholarship Benefits

| Benefit | Amount | Details |
|---------|--------|---------|
| **Monthly allowance (Master's)** | **€1,200 per month** (effective January 1, 2026) | [20][21][22] |
| **International round-trip airfare** | Reimbursed (up to 50% of flight costs in some versions) | [20][21] |
| **National transport assistance** | Included | Within France [21] |
| **Health insurance** | Student social security + complementary health insurance | Included [20][21] |
| **Housing search assistance** | Included | Service provided [20][21] |
| **Cultural and social activities** | Included | Organized for scholarship holders [21] |
| **Tuition fees** | **EXEMPTED** at public institutions for national diplomas | Not directly paid; recipients do not need to pay differentiated fees [20][21] |

**Duration (Master's):**
- Up to **12 months** for M2 entry
- Up to **24 months** for M1 entry
- Up to **36 months** for engineering degrees
- Must begin between September 1 and November 30 of the award year. No deferral possible. [21]

#### Application Process — Institution Nominates (Students Cannot Apply Directly)

**CRITICAL**: Students **cannot apply directly** for the Eiffel scholarship. Applications must be submitted **SOLELY by French higher education institutions** on behalf of their selected candidates. [19][20][21]

The process for a student at Université Paris-Saclay:
1. Apply to a Master's program at Paris-Saclay (via INCEPTION)
2. Paris-Saclay selects which admitted students to nominate for Eiffel
3. Paris-Saclay submits the application to Campus France / the Ministry

#### Deadlines for 2026 Cycle (Just Concluded)

- **Deadline for institution submission to Campus France: January 8, 2026** [19][20][21]
- The 2026 call is now **closed**. The next call will open in **October 2026** for the 2027-28 intake. [20]

#### Can It Be Combined with Charpak?
**NO.** The Eiffel scholarship **CANNOT be combined with the Charpak scholarship** or any other French government scholarship. [20][21][22]

The Eiffel regulations explicitly state: "La bourse Eiffel n'est pas cumulable avec une autre bourse du gouvernement français" (The Eiffel scholarship cannot be combined with another French government scholarship). The Charpak Master Scholarship is explicitly a French government scholarship (awardees receive "le statut de Boursier du Gouvernement Français"). [10][20]

### 3.4 Tuition Fee Exemption/Reduction Policies

#### Automatic Partial Exemption at Université Paris-Saclay

**This is automatic — no application needed.** Non-EU students at Université Paris-Saclay **automatically pay the same registration fees as domestic (EU) students** for the entire study cycle, without a specific request. [9][25]

| Level | Fees Paid by All Students (EU and Non-EU) |
|-------|-------------------------------------------|
| **Master's** | **€254 per year** |
| Bachelor's | €178 per year |
| Doctoral | €397 per year |
| **CVEC** | **€105 per year** (paid to CROUS) |

**Key Points:**
- This exemption applies for the **entire duration of the study cycle** (e.g., full 2-year Master's) [9][25]
- **No application is required** — it is automatically applied to all non-EU students [9]
- Payment can be made in **3 installments online** at no extra cost [9]
- This is a **partial exemption** — it brings the fee down from the "differentiated" rate (which would be several thousand euros for non-EU students) to the national rate (€254/year for Master's) [9][25]

#### Requesting Additional Fee Exemptions (Local Exemption Commission)

Students facing financial difficulties or medical challenges who are not automatically eligible for fee exemptions can apply through their faculty's **Local Exemption Commission (Commission Locale d'Exonération — CLE)** . [26]

**Eligible criteria for additional exemption requests:**
- Severe financial hardship
- Refugee status (with OFPRA card)
- Recognized disability (RQTH or MDPH)
- Judicial oversight
- International government scholarships
- Serious health issues requiring study interruption

**Deadlines for 2025-2026 requests:** [26]
- Most students: October 31, 2025
- Doctoral candidates: November 30, 2025

## 5. Conclusion

The **Master's in Economics at Université Paris-Saclay** is a highly selective, English-taught program with rigorous academic prerequisites, requiring a BA in Economics or BS in Mathematics. For Indian applicants, the application process involves the **INCEPTION platform** for admission followed by the **Études en France (EEF) procedure** for visa processing. Tuition fees are exceptionally affordable at **€254/year** for all students (EU and non-EU) due to the university's automatic partial exemption policy.

The **France Excellence Charpak Master Scholarship** offers Indian students substantial financial support (**€860/month** plus fee waivers and health insurance) but does not cover tuition fees or travel expenses. The 2026 cycle has just concluded with results due at the end of May 2026. The next cycle (2027) will likely open in early 2027 with a late March deadline.

**Alternative funding pathways** include the **Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship** (€10,000/year), the **Eiffel Scholarship** (€1,200/month), and the **Legrand Empowering Scholarship** (INR 59,500/month). Note that the Paris-Saclay International Master's Scholarship **cannot be combined** with Charpak or any funding exceeding €600/month. All scholarships have strict non-cumulation rules with other French government funding.

**Final recommendation**: Indian applicants should start preparing documents by **November-December 2026** for the 2027-2028 cycle, take English proficiency tests early, secure two academic referees, prepare an original statement of purpose, and apply to both the Master's program and all eligible scholarships simultaneously.

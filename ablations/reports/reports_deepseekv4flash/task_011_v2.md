## Part 1: Technical & Operational Mechanics of Partner Offerings

### 1.1 SuperMoney: Credit Inquiry Methods and Pre-Qualification Technology

#### Soft Pull vs. Hard Pull Mechanics

SuperMoney uses a **soft credit pull** (soft inquiry) as its foundational technology for the loan offer engine. Multiple sources from SuperMoney's own knowledge base confirm this:

- "A soft pull is a review of your credit report that has no impact on your credit score" [SuperMoney Knowledge Base Article 47](https://help.supermoney.com/article/47-what-is-a-soft-pull-or-soft-inquiry-on-my-credit-score)
- "When you use SuperMoney's loan offer engine, our lending partners perform a soft credit pull" [SuperMoney Knowledge Base Article 52](https://help.supermoney.com/article/52-does-using-supermoney-affect-my-credit-score)
- "This type of inquiry allows them to evaluate your credit profile and determine what offers you may qualify for, without impacting your credit score" [SuperMoney Knowledge Base Article 52](https://help.supermoney.com/article/52-does-using-supermoney-affect-my-credit-score)

The distinction between soft and hard pulls is critical:

| Feature | Soft Pull (SuperMoney) | Hard Pull (Direct Lender) |
|---------|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Credit Score Impact | None | May slightly lower score |
| Credit Report Appearance | Not visible | Visible for up to 2 years |
| When It Occurs | Pre-qualification stage | Full application submission |
| Authorization | Implicit for comparison | Explicit for final approval |

SuperMoney's knowledge base explicitly states: "Hard pulls only occur with your authorization and after you submit a full application through the lender's site" [SuperMoney Knowledge Base Article 47](https://help.supermoney.com/article/47-what-is-a-soft-pull-or-soft-inquiry-on-my-credit-score).

The technical mechanism is described by Finovate: "SuperMoney, led by CEO Miron Lulic and Managing Partner Harry Langenberg, offers a fintech platform that brings a Kayak-like user experience to the personal loan market. Unlike traditional loan aggregation sites that use a 'ping tree' model selling leads to the highest bidder, SuperMoney provides consumers with real-time, transparent, and personalized loan offers through a single soft credit pull" [Finovate](https://finovate.com/supermoney-brings-kayak-like-ux-online-borrowing-process).

#### Technical Operation of the Pre-Qualification Platform

SuperMoney's pre-qualification platform operates through three integration methods for affiliates:

1. **Money Marketplace**: Full co-branded marketplace page where the affiliate's brand remains front and center
2. **Pre-qualification Platform**: Embeddable widgets using "just a few lines of JavaScript code" [SuperMoney Pre-Qualification Platform](https://www.supermoney.com/monetize/prequalification-platform)
3. **Super Links**: Simple offer links with tracking capabilities [SuperMoney Super Links](https://www.supermoney.com/monetize/super-links)

**Data Flow When a Consumer Submits an Application Through a Mesa Group Co-Branded Page:**

**Step 1 — Consumer Lands on Affiliate Page:** The affiliate (Mesa Group Consulting) embeds a SuperMoney widget or co-branded offer engine page on their site, or sends users via a Super Link. The page displays Mesa Group's branding throughout the experience.

**Step 2 — Data Submission:** The user completes a form (name, SSN, income, address, loan purpose, desired amount). Data is transmitted via HTTPS encrypted connection to SuperMoney's platform using bank-grade 256-bit encryption. SuperMoney is audited against SOC 2 Type II compliance [SuperMoney](https://www.supermoney.com/quotes/personal-loans/professional-services).

**Step 3 — Routing Algorithm:** SuperMoney's loan offer engine processes the application. The platform matches the consumer's credit profile (via soft pull) against its database of lender criteria. Unlike "ping tree" models that sell leads to the highest bidder, SuperMoney claims to rank offers based on consumer benefit.

**Step 4 — Real-Time Offer Display:** Real-time offers are returned to the user on the co-branded page. As SuperMoney states: "Your customers get competing loan offers back instantly with your brand, front and center" [SuperMoney Monetize Page](https://www.supermoney.com/monetize).

**Step 5 — Affiliate Attribution:** The affiliate's unique tracking ID/cookie is embedded in the initial link. Affiliates earn industry-leading commissions when consumers complete funded loans.

#### The "Refi Robot" — Technical Operation

The Refi Robot is an AI-powered, automated loan refinancing discovery engine embedded within the SuperMoney mobile app (part of the Sense AI ecosystem), introduced with the 2025-2026 launch of the SuperMoney AI-powered personal finance app.

**Technical Capabilities:**

- **Passive/Background Operation**: The Refi Robot operates "behind the scenes" continuously — it does not require manual requests from the user. Once a user connects their financial accounts, the robot monitors their existing loan portfolio [Apple App Store](https://apps.apple.com/us/app/supermoney-budgeting-ai/id6504295113).

- **Rate Shopping Automation**: It automatically scans the marketplace for loan refinancing opportunities offering better rates than the user's current loans.

- **Trigger-Based Recommendations**: When the robot identifies a refinancing opportunity that would result in meaningful savings (lower APR, lower monthly payment, or better terms), it surfaces a recommendation through the app's feed/insights interface.

- **Integration with Loan Offer Engine**: It uses the same underlying loan offer engine and lender network that powers SuperMoney's core marketplace.

- **AI-Driven Personalization**: Powered by Sense AI, it contextualizes recommendations based on the user's full financial profile — existing debts, credit score, income, and goals.

**Important Limitation**: The Refi Robot does not directly interact with lenders autonomously. It identifies opportunities and presents them to the user, who must still authorize and submit a pre-qualification or full application to proceed.

## Part 2: Regulatory Liability & Compliance Burdens

### 2.1 Federal Regulations Affecting Mesa Group Consulting

#### Credit Repair Organizations Act (CROA) — 15 USC §§ 1679 et seq.

CROA defines a Credit Repair Organization (CRO) broadly as "any person who uses any instrumentality of interstate commerce or the mails to sell, provide, or perform ... any service ... for the express or implied purpose of improving any consumer's credit record, credit history, or credit rating" in return for payment of money or other valuable consideration [BoostCredit101](https://www.boostcredit101.com/credit-repair-organizations-act).

**Key Prohibitions Relevant to Mesa Group:**

1. **Advance Fee Ban**: "No credit repair organization may charge or receive any money or other valuable consideration...before such service is fully performed" [Debt.com](https://www.debt.com/credit-repair/law/croa)

2. **Mandatory Disclosures**: Before contract execution, consumers must receive:
   - Notice of right to dispute inaccurate information by contacting credit bureaus directly
   - Notice of right to cancel within 3 business days without penalty
   - Written, dated contract with detailed service descriptions, payment terms, and cancellation notice [BoostCredit101](https://www.boostcredit101.com/credit-repair-organizations-act)

3. **Prohibition on Misrepresentation**: False or misleading statements about a consumer's creditworthiness, advising consumers to misrepresent their identity, and misleading claims about services are all prohibited

4. **Contract Cancellation**: Any waiver by any consumer of any CROA protection is void; non-compliant contracts are void [BoostCredit101](https://www.boostcredit101.com/credit-repair-organizations-act)

**Critical Case Law — Zimmerman v. Puccio (1st Cir. 2010):**
The First Circuit held that "credit counseling aimed at improving future creditworthy behavior is the quintessential credit repair service." The court found that even a tax-exempt, nonprofit credit counseling agency operated as a "credit repair organization" within CROA's scope. Damages awarded: **$256,527,000** [aequum Health](https://aequumhealth.com/blog/the-credit-repair-organizations-act-croa-who-must-comply).

**Implication for Mesa Group**: If Mesa Group's credit repair services involve anything beyond simple DIY dispute software — including counseling, advice, or direct dispute submission — CROA applies. The broad interpretation in *Zimmerman* means even "credit education" could trigger CROA if it promises to improve credit behavior.

#### FTC Telemarketing Sales Rule (TSR) — 16 CFR Part 310

The TSR governs telemarketing practices and applies to for-profit debt relief service providers who use telemarketing, including both outbound and inbound calls [FTC Guide](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/debt-relief-services-telemarketing-sales-rule-guide-business).

**Key Prohibitions for Debt Relief Services:**

1. **Advance Fee Ban**: "It's illegal to charge upfront fees" for debt relief services sold via telemarketing. Fees may not be collected until:
   - A settlement has been reached
   - The consumer has made at least one payment to the creditor
   - Proof of a dedicated account has been provided [FTC Press Release](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2010/10/debt-relief-companies-prohibited-collecting-advance-fees-under-ftc-rule-takes-effect-october-27-2010)

2. **Mandatory Disclosures**: Before signing up consumers, debt relief companies must provide clear disclosure of fees, program duration, potential negative consequences, and details about dedicated accounts [FTC Guide](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/debt-relief-services-telemarketing-sales-rule-guide-business)

3. **Misrepresentation Ban**: Companies cannot misrepresent success rates or nonprofit status [FTC Guide](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/debt-relief-services-telemarketing-sales-rule-guide-business)

4. **Substantial Assistance Liability**: The rule covers not only direct sellers but also those providing "substantial assistance or support" to any seller or telemarketer when that person knows or consciously avoids knowing that the seller or telemarketer is engaged in deceptive acts [FTC Guide](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/debt-relief-services-telemarketing-sales-rule-guide-business)

**Implication for Mesa Group**: If Mesa Group refers consumers to debt relief providers (Novae or others) and has knowledge of non-compliance, they could face "substantial assistance" liability under the TSR.

#### Fair Credit Reporting Act (FCRA)

**Key Lead Generator Case — FTC v. ITMedia Solutions LLC (January 2022):**

The FTC secured a **$1.5 million civil penalty** against ITMedia Solutions for violating FCRA and Section 5(a) of the FTC Act. Key findings:

- The defendants deceptively solicited loan applications, assuring consumers their data would be shared with a "trusted network of lenders"
- However, the firm sold sensitive consumer data to a lead marketplace where bidders could purchase information without restrictions
- The FTC alleged that **84% of loan applications** were distributed to marketers, debt relief, and credit repair sellers without regard for how consumer information would be used [Ballard Spahr](https://www.ballardspahr.com/insights/alerts-and-actions/2022/02/ftc-targets-lead-generation-industry-with-1.5-million-fcra-penalty)

**Implication for Mesa Group**: As an affiliate generating consumer leads for SuperMoney and Novae, Mesa Group must ensure that consumer data is not misused by downstream partners. The FTC has signaled it will hold lead generators accountable for the actions of downstream purchasers of consumer data.

### 2.3 How Affiliate Partnerships Shift Regulatory Burdens

#### The Affiliate Structure as a Risk Mitigation Tool

The key legal distinction for Mesa Group is between acting as an **intermediary/affiliate** (referral-based) versus acting as a **direct provider** of regulated services.

**General Principle:** Sharing a simple referral link is not considered brokering; but arranging terms or discussing rates is [NJCatalyst](https://njcatalyst.com/blog/commercial-lending-licensing-requirements-by-state).

**How Affiliate Structure Reduces Mesa Group's Compliance Requirements:**

1. **CROA Compliance**: By routing consumers to SuperMoney for loan comparisons and to Novae for debt relief/business services, Mesa Group does not directly provide credit repair services. However, this protection is limited — Mesa Group's own credit repair services (offered directly) must still comply with CROA.

2. **Debt Relief Regulations**: By referring consumers to Novae's licensed debt restructuring partners, Mesa Group avoids the need for direct debt relief licensing. However, the "substantial assistance" provision of the TSR means Mesa Group could still face liability if they know or should know that Novae's partners are non-compliant.

3. **Business Funding Brokering**: By using Novae's white-label programs rather than directly arranging terms between clients and lenders, Mesa Group potentially avoids commercial finance broker licensing requirements.

#### SuperMoney's Licensing Status

SuperMoney is explicitly NOT a lender or a credit repair organization. It is a comparison marketplace that partners with lenders. SuperMoney holds:
- **SOC 2 Type II certification** for data security [SuperMoney](https://www.supermoney.com/quotes/personal-loans/professional-services)
- No direct lending licenses (not applicable)
- No credit repair licenses (not applicable)

SuperMoney's model is legally structured as a lead generation/marketplace platform, which carries significantly lower regulatory burden than direct lending or credit repair.

#### Novae's Licensing Status

**Critical Finding: Novae LLC itself is NOT directly licensed for debt relief in any state.** Novae explicitly discloses that it relies on partnerships with licensed entities:

- "Our platform partner holds licenses for debt resolution services" [Novae Debt Help](https://novaemoney.com/base/debt-help)
- "Novae does not perform debt settlement services. Our platform partner holds licenses for debt resolution services" [novdev2.com]

Novae's stated accreditations:
- **Direct Selling Association (DSA)**: Verified member in good standing [DSA](https://www.dsa.org/forms/CompanyFormPublicMembers/view?id=6E916F00000006)
- **BBB Accredited**: Since December 1, 2022, with A+ rating [BBB](https://www.bbb.org/us/ga/conyers/profile/financial-technology/novae-0443-27963932)
- **American Association for Debt Resolution (AADR)**: Claimed on Novae's website, though independent verification was not conclusively established

Novae does **not** appear on the FTC's list of companies and individuals banned from debt relief businesses [FTC Banned List](https://www.ftc.gov/legal-library/browse/cases-proceedings/banned-debt-mortgage-relief-providers/list).

### 2.4 Additional MLM Regulatory Considerations for Novae

#### State MLM/Chain Distributor Laws

Georgia (where Novae is headquartered) has specific laws governing multilevel distribution companies under O.C.G.A. § 10-1-410 through § 10-1-417:

- "No multilevel distribution company or participant shall operate a program where financial gains depend primarily on recruiting other participants rather than product sales" [Georgia Attorney General](https://consumer.georgia.gov/business-services/multilevel-distribution-companies-sale-business-opportunities)
- Any business opportunity requiring total initial payment over $500 must secure a $75,000 surety bond
- Income representations must be substantiated by documented data
- Participants have a right to cancel at any time

**26 states** have adopted business opportunity laws requiring disclosure, registration, and sometimes escrow accounts, with minimum initial investment thresholds as low as $200.

#### FTC Business Opportunity Rule

The FTC's Business Opportunity Rule (16 CFR Part 437) requires:
- Required disclosures at least seven calendar days before contract or payment
- Disclosure of litigation history, cancellation rights, earnings claims, and refund policies
- Substantiation of earnings claims with documented data

**Important**: MLMs are essentially exempt from the Business Opportunity Rule; instead, the FTC relies on Section 5 of the FTC Act to address deceptive practices.

#### FTC MLM Earnings Claims Guidance (2024)

In April 2024, the FTC released updated Business Guidance on Multi-Level Marketing requiring:
- Earnings claims must account for both income and expenses
- Income Disclosure Statements must reliably reflect typical distributor earnings
- Excluding participants who lost money or earned no money is misleading
- Atypical earnings claims must be accompanied by typical participant data [FTC](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/business-guidance-concerning-multi-level-marketing)

**Proposed MLM Earnings Claim Rule (January 2025):**
The FTC proposed a new rule specifically for the MLM industry that would:
- Prohibit deceptive earnings and related claims
- Require MLMs to maintain substantiation for earnings claims for three years
- Forbid providing participants with recruitment materials containing deceptive earnings claims

**Current Status**: On January 20, 2025, President Trump issued a regulatory freeze pending review. FTC Chair Andrew Ferguson closed public comments on these proposals. The fate of the proposed rules depends on future agency and administration decisions.

## Part 3: Risk and Compliance Analysis

### 3.1 Specific Regulatory Risks for Mesa Group Consulting

#### Risk 1: CROA Compliance for Direct Credit Repair Services

Mesa Group Consulting offers credit repair services directly. Their own terms state: "Credit repair services will be billed monthly beginning 30 days after enrollment" [Mesa Group Terms](https://www.mesagroupco.com/terms-and-conditions).

**Key Risks:**
- **Advance fee prohibition**: CROA strictly prohibits charging fees before services are fully performed. The 30-day billing cycle may be compliant, but careful structuring is required.
- **Broad definition of credit repair**: Under *Zimmerman v. Puccio*, even credit education/counseling aimed at improving credit behavior qualifies as credit repair.
- **Required disclosures**: Must provide written contract with three-day cancellation right, notice of right to dispute directly with credit bureaus.
- **Non-compliant contracts are void**: Any waiver of CROA protections by consumers is void.

#### Risk 2: FTC Telemarketing Sales Rule (Debt Relief)

If Mesa Group promotes or refers consumers to debt relief services (through Novae or otherwise), the TSR applies.

**Key Risks:**
- **Advance fee ban**: Cannot charge upfront fees for debt relief
- **Substantial assistance liability**: If Mesa Group knows Novae's partners charge advance fees or make misleading claims, Mesa Group could face liability
- **Recordkeeping**: Providers must keep written records for at least two years

#### Risk 3: FCRA Lead Generator Liability

Following the FTC v. ITMedia precedent, Mesa Group faces risk as a lead generator:

- **Consumer data misuse**: If consumer data submitted through Mesa Group's affiliate links is sold to credit repair marketers without consumer consent, Mesa Group could face FCRA violations
- **84% red flag**: The ITMedia case found 84% of loan applications went to marketers, not lenders. Mesa Group should verify that SuperMoney and Novae use consumer data appropriately

#### Risk 4: Affiliate Marketing Disclosure Requirements

Under FTC Endorsement Guidelines:

- **Material connection disclosure**: Any compensation from affiliates must be disclosed clearly and conspicuously [FTC](https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/endorsements-influencers-what-people-are-asking-ftc-staff)
- **Placement requirements**: Disclosures must be placed prominently — not buried in hashtags or comments
- **Language requirements**: Simple language like "advertisement," "ad," "sponsored," "paid link" — vague hashtags like "#sp" are insufficient [Gordon Law](https://gordonlaw.com/learn/ftc-endorsement-guidelines)

Mesa Group's current website appears to comply with these requirements, stating: "We may receive compensation through affiliate partnerships and referral arrangements" and "We partner with industry leaders like SmartCredit, SuperMoney, and Novae" [Mesa Group Homepage](https://www.mesagroupconsulting.com).

#### Risk 5: State Credit Repair Licensing

### 3.2 Multilingual Targeting Compliance (Spanish and Punjabi)

Mesa Group's trilingual capability (English, Spanish, Punjabi) is a competitive advantage but introduces additional compliance requirements.

#### Federal Requirements

**Executive Order 13166**: Requires federal agencies to ensure meaningful access for Limited English Proficiency (LEP) individuals. While this applies directly to federal agencies, the CFPB has extended expectations to financial services providers.

**CFPB Language Access Plan (2017, updated 2023)** :
- Over 26 million people in the U.S. have limited English proficiency
- Spanish speakers constitute nearly 64% of the LEP population
- The CFPB has found failure to offer account disclosures in languages other than English could constitute an abusive act or practice under UDAAP (Unfair, Deceptive, or Abusive Acts and Practices) [CFPB](https://www.consumerfinance.gov/about-us/the-bureau/)

**UDAAP Risk:** The CFPB's enforcement action against **Libre by Nexus** established that failure to provide Spanish-language disclosures when marketing in Spanish can be an abusive practice.

#### State Requirements

States like California, Arizona, Illinois, Oregon, and Texas generally require:
- Translations of sales contracts and disclosures in the consumer's native language if negotiations were conducted in that language
- Disclosure of terms clearly in the consumer's language
- Translation services where appropriate

**Consequences of Non-Compliance:**
- Consumer right to rescind the contract
- Exposure to regulatory investigations
- UDAAP violations enforced by the CFPB

**Punjabi-Specific Implications:**
While Punjabi is not one of CFPB's top translated languages, the general UDAAP framework applies equally. If Mesa Group markets extensively in Punjabi (through Chaklo Credit), failure to provide full translated contracts and disclosures in Punjabi could constitute a UDAAP violation.

### 3.3 Novae's WakeUpNow History — Reputational and Legal Risks

#### WakeUpNow Collapse Overview

WakeUpNow (WUN) was a multi-level marketing company founded in 2009 by Troy Muhlestein in Provo, Utah. The company marketed health and financial products including fitness supplements, energy drinks, telemedicine, personal financial management software, tax software, identity theft protection, and travel discounts [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WakeUpNow).

New CEO Phil Polich stated: "By taking advantage of the office of Chief Executive Officer, Kirby Cochran had made decisions that put the company on an irreparable negative trajectory" [TruthinAdvertising](https://truthinadvertising.org/articles/wake-now-put-sleep-closes-u-s-affiliate-operations).

### 3.4 Income Disclosure Reality and Earnings Claims Liability

#### Novae's Official Income Disclosure

Novae's disclaimer page explicitly states: "**The average participant in this business earned $1,408 in 2023**, your results may vary" [Novae Disclaimer](https://novaemoney.com/nsanders24/disclaimer).

#### DSSRC Monitoring Inquiry (Case #231-2025) — Closed October 14, 2025

The Direct Selling Self-Regulatory Council (DSSRC) operated by BBB National Programs conducted a monitoring inquiry into Novae LLC focusing on income and earnings claims.

**Claims Investigated (10 total):**
- "Over the first year, I was able to make enough money to quit my job, pay off all my debt. The next year I was making six figures in income, and three years later there were months where I was making six figures per month in income"
- "You can make up to thousands of dollars with referring people to Novae" [DSSRC](https://bbbprograms.org/programs/advertising/dssrc/cases/novae)

**Novae's Response:**
- Removed six of ten identified claims
- Suspended accounts of non-compliant salesforce members
- Coordinated with Facebook to remove unauthorized content

**DSSRC Assessment:**
"Earnings representations must reflect what a typical participant can realistically expect to achieve in income... Unqualified statements implying substantial or atypical success may mislead potential recruits" [DSSRC](https://bbbprograms.org/programs/advertising/dssrc/cases/novae).

**Case Resolution:** Closed October 14, 2025, with DSSRC acknowledging Novae's good-faith efforts but recommending continued monitoring.

#### FTC Enforcement Against Individual MLM Participants — April 2026

In a landmark action, the FTC took legal action against **Steven and Gina Merritt**, senior participants in the MLM company **LifeWave**, for making false and deceptive earnings claims. This is the FTC's **first major action against individual MLM participants** (not the corporate entity) [FTC](https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/04/ftc-takes-action-against-high-level-mlm-participants-who-deceived-workers-about-amount-money-they).

**Specific Claims:**
- Gina Merritt: "We're going to make you a — can I say buttload — a buttload of money... $25,000 or more a week"
- Steven Merritt: "The money keeps coming, even if you don't show up, and you can't stop it. It's like a spigot of water but a spigot full of $100 bills"

**Reality:**
- **79%** of active LifeWave participants earned **$0** in 2024
- Only **0.035%** earned more than $25,000 a week

**Implication for Novae Affiliates (including Mesa Group):** The FTC's willingness to pursue individual MLM participants for earnings claims represents a significant escalation. If Evert Calderon or Mesa Group makes earnings claims about Novae's income potential that deviate from the disclosed average ($1,408/year), they could face direct FTC action.
